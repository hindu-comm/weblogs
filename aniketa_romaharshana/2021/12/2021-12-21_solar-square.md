The Solar Square

``` wp-block-verse योऽशं  लुम्बंस्त  आदित्य भानो   नरध   भेश   ब। जिघर्ति     गगने     पेरो शं  नो  धिय  नमो  भग।। ```

``` wp-block-verse Yo'śaṃ   lumbaṃsta   Āditya Bhāno Naradha Bheśa Ba Jigharti     gagane     Pero śaṃ no dhiya namo Bhaga ```

This is a verse I composed recently. Here is its *Anvaya*, followed by its English translation:

``` wp-block-verse यः अशं लुम्बन् जिघर्ति गगने [हे] आदित्य भानो नरध [नरान् धरति यः] भेश [भाया ईशः] ब पेरो भग। ते नमः। शं नः धिय। ```

``` wp-block-verse O Ye who, destroying ill fourtune, glows in the sky, o son of Aditi! Promulgator of light! Sustainer of mankind! Lord of light! Ba! Nourisher! The brilliant one! Obeisance to Thee. Sustain fortune for us. ```

Superficially, the verse looks like just another hymn to the Sun God. But it hides a secret: the real highlight of this verse isn’t its meaning. Every pair of letters stands for a 2 digit number encoded using the [Kaṭapayādi map](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katapayadi_system#Rules_and_practices). Arranging the letters of the verse in 4 rows of 4 pairs (thus forming a 4×4 grid as shown), then substituting in the numbers according to Kaṭapayādi, we get a 4×4 [pan diagonal magic square](https://youtu.be/n65SJPmgTao)! (Traditionally called a *“*sarvatobhadra*“*, this is a magic square in which every diagonal, broken diagonal, and sub 2×2 square also adds up to the magic sum in addition to every row and column)
