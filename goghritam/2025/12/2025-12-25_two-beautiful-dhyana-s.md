devAmR^ita-varShiNI – देवामृत वर्षिणी

## Two beautiful dhyAna-s

[![](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/gemini_generated_image_ney6hnney6hnney6.png?w=687)](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/gemini_generated_image_ney6hnney6hnney6.png)

A few genuine, erudite, revered and powerful shAkta lineages both from the North and South seem to have an altered version of the jAtavedAgni durgA’s dhyAna from the shAradA for their chaNDikA/saptashatI dhyAna. The dhyAna shloka in discussion is the standard vidyuddAma samaprabhAM mR^igapati skanda stithAM bhIShaNAM. Their version gives the interpretation of devI holding a pAsha and displaying the tarjanI in the front hands and in the backmost hands are seen the chakra and gada. These great lineages see a good reason why devI is specifically conceived in their tradition this way and people from this lineage are expected to stick to this dhyAna.

However the original form intended in the texts is mostly same but with two differences. The front hands hold the trishUla and tarjanI and the back hands hold the chakra and sha~Nkha. There seem to be two reasons for the alternative interpretation among these great lineages and the first is due to the understanding of the pada, guNa as pAsha which is not wrong per se, as the dictionary meaning for guNa is a string or thread and is used in some contexts to indicate deity holding a pAsha. The second reason seems to be the shloka being transmitted as hastaishchakra gadAsi instead of the original hastaishchakra darAsi. dara being sha~Nkha/conch shell. The gIta press version of the durgA saptashati translates the shloka as devI holding gada and pAsha and even shrI sudhAkara mAlavIya’s Hindi translation of the shAradA tilaka translates it likewise. Below, we will look at the original sources and the meaning.

The popular source of the shloka is the shAradA tilaka and is as follows.

vidyuddAmasamaprabhAM mR^igapatiskandhasthitAM bhIShaNAM  
kanyAbhiH karavAla kheTavilasaddhastAbhirAsevitAm \|  
hastaishchakradarAsikheTavishiikhaM chApaM guNaM tarjanIM  
bibhrANAmanalAtmikAM shashidharAM durgAM trinetrAM smaret

विद्युद्दामसमप्रभां मृगपतिस्कन्धस्थितां भीषणां  
कन्याभिः करवाल खेटविलसद्धस्ताभिरासेविताम्‌ ।  
हस्तैश्चक्रदरासिखेटविशीखं चापं गुणं तर्जनीं  
बिभ्राणामनलात्मिकां शशिधरां दुर्गां त्रिनेत्रां स्मरेत्‌

This is the dhyAna given for jAtavedAgni durgA. The commentator on the shAradA tilaka, rAghava bhaTTa in his work padArthAdarshA, clarifies that dara here refers to sha~Nkha/conch shell and guNaM is a reference to the trishUla. He refers to the relevant section of the prapa~nchasAra tantra where the same mantra/deity is expounded to clarify the same.

The prapa~nchasAra in unambiguous terms shows devI as holding .ari sha~NkhAsi kheTeShviShvAsAkhya trishUlAnari gaNa bhayadAM tarjanIM i.e. ari – chakra, sha~NkhaM, asi – sword, kheTa – shield, iShuH – arrow, iShvAsa – dwelling(?) of the arrow i.e. bow, trishUla – trident and and ari gaNa bhayada tarjanI – a gesture threatening groups/multitudes of foes. rAghava bhaTTa further cites another long dhyAna from a different text as a reference, to show that this is the set of Ayudha-s meant to be carried by the deity. Since the deity is jAtavedAgni durgA, it does makes sense to show her with sha~Nkha and chakra with durgA being primarily a vaiShNava deity. Likewise, the trishUla is more appropriate for the durgA tattva and her activities highlighted in the durgA/chaNDI saptashati.

Both versions of the dhyAna are beautiful and meaningful and sAdhaka-s are expected to follow what is taught as per lineage, unless there is a direct instruction or sign from the devata to lean towards a particular dhyAna. Even in such cases, if one learnt this from a shAkta/tAntrika lineage, it is a good idea to discuss or get permission from the teacher. For sAdhaka-s from the smArta lineages practicing the same, the shift is probably easier and less prone to risk. As I was wondering whether this note should be made or not and flipping the pages of a book on another shAkta deity, who is known to prominently hold a trishUla and kapAla in her front hands, chanced upon her dhyAna shloka which had the description “..guNa satkapAlinI…” i.e. holding the guNa – trishUla and a good kapAla. Moving back further to much ancient times, the tarjanI is most likely the dakShiNAchAra or smArta attempt to sanitize the fearsome kapAla as we have alternate dhyAna-s of vanadurgA describing her as holding shUla kapAla in front hands in one shloka and as holding shUla and displaying tarjanI in another shloka. This maybe similar to how the khaTvA~Nga that is held in one of the hands of pa~ncha mukha A~njaneya/hanumAn or in the hands of nIlakaNTha mahArudra, is replaced by the leg of a cot in some traditions. The ari-gaNa-bhayada-tarjanI is to have the same effect as a kapAla filled with blood (of pashu/enemies).

To summarize, both dhyAna-s are correct, beautiful and have special meanings and context, though the version with trishUla tarjanI(or kapAla/karoTikA in an ancient sense) and sha~Nkha chakra is more accurate.
