devAmR^ita-varShiNI – देवामृत वर्षिणी

## The vaiShNava and shaiva reflections of sAlva-rAjabhairavI

There seem to be two distinct but closely related/woven traditions concerning the deity, with the earlier version of the deity showing vaiShNava leanings and the same deity with a more shaiva flavor from a later time. The similarities and the differences between these two aspects will be explored below along with a list of both the general and special prayoga-s associated with the deity.

The deity with clear vaiShNava aspects can be found in compendiums like prapa~nchAsAra, shAradA tilaka and the paddhati of the Ishana shiva guru deva, of which some of the features are:  
1) No particular association with pakShi rAja is noted.  
2) The kanya-s/dUti-s associated with, are jayA, vijayA, subhadrA, & shUla kAtyAyanI  
3) Along with mUla mantra there are fewer mantra-s deployed.  
4) Worshipped in a simple ShaTkoNa yantra with aShTa dala and bhUpura outside  
5) Is not necessarily associated with other devata mantra-s, though it can be combined based on the situation and taste of the mantrin.  
6) She is primarily a vaiShNava devata and is bhaginI of kR^iShNa-devakI putra as vindhyavAsinI. The a~Nga-s are five without the netra traya like for many vaiShNava deities.  
7) Sometimes prefixed with mAyA, nR^isiMha and sva-bIja of the devata for particular viniyoga, as explained in the commentary for shAradA or prapa~nchasAra  
8) No particular stress on a special gAyatrI in the texts, though there is a gAyAtri followed in actual traditions.  
9) The iconography of the four maidens is different. Each have their own varNa, hasta-s and Ayudha-s.  
10) The association with pratya~NgirA is in a subtle non explicit manner.  
11) Takes on an altered form when deployed for prayoga and there is no mention of the permutations and combinations of mUla mantra unlike in the other tradition.  
12) AvaraNa pUja does not necessarily include brAhmyAdi mAtR^ika-s.

While the tradition where her shaiva reflection is prominent, has the following special features:  
1) She is pakShirAja patnI and is meditated as stationed upon one of the wings.  
2) The chaturbhagini are given as the kanya-s: jayA, vijayA, ajitA, & aparAjitA – As outlined in the kalpa and tantra and the kavacha, with only jayA and vijayA being common with other tradition.  
3) There are several associated mantra-s and the mUla mantra is prefixed or suffixed with special bIja-s for various ends.  
4) Invoked in yantra-s which are relatively complex compared to the other tradition.  
5) Is frequently combined with other devata-s and invoked in combined mantra-s.  
6) Clear and rather aggressive shaiva associations with slightly anti-vaiShNava themes at times.  
7) Presence of the prayoga-s involving permutations and combinations of the various pada-s of mUla mantra for various ends.  
8) Presence of six a~Nga-s instead of five unlike the other tradition  
9) Appears rather late in time, and is more elaborate.  
10) Associated with pratya~NgirA and other deities explicitly  
11) Has a specific gAyatrI used for mantra sandhyA and for arghya  
12) The mUla mantra is prefixed with ramA, parA and sva-bIja as per the tantra of this devata.  
13) AvaraNa includes mAtR^ika-s and other deities like rudra vIrabhadra etc

Here are a few inferences that we can draw from the above. The details mentioned in the shAradA were most likely extracted from 1) An earlier source which is different from the current available kalpa published by Thanjavur sarasvatI mahal or 2) Portions of the earlier text were lost and what we have now is rather a shaiva redaction retaining lot of material from the older texts.

The commentary on the shAradA gives an example of viSha chikitsa briefly and mentions, “like this, other viShas can be removed”. The commentator apparently is referring to the viSha chikista portion of the current kalpa, which means this portion of the text was retained from earlier times(i.e. before the possible shaiva redaction).

The shaiva reflection of the deity is quite aggressive towards other deities and even has names against vaiShNava deities like sudarshana and nR^isiMha like sudarshanAstra darpaghnI, in her sahasranAma. The sahasranAma of this deity is a great work, is quite pleasing to hear or recite, and has only 4 or 5 names against nR^isiMha, sudarshana and varAha. A regular upAsaka is not required to focus on the literal meanings of these four or five names unless there is a special context which calls for the same. Despite this, some tAntrik traditions still have the sudarshana ShaDakShari as the astra vidyA of this deity. In terms of raw power, the deity maybe equivalent to the combined sudarshana-nR^isiMha form and can even handle equivalent functions along with ability to even handle attacks from at-least some mahAvidya-s.

While not meant to be a comprehensive list, here is a list of sAmAnya and visheSha prayoga-s associated with the devata.

1\) viSha chikitsa : To remove, mUShika, vR^ishchika, sarpa, shunakAdi viSha-s.  
2) vandhyA chikitsa /Treatment for infertility – This subject seems to have been dealt with at some length. To treat many types of vandhya-s/infertile conditions in females.  
3) vashya & sammohana prayoga-s  
4) mArana & uchchATana  
5) digbandhana prayoga – Longer digbandhana prayoga with many mantra-s and a shorter one with a single mantra.  
6) pratikriyA prayoga  
7) pratikriyA digbandhanaM  
8) sarva shAnti prayoga with the sarva shanti stavaM along with its procedure  
9) The pada mantra prayoga-s – Permutations and combinations with the padas of the mUla mantra – a total of 94 viniyoga-s are mentioned.  
10) Various prayoga-s involving long mantra-s which also includes the long mAlA mantra  
11) As I am unsure how to categorize this, we will call them the “bahurUpa prayoga-s”. These mantra-s are in a special format asking to deity to fulfill various wishes by taking on the forms of other deities or her gaNa-s taking the natural form like a tiger or snake to achieve mundane objectives.  
12) The hiraNyeshvarI prayoga for wealth  
13) The grAsinI prayoga to punish the wicked.  
14 The chatuShShaShThi laxmI prayoga – This is found only in one copy so far but a hint of it is given in one of the stotra-s of the deity.  
15) svapna prayoga-s: Prayoga-s meant to manipulate svapna-s similar to those of vArAhI.

While most prayoga-s are standard, the pada mantra prakaraNa and the bahurUpa prayoga-s stand out as being exclusive for this deity. Some of the interesting bahurUpa prayoga-s are 1) To get one’s enemy robbed by thieves 2) To make the king of the place angry with one’s enemy and punish him. 3) Make the enemy bitten by serpents based on the command of the deity.
