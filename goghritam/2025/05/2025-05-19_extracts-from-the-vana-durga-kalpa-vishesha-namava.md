devAmR^ita-varShiNI – देवामृत वर्षिणी

## Extracts from the vana durgA kalpa – visheShanAmAvali

[![](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-1.png?w=390)](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-1.png)

A generic stuti which may be used to address any durgA and maybe uttered towards the end of the anuShThAna. This is from the second paTala of [vana durgA kalpa](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/2020/10/20/two-streams-of-vanadurga-upasana/) and the context is durgA dhyAna after pUrNAhuti and pradakShiNa.

tataH pradakShiNaM kR^itvA praNamya prArthayedbudhaH

durge durge mahAdurge sarva durga nivAriNi.

aGYAnato GYAnato vA mohAdvAyatkR^itaM mayA

kShamyatAM deva deveshI prasIda parameshvarI

yadyadiShTatamaM loke prayachCha mama suvrate

varaM me dIyatAM devi bhaktasya mama sundari

karmaNA manasA vAchA tuShTA.asi parameshvarI

yanmayA prArthitaM devI tatkuruShva mahAvrate

tvatprAsAdAnmahAdevI shubhaM bhavatu me sadA

evaM prArthya mahAdevIM namaskuryAt pR^ithak pR^ithak

ततः प्रदक्षिणं कृत्वा प्रणम्य प्रार्थयेद्बुधः

दुर्गे दुर्गे महादुर्गे सर्व दुर्ग निवारिणि.

अज्ञानतो ज्ञानतो वा मोहाद्वायत्कृतं मया

क्षम्यतां देव देवेशी प्रसीद परमेश्वरी

यद्यदिष्टतमं लोके प्रयच्छ मम सुव्रते

वरं मे दीयतां देवि भक्तस्य मम सुन्दरि

कर्मणा मनसा वाचा तुष्टाऽसि परमेश्वरी

यन्मया प्रार्थितं देवी तत्कुरुष्व महाव्रते

त्वत्प्रासादान्महादेवी शुभं भवतु मे सदा

एवं प्रार्थ्य महादेवीं नमस्कुर्यात्‌ पृथक्‌ पृथक्‌

vanadhUrgatAyai namaH or vanadurgAyai namaH – total 37.

jala durgA & giri durgA: Given the context it is highly unlikely that forts or structures are being referred to. We can be sure of giri durgA which is a specific mantra of durgA meant for shatru nAshana. Same may be applied to jala durgA, pavana durgA & pAtAla durgA (similar to pAtAla bhairavI or pAtAla nR^isiMha).

dIpa-durgA – Well known form of durgA to help sAdhaka-s with trikAla GYAna/atIta-anAgata GYAna i.e. knowledge of past present and future regarding any specific issue/trouble.

**\*** pa~ncha durgAyai namaH – namaH to the five durgA-s. It is unclear which set of durgA-s is being referred to. This is a possible reference to the five durgA-s i.e. ati durgA, gANi durgA, vishva-durgA, sindhu-durgA & agni durgA. Or it might be referring to the set of five durgA-s invoked in the durgA dIpa namaskAra vidhi i.e. shakti-durgA, rAja-durgA, agni-durgA, aishvarya-durgA & rakShA-durgA. Or one may think of both sets of durgA-s while uttering the name.

**\*\***– My version of the vanadurgA kalpa gives tokadA – One who gives sons. I speculate it could be [trotalA – A devI associated with tvaritA](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/1878183319051088255) which is the next name and seems appropriate by context, though both names are meaningful. The name tvaritA being followed by the name tUrNA makes the case for trotalA even stronger. “Her gAyatrI identifies her with trotalA another goddess from the garuDa-srotas: [tvaritā-vidyāyai vidmahe trotalā-vidyāyai dhīmahi \| tan nas tūrṇā pracodayāt \|”](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/1878183319051088255)

The thirty seven names are most likely meant to correspond to the 37 letters of the vanadurgA mantra. Among the few versions of the vanadurgA mantra, the thirty seven lettered mantra is what is expounded and recommended in the vanadurgA kalpa. All rites of this text are to be performed with this 37 lettered mantra, though additional bIja-s might be added based on context as suggested in this kalpa.
