devAmR^ita-varShiNI – देवामृत वर्षिणी

## bhairava nAma nirukti

![](https://goghritam.files.wordpress.com/2021/01/vatuka-1.jpg?w=642)

bhayA (bhiyA) sarvaM ravayati sarvado vyApako.akhile

iti bhairava shabdasya santatochchAraNAchChivaH

By constant muttering of the bhairava shabda, with the anusandhAna, that
bhairava is samvit prakAsha (luminous consciousness) which
resounds-reflects/perceives(ravayati – viMR^ishati) everything,
bestowing everything and as present in all space & time, one attains
shiva.

For alternate reading of bhiyA: bhairava is one who protects all from
fear, by showing that the mass of external knowledge/experiences
(vedyarAsheH) like blue (or the lack of it) and internal experiences
like happiness (or the lack of it), as non-different from oneself. The
vAkya of the bR^ihadAraNyaka upaniShad, “dvitIyAd vai bhayaM bhavati” is
recalled by commentators to impress upon the student that fear always
arises from something different than oneself. By granting such abheda
bodha, bhairava relieves beings from fear. A devata under the right
circumstances teaches either by leading the sAdhaka to the relevant
texts or by orchestrating a set of events to generate the relevant
anubhava/experience to the sAdhaka or by both if required. The anubhava
of pUrva siddha-s as also the anubhava of contemporary
practitioners/siddha-s gives us this impression.

Alternately, bhairava is so called because he removes fear of saMsAra or
any lesser or greater fear. Or bhairava is one who performs bharaNa
(maintenance), ravaNa (withdrawal) and vamana (projection)of the
universe. Or “bhairavo bharaNAtmako mahAmantraravAtmakashcha” – bhairava
is one who supports and protects the cosmos and constantly sounds the
great mantra of I (ahaM).

Uttering the shabda bhairava-bhairava constantly with the above
anusandhAna, is said to pierce the hR^idaya granthi and relieve one from
the bonds of the mass of pashubhAva, thereby providing the sAdhaka the
experience of his svakAya as being made-of/filled-with parAkAsha as also
providing the experience of possessing of ananta sharIra-s.

Or one can see parallels to the anuShTup.h:

bhiyA sarvaM ravayati – bhiyA /bhayA can be compared with the pada-s
bhIShaNaM, sarvaM ravayati with jvalantaM & sarvatomukhaM of anuShTup.

-भीषणमित्याह भीषा वा अस्मादादित्य उदेति भीतश्चन्द्रमा भीतो वायुर्वाति
भीतोऽग्निर्दहति भीतः पर्जन्यो वर्षति.

-अथ कस्मादुच्यते ज्वलन्तमिति यस्मात्स्वमहिम्ना
सर्वाॅंल्लोकान्सर्वान्देवान्सर्वानात्मनः सर्वाणि स्वतेजसा ज्वलति
ज्वालयति ज्वाल्यते ज्वालयते सविता प्रसविता दीप्तो दीपयन्दीप्यमानः ज्वलं
ज्वलिता तपन्वितपन्त्संतपन्रोचनो रोचमानः शोभनः शोभमानः
कल्याणस्तस्मादुच्यते ज्वलन्तमिति ॥-सर्वतोमुखमित्याह सर्वतः खल्वयं
मुखवान्विश्वरूपत्वात् ।

sarvado vyApako.akhile…shivaH- sarvadA & shivaH can be compared with the
pada bhadraM & vyApaka with mahAviShNuM.

-भद्रमित्याह भद्रः खल्वयं श्रिया जुष्टः

As bhairava is one who saves beings from all fears including mR^ityu,
the pada will be compared with mR^ityumR^ityuM. This also becomes
relevant as yamAntaka is the deity for one of the akShara-s of the
anuShTup.

-मृत्योर्मृत्युमित्याह मृत्योर्वा अयं मृत्युरमृतत्वं प्रजानामन्नादानाम्

``` wp-block-preformatted
-अथ कस्मादुच्यते मृत्युमृत्युमिति यस्मात्स्वमहिम्ना स्वभक्तानां स्मृत एव मृत्युमपमृत्युं च मारयति ।
य आत्मदा बलदा यस्य विश्व उपासते प्रशिषं यस्य देवाः यस्य छायामृतं यो मृत्युमृत्युः कस्मै
देवाय हविषा विधेम तस्मादुच्यते मृत्युमृत्युमिति ॥
```

As the para shaMbhu mahimna stuti says:

ugraM shAntaM ca vIraM sakalamaya mahAviShNuM agniM jvalantaM

sarvatrAnantavaktraMnaramR^igavapuShaMbhIShaNaM sarvabhadram.h.

sarvaGYaM mR^ityumR^ityuM bahuvidhanutibhirvanditaM sarvalokai

rvishvAhaMkAra bIjaM paramashiva guruM tvAM bhaje mantrarAjaM..86..

shrI VV Dvivedi’s and shrI Bapulal’s works.
