devAmR^ita-varShiNI – देवामृत वर्षिणी

## The roar ofmahArudra

[![](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/khadga-ravana-yantra.jpg?w=344)](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/khadga-ravana-yantra.jpg)

[![](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/image-8.png?w=221)](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/image-8.png)

There seems to be a misconception among some from the Telugu lands about the nature of a certain mantra. During a talk on the efficacy of mantra-s and an ensuing general discussion on mantra practice, the learned person in his Telugu YouTube channel, mentioned that, though quite effective in the context of controlling bhUta-s/duShTa graha-s and in destroying one’s enemies, it makes one angry and lustful because the khaDga rAvaNa mantra is primarily a mantra of rAvaNa the asura of lanka.

The reason given is that the elders he follows including a certain pIThAdhipati, mentioned that rAvaNAsura is the deity for this mantra. It is further added that rAvaNa did shaktyupAsana and obtained a khaDga and hence he became khaDga rAvaNa as mentioned in one of his works, by a learned Telugu poet shrI vishvanAtha satyanArAyaNa.

The AvaraNa pUja of rudra in his form as khaDga rAvaNa does include kumbhakarNa and shmashAna-vibhIShaNa due to which, at first it seemed possible that this potentially added to the confusion. However, someone who went through the detailed AvaraNa pUja would notice that it is various shaiva devata-s who form the majority of the deities in the AvaraNa. It is clear then that the word rAvaNa is being misunderstood here to mean the rAvaNAsura, due to 1) Non availability of proper tAntrika material related to the deity and 2) As Michael Slouber (MS) noted, this might be due to “Texts which I suggest are later than both of these, such as the Ravanakumara tantra, go further by losing the distinction between Ravana and Khadga Ravana altogether.”

MSs thesis on the kriyAkAla guNottara helps us understand the context of the mantra better. The deity here is called as rAvaNa because the deity khaDga-rAvaNa arose from shiva’s rAva i.e. roar.

[![](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/image.png?w=798)](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/image.png)

[![](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/image-2.png?w=849)](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/image-2.png)

MS notes that the text does not explain the prefix khaDga. The itihAsa, we are told, gives an [account of the detail of rudra’s association with the khaDga](http://Texts%20which%20I%20suggest%20are%20later%20than%20both%20of%20these,%20such%20as%20the%20R!va%22akum!ratantra,%20go%20further%20by%20losing%20the%20distinction%20between%20R%22-%20va#a%20and%20Kha!gar%22va#a%20altogether.)/sword, some of which is reproduced below from the AchArya’s blog.

“*Then the great prajApati declared: This being whom I have conceived is known as asi, and he shall effect the destruction of the enemies of the gods. Then that being assumed the form of a blazing, sharp-edged sword glowing like the flame at the end of the kalpa. brahmA then gave that sword to the blue-necked rudra with the bull-banner and asked him to put down pApa and adharma.*

*Then that rudra praised by the great R^ishi-s took up that sword and assumed a terrible form. He put forth four arms and filled the space between heaven and earth. Flames gushed out of his mouth and he assumed diverse colors of blue, white and red. He wore an upper garment of black deerskin studded with stars of gold and he bore an eye on his forehead that resembled the sun in splendor. His other two eyes were black and tawny. The awful mahAdeva also picked up huge shield with 3 studs resembling a black mass of clouds with flashes of lightning. Then the great deva uttered terrible roars and laughing awfully, began to whirl the sword in the sky desiring a showdown with the demons…. …rudra gave this sword dyed with the blood of the daityas to the deva viShNu. viShNu gave it to the deva indra. indra then gave it to the other devas. They presented that mighty sword to manu. Giving it to him they said: O manu protect the world with this sword having dharma in its womb….*

*…kR^ittika is the nakShatra of the sword. rohiNi the gotra, agni the deity, and rudra the maharshi. It is verily dharmapAla the upholder of dharma.*“

The following additional points can be considered to refute that rAvaNAsura is the deity. 1)The devata is called as khaDga rAvaNa rudra or otherwise as bhUtesha which is just another name for rudra/shiva. 2) The dhyAna for the khaDga rAvaNa mantra clearly shows that it is rudra in a special form and clearly not rAvaNa the asura who is ten headed. 3) Even the mantra has explicit and clear features of rudra/shiva and no features or attributes of rAvaNa the asura are found in the mAlA mantra. The khaDga rAvaNa mantra itself calls the deity as pashupati and rudra. 4) The majority of the deities in the AvaraNa pUja of this deity are a mix of both common and uncommon shaiva deities. So identifying or equating the devata with rAvaNa the asura is incorrect.

Also an afterthought based on only speculation with remote chance of being true or even absurd from a historical point of view for multiple reasons: Is it possible that an asura named rAvaNa mastered this mantra, had darshana of this mantrAdhiShThAna devata who is a special form of rudra and this mantra being the probable source of the asura’s power and maybe oral traditions remember the asura’s connection with this mantra?
