devAmR^ita-varShiNI – देवामृत वर्षिणी

## Indra upAsana outside the realm of theshruti

[![](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/gemini_generated_image_3xvsck3xvsck3xvs.png?w=535)](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/gemini_generated_image_3xvsck3xvsck3xvs.png)

Being a vaidika devata, it makes sense to look to the shruti and ancillary works derived from the same, to understand the deity and technicalities of invoking the devata. Though this can in theory be done by all three varNa-s, for most practical purposes this seems now limited to a section of brAhmaNa-s.

It might be reasonable to speculate that indra upAsana outside the shruti is quite limited. By upAsana here is meant, dedicated practice of an indra mantra which is not directly from the veda, yantra making, prayoga-s and transmission of the same and not public practices like the indra jAtra in honor of the deity. It probably happens, but on such a small scale to make it look non-existent. Here a brief note on indra upAsana will be made. Even here, the practice is quite dependent on notions derived from the shruti.

In the context of vAstu remedies, installation of an indra yantra is suggested for removing the following vAstu doSha-s. The house being built on land which is not level, doSha-s related to the siMhadvAra on the East, viSha bAhu sthala doShaM, vyajanAkAra sthala doShaM, IshAnya shalya doShaM, having inappropriate constructions on the North and North-Eastern directions, gR^iha shUla, digvedha, vR^ikShAchChAdana, parvata chChAyA saMbhava shatru dR^iShTi, vIdhi shUlAdi doSha-s, vatyasta prAkAra dvAra nirmAna doSha-s etc. and many other doSha-s, it is said can be remedied by indra yantra in addition to granting, kShema-safety, saukhya-happiness, lAbha-profit, yashas -fame and saMpada
-riches.

Being the yantra of devarAja, who wields the great vajra and of amogha shakti, indra is seen as a great and appropriate devata for shatru saMhAra, pratikUla nAshana i.e. getting rid of unwanted or unprofitable situations, and for attaining high positions. It is generally added that his propitiation in general, brings in good things like Ayu, Arogya, vamsha vR^iddhi, sukha and jaya.

Similarly an indra-vAruNI yantra which requires propitiation with an indra-vAruNI mantra is suggested for arresting great ariShTa-s/calamities, and for all general good mentioned above in addition to removing vAstu doSha-s like pa~nka vedha, jalAshaya doSha, building houses in the middle of rivers, lakes, ponds, other water related vAstu doSha-s and for situations where a sewer is flowing before the siMha dvAra of the house.

**indra & indra vAruNI mantra-s:**

asya shrI indra mahA mantrasya, brahma R^iShiH, pa~nktishchandaH, indro devata, laM bIjaM, jaM shaktiH, svAhA kIlakaM, samasta upadrava nAshanArthe jape viniyogaH. nyAsa – lAMAdi ShaT.

pIta varNaM sahasrAkShaM vajra padmakaraM vibhuM

sarvAlaMkAra saMyuktaMnaumIndraM dikpatIshvaraM

indramairAvatArUDhaM marudgaNa samanvitaM

sahasrAkSha surA sevya dhyAyechChatru palAyanaM

manu: praNava, namo vishva rUpAya! shachI pataye! svarga kAma phala pradAya! mAyA-ramA-bija-shakti ***vajrapANaye*** agni vadhU.

indra gAyatri: sahasrAkShAya vidmahe, vajra-hastAya dhImahi, tanno indraH prachodayAt

asya shrI indra vAruNI mahA mantrasya, bhArgava R^iShiH, triShTup chandaH, indra vAruNyau devau, mAyA bIjaM, laM shaktiH, vaM kIlakaM, sarvAbhIShTa siddhyarthe jape viniyogaH – nyAsa hrAMAdi ShaT.

dhyAnaM:

indro vAruNo yugmakaM praNavakaM bindu trinetrojjvalaM

bhAsvadrudra mukhaM shivAMghri yugalaM pArshvastha kAlA .analaM

udyad bhAskara koTi koTi sadR^ishaM devau jagadvyApinaM

shakrApaH ddaivate hR^idaMbuja puTe nityaM sadA bhAvaye

manuH 1: praNava, bIjaM, indra-manu-shaktiH, kIlakaM, mAyA, ramA, mahA bala parAkrama shAlinyau deva jalA .adhipatau indra vAruNIbhyAM namaH.

manu 2: praNava, pAsha, mAyA, ankusha, bIjaM, indra-manu-shaktiH, kIlakaM mAyA ramA kAma-bIja, deva jalA .adhipatyAbhyAM namaH

Additionally there is a mantra in Telugu for indra for stopping bleeding when injured. One may imagine that this might have been deployed extensively during and after the ferocious battles with the unmatta-s that the Telugu people fought with, before and after the fall of the kAkatIya-s. This was mentioned in one of the excellent books published by the siddheshvarI pIThaM in Courtallam, Tamil Nadu. It goes ” indruDA! chandruDA! debba tagilinadi devendruDA! netturu kaTTu, nA guruvAna (guruvu+Ana) nA yAna (Ana).” “इन्द्रुडा! चन्द्रुडा! देब्ब तगिलिनदि देवेन्द्रुडा! नेत्तुरु कट्टु, ना गुरुवान ना यान.” “**ఇంద్రుడా! చంద్రుడా! దెబ్బ తగిలినది దేవేంద్రుడా! నెత్తురు కట్టు, నా గురువాన నా యాన**.” It can be translated into ” Oh indra, oh chandra, (I or a specific person or we) was wounded devendra. Stop the bleeding (netturu – blood, kaTTu – stop) as it is my guru’s command and my command”. In the Telugu lands and probably outside too, it is common to praise someone great as indra and chandra. However the deity of focus for this mantra is clearly indra as he is exclusively called upon with the term devendra. As is the practice for all shAbhara mantra-s, it ends with, it is my Guru’s command and my command which is found even in shAbara mantra-s of narasiMha and other deities. Apart form this we have the popular mantra from arresting thieves from the mantra mahArNava which goes “indrAgni bandha bandha”. While this post is not meant to present a comprehensive list of all indra mantra-s not directly derived from the shruti, in the future, such an exercise might be undertaken by those interested.
