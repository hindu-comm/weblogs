devAmR^ita-varShiNI – देवामृत वर्षिणी

## Notes from sarvAtma shaMbhu’s siddhAntaprakAshika

[![](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/sadashiva.jpg?w=720)](https://goghritam.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/sadashiva.jpg)

pa~ncha vidhAni shAstrANi: laukikaM, vaidikaM, AdhyAtmikaM, atimArgaM, mAntraM cha iti.

shAstra is of five types: laukikaM, vaidikaM, AdhyAtmikaM, atimArgaM, and mAntraM/mantra mArga

**laukikaM** – Shastra-s like Ayurveda and daNDa nIti which give visible results.

**vaidikaM** – vaidika kriya-s, pUrva mImAMsa, nyAya vaisheShika etc

**AdhyAtmikaM** – sAMkhya, yoga and vedAnta/uttara mImAMsa.

Between the enumeration of the atimArga and AdhyAtmika, SP discusses lokAyata, bauddha, jaina, and pA~ncharAtra and also explains where in the scheme of 36 tattva-s, these paths belong to or where they end.

**atimArgaM** – The systems of the pAshupata, kApAlika and mahAvrata revealed by rudra.

**mAntraM** – mantra mArga – The siddhAnta mArga revealed by shiva.

Further details of atimArga and mAntraM will be noted below:

**[pAshupataM](https://manasataramgini.wordpress.com/2009/07/31/further-considerations-on-the-pashupata-system/)** – There is no ANava mala. Things experience happiness and misery due to mAyA mala and karma pAsha. At an appropriate time when the individual is mature and ripe with vairAgya, and on acquiring dIkSha into the pAshupata system, the [highest knowledge](https://manasataramgini.wordpress.com/2009/08/03/the-accomodation-of-women-and-shudra-s-in-the-pashupata-system/) aspect of parameshvara is transferred.

**mahAvrataM**: An ANava mala along with the bandhana that comes with with karma and mAyA is recognized. Moxa here comes by acquiring the dikSha in this system followed by the AchAra that comes along with it like wearing of bones etc. Such individuals are granted the GYAna shakti by parameshvara but not the kriyA shakti. The possession of the shakti dvaya it is said, is exclusively for parameshvara.

**kApAla/kApAlika mataM**: Their impression of the characteristic of the self (Atma laxaNaM) and the characteristics/nature of bondage i.e. bandha laxaNaM is similar to those of pAshupata and mahAvrataM. Moxa is attained by the dIkSha into the kApAlika system and following the practices prescribed such as unfurling-the-black-flag(? text says: shyAmaikadhvaja patAkAyAM vyAptAyAM) and begging and eating in a bowl made of skull. Three rudra-s who reside in the mayA and vidyA tattva-s are said to have revealed these paths of pAshupata, mahAvrata and kApAla.

**mAntraM/[mantra mArgaM](https://manasataramgini.wordpress.com/2006/12/10/the-matta-mayura-vamsha-of-mantra-marga-siddhantin-s/)/siddhAnta shAstra**: This shAstra is to two types. adhaH srotas i.e. lower [streams of scriptures](https://manasataramgini.wordpress.com/2005/03/26/the-primary-tantras-of-rudra/) which refer to gAruDa, dakShINa vAma and bhUta associated with the tatpuruSha, aghora, vAmadeva and sadyojAta faces of shiva.

**gAruDaM**: The tatpuruSha mukha of shiva is to be seen as brahma svarUpa. The subject of these gAruDa tantra-s is mantra-s and herbs for curing snake bites and other sarpa (viSha) chikitsa.

**dakShiNa**: The aghora mukha of shiva is seen as parameshvara and the practitioners seek to to attain the darshana (pratyakShIkaraNa) of [aghora-parameshvara](https://manasataramgini.wordpress.com/2010/01/05/a-discursion-on-the-sanatkumariya-panchabrahma-siddha-shamkara-tantra-and-the-early-evolution-of-the-dakshinashaiva-system/) through yoga, along with the abilities to conquer shatru-s through mantra-yoga.

**vAma**: Importance to vAmadeva mukha and from information outside the siddhAnta prakAshika, is related to tumburu rudra and chatur bhagini. According to SP, it is also associated with rasa-vAdaM i.e. alchemy

**bhUta**: Is associated with the sadyojAta mukha with a primary focus on the usage of mantra-s and herbs to resolve problems caused by bhUta-preta-pishAchAdi.

By something originating in the shakti tattva (shakti tattva vAsinA kenachidAtmanA shAktaM tantraM uktaM) comes the shAkta tantra. All four streams mentioned above were propagated by shiva (shiva praNItAni) to instill faith (due to the visible or perceptible results arising from their practice or prayoga) in the siddhAnta system.

The other/other part of siddhAnta shAstra has two parts. The shiva bheda and rudra bheda with 10 and 18 Agama-s respectively. The ten Agama-s begining with kAmikA were said to be spoken of by sadyojAta and vAmadeva mukha-s, while the 18 Agama-s are spoken by aghora, tatpuruSha and IshAna mukha-s of shiva. These 28 Agama-s constitute the proper siddhAnta mArga.
