## Notes

Trulkhors numbered thirty or thirty-two seem to be common in various Tibetan Buddhist traditions such as the Shangpa and Sakya lineages, for example Amrtasiddhi thirty-two trulkhors, Sakya lamdre thirty-two trulkhors. This practice is associated with the Drukpa Kagyu tradition. The text is called Tiphu thirty trulkhor. It is not clear whether the thirty refers to the number of trulkhors or to the thirty lines of verse. There are few different versions of this text and practice in the Drukpa Dharma Treasury texts. This article is based on two texts written by two different people, Ngawang Nyendrak Palzangpo and Ajita Tingzin Phuntsok.I will add the Tibetan texts with the full translation of this text in the future.

# Tiphu Thirty Trulkhors

## I.

Place your shinbones and toes on the floor and your buttocks on your heels. Place your hands on your knees. Blow out the residual harmful air three times. Inhale and hold the Kumbhaka Breath. Then massage your entire body.

## II.

With the same posture and breathing technique as above, lift the vajra fists \[hands\] from the knees and raise them to the sky, looking up. Then strike the vajra fists on the chest and press the throat with the chin. Do this three times.

## III.

In the sattva posture, raise your hands from your knees and place the left palm on your forehead and the right palm on the back of your neck. Then rub your head all over.

## IV.

In the Sattva posture, fix the hands with vajra fists on the hip joints. Squeeze the upper arm muscles. Without turning the head, bend the head three times from right to left and from left to right towards the shoulders.

## V.

Place your knees on the ground. Place your left hand on your left waist, extend your right arm and turn your wrist clockwise and counterclockwise three times.

## VI.

Strike the back with the fists. In the same posture as above, place the vajra fists \[hands\] on the chest. First extend the right arm forward, then bring the hand around to the back and strike the seventh joint of the back with the right fist. At the same time, extend the left hand forward and strike the left fist on the back, bringing the right hand forward. After doing this three times, bring your arms next to your hips and shake off your hands with open hands.

## VII.

In the sattva posture, bring the hands to the chest and interlock the thumbs. Then stretch your arms out and bring them back by applying force without separating the thumbs three times.

## VIII.

Binding the six vital points of the body. In the sattva posture, interlock the ten fingers and bind the crown of the head with the palms, then rub the crown from side to side three times with the palms. \[Next\], with the fingers intertwined, place the hands at the base of the head and rub them from side to side. \[Next\], with the forearms crossed over the chest, place the hands on the opposite shoulders and pull the shoulders one by one to the left and right three times. \[Next\], Cross your arms behind your back and bind the rib cage with the opposite hands. \[Next\], rise from the sattva posture and stand on your knees. Then, holding the vajra fist next to your hips, squeeze your body while bending the upper body slightly.

## IX.

Churning the ocean. In the sattva posture, place your hands on your knees with your arms straight. Turn your stomach clockwise and counterclockwise three times.

## X.

With the sound of 'hab', strike the hands on the abdomen. In the sattva posture, interlock the ten fingers on the chest and extend the arms opposite the forehead with the palms facing outwards. Then bring the hands down and strike the abdomen with the palms. At the same time, make the sound 'hub' from the mouth.

## XI.

Standing, place the sole of the right foot inside the left thigh. Place your right vajra fist on top of your left fist. Then extend your arms to the right and turn your head to the left. Do the same with your left side.

## XII.

Vulture taking off. Standing, without bending your knees, keep four fingers between your feet. Extend both arms opposite the shoulders with the fingers straight. Bounce up and down on your feet while rotating your palms up and down.

## XIII.

Sitting in the vajra posture. With the elbows outstretched, place the thumb and forefinger part of the fists on the chest. Sitting with the knees slightly upright, place the sides of the soles of the feet against the perineum. Stand up with your legs and arms spread to the left and right. Then cross your wrists over your chest and perform a yogic drop in the vajra posture.

## XIV.

Shaking the mountain. Sit in the vajra posture. Place your hands on the ground beside your hips. Then, with the weight of the body resting on the hands, separate the buttocks from the ground and slightly swing the crossed legs forward, striking \[the buttocks on the ground\].

## XV.

Holding the bird's legs. Standing position, keep a span distance between feet. Pass your hands outwards through the inside of your knees. Extend the fingers and bring the hand together. With the head tilted back and the buttocks pointing skywards, take steps forward one after the other.

## XVI.

Pulling the big toes from behind. In the vajra posture, cross the arms behind the back, hold the big toes with the opposite hands and pull.

## XVII.

Straighten the ring finger. In vajra posture, fix the ring finger on the ground next to the hips. Raise the buttocks from the ground by putting the weight on them and throw the buttocks onto the ground. \[If this is not possible, use the middle finger as well.\]

## XVIII.

Karana Method. From the sattva posture, keep the right knee erect. Wrap your arms around your right leg, interlocking your fingers. Then rotate the abdomen clockwise three times. Next, reverse the position of the legs and arms and rotate the abdomen counterclockwise three times. Then take both legs in your arms and rotate the abdomen clockwise and then counter-clockwise three times each.

## XIX.

Extending the right leg and squeezing the left. From the sattva posture, keep the left knee erect. Interlock your fingers and wrap your arms around the left knee. Then stretch the right leg forward with strength.

## XX.

Extending the left leg and squeezing the right. Same as above, but with the position of the legs and arms reversed.

## XXI.

Pandiculation in the supine position. Lie on your back. Keeping your hands and feet one cubit apart, stretch your arms and legs three times in all four directions.

## XXII.

Stretch out your arms and legs in the prone position. Lie on your stomach. Then stretch your arms and legs in the same way as above.

## XXIII.

Grasping the big toes by passing your hands inside the legs behind the knees. In standing position, keep feet one span apart. Wrap your arms around your legs by passing your hands inside your legs behind your knees and grasp your big toes with your hands. Then pull your bottom up and release. Pull up and release the butt three times.

## XXIV.

Grasping the big toes by passing hands outside the legs behind the knees. Posture and technique are similar to above, but the arms are wrapped around the legs by passing the hand outside the legs.

## XXV.

In the Lalita Pose, place the heels on the shoulder blades. Place each foot on each shoulderblade. If this is not possible, cross your heels slightly and place them behind your neck. Then place the hands on the ground near the perineum and lift the buttocks slightly off the ground.

## XXVI.

Run without bending the knees. In a standing position, keep a distance of four fingers from each foot. Cross your wrists over your chest and take three quick steps without bending your knees.

## XXVII.

Trot. Posture and instruction are almost the same, except that here you walk slower.

## XXVIII.

Jumping. Keep the same posture as above. Instead of taking steps, jump up three times without bending your knees.

## XXIX.

Rotating clockwise and counterclockwise. Maintain the same position as above. Turn your body clockwise by jumping up and down on your feet three times without bending your knees. Then do the same three times with your left side.
