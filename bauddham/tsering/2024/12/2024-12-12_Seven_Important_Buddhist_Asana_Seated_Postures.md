## Notes

There are several different Tibetan terms for the Sanskrit term *āsana*, as the term āsana has a wide range of meanings. Such as གདན་ and སྟན་ (seat), ཁྲི་ (throne/seat), འདུག་སྟངས་ (way of sitting or standing), ལུས་གནད་ (posture), etc. The Tibetan term འདུག་སྟངས་ ('dug stangs') is used here for *āsana*. The Sanskrit term *paryaṅka* (སྐྱིལ་ཀྲུང་), which literally means bed, couch or sofa, is a type of *āsana* that is more common in Buddhism. *Vajrāsana* (vajra sitting posture) is one of the famous Buddhist *āsanas* adopted by the Buddha Shakyamuni, and the name was later attributed to the place where he attained enlightenment (Dorjeden/Bodhgaya). This seated leg position is now commonly known as Padmāsana (Full Lotus) due to the influence of the Hindu Hathayogic tradition.

There are problems in identifying some of the basic postures in Buddhism. This may be because different traditions and different texts have different descriptions of the same postures. Lopon Sangye Yeshe (Buddhajnnana) wrote a text available in the Tibetan Tengyur collection called '*stangs stabs kyi bkod pa*' (description of various sitting and standing postures). In this text he has listed seven sitting postures with their descriptions. I have provided some images of them, collected from Himalayan Art Resources and the Himalayan Buddhist Art website. The images provided with each of the postures may not be accurate. If the readers have any suggestion regarding the inaccuracy in the image I have provided with the corresponding posture. Then please write in the comments section. For example, Terdaklingpa describes the sattva posture as placing the right ankle over the left ankle in cross-legged position. Some texts describes Lotus cross-legged posture as sitting with soles of the feet touching each other.

1.  Vajra sitting posture: *Vajrāsana* \[བཛྲ་ཨཱ་ས་ན་\], *Vajraparyaṅka*
    \[བཛྲ་པཪྻཾ་ཀ་\]

2.  Lotus sitting posture: *Padmāsana* \[པདྨ་ཨཱ་ས་ན་\]

3.  Squatting sitting posture: *Utkuṭakāsana* \[ཨུངྐུ་ཊུ་ཀ་ཨཱ་ས་ན་\]

4.  Gracious sitting posture: *Bhadrāsana* \[བྷ་དྲ་ཨཱ་ས་ན་\]

5.  Hero’s sitting posture: *Vīrāsana* \[བཱི་ར་ཨཱ་ས་ན་\]

6.  Sattva sitting posture: *Sattvaparyaṅkāsana* \[སཏྭ་པཪྻ་ཀ་ཨཱ་སཱ་ན་\]

7.  Playful sitting posture: *Salīlāsana* \[ས་ལཱི་ལ་ཨཱ་ས་ན་\]
