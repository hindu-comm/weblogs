#### Translator’s Note

This text is referred to as a commentary on the Virupa's Amrtasiddhi text. It is available in the Tibetan Tengyur collection and the Shangpa teaching cycle under different titles. There are various different names for the title of the Amrtasiddhi text in Tibetan, such as Attainment of Nectar, Attainment of Deathlessness, Attainment of Immortality, and Attainment of Indestructibility. This text here is called Swastika-siddhi.

The first three lineage masters of the Amrtasiddhi tradition are Virupa, Black Brahmin (Krishna Vipra) and Black Charnel Ground. According to the text's colophon, when Krishna Vipra didn't understand the Amrtasiddhi teaching, he asked Virupa to explain it to him by offering him wine. Under the influence of the wine, Virupa spoke this commentary and Krishna Vipra wrote down these vajra lines. E Deva (aka Black Charnel Ground) visited Tibet three times and translated it into Tibetan himself. Sangye Nyenton, the first Shangpa master to hold the Amrtasiddhi lineage, received the Amrtasiddhi teachings from Latoe Gonpo. And Latoe Gonpo's teacher is most likely the Black Charnel Ground (aka E deva).

This text is very similar to part of the Sanskrit Amrtasiddhi text written by Madhvacandra (translated into English by James Mallinson). See my previous posts for the Sanskrit and Tibetan texts related to this text. The whole of the text is too long for a single post. So I am going to present the little bit of the text that is left some other time.

\[The sun\] has twelve times[1](#footnote-1). The fire dwells day and night in the centre of *Sūrya*-*maṇḍala*. (There) the precious fire is fully formed.
