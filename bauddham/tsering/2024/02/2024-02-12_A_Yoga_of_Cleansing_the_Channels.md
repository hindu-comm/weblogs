#### Translator’s Note

The title of this text is rather difficult to translate into English without an understanding of the actual meaning. The author Amoghavajra, in his Amrtasiddhi works (attributed to Virupa) and this work attributed to Krishnacharya, transliterates the Sanskrit name of this trulkhor into Tibetan as ས་ར་ན་ (*sarana*), which I believe may be a misspelling of the Sanskrit word *Kṣālana*, which means 'washing and cleansing'. Amoghavajra says,

> ས་ར་ན་ཞེས་པ་རྩ་བཤལ་བཏང་ཞིང་རྩ་སྟོང་དུ་བྱེད་པའོ།

Sarana means to cleanse the channels, (thereby) channels are made empty (of impurities)

So we understand that the term རྩ་སྟོང་ (empty channel) in the title of this text means cleansing and emptying the channels of impurities, and the term འཁྱིལ་བ་ (*kuṇḍali*) is sometimes described as complete purification (འཁྱིལ་བ་ནི་རྣམ་པར་དག་པའོ་). I have translated the title of this physical yoga practice 'རྩ་སྟོང་འཁྱིལ་པའི་སྦྱོར་བ་' into English as 'A Yoga of Cleansing the Channels', assuming that སྟོང་ here means 'empty' rather than 'thousand'. However, there is a possibility that the Sanskrit term *sarana* transliterated into Tibetan could also be a misspelling of *sahasra*, which means 'thousand'. For the term སྟོང in the title can also be read as སྟོང་འཁྱིལ་ (swirling with a thousand) instead of རྩ་སྟོང་ (empty channel). And there is a goddess with the name of སྟོང་འཁྱིལ་མ (*Sahasra-avartā*).

The term འཁྱིལ་བ་ in the title of the text refers, I believe, to རླུང་འཁྱིལ་བ་ or འོད་འཁྱིལ་བ་. The channels and chakras involved in this physical yoga practice are only between the navel and the throat. The breathing technique involved in this practice is (read the breathing technique in the main phase of yoga) to press the upper air down (from the throat) to the heart, to draw the lower air up (from the navel) to the heart, and to hold/keep (བསྐྱིལ་བ་) the union of these two airs in the heart region. forcefully press down the upper air, forcefully draw up the lower air, and hold (the union of the upper and lower air) at the heart (chest).

In the visualisation of the main practice section of this text, it says that the meeting (union) of the bindu of the throat chakra (ཛཾ/*yaṃ*) and the navel chakra (བཾ/*vaṃ*) in the heart region emits rays of light that spread throughout the body (this is done by pressing down the upper air and drawing up the lower air and holding the union of air in the heart region). So འཁྱིལ་བ་ could also mean འོད་འཁྱིལ་བ་ (swirling/shining with light).
