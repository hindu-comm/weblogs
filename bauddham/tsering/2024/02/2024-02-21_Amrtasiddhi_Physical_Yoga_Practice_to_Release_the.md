#### Translator’s Note

There is a Tibetan Tengyur text called 'Amrtasiddhi Trulkhor to Release the Eight Channel-knots' in Amoghavajra's Amṛtasiddhi corpus. The text is attributed to Virupa and written by Amoghavajra. Some parts of the text are missing or non-existent. The missing sections of the scripture are the knots of bliss, clarity and no-thought. It is written in verse form. It is a yoga manual for releasing the channel knots through a sequence of physical yoga. These physical yoga practices involve various postures and movements (including breathing practices in their main phase of yoga) such as sitting, lying down, squatting and inverting, jumping with the stick and various movements of the limbs etc.

The *nāḍī* (channel) is generally a pathway for the *vāyu* and the *bindu*. The *vāyu* and the *bindu* are also the vehicle on which the consciousness moves. According to Amoghavajra's Amrtasiddhi text, *nāḍīs* exist in various forms. For example, some exist like a column (axle-tree/སྲོག་ཤིང་/*iṣika*), like a net (eyes of the net/དྲ་མིག་/*gava-akṣa*), like a thread, like a tree trunk, like a bundle of snakes, like *nāḍī* petals, like knots, etc. Here the channel knot is a knot that binds sentient beings to *Saṃsāra*. It is therefore sometimes called the Samsaric channel knot (*'khor ba'i rtsa mdud*). According to the text, these knots also correspond to our negative emotions (*kleśa*) binding us to Saṃsāra. For example, one of the channel knots is called the knot of attachment. When these channel knots are released, the karmic air (*las rlung*) ceases and transforms into the air of pristine consciousness (*ye shes kyi rlung*), and the clear essential drops (*bindu*) spread throughout the body. The commentary states,

> "When these eight channel knots are released, the yogi obtains eight
> powers of *samādhi*".

Amoghavajra gives a detailed account of the system of channels, air, bindu, sun and moon in his commentary. The central channel runs from the crown of the head through the six chakras of the body down to the genitals. Thirty-two secondary channels branch off from the crown and carry the essential drops (*bodhicitta bindu*) down the body. According to the commentary, these thirty-two channels form eight channel knots. Each knot consists of four channels connected to the main channel. These eight channel knots are located between the throat chakra and the navel chakra. However, the text doesn't specify the exact location.

In the commentary it says, 'The methods for releasing the channel knots are Posture, Object of Observation, Substances and Blessings’. According to the commentary, it seems that the following text on physical yoga practice is the postural method. The trulkhor (physical yoga practice) for releasing each of the channel knots consists of three phases: initial, main and final. However, as mentioned earlier, it appears that some of the scriptures are missing from the text and do not mention the trulkhor for releasing certain channel knots.

#### **Eight channel knots are:**

1\. Knot tied to the conceptual mind.

2\. Knot tied to the attachment.

3\. Knot tied to the clinging.

4\. Knot tied to the Sense perception.

5\. Knot tied to the phenomena.

6\. Knot tied to the clarity.

7\. Knot tied to the bliss.

8\. Knot tied to non-thought.

(Fixation on bliss, clarity and non-thought could result in the bondage of samsara, which is perhaps why they are included here).

Other Amṛtasiddhi texts such as, Sanskrit Amṛtasiddhi text by Madhvacandra and Tibetan Amrtasiddhi text by E deva mention three knots (*granthi*): *Viṣṇu* knot, *Brahmā* knot and *Śiva* knot.

To remove the defects of the knot of the fetter of conceptual mind, there are initial, main and the final practices.

First, the initial phase: Place your feet in the lotus position. While holding a fist in your left hand, raise your left arm vertically upwards. Gathering strength in your right arm, press down on your left shoulder seven times with your right palm. Do the same with your right (shoulder).

Second, the main phase: Place your feet in the lotus position. Straighten your spine, bend your throat and place your hands in a meditation mudra (right hand over left, palms facing up). Forcefully draw the lower air up and forcefully press the upper air down. Gather your strength and practise.

Third, the final phase: Place your feet in the lotus position. With your arms folded behind your back, grasp your arm muscles tightly. With the forearms of the folded arms, push the seventh upper vertebra forward seven times.
