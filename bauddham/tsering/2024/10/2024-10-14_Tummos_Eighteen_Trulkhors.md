## Translator’s Note

These are the eighteen essential trulkhor for the practice of tummo. The root text was composed by Marpa Lotsawa (11th century) and written down in accordance with Naropa's tradition of practice. There are many different versions of the instructions for practising these eighteen trulkhors. In order to avoid confusion and to keep things simple, I haven't included the instructions for the breathing technique \[at the beginning of the practice\] and the yogic drop \[at the end of the practice\] in many of the trulkhor. This translation is mainly based on the text attributed to Marpa Lotsawa. This translation is for educational purposes only and is not suitable for using as a practice manual.

The names of the eighteen Tummo’s Trulkhors are listed below. They are grouped into eight sections: 1. One Trulkhor to clear the harmful air. 2. Four trulkhors for Tummo sitting posture. 3. Two Trulkhors to generate heat forcefully. 4. Two Trulkhors to eliminate mental drowsiness and restlessness. 5. One Trulkhor for sleeping position during the night. 6. Two Trulkhors for walking. 7. Two Trulkhors for clearing obstacles. 8. Four Trulkhors to draw the bindu up.

**What is Trulkhor?** Tibetan texts do not give a definition of Trulkhor. Trulkhor is a physical technique that manipulates the body's channels, vital air and bindu to eliminate disease, bring good health, increase longevity and achieve spiritual attainment. Trulkhor has three main components: posture, breathing technique and object of observation.

### Some notes on the text

- The first Trulkhor for clearing the residual harmful air is basically
  a *rlung ro dgu phrugs* practice/blowing out the air thrice from each
  of the right, left and both nostrils\]

- The second trulkhor \[dhyana mudra\] is basically a sevenfold sitting
  posture of vairocana \[also used in tummo practice\], that is, legs in
  vajra crossed-legged posture, spine straight, hands in dhyana mudra
  \[which helps open the mouth of the central channel\], neck slightly
  bent \[chin on chest\], shoulders spread like a bird's wing, eyes
  resting on the tip of the nose, and tongue touching the palate.

- The third trulkhor, 'Strapping the Six Corners Fire Stove', is called
  six corners, I believe, because of the two triangles \[fire hearth\]
  formed by the legs below and the arms above, which are strapped with
  the yoga belt. There are three different Tibetan names used for this
  tummo posture: མེ་ཐབ་དྲུག་འཆིང་བ་ \[*me thab drug 'ching ba*\], ཐབ་འགལ་
  \[*thab 'gal/*Sakya Lamdre-pa uses this term more often\] and
  རྒྱ་མདུད་འཆིང་བ་ \[*rgya mdud 'ching ba*\].

- The fourteenth Trulkhor ‘Lion’s play’ \[སེང་གེའི་རྣམ་རོལ་\] is known as
  ‘Lion’s liberation mudra’ \[སེང་གེའི་རྣམ་གྲོལ་\] in Dzogchen practice.

- The fifteenth Trulkhor is called ‘Meru flying up’, may be because
  pushing the belly \[ocean\] to the spine \[Meru\], the air and the
  bindu fly or flow upwards. In another text this tulkhor is called
  རི་རབ་འཕུལ་ which could mean pushing your belly towards the Meru
  \[spine\]

- The sixteenth Trulkhor, a strange name, but it seems the fox does make
  a laughing sound.

## Tummo’s Eighteen Trulkhors

### གཏུམ་མོའི་འཕྲུལ་འཁོར་བཅོ་བརྒྱད༎

> རླུང་སྐྱོན་སེལ་དང་མཉམ་བཞག་དང། ཐབ་འགལ་དྲུག་དང་ལྗོན་ཤིང་བསྣོལ།
> སྐུ་གསུམ་བསྣོལ་དང་དྲོད་ཆེན་འབེབས། མཁལ་ལྡིང་སྦྲུལ་འཛིན་བྱ་རྒོད་འཕུར།
> རྒོད་སེལ་ཉལ་དང་མཁའ་ལ་དབབ། འགྲོ་བ་དང་ནི་འདྲེན་པ་བཞི། འབར་འཛགས་གཉིས་དང་བཅོ་བརྒྱལ་ལགས༎

### I. One Trulkhor for the removal of residual harmful air

#### 1. Trulkhor to clear the residual harmful air རླུང་སྐྱོན་སེལ་

Sit in the vajra crossed-legged posture \[full lotus\]. Place your right hand on your left knee. Raise the left hand and close the left nostril. Blow out the residual harmful air through the right nostril three times. Then slap the inner arm \[to the side of the chest\] and place the left hand on the right knee. Repeat with the right side in the same manner. Then \[having placed both hands on the knees\] blow out the residual harmful air through both nostrils three times.

### II. Four sitting Trulkhors

#### 2. Dhyana mudra posture མཉམ་བཞག་

Sit in the sevenfold posture of vairocana or the sevenfold posture of Tilopa with the hands in dhyana mudra \[the right hand over the left hand, palms turned up below the navel, thumbs pressed together\]; this will open the central channel and generate bliss.

#### 3. Strapping the six-corner fire stove posture མེ་ཐབ་དྲུག་འཆིང་བ་/ཐབ་འགལ་

Cross legs at ankles, keeping knees straight. Cross your arms over your chest and place your hands on opposite shoulders. Tie the yoga belt around your back and legs and place your elbows over your knees. Then hold kumbhaka breathing. This helps to generate heat more quickly.

#### 4. Intertwined tree posture ལྗོན་ཤིང་བསྣོལ་བའི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་

Cross legs at ankles, keeping knees straight. Interlink hands by grasping opposite wrists. Clasp your knees and bind them inside your arms; and hold a kumbhaka breathing. This will immediately eliminate the ailments of cold.

#### 5. Intertwined-three body posture སྐུ་གསུམ་བསྣོལ་བ་

Place the sole of the right foot on the ankle of the left foot. Cross your arms over your knees, and with your body straight, hold Kumbhaka Breath.

### III. Two Trulkhors to generate heat forcefully

#### 6. Generating great heat forcefully དྲོད་ཆེན་འབེབས་/གར་གྱི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་

Cross legs at ankles and keep knees straight. Cross your arms and hold opposite big toes. Turn your abdomen three times to the right and three times to the left. This will produce great heat like a blazing fire and destroy the diseases of indigestion and cancer. You will even be able to sleep safely naked on the snow and you will become as powerful as a lion.

#### 7. Garuda holding a snake Trulkhor མཁའ་ལྡིང་སྦྲུལ་འཛིན་

From a standing position, plant your thumbs and big toes on the ground. Twist your body to the right and left. Bend your arms and head inwards \[towards your legs\] and then do a big yogic drop \[Beb chen\]. This has the same benefit as above.

### IV. Two Trulkhors that eliminate drowsiness and restlessness

#### 8. Vulture in flight Trulkhor \[to eliminate drowsiness\] བྱ་རྒོད་འཕུར

\[From a standing position\], raise your arms. Move and shake your arms. Meditate a bindu \[thigle\] in the sky.

#### 9. kumbhaka \[to eliminate mental restlessness\] རྒོད་སེལ་

In the vajra crossed-legged posture \[full lotus\], cross your arms and cover the soles of your feet with the palms of your opposite hands. The gaze should be downward. Hold the kumbhaka breath. When you can no longer hold it, let the air out.

### V. One trulkhor for sleeping position during the night

#### 10. She-Wolf Sleeping Trulkhor སྤྱང་མོ་ཉལ་བ་

Lie down on the right side of your body and bring your knees up to your chest. Place your right arm over your kidney and use your left arm to clasp and bind your knees together. This will quickly generate heat.

### VI. Two Trulkhors for walking

#### 11. Falling on sky Trulkhor མཁའ་ལ་དབབ

Lie down on your back. Leap up \[from the ground\] by throwing your arms and legs to the sky. This will disperse the bindu in the body and the body will become beautiful.

#### 12. Mouse-hawk Trulkhor ནེ་ལེ་

From a standing position, place your left hand on your left waist. Then fling your right arm and right leg down three times. As you bend your arm, hit your inside arm to the side of your chest \[where armpit is\]. Repeat on the other side. \[Imagine the Goddess of Air lifting your body from your armpits and the circle of air underneath you being propelled from below.\] This will help you cover a distance in one day that normally takes seven days by walking.

### VII. Two Trulkhors for clearing obstacles

#### 13. Revealing the strength of the Lion སེང་གེའི་རྩལ་སྤྲུགས

In the sattva crossed-legged posture, place your hands on your knees. Bring the hands up and hit the inside arm on the side of the chest \[where the armpits are\]. Shake your hands and let the air out \[Say phat ha ha three times\] so that the bindu diffuses in the body. One's upper body will become like a lion.

#### 14. Lion’s play Trulkhor སེང་གེའི་རྣམ་རོལ་

In the vajra crossed-legged posture, close the mouth \[with the little and ring fingers\], the nose \[with the middle fingers\], the eyes \[with the index fingers\] and the ears \[with the thumbs\]. Then rotate your upper body three times to the right and three times to the left like a wheel. Then release your fingers and let the air out \[say phat ha ha three times\]. This will eliminate the hindrances of nadi, vayu and bindu and diffuses the pure essences into the four nadi-chakras.

### VIII. Four Trulkhors to draw the bindu up

#### 15. Meru flying up Trulkhor རི་རབ་འཕུར་

Sit in the vajra crossed-legged posture. Make a fist by pressing the thumbs together at the root of the ring fingers and place the fists on the corners of the thighs. Draw \[the bindu up with the air\] by making a twenty-one sound of long hum and short hum by attaching the ocean \[abdomen\] to the Meru \[spine\]. This will draw the bindu up and prevent it from falling.

#### 16. Fox laughing \[sound\] Trulkhor ཝའི་བཞད་ཚུལ་

Bring the hands to the chest and sit in the vajra crossed-legged posture. Let the air out with the sound of 'hum' \[make a small sound\] and draw the bindu up.

Another variation: Sit in a squatting position. Bring your fists close to your mouth, fists facing outwards, make the sound of 'he' and draw the bindu up forcefully.

#### 17. Hungry ghost Trulkhor ཡི་དྭགས་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་

Bring the soles of your feet together. Stand up and place your hands on your knees. Then make a strong 'hi-ka, hi-ka' sound.

#### 18. Upturned Lion Trulkhor སེང་གེ་གྱེན་ལོག་

Sit in the vajra crossed-legged posture and place your hands \[on the ground\] inside your legs. Then make a strong sound of 'Hum kara, hum kara'. Then do the 'Revealing Lion Strength Trulkhor' and remain relaxed in a dhyana mudra.
