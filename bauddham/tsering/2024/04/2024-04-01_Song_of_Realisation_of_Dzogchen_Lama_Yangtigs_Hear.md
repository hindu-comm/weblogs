#### Translators Note:

I have been searching for the Tibetan text of this *mGur* (song of realisation) sung by Ani Choying Drolma. I found this *mGur* in two texts, the Epic of King Gesar (edited by Lopon Pemala, Bhutan) and in the biography of Khandroma Tshultim Wangmo. I am not sure who is the main source of this *mGur*. The name of this *mGur* is 'Adivse and Introduction to the Dzogchen Lama Yangtig's Hearing through Liberation'. Ani Choying Drolma sang this song very beautifully. I thought I would share the lyrics of this song with you. I have added the English translation at the end.

#### TRANSLATION

om ma ni pemé hum lu tha la tha la tha la ré tha la lu yi len luk ré a la ngak gi guk luk ré Father Vidhyadhara Padmasambhava Mighty Vajra Garland of Skulls, I pray you to come for the benefit of the mother sentient beings of the six realms, and may all beings attain enlightenment. If you don't recognise this place, it's a gathering place, the beautiful flower. If you don't recognise me, a person like me, I'm the supreme conqueror, the wish-granting jewel. If you don't recognise this song, it is the laughter of Shri Heruka. I pray to my excellent masters, bless your spiritual son to attain (the life of) hermit. May the people who have obtained this precious human body once, may their this and next life aspired wishes be accomplished. The suffering of lack of wealth is superfluous when the mind sees the Seven Noble Riches. The suffering of hunger for food is superfluous when one is able to maintain the (bliss of) samadhi. There is no need to fear the suffering of the body feeling cold if one is able to light the blissful fire of Tummo. There is no anguish in the mire of samsara when one sees one's mind as dharmakaya. One has (assurance of) no aspiration to the Buddha realm when one's mind returns to the (primordial) luminous ground. The teaching of the Glorious Ever-Excellent Master that you possess, cherish and practise. If one's mind doesn't know itself (the nature of mind), even if one searches everywhere, there will be no finding. In your daily activities such as walking, sitting, eating and sleeping, maintain your practices without clinging (and fixating) on whatever (thoughts) arise in your mind. Do not forget the sentient beings of the six realms of samsara, keep them dear to your heart by all means.

Whatever good comes your way, make offerings and propitiate the Buddhas.

Whatever suffering of misfortune comes, think that all these sufferings are from your past karma.

Meditate all the displays of physical appearances as pure dharakaya.

Samaya is the root of Dharma, so always protect it as zealously as your eyes.

Practice accomplishment (sādhana) all alone, untainted by the mud of samsara.

Perform religious rites for six sessions unattached, untainted by bad company.

Bring this into your practice, showing no partiality of hatred towards your enemy and love towards your kin.

Consider everything as of one value, whether it be gold, silver or earth, stone.

Do not feel strongly attached to your home and hometown, leave them far behind you.

If practitioners become attached to their home, they will suffer in the end (unable to complete the practice) because of being on the wrong path. There would be great sorrow unable to accomplish divine dharma.

Without returning (your mind) to the primordial ground, do not deceive yourself by passing time.

Without practising the generation and completion stages together in union, do not crave for charity (religious offerings)!

Without seeing the path of great bliss yourself, do not give spiritual guidance and do not perform (the ritual of) guiding (the person) to the (higher) realm (at the time of death).

Without actually seeing the deity through sadhana, do not perform the name inscription rites and give empowerment.

If the untainted realisation has not arisen in you, do not act; you have clairvoyance in deception.

Without receiving Dakini's prophecy, do not indulge in women, meat and wine.

Confess intensely from your heart with repentance for unwholesome actions of past misdeeds.

Keep this in mind, the people who have gathered in this market place.
