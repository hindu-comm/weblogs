### *Vāyu Tattvaṁ bhāvana Upadeśa*

## ༄༅༅། རླུང་གི་དེ་ཉིད་བསྒོམ་པའི་མན་ངག་

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> Gorakṣa protects the cows.
> The cowherd tends his cows.
> While tending, they became nondual. 
> The duo Sun and Moon,
> Merge into one-taste (eka-rasa). 
> How amazing the Gorakṣa,
> Rāhu eating the sun.
>
> If the yogi binds (the vāyu), 
> Forcefully at the backbones,
> The constituent element is attained,
> Indestructible and completely pure, 
> Emptiness and things become very clear. 
> The duo sun and moon,
> Merge into one-taste. 
> How amazing the Gorakṣa,
> Rāhu eating the sun. 
>
> A piece of wood sinks to the bottom and, 
> A rock emerges upwards.
> With joy, ‘I am churned’,
> Thus uttered the Gorakṣa. 
> The duo sun and moon,
> Merge into one-taste. 
> How amazing the Gorakṣa,
> Rāhu eating the sun.
>
> The vajra pierces all the jewels.
> The yoga of Gorakṣa,
> Is accomplished by the grace of the guru.
> The duo sun and moon,
> Merge into one-taste (eka-rasa). 
> How amazing is Gorakṣa,
> Rāhu eating the sun.
> ```

#### Colophon of the text

Here ends the text '*Vāyu Tattvaṁ bhāvana Upadeśa*' composed by Gorakṣa. (From Tibetan Tengyur Derge collection)
