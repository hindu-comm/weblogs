#### TRANSLATOR’S NOTES

- *Haṭhapradīpikā* (popularly known as *Haṭhayogapradīpikā)* is a
  fifteenth-century Brahmanical (Hindu) text written by *Svātmārāma*.
  ‘More recently, eight works of early *Haṭha* yoga have been identified
  that may have contributed to the *Haṭhapradīpikā*’, including the
  11th-century Vajrayana Amrtasiddhi text attributed to Virupa.

- In this post I have presented my Tibetan translation of the
  (incomplete) first chapter (*āsana* section) of the *Haṭhapradīpikā*.
  Some of these yoga postures are also recorded in the Tibetan
  Amrtasiddhi trulkhor text attributed to Virupa with no names.

- The Sanskrit text and the English translation are borrowed from
  Hathapradipika.online (beta version).

अथ हठयोगप्रदीपिका।

> དེ་ནས་བཙན་ཐབས་སྦྱོར་བ་གསལ་བའི་སྒྲོན་མེ་བཤད་པར་བྱ་སྟེ

मारोढुमिच्छोरधिरोहिणीव॥

These and other great adepts used the power of hathayoga to smash the rod of death and \[so\] are roaming the worlds.

Now, the adept’s pose (*siddhāsana*). \[The yogi\] should put the heel at the perineum, firmly place the \[other\] foot on the penis, focus the mind, hold the body erect and \[remain\] motionless, his senses restrained, gazing between the brows with his eyes unmoving. This, which breaks open the door to liberation, is called the adept’s pose.
