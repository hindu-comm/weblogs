#### TRANSLATOR’S NOTES

- *Haṭhayogapradīpikā* is a fifteenth-century Brahmanical (Hindu) text
  written by *Svātmārāma*. ‘More recently, eight works of early hatha
  yoga have been identified that may have contributed to the
  *Haṭhayogapradīpikā*’, including the 11th-century Vajrayana
  Amrtasiddhi text attributed to Virupa.

- In this article I have presented my Tibetan translation of the Mudrā
  section of *Haṭhayogapradīpikā*, which contains the Mahamudra,
  Mahabandha and Mahavedha parts. These three mudras are the three main
  practices of Amrtasiddhi Yoga taught by Virupa. If you would like to
  see how these mudras were practised in Tibet, see my previous articles
  on Amrtasiddhi trulkhor texts written by [Shangpa
  masters](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/amrtasiddhi-thirty-two-trulkhor-by?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web)
  and
  [Taranatha](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/amrtasiddhi-yoga-manual-by-taranatha-518?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web).

- The English translation is not mine. It is borrowed from the book
  Hathayogapradipika, Adyar Library publication.

- Tibetan Vajrayana Buddhist texts (such as the Shadanga Yoga text) use
  the term བཙན་ཐབས་ཀྱི་རྣལ་འབྱོར་ or དྲག་པོའི་སྦྱོར་བ་ for the Sanskrit term
  hathayoga.

As the Lord of Serpents is the support of the earth with its mountains and forests, so Kundalini is the support of all the yoga (tantra) practices.

When the sleeping Kundalini is awakened by the grace of the Guru, then all the chakras and knots are pierced.

Then Sushumna becomes the royal road for prana. Then the mind remains objectless. Then death is deceived.

Suṣumnā, śūnyapadavī, brahmarandhraṁ, mahāpathaḥ, Śmaśānaṁ, śāmbhavī, and madhyamārga- these refer to same thing.

Therefore, making every effort, mudras should be practised to awaken the goddess (isvari) who sleeps at the mouth of susumna.

Mahāmudrā, Mahābandha, Mahāvedha, Khecarī, Uḍḍīyāna, Mūlabandhaśca bandha, Jālandharābandha, Viparītākaraṇī, vajrolī and śakticālana- These are the ten Mudras. They destroy old age and death.

### Mahāmudrā

VII\.

Pādamūlena vāmena yoniṁ sampīḍya dakṣiṇam\|

Prasāritaṁ padaṁ kṛtvā karābhyāṁ dhārayeddṛḍham\|\|

Kaṇṭhe bandhaṁ samāropyaṁ dhārayedvāyumūrdhvataḥ\|

Yathā daṇḍahataḥ sarpo daṇḍākāraḥ prajāyate\|\|

Ṛjvībhūtā tathā śaktiḥ kuṇḍalī sahasā bhavet\|

Tadā sā maraṇāvasthā jāyate dvipuṭāśrayā\|\|

Tataḥ śanaiḥ śanaireva recayennaiva vegataḥ\|

Iyaṁ khalu mahāmudrā mahāsiddhaiḥ pradarśitā\|\|

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> རྐང་གཡོན་རྩ་བས་གསང་གནས་མནན།    
> རྐང་པ་གཡས་པ་བརྐྱང་བྱས་ནས།   
> ལག་པས་བརྟན་པོར་གཟུང་བྱ་ཤིང།   
> མགྲིན་པ་བསྡམ་ཅིང་རླུང་ཡར་བཀོད།
>
> སྦྲུལ་ལ་དབྱུག་པས་བརྡུང་བ་བཞིན།   
> དབྱུག་པའི་གཟུགས་ཅན་དུ་ནི་ལངས།   
> ཀུནྜ་ལི་ཅེས་བྱའི་ནུས་མ་དེ།   
> དེ་མ་ཐག་ཏུ་དྲང་པོར་འགྱུར།
>
> དེ་དུས་གཡས་གཡོན་རྩ་གཉིས་ནི།   
> འགག་པར་འགྱུར་བའི་དུས་ཡིན་ནོ།
>  དེ་ནས་རླུང་ནི་དལ་པོ་བཞིན།   
> མྱུར་བ་ཕྱི་རུ་མི་དབྱུང་བྱ།
> ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཆེན་པོ་འདི་ཉིད་ནི།   
> གྲུབ་ཐོབ་ཆེན་པོས་གསལ་བར་བསྟན། 
> ```

Pressing the perineum with the left heel and stretching out the right leg take firm hold with the hands. Contract (bind) the throat and hold the breath in the upper part. Then the (shakti) the kundalini becomes at once straight just as a snake when struck be a rod straightens itself out like a stick. Then the other two Nadis become lifeless. Then one should breathe out very slowly and never quickly. This has declared to be Mahamudra by the great siddhas.

> ཉོན་མོངས་ཆེན་པོའི་སྐྱོན་དང་བཅས།   
> འཆི་བ་ལ་སོགས་འཇོམས་པར་བྱེད།

The great kleśa and death etc. are vanquished. Therefore, the wisest of men call it the Mahamudra.

There is nothing wholesome or unwholesome. All things of whatever taste, even those which are insipid and even virulent poison, if consumed, are digested as if it were nectar.

The maladies of one who practises Mahamudra- consumption, leprosy, constipation, abdominal diseases, indigestion, etc.- are overcome.

### Mahābandha

XI\.

Pārṣṇiṁ vāmasya pādasya yonisthāne niyojayet\|

Vāmorūpari saṁsthāpya dakṣiṇaṁ caraṇaṁ tathā\|\|

Pūrayitvā tato vāyuṁ hṛdaye cibukaṁ dṛḍham\|

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> ཞབས་གཡོན་རྟིང་པ་གསང་གནས་སྦྱར།   
> གཡོན་པའི་བརླ་སྟེང་ཞབས་གཡས་གཞག།   
> རླུང་ནི་ནང་དུ་བཀང་བྱས་ནས།   
> ཨོག་མ་སྙིང་གར་གཟུགས་བྱས་ཅིང། 
> དེ་ནས་རླུང་ནི་བསྐུམ་བྱས་ཏེ།   
> དམིགས་པ་དབུ་མར་འཇོག་པར་བྱ། 
> ```

Place the heel of the left foot on the perineum and place the right foot upon the left thigh. Having drawn in (fill in) the breath, and pressing the chin steadily on the breast, contract the anus (vāyumākuñcya) and fix the mind on the central channel.

> ཅི་ནུས་རླུང་ནི་བཟུང་ནས་ནི།   
> དལ་བུས་རླུང་དེ་ཕྱིར་ལ་བཏང།   
> གཡོན་གྱི་ཆ་རུ་སློབས་བྱས་ནས།

Having retained the breath as long as possible, the air should be breathed out slowly. Having practised well on the left side, then practise on the right side.

> གྱེན་དུ་བགྲོད་པ་རྣམ་པར་འགོག།    
> བསྡམས་པ་ཆེན་པོ་ཞེས་པ་འདི།

> ```

This (bandha) stops the upward course through all the Nadis. This Mahabandha confer great siddhis.

XIV\.

Kālapāśamahābandhavimocanavicakṣaṇaḥ\|

> རྩ་གསུམ་གཅིག་ཏུ་འཛིན་མཛད་ནས།

This is efficacious in freeing one from the great noose of time (Yama). It brings about the union of the stream (channels). It enables the mind to reach Kedara.

As a women endowed with beauty and charm is unfruitful without a husband, so are Mahamudra and Mahabandha without the Mahavedha.

### Mahāvedha

XVI\.

Mahābandhasthito yogī kṛtvā pūrakamekadhīḥ\|

> ``` text
> བསྡམས་པ་ཆེན་པོའི་ལུས་གནད་ངང།   
> རྣལ་འབྱོར་པ་ཡིས་སྐད་ཅིག་ལ།   
> རླུང་ནི་ནང་དུ་བཀང་བྱས་ནས།

> རོ་རྐྱང་གཉིས་ཀྱི་མར་སྣ་ནས། 
> འཐོན་ཏེ་རླུང་ནི་དབུ་མར་ཞུགས།

The Yogin, assuming the Mahabandha, should draw in the breath with concentrated mind and stop the course of the prana etc. by the jalandharabandha. Placing the two palms straight upon the ground, he should strike slowly with the buttocks. Then the prana leaving the two Nadis the courses through the middle.

Then take place the union of moon and sun (fire) that surely leads to immortality. There is a death-like state; then the air should be exhaled.

This is Mahavedha which, with practice, confers great siddhis. This staves off wrinkles, grey hair and trembling, and the best of practitioners devote themselves.
