#### Translator’s Note:

This text is from Tibetan Tengyur collection, taught by Kashmiri Shakya Shri Bhadra, translated into Tibetan by Lotsawa Jampay Pal.

**Meaning of dissolution of elements**

Tsongkhapa says, “The dissolving of the earth into water does not mean that the earth element ceases to exist. When the earth element ceases to support consciousness, the earth element is said to dissolve into the water element. The true meaning of the air element dissolving into the consciousness is that the gross air flowing through the nostrils ceases to support the consciousness, or the gross air dissolves into the subtle air within the central channel so that the gross air no longer supports the consciousness”.

One day, when one is about to die, abandon all expectations (āpekṣa). Having first performed offerings with whatever you can afford, cultivate a firm mind of bodhicitta (altruistic mind of awakening).

When the four elements dissolve into each other, meditate the four Deities. When the consciousness dissolves into the clear light, meditate the personal deity, and this is the clear light of death. After the dissolution of the generation phase, practise the perfection phase.

(When the earth dissolves into water) the physical strength diminishes and will perceive a mirage, (at that time) meditate one's master at the chest. (When the water dissolves into fire and) the nine doors (of the body) dry up and will perceive the smoke, (at that time) meditate Vajrasattva at the navel.

(When the fire dissolves into air) the heat of the body diminishes and will perceive the firefly, (at that time) meditate Amoghasiddhi at the perineum. (When the air dissolves into space) the breath stops and the glowing butter lamp is perceived, (at that time) meditate Hayagriva on the forehead.

When everything gathers into the (luminosity of death) like a space, meditate the Supreme (your personal) Deity. After that, there is no need to correct (your mind) and there is no coming and going.

While alive, practise seven dissolutions in sequence through pranayama. At the moment of death, the dissolution actually takes place. It is said to be accomplished simply by remembering.
