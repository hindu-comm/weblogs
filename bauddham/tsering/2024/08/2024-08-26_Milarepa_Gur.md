ONCE WHEN MILAREPA WAS IN THE GUNGTHANG AREA, A WOMAN WITH A BABY CAME NEAR WHERE MILAREPA WAS MEDITATING. WHEN THE BABY BEGAN TO CRY, THE WOMAN ASKED MILAREPA, 'YOU SEEM TO HAVE A LOT OF FREE TIME. WILL YOU LOOK AFTER THE BABY WHILE I LOOK AFTER MY HOUSE? THEN MILAREPA THOUGHT, 'WHEN I DON'T EVEN HAVE TIME TO EAT, THIS WOMAN THINKS I AM IDLE. THEN MILAREPA SAID, 'PATRONESS, YOU ARE BOTH RIGHT AND WRONG. AND MILAREPA SANG THIS SONG OR GUR.

> ``` text
> In your eyes I look like an idle and leisurely,
> But I see myself as busy and ever toiling.
> I, in the plane of boundless non-arising,
> While building and building the castle of dhyāna,
> I have no time to build a castle of earth and rock.
>
> I, in the desert of the empty nature of reality,
> While tilling and tilling the barren land of Kleśa,
> I have no leisure to plough my father's farm.
>
> I, on the threshold of ineffable non-duality,
> while subduing and subduing the enemy of the egoic demon,
> I have no leisure to vanquish the hateful enemy.
>
> My mind, in the mansion of nonduality,
> While waiting and waiting for the bride of spiritual practice,
> I have no leisure to begin a domestic life.
>
> My body, in the mandala of Buddhas,
> While nurturing and nurturing the infant Rigpa,
> I have no leisure to raise a child.
>
> I, in the inner pleasure grove within me,
> While gathering and gathering the wealth of listening and contemplation,
> I have no leisure to save illusory money and wealth.
>
> I, on the mountain of unchanging dharmakaya,
> While tending and tending the wild horse of Rang Rig,
> I have no leisure for rearing sheep and cattle.
>
> On the summit of the triangular heart,
> While offering and offering the luminous lamp,
> I have no leisure to invoke deities.
> ```
