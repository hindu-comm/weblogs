### Translator’s Note

Navel \[chakra\] is generally associated with earth element. In the context of Vajrayana Dzogrim yogic practice, Tārānātha explains that the ‘surface of the earth’ \[*bhūtala*\] refers to the *nābhi-maṇḍala* or *nābhi*-*cakra* \[navel chakra\] where the goddess herself resides who symbolises the Tummo \[*caṇḍālī*\], the fire of primordial wisdom which breaks the channel-knots.

He explains that the 'seven subterranean realms' \[*saptapātāla*\] refer to the 'fire of Brahma' which coils in seven circles \[like a snake\] below the navel \[earth\] at the lower opening of the central channel. Tummo \[*caṇḍālī*\] is referred to in some texts as a 'coiled serpent' \[ཀླུ་དབང་འཁྱིལ་བ་\], showing some similarity between the Vajrayana *caṇḍālī* \[tummo\] and the Brahmanical *Kuṇḍalinī*. This is how it is described in some texts \[ཨ་ཤད་/a stroke generally refers to the shape of Tummo\]:

> དེ་ཡང་ལྟེ་བ་ན་ཨ་ཤད་འོད་ཀྱི་རི་མོས་ལན་བདུན་གཡས་སུ་བསྐོར་བ་ཀླུ་དབང་འཁྱིལ་བ་ཞེས་བྱ་

At the navel, there is ‘a stroke' \[ཨ་ཤད་\], which curls clockwise seven times in a form of light. It is called 'a coiled snake'.

Drukchen Padma Karpo (16th century)

> ལྟེ་བ་སྤྲུལ་པའི་འཁོར་ལོར་ཨ་ཤད་ཀྱི་རྣམ་པ་ལྟ་བུ་འགྲུབ་ལ།
> དེ་ཉིད་ལ་གཞུང་གཞན་ནས་ཀླུ་དབང་འཁྱིལ་པའི་རྩ་ཞེས་ཀྱང་ཟེར་རོ།

It exists in the form of ‘A stroke' at the navel chakra. In other scriptures it is also called the Nadi of the coiled serpent.

Marpa Choekyi Lodoe (11th century)

### English translation of the Tibetan verse 14

Homage to you \[Tara\], who on the earth’s surface strike your palms and stamp your feet; Your brow deeply furrowed, with *hūṃ* you smash the seven netherworlds to nothing but dust.

### Hidden meaning behind this verse as explained by Tārānātha

namaḥ kara talāghāta caraṇāhata bhūtale \|  
  
bhṛkuṭīkṛta humkāra sapta pātāla bhedini \|\|

Bhūtala \[surface of the earth/ས་གཞིའི་ངོས་\] here refers to nābhi-maṇḍala \[navel chakra/ལྟེ་བའི་དཀྱིལ་འཁོར\]. Because the tummo \[caṇḍālī\], the fire of primal wisdom, dwells there, which is the true representation of the Goddess, she strikes down with her feet \[caraṇāhata/ཞབས་ཀྱིས་བརྡུང་\] and pierces with the flames of her hands \[talāghāta/ཕྱག་གི་མཐིལ་གྱིས་བསྣུན་\].

There is the fire of Brahma, coiled seven times \[sapta\], under the earth/navel \[pātāla\] at the lower opening of the central channel. In this fire, as the tummo burns, bodhicitta bindu flows down, pacifying the heat of the fire, breaking open/destroying \[bheda\] the channel-knots \[Nāḍīgrantha/རྩ་མདུད་\].
