This short post contains the three breathing out techniques for the path of Prāṇāyāma and *Dhyāna*. This is part of the Sakya Lamdre breathing yoga text by Phagmo Drupa Dorje Gyalpo \[12th century\]. The Tibetan word for the Sanskrit term *Prāṇāyāma* is སྲོག་རྩོལ་ \[*srog rtsol*\]. In Tibetan breathing practice, སྲོག་རྩོལ་ \[*prāṇāyāma*\] also refers to the breath as སྲོག \[*prāṇā*\] and རྩོལ་བ་ \[*āyāma*\]. The text explains what སྲོག \[*prāṇā*\] and རྩོལ་བ་ \[*āyāma*\] are as follows.

> The air that moves through the left \[nostril\] is *prāṇa* \[སྲོག་\],
> and the air that moves through the right \[nostril\] is *āyāma*
> \[རྩོལ་བ་\]. Also, the breath that comes in is *prāṇa* and the breath
> that goes out is *āyāma*.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### I. First breathing technique

Press the right nostril with your right finger. Then exhale through the left nostril and hold the air outwards. Focus your mind inside the left nostril. If you couldn’t hold it any longer let the air come in. The name \[of this breathing technique\] is breathing out through the prāṇa \[left nostril\] by closing the āyāma \[right nostril\].

#### Eliminates problems

(Could not understand what's written)

#### Attains Samadhi of

Bliss-warmth

#### Signs of having perfected

When you stop/hold the air out and there is no air coming back, it means that the air has been pacified in its own place. This is the sign that you have perfected this technique.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### II. Second breathing technique

Exhale through both nostrils and hold the air outwards. Fix your mind on the spot between your eyebrows in such a way that, although your mind is focused there, you cannot tell where it is focused. When you can't hold it outward any longer, let the air come in through both nostrils. The name of this technique is ‘exhaling through both nostrils by gathering strength’.

#### Eliminates

All headaches

#### Attains samadhi of

Any of the fifteen yogic experiences

#### Signs of having perfected

When you press/hold the air out and there is no air coming back, it means that the air has been pacified in its own place. This is the sign that you have perfected this technique.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### III. Third breathing technique

Breathe out through your mouth with a 'si' sound between your teeth, a gap the size of a horsetail hair. Press/hold the air out and focus on the non-conceptuality. When you can't hold it outward any longer, let the air come in through your mouth.

#### Eliminates

The problems of blood \[thickening!?\]

#### Attains samadhi of

Any of the fifteen yogic experiences

#### Signs of perfection

Same as above
