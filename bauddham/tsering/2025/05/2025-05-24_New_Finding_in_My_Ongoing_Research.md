A *Trulkhor* text attributed to a 13th-century Taklung Kagyu teacher bears a striking resemblance to the thirty-two *Trulkhor* sequences found in the *Amṛtasiddhi thirty-two trulkhor text*, as transmitted by the Shangpa master Tsundue Singhe. Notably, this practice is titled *Kālacakra Trulkhor* and opens with an homage to Śrī Kālacakra. I am currently preparing a long-overdue article that examines how Virūpa’s *Amṛtasiddhi* practices—particularly its three principal practices: *Mahāmudrā*, *Mahābandha*, and *Mahāvedha*—were transmitted, adapted, and assimilated into Tibetan Buddhist traditions. Although I have shared many of these insights over the years via my Substack and Facebook platforms, the forthcoming article will present them in a more comprehensive and academically rigorous format.
