## Translator’s Note

This practice is called the *Twenty-Five Forceful-Breathing Trülkhors*, drawn from the text composed by Drigung Chungtsang Chökyi Drakpa (16th–17th century). Preceding this section, the text teaches the method of drawing the white and red elements in the body. Once these elements have been successfully drawn, he explains, it is essential to diffuse or spread them throughout the entire body. If this is not done, various problems may arise, such as the body becoming slack or crooked, or swelling. The present practice serves to ensure that these elements are properly spread within the body.

This practice consists of twenty-five Trülkhors, each with its own distinctive and interesting posture, and each given a name. They are presented here not in the exact sequence of the root text. A couple of the Trülkhors are difficult to interpret, so I have left them untranslated. The preliminary practices include generating bodhicitta, meditating on one’s personal deity (Vajradhara), massaging the body, and performing *lüjong* exercises involving the five limbs of the body (the two arms, two legs, and the head/torso). Where possible, I have inserted comparable images.

## Twenty-five forceful-breathing Trülkhors

**1. Knees-Shackle**  
Kneel with the fronts of the feet flat on the ground. From this position, thread the arms through the perineal space, grasp the big toes with the opposite hands, and pull.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**2. Stretching the Heel and Shanks**  
From a standing position, draw the right foot upward, bringing the right heel to the right buttock and holding it with the right hand. Extend the left arm straight down alongside the hip.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**3. Pulling Crossed Legs Behind the Back**  
Assume the vajra cross-legged posture (full lotus). Cross the arms behind the back, extend them fully, grasp the big toes with the opposite hands, and pull.

**5. Tortoise Posture**  
Sit with the legs extended forward and the soles of the feet joined together. Pass the arms beneath the thighs and bring the hands behind the buttocks. Interlace the fingers and pull.

**6. Lotus Sitting Posture**  
Sit with the legs extended forward and the soles of the feet joined together. Pass the hands beneath the shanks and grasp the toes, pulling firmly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**7. Stretching the Demon (Rākṣasa)**  
From a seated position, lower the head backward while supporting the waist with the hands. Rest the elbows and the back of the neck on the ground. Extend both legs vertically upward. Then bend the right leg, placing the sole of the right foot at the crease between the thigh and the hip, while keeping the left leg extended. After switching the legs in this way three times, assume the vajra cross-legged posture in the air. In this position, twist the body to the right, to the left, and to the back.

**8. Vajra Equipoise**  
From a standing position, cross the legs with the left leg behind and the right leg in front, the feet placed closely together. Bend forward and, with the arms crossed, grasp the big toes with the opposite hands and pull. Then twist the head and shoulders to the right. Repeat the posture with the legs in the opposite position, twisting to the left.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**9. Standing with Right Leg Bent**  
From a standing position, bend the right leg and place the foot at the crease between the thigh and the hip. Extend the arms downward, keeping the hands beside the hips.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**10. Snake Intertwined**  
From a standing position, entwine the right leg around the left. Raise the arms and entwine the right arm over the left. Curl the right fingers into a rounded shape, like a snake’s head, at the forehead. Twist the body to the left and to the right.

From a squatting position, place the shins on the ground with the heels raised. Bring the arms behind the back and join the palms together \[in a prayer gesture\].

**12. Fish Movement**  
Lie prone with the fronts of the legs firmly planted on the ground. Extend the arms slightly forward and place the palms on the ground. Draw in the waist while lifting the head and chest backward, and hold the breath out……

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**13. Bliss-Sustaining**  
Lie prone and cross the legs at the back. Rest the forearms on the ground with the arms crossed, right over left. Then raise the hands (vajra-fists) upward and twist the body to the right.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**14. Lion’s Play**  
Exhale fully and, without inhaling, block the ears with the thumbs, close the mouth with the ring fingers and little fingers, close the nostrils with the middle fingers, and cover the eyes with the index fingers. Rotate the head three times clockwise and three times counterclockwise.

**15. Reversing-Up the Vajra (Hands)**  
Assume the posture and hold the breath. Interlace the thumbs and extend the arms forward with the hands turned upside down. As the hands are drawn back toward the chest, turn them upward while releasing the thumbs. Extend the arms forward again, and repeat this sequence three times.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**16. Shooting a Vajra Arrow**  
Sit in the sattva posture. Clap the hands together and move them as if shooting an arrow.

**19. Deva’s Posture**  
Stand with the legs close together. Place the hands behind the back at the level of the waist in a mudrā, pressing the elbows downward.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**20. Supreme-Path Uniquely-Perfect**  
Stand with the legs close together. Draw the vajra-fists upward along the sides of the torso and strike the armpits, then extend the arms forward. Join the wrists together from the right, middle, and left sides, twisting the arms three times each. Cross the fists at the point between the eyebrows. Finally, relax the hands downward and perform the yogic-drop.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**21. Reversing the Buttocks Upward**  
This is similar to the *Stretching the Demon (Rākṣasa)* trülkhor described above.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**22. Abdomen Trülkhor**  
Assume the posture and hold the breath. Rotate the abdomen three times clockwise and three times counterclockwise.

**23. Myriad-Diffusion**  
Hold the breath, bend forward, and place the knees and hands on the ground. Twist the body to the right and left three times, then shake the whole body.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**24. Yogic-Drop with the Sound “Ha”**  
Sit in a squatting posture with the hands resting on the knees. Utter the sound *Ha* and perform a small yogic-drop, striking the buttocks against the ground.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**25. Meditate in a Vairocana Posture**  
Sit in the seven-point Vairocana posture and remain in meditation for a short period.
