### Translator’s Note

This particular practice is called Trülkhor-Künzog (Skt. *Saṃpūrṇa* – “Completely Perfect Trülkhor”). The following excerpt is taken from the text with the title (shortened here) Profound Instruction on the Extraordinary Breathing and Trülkhor Practice. It was composed by Drigung Chungtsang Chökyi Drakpa (16th–17th century).

The lineage masters of this oral instruction as written in the text are as follows: Vajradhara, Vajrayoginī, the Mahāsiddha Saraha, Śavaripa, Paṇḍita Vibhūticandra, Kunkhyen Chösku Özer (13th century), Butön Rinchen Drub (13th–14th century), Lotsāwa Jangchub Tsemo (14th century), Lochen Drakpa Gyaltsen (14th century), Jigme Drakpa (Bodong Choklé Namgyal, 14th–15th century), Rechen Paljor Zangpo, Chöje Namkha Lhungrub, Palden Zangpo, Chöje Kunga Namgyal, Sangye dBon, Drung Repa Jamyang Pal, Khenchen Namjom Phuntsok (16th century), and subsequently Chungtsang Chökyi Drakpa himself.

In the Trülkhor-Künzog practice, seven distinct forms of the yogic-drop (Beps) are taught. Each is characterised by a specific hand posture and attributed to a great master of the Kagyü lineage, beginning with Śākyamuni Buddha and continuing through Vajradhara, Tilopa, Nāropa, Marpa, Milarepa, and Lingrepa (Pema Dorji). The text describes not only the movements of the hands and arms, but also the purpose and benefits of these yogic-drops—clearing away or expelling of bodily impurities downwards through the lower gate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Trülkhor-Künzog

### **Preparing the Pit for the Yogic-drop (འབེབས་ཁུང་)**

On level ground, dig a square pit one cubit wide (measured from elbow to fingertips) and one span plus four fingers deep. In this pit, place a Beps-cushion measuring one shortened cubit (from fist to elbow) in width and seven fingers in height. The Beps-cushion may be fashioned from materials such as musk-deer hide and stuffed with musk-deer fur.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Breathing technique (རླུང་རྩོལ་)

Through the upper gate, inhale air through the nose, while also drawing in the air through the lower gate (the anus). Drink plenty of boiled water.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Visualisation (དམིགས་པ་)

Visualise your body in the form of Vajradhara. Within the body, see the central channel, yellow in colour and the size of a bamboo stalk, its upper tip pierced at the crown aperture and its lower tip pierced at the lower gate (anus). Inside this channel, visualise a five-pronged golden vajra. As you rise up, imagine the vajra moving upward towards the crown aperture. When performing the yogic-drop (Beps), visualise the vajra emerging from the lower gate at a distance.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **Yogic-Drops (འབེབས་)**

1.  **Śākyamuni’s Cranial Protuberance (ཤཱཀྱ་ཐུབ་པའི་གཙུག་ཏོར་)**  
    Interlace the fingers and cover the crown of the head with the
    palms, leaving the middle fingers joined and pointing upwards. In
    this posture, perform the yogic-drop.

2.  **Vajradhara’s Hand Mudrā (རྡོ་རྗེ་འཆང་གི་ཕྱག་རྒྱ་)**  
    As you rise to stand, strike the fists against the sides of the
    chest. Then extend the arms forward and cross the hands \[in vajra
    posture\] over the chest. As you strike the fists against the chest,
    simultaneously perform the yogic-drop.

3.  **Tilopa’s Pounding the Sesame Seed (ཏིལླིའི་ཏིལ་རྡུང་)**  
    Extend both arms downwards alongside the hips. In this posture,
    perform the yogic-drop.

4.  **Nāropa’s Threatening Hand Mudrā (ནཱ་རོའི་སྡིགས་མཛུབ་)**  
    Extend both arms forward, forming vajra-fists with the index fingers
    pointing in a threatening gesture. Then curl the index fingers back
    in, strike the fists against the chest without crossing the hands,
    and perform the yogic-drop.

5.  **Marpa’s Vulture’s Wings (མར་པའི་རྒོད་གཤོག་)**  
    With vajra-fists, extend both arms fully straight out to the right
    and left. In this posture, perform the yogic-drop.

6.  **Milarepa’s Firm Resolve (མི་ལ་རས་པའི་ཐགས་གཅོད་)**  
    With the hands in vajra-fists, raise them to the level of the
    hairline. As if making a firm resolve, perform the yogic-drop.

7.  **Lingje Repa’s Breaking Rock (གླིང་རས་ཀྱི་རྡོ་གཅོག་)**  
    With vajra-fists, cross the hands over the chest. Strike the fists
    against the abdomen and perform the yogic-drop.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Concluding Remarks

If the practitioner diligently engages in this Trulkhor (Beps), combining breath-work, visualisation, and the drinking of plenty of boiled water, all bodily impurities together with pain will be expelled through the lower gate (anus). It is essential that no impurities are left behind; if they remain, they may give rise to disease. When impurities persist, drink boiled water, then continue with the yogic-drop (Beps) and breath-work until the impurities are fully expelled.
