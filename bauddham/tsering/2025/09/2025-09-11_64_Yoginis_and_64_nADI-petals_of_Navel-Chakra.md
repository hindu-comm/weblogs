Drawing of navel *nāḍī-*chakra and its twelve lagana or houses (12 zodiac constellations). Large circle in the centre is the central channel which is the channel of Rahu or void. Two small circles on the left and right are left and right channels the path of moon and sun. The navel *nāḍī*-chakra from the inside to the outside consists of four *nāḍī*-petals, then eight *nāḍī*-petals (representing eight parts of the day, Skt.*aṣṭapraharāḥ*, Tib. *thun brgyad*), then twelve *nāḍī*-petals (representing twelve southern and northern suns transiting through twelve zodiac constellations, Skt. *dvādaśasaṃkrāntināḍya*, Tib. *'pho ba bcu gnyis kyi rtsa*), and finally 64 *nāḍī*-petals (representing 64 *daṇḍa* units of time, Tib. *dbyu gu drug cu rtsa bzhi*), consisting of 60 outermost *nāḍī*-petals and the innermost four channels, known as the four void channels.

## Title of the text:

**Sanskrit:** *Aṣṭa aṣṭa catuḥṣaṣṭiḥ yoginī*  
**Tibetan:** *brgyad brgyad drug cu rtsa bzhi’i rnal ’byor ma*  
**English:** *Eight, eight, and sixty-four yoginīs.*

### **The configuration of eight by eight—sixty-four yoginīs** (according to this text)

There are eight lotuses, each with eight petals, positioned in the eight directions. On each of the eight petals of these eight lotuses sits one of the sixty-four yoginīs. At the center (the anthers) of each lotus abides the chief yoginī of that direction, surrounded by her attendant yoginīs. The eight principal yoginīs presiding over the eight directions are as follows:

1.  East: Yoginī Cārcikā (ཙཱ་རྩི་ཀཱ་).

2.  South-East (Agnikoṇa / Tib. མེའི་མཚམས་): Yoginī Vaiṣṇavī (ཁྱབ་འཇུག་མ་).

3.  South-West (Nairṛtya / Tib. བདེན་བྲལ་): Yoginī Vārāhī (ཕག་མོ་).

4.  South: Yoginī Ṣaṇmukhī (གདོང་དྲུག་མ་, “Six-Faced One”).

5.  North (Uttara): Yoginī Raudrī Śuklā (དྲག་མོ་དཀར་མོ་, “The White
    Wrathful One”).

6.  North-East (Aiśānī / Tib. དབང་ལྡན་): Yoginī Lakṣmī (དཔལ་མོ་).

7.  North-West (Vāyavya / Tib. རླུང་): Yoginī Brahmāṇī (ཚངས་མ་).

8.  West (Varuṇakoṇa / Tib. ཆུ་ལྷར་): Yoginī Śacī (དབང་མོ་, the consort of
    Indra).”

## The Sixty-Four Yoginīs (*Catuḥṣaṣṭi Yoginī*)

*(Tibetan names alongside their Sanskrit counterparts (as rendered here)*

### Principal Eight Yoginīs of the Directions

1.  ཙཱ་རྩི་ཀཱ་ — **Cārcikā** : sword & trident (right); skull-cup &
    khaṭvāṅga (left).

2.  ཁྱབ་འཇུག་མ་ — **Vaiṣṇavī** : staff & wheel (right); lotus & spear
    (left). Mount: Garuḍa.

3.  ཕག་མོ་ — **Vārāhī** : staff & sword (right); armor & iron chain
    (left). Mount: buffalo.

4.  གཞོན་ནུ་མ་ — **Kumārī (Kaumārī)** : spear & iron hook (right); jewel &
    noose (left). Mount: peacock.

5.  དབང་མོ་ — **Śacī / Indrāṇī** : arrow & vajra (right); bell & bow
    (left). Mount: elephant.

6.  ཚངས་མ་ — **Brahmāṇī** : lotus & brahmadaṇḍa (right); pitcher & cup
    (left). Mount: swan.

7.  དྲག་མོ་ — **Raudrī** : trident & ḍamaru (right); khaṭvāṅga & snake
    (left). Mount: bull.

8.  བཀྲ་ཤིས་ཆེན་མོ་ — **Mahālakṣmī / Maṅgalā** : lotus & jewel. Mount: lion.
