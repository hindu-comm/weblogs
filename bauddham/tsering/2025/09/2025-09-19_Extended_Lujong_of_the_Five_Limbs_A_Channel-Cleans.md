**Translator’s Note**

This Lujong-Trulkhor practice is connected with the Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga (Six Limbs of Yoga). The translation presented here draws on two works by Tāranātha: one composed in verse and the other written as a kind of commentary on the verse text. Tāranātha gives two principal names for this practice: *rtsa khrus chen mo* (“The Great Channel-Cleansing”) and *yan lag lnga sbyong rgyas pa* (“The Extended Practice of Lujong of Five Limbs”).

This translation is not intended as a practice manual. Those interested should seek instruction from a reliable teacher before attempting the practice.

Connected with Vajra Yoga — the Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga practice.

## Cleansing the channels

Sit in *Sattva* cross-legged posture, placing your hands upon your knees.

> published
>
> ``` text
> །ཀུན་གྱི་ཐོག་མ་རྩ་ཁྲུས་ནི། 
> །ལུས་སྦྱོང་ཀུན་གྱི་གཞི་བཅས་ནས། 
> །མགོ་བོ་ལག་པ་ལུས་སྟོད་སྨད། 
> །རྐང་པ་རྣམས་ལ་ཕུར་མཉེ་བྱ།
> ```

Channel-cleansing precedes all Lujong-Trulkhor;  
Making this the foundation of every practice,  
Then rub and massage the head and arms,  
The upper and lower body, and the legs.

### Head — Four Movements

In Sattva posture:

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> །མགོ་ནི་བསྐོར་དང་མདུན་རྒྱབ་འཕངས། 
> །གཅུ་ཞིང་སྤྲུག་པ་རྣམ་བཞིའོ།
> ```

Rotate the head, bend forward and backward,  
Tilt it side to side, and shake—these are the four movements.

- **Rotation:** Rotate the head clockwise three times, then
  counterclockwise three times.

- **Bending:** Bend the head forward and backward, and then to the right
  and left, three times each.

- **Turning:** Turn the head to the right and to the left three times
  each.

- **Shaking:** With the mouth open, tongue extended, and the arm and leg
  muscles relaxed, shake the head three times.

### Arms — Four Movements

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> །དཔུང་བ་བསྐོར་དང་ཞགས་པ་འཕངས། 
> ཁུ་ཚུར་འཕང་དང་སོར་ཚིགས་འཐེན། 
> །བཞི་པོ་ལག་པའི་རྩ་ཁྲུས་ཡིན།
> ```

Shoulder rotation and lasso swing,  
Fist swing and finger-joint pulling—  
These four are the arm channel-cleansing.

- **Shoulder Rotations:**

  - Place the fists at the intersection of thigh and hip, with the
    elbows pointing outward.

  - Rotate the right shoulder inward three times, then outward three
    times.

  - Rotate the left shoulder inward three times, then outward three
    times.

  - Rotate both shoulders simultaneously inward three times, then
    outward three times.

- **Lasso Swing:**

  - With the right hand in a fist, circle it over the head clockwise and
    swing the arm to the right, stretching and shaking the fingers.

  - Repeat three times.

  - Do the same with the left hand, circling it counterclockwise over
    the head.

- **Fist Swing:**

  - Bring both fists in front of the shoulders, with fists facing
    outward.

  - As you swing and extend the arms, turn the fists so they face
    upward.

  - Repeat three times.

- **Finger-Joint Pulling:**

  - Clap the palms together in front of the chest.

  - With the right thumb and forefinger, grasp the left thumb and pull
    from the base joint upward.

  - Repeat with each finger in turn.

### Upper Body — Four Movements

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> །པུས་འཇུས་རོ་སྟོད་གཅུད་པ་དང༌། 
> ཁུ་ཚུར་བརླར་བཞག་རོ་སྟོད་གཅུ། 
> །དཔྱང་ཕྲག་རྡེབ་དང་ནུ་བརྡེག་བཞི།
> ```

Holding the knees, turn the upper body;  
Hands on the thighs, twist the upper body;  
Strike the chest, and strike the side of the torso—  
These four are the upper-body movements.

- Grasp the right knee with your hands in a vajra-lock (fingers
  interlocked).  
  Rotate the upper body clockwise three times, then counterclockwise
  three times. Repeat the same movement while holding the left knee.

- Place your hands on your thighs. With elbows straightened, twist the
  upper body to the right and left three times each.

- Form fists, extend your arms, and strike the fists against the chest
  and the arms against the side of the torso. Repeat three times.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Lower Body — Four Movements

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> །ལྟོ་བ་བསྐོར་དང་བསྲུབས་ཤིང་སྲུབ། 
> །སྟ་ཟུར་གཅུད་དང་ཁུ་ཚུར་བརྡུང༌། 
> །སྨད་ཀྱི་བཞིའོ་[ཕོ་བའི་གསུམ]།
> ```

Rotate the abdomen, churn the churning stick;  
Twist the hips, strike with the fist—  
These are the four lower-body movements.

- **Abdominal Rotation**:

  - Place your hands on your knees.

  - Straighten your spine and bend your upper body slightly forward.

  - Rotate your belly clockwise three times, then counterclockwise three
    times.

- **Churning Motion**:

  - From a cross-legged position, press down your left knee with your
    left hand.

  - With your right hand, pull from over the right knee about a cubit
    toward you, across the thigh.

  - Twist the lower body forcefully to the right while simultaneously
    pulling the shoulders to the right.

  - Repeat three times, then switch sides and repeat three times.

  - Keep the arms straight—do not bend them.

- **Hip Turning**:

  - Plant your hands on the ground beside your hips.

  - Lift one knee at a time while keeping your buttocks on the ground.

  - Twist your hips as you move.

  - Repeat three times.

### Abdominal - Three Movements

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> [།སྨད་ཀྱི་བཞིའོ་]ཕོ་བའི་གསུམ། 
> །རྡོ་རྗེ་བསྡམས་དང་ལག་ངར་དང༌། 
> །གྲུ་མོ་ཕོ་བར་བརྡེག་པར་བྱ།
> ```

Three lujong of the abdomen—  
Strike the abdomen with vajra-locked palms,  
With forearms, and with elbows.

- With your hands in vajra-lock (fingers interlocked), lift them up
  overhead with palms facing upward, and strike the palms down onto your
  abdomen. Repeat 3 times.

- Hold the elbows with the opposite hands. Lifting the arms to the level
  of your hairline, strike the forearms onto your abdomen. Repeat 3
  times.

- Cross your arms and hold the shoulders with the opposite hands,
  stacking the elbows on top of each other. Bending the upper body
  slightly forward, lift the arms, and when they reach the level of your
  eyes, strike the elbows onto your abdomen. Repeat 3 times.

> published

> ``` text
> །རྐེད་པ་གཡས་གཡོན་མདུན་རྒྱབ་འཕུལ།

Bend your waist—right and left, forward and back.

### Legs — Three Movements

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> །སོར་འཐེན་རྐང་འཕྲ་ལངས་འདུག་གཉིས།
> ```

Pulling the toes, and two legs-stretching—  
In seated and standing position.

- **Toe Pulling (seated):**

  - From a cross-legged position, place your hands on the ground.

  - Lift your buttocks slightly and extend your legs forward.

  - One by one, pull the toes.

  - While pulling, thrust the heels outward; when the toes slip from
    your grip, curl the toes in and bend the tips of the feet downward.

  - If possible, pull each of the toes of both feet together using the
    corresponding hands.

  - After pulling the toes, place your hands on the ground, lift your
    body slightly, and draw the legs back into the cross-legged posture.

- **Leg Stretching (seated and standing):**

  - From a sitting position, rise to stand on your feet.

  - Stretch your legs forward, then bend them back—repeat three times.

  - Then bend down, plant your hands on the ground; then stand up again
    and continue the bending and stretching your legs.

  - During this practice: when extending the legs, the heels should be
    fully stretched; when bending, the toes should curl inward.

### Four Limbs - Two Movements

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> །གླིང་བཞི་ནམ་མཁར་སྤྲུག་དང་འཕགས།
> ```

In the sky shake the four limbs,  
Then bounce upon the back.

- Lie on your back with the spine firmly planted on the ground.

- Bend and stretch both arms and legs together upward in the air, three
  times.

- Then, bounce gently on your back three times.
