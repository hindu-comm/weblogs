> The secret [yogic] homa-offering is explained thus:
> The [yogi’s] head is the receptacle of the homadravya, the burning substance.
> Roma, the right channel, is the śruva, the pouring ladle.
> The heart chakra and Kyangma, the left channel, are the pātra, the filling ladle.
> At the navel chakra lies the fire-pit,
> Fanned by the karmic winds.
> There, at the junction of the three channels,
> Abides the primordial fire—the fire of Brahmā.
> ```

#### Taranatha’s commentary

- Since the bodhicitta bindu abides in the crown of the head, it is
  called the receptacle—the vessel of the *homadravya*, the burning
  substance, ghee.

- Roma, the right channel, is called the pouring ladle, for through it
  the fire of *tummo* rises and melts the bodhicitta bindu.

- Kyangma, the left channel, is called the filling ladle, for the
  bodhicitta bindu flows through it. The heart chakra is so named
  because, in the practice of *Vasanta-tilaka*, the bodhicitta bindu
  descends from the heart chakra.

- The navel chakra is the *kuṇḍa*, the fire-pit, the place where the
  burning substance—ghee—is offered.

- The primordial fire, the fire of Brahmā, residing four inches below
  the navel at the junction of the three channels, is the fire-nest of
  that fire-pit.

Now, the secret homa-offering is explained:  
The fire-pit is the navel, or the channel’s end.  
It is surely the source of liberation through the path of passion.  
Both natural and secret *tummo*—  
The fire of primordial wisdom blazes forth.

Using the right and left channels as *śruva* and *pātra*,  
Burn the *homadravya*—the subtle conceptual mind,  
Of *nāḍīs*, *vāyus*, and *śukra*—  
And make the offering to the fire-deity of bliss.

For what purpose is the homa performed?  
It bestows the supreme siddhis.  
As it burns away conceptual thought—thus is the fire-offering;  
Burning wood and the like is not true homa.
