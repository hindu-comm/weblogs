#### Ideal Age

The practitioner should be between 14 and 60 years of age.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Best Time of the Year to Practice

The ideal times for practice are late summer and early winter.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### The Significance of Conquering the *Vāyu*

The practitioner should choose a secluded place of their preference and practice subduing the five primary and five secondary *vāyu*. The root of all samsaric suffering lies in the conceptual mind. To transform the conceptual mind into pristine consciousness, one must first subdue the *vāyu*. For example, although a spear has a long wooden handle, the most effective part is the four-inch iron tip. Similarly, regardless of the many secret instructions in the generation and completion stages of the four tantras, the key lies in achieving stability of the *vāyu* within the body, which ultimately leads to non-conceptual pristine consciousness. If you practice well-established kumbhaka for five days, this can prolong your life by five years. Therefore, to gain stability and control over the *vāyu*, it is crucial to cleanse and purify the channels, and to purify the channels, it is essential to train and exercise the body.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Importance of training the body before performing breathing practices

To gain stability and control over the *vāyu*, it is essential to first cleanse and purify the channels. This purification, in turn, depends on training and exercising the body. Attempting to perform breathing practices without proper physical preparation is considered harmful. It is likened to bending a dry birch bark or trying to separate dry, brittle layers of birch—inevitably, they crack or tear.

**Why Training Is Necessary**  
From the first day of human life in the womb until the twelfth month, 200 subtle channels (*nāḍī*) develop each day, eventually forming a complete network of 72,000 channels within the body. However, as one ages, two channels dry up each day. As a result:

- The *phlegm channels* (*śleṣmanāḍī*) begin to stick together and
  close,

- The *bile channels* (*pittanāḍī*) begin to rot,

- The *blood channels* become enlarged and irregular.

If one performs breathing practices forcefully without first preparing the body, the air (*vāyu*) may involuntarily tear these dried and constricted channels. This can lead to serious consequences, such as:

- Skin conditions like urticaria (*dadruḥ*), resembling leprosy,

- The accumulation of air between the skin and flesh, leading to
  swelling or oedema,

- Various mental afflictions caused by disrupted internal balance.

**Benefits of Physical Training**  
The process of preparing the body can be compared to soaking dry birch bark that has become stuck together. By wetting it and gently massaging it, the bark softens and separates without damage. Similarly, through regular physical training and exercises:

- Dried and constricted channels are softened and reopened,

- The flow of vital air (prana) and vital fluids becomes smooth and
  balanced,

- Disorders caused by imbalances of the Four Elements are prevented,

- Mental clarity is enhanced,

- Signs of aging such as grey hair and wrinkles are delayed,

- Lifespan is extended.
