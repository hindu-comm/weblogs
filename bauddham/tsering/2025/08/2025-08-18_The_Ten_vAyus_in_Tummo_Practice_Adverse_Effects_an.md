# TRANSLATION

## Adverse Effects and Remedies of the Ten Vāyus in Tummo Practice

*(Five Primary Vāyus and Five Secondary Vāyus)*

When practising Tummo, disturbances in the ten vāyus (*rlung*) can produce various adverse effects.  
In this text, ill effects of the ten vayus and together with their corresponding remedies are taught.

## I. Prāṇa Vāyu

- **Name:** Life-Sustaining Air (*Tib.* སྲོག་འཛིན་གྱི་རླུང་, *Skt.* *prāṇa
  vāyu*)

- **Location:** Chest

- **Element:** Of the five elements, it is the air of water.

- **Function:** Responsible for feelings of happiness and sadness, as
  well as the conception of self or ego.

- **Adverse effects.**

- **Remedies**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Adverse Effects

### **I. Agitation and Unhappiness**

This manifests in two types: major and minor.

#### **1. Major**

**Symptoms** — A sudden surge of heat in the heart centre (associated with fear and anxiety), inability to control or focus the mind, and restlessness about remaining in one place.

#### Methods to Remove This Problem

There are three methods:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

**(i) Body posture:**  
Assume the six-point posture of Vairocana.

**(ii) Object of Observation:**  
Visualise at the heart centre, within the empty chest, the shape of a dove’s egg with a very thin shell. Inside it is a pea-sized drop of colostrum, radiant and imbued with the nature of bliss. Imagine the upper body being flooded with its light.

**(iii) Breathing Practice:**  
From a slight distance away from the nostrils, gently draw in a long stream of air down into the chest. Visualise this stream of air softly descending onto the bindu, making it cooler than before. When the breath remains inside and you can no longer hold it, exhale forcefully through the nose, imagining that all illnesses leave with the outgoing air.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **(ii) Minor**

**Symptoms** — A sense of unhappiness and restlessness, loss of appetite, and the breath being pushed upward into the chest, etc.

**Methods to Remove This Problem**  
There are three methods:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

**(i) Body Posture:**  
Assume the six-point posture of Vairocana.

**(ii) Object of Observation:**  
Visualise the heart *nāḍi-chakra*, which normally faces downward, as now turned upward with a small opening at its tip. From this opening, imagine the air of poison and the air of illness rushing out like a stream of smoke. Then, at the navel of the four-petaled heart chakra, visualise a cool white light radiating and filling the entire upper body.

**(iii) Breathing Practice:**  
Draw the upper air downward (inhale) in a continuous, long, and smooth stream. Then gently draw the lower air slightly upward. Hold the air for as long as your capacity allows. When you can no longer retain it, release the air forcefully through the nose. Repeat this practice several times.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **II. Stabbing Pain at the Chest**

**Cause —** While practising Tummo, if the *prāṇa vāyu* becomes blocked, instead of entering the central channel it diverts into the life-*nāḍī* (runs close to the three primary channels). As a result, the air is unable either to pacify or to flow properly into the other channels.

**Symptoms** — This will give rise to stabbing pain in the chest. Pain in the upper body. Pain in the livers and pain in the right and left armpits.

**Methods to Remove This Problem**  
There are three:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

**(i) Body Posture**  
Sit in the vajra cross-legged posture, and hold your knees with both hands.

**(ii) Object of Observation**  
Visualise the flame of Tummo, the size of a needle, precisely at the point where the pain arises.

**(iii) Breathing Practice**  
Inhale a long and smooth breath, and imagine the air gently blowing toward the place of discomfort, dispersing the pain like ashes scattered by the air from a bellows. When you can no longer retain the air, let the air out forcefully as before.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **III. Full and Empty**

**Cause** — While practising Tummo, if the air fails to enter the central channel and instead circulates within the chest, the problem of “full and empty” arises. Just as a frog is lifted and held up by water, the air becomes unwilling to descend, causing the chest to feel unnaturally full of air.

**Symptom** — Feeling pain with chest puffed up with air. Unable control or focus the mind.

**Methods to Remove This Problem**  
There are three:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

**(i) Body Posture**  
Sit in the vajra cross-legged posture, and hold your knees with both hands.

**(ii) Object of Observation**  
Visualise a flame about four finger-widths long at the spot where the pain is located.

**(iii) Breathing Practice**  
Inhale a long, smooth breath, and imagine the air moving toward the flame, where the illness-air is burned away. When you can no longer hold the breath, exhale through the mouth, visualising the diseased air being expelled. Then draw the air in again through the mouth and release it in the same way, repeating several times. After seven rounds, exhale forcefully from the belly with the sound “Ha…Ha.”

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### IV. Heaviness and Unclarity

This manifests in two types: major and minor.

#### 1. Minor

**Symptoms**: Heaviness — The body drops down with heaviness. Unclarity — Unclarity in mental focus or meditation.

**Methods to Remove This Problem**  
There are three:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

**(i) Body Posture**  
Sit in the vajra cross-legged posture, and hold your knees with both hands.

**(ii) Object of Observation**

Meditate as if the “upper city of the gods” (the head) is not present — as though the head had been severed by a blade.

Then, meditate as if from the “lower city of the nāgas” (the torso), smoke is rising upward into the sky. Visualise the air of poison and illness streaming out through the channel at the point where the neck has been cut.

**(iii) Breathing Practice**

Gently inhale and hold the breath for as long as your capacity allows. When you can no longer retain it, exhale forcefully through the nose. Repeat this several times.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **2. Major**

**Symptoms** — Loss of consciousness, as if fainting.

**Cause** — Within the body there are channels for the *bindu* and channels for the air. When the air mistakenly enters the channel of the *bindu*, it gives rise to such conditions.

**Methods to Remove This Problem**  
There are three:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

**(i) Body Posture**  
Assume the six-point posture of Vairocana.

**(ii) Object of Observation**  
Visualise a red *bindu*, the size of a pea, at the spot between the eyebrows, with a white *bindu* resting upon it.

**(iii) Breathing Practice**  
Inhale gently through the nose. Visualise the two *bindus* (red and white) resting on the tip of the breath, fine as a horse’s hair, at the spot between the eyebrows. As you inhale, imagine them moving outward to the edge of the sky. Then, visualise them returning toward the brow, flowing down through the opening of the central channel, and settling at the meeting point of the three channels below the navel. Focus your mind on the union of the red and white *bindus*. Press the upper air downward, draw the lower air slightly upward, and imagine the body quivering with bliss. Repeat this several times.
