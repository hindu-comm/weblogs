# **TRANSLATION**

## III. Apāna Vāyu

- **Name:** Downward-Clearing Air (Tib. ཐུར་སེལ་གྱི་རླུང་)

- **Location:** Between the navel and the testicles (perineum/urethra).

- **Element:** Of the five elements, it is the air of air.

- **Function:** Responsible for the opening and closing of the anus and
  genitalia \[for defecation and urination\], as well as for sexual
  activity. When it kindles into wisdom, it causes the lotus of
  knowledge to open — the knowledge of bliss-emptiness.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Adverse Effects

**Symptoms** — In the area between the testicles (perineum) and the anus, there arises a sensation as if pricked by nettles or a needle, accompanied by itching and a burning-stinging feeling. Blockage of feces and urine may occur, or conversely, an inability to stop when diarrhea arises.

### Remedies

**Physical Yoga (Trulkhor)**  
Sit in a squatting posture (legs crossed with the knees upright) and place your hands on the ground near the base of the spine. Stretch out one leg while releasing the lower air downward. Then, as you bend the leg back, draw the lower air upward \[toward the navel\]. Practise this in one breath, alternating the legs.

Next, stand on your feet. On even ground, bend one leg forcefully so that the heel strikes against the buttock. At the same time, draw the lower air upward and, by twisting the heel slightly, let it remain there. Then, forcefully plant the foot on the ground, releasing the lower air downward. Practise this by alternating the legs.

\[Finally,\] massage and rub the area between the anus and the testicles with your hand.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## IV. Udāna Vāyu

- **Name:** Upward-Moving Air (Tib. གྱེན་འགྱུའི་རླུང་)

- **Location:** Throat

- **Element:** Of the five elements, it is the air of fire.

- **Function:** Responsible for speech, laughter, inhalation and
  exhalation, and so forth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Adverse Effects

**Cause** — When practising, if the vital air at the throat becomes obstructed, it is unable either to travel into the other parts of the channels or to pacify within.

**Symptoms** — Pain in the neck; pain in the jaw and molar teeth; swelling in the larynx with hoarseness of voice; and pain in the vocal cords, etc.

## Remedies

There are two: Tummo and Trulkhor.

### **1. Tummo Yoga**

**(i) Body Posture**  
Assume the posture of Tummo practice.

**(ii) Object of Observation**  
Visualise the flame of Tummo, the size of a needle, precisely at the point where the pain arises.

**(iii) Breathing Practice**  
As you inhale, imagine the air falling softly onto the flame and focus your mind on it. Unite the upper air and lower air, and hold the breath to your capacity. While retaining the breath, twist your neck back and forth. When you can no longer hold it, exhale, visualising all illness and the air of poison clearing out with the breath.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **2. Trulkhor**

**(i) Body Posture**  
Sit in the vajra cross-legged posture and hold your knees with both hands.

**(ii) Object of Observation**  
As mentioned earlier.

**(iii) Breathing Practice**  
Unite the upper air and the lower air. Stretch the neck upward, then twist the head to the right, and do the same to the left. You may also bend the neck backward and forward. If this cannot be done due to pain or swelling in the neck, practise the following:

- Join the wrists together. Holding the breath, bend the head back and
  pull the arms in front of the knees, flexing the body until it
  quivers. Then twist the upper body to the right and left.

- Alternatively, sit in a squatting position. Place the elbows on the
  knees and support the jaw with the palms of your hands. Focus your
  mind on a flame the size of a needle at the spot of pain or illness.
  Inhale a long, smooth breath, imagining the air falling softly onto
  the flame. Shake the head gently as if quivering while holding the
  breath to your capacity. When you can no longer retain it, release the
  air forcefully.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## V. Vyāna Vāyu

- **Name:** Pervasive Air (Tib. ཁྱབ་བྱེད་ཀྱི་རླུང་)

- **Location:** In the head and the twelve main

  joints of the body.

- **Element:** Of the five elements, it is the air of space.

- **Function:** Responsible for walking, sitting, bending, stretching,
  and the movement of the body parts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Adverse Effects

**Symptoms** — Pain in the joints and headaches; in severe cases it can lead to crippling. Walking becomes bent and crooked, like one who is lame. The arms and legs may also grow crooked, and so forth.

### Remedy

**Trulkhor**

If it is summer, wash the entire body from head to toe. If it is winter, wash with warm water. Then dry the body with a smooth cloth and put on warm clothing.

Sit in a squatting posture (legs crossed with the knees upright), using your arms as a yoga strap to hold the knees in place. Holding the breath in *kumbhaka*, lie down on your right side and remain until you can no longer retain the breath, then release the air through the nose. Repeat this lying on the left side, and then in a prone position.

Next, hold the breath again and massage and rub the joints—\[such as the knees, ankles, wrists, elbows, shoulders, and hips\]—until you feel heat rising in those areas.
