## **TRANSLATION**

For the yogi who wishes to practice the *dhyāna* of the breathing practice, there are preliminary, main, and postliminary practices.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **I. Preliminary practice**

- In a solitary place, sit on a comfortable seat and purify the mind
  through Guru Yoga.

- Assume the vajra cross-legged posture (*vajraparyaṅka*), resting the
  hands over the knees. With the neck slightly bent forward, exhale the
  residual breath three times, and then remain steadily in this posture.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### II. Main Practice

The main practice consists of five methods:

1.  **Counting the breaths.**

2.  **Meditating on the breathing in and breathing out.**

3.  **Meditating on the shape of the breath.**

4.  **Meditating on the colour of the breath.**

5.  **Meditating on the breath entering the pot.**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 1. **Counting the breaths**

A young and healthy person generally breathes in and out 21,600 times in day and night.

- Without letting the mind wander, fix your gaze on empty space.

- Count one bead for each breath out, and one bead for each breath in.

When you count your breaths in this way continuously for day and night without distraction, the movement of thoughts will decrease — and so will the number of breaths.

- When the number of daily breaths decreases to 10,000, the body becomes
  very light.

- At this time, exhale with a long breath, and inhale with a full belly.

If you practise in this way, the eleven and a half breaths will come to a stop — either during the day or night.

Through repeated practice:

- The movement of karmic airs — flowing in and out through the sun and
  moon channels — will gradually cease.

- You will gain the ability to place the mind wherever you wish.

- Abiding in non-conceptual samādhi is the measure of perfection.

As karmic airs are exhausted,  
 — the conceptual mind is exhausted.  
As the conceptual mind is exhausted,  
 — karmic appearances are exhausted.  
Then, one abides continually in the state of non-conceptual wisdom.

At this stage, many miraculous powers (siddhis) may arise —  
such as:

- Walking through the sky,

- Walking on water without sinking,

- Sitting cross-legged on the tip of a blade of grass, and more.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 2. Meditating on the Air Coming In (Inhalation) and Air Going Out (Exhalation)

- Visualise a very clear red syllable ཨ (A) inside the chest.

- As the breath goes out, visualise a second red ཨ emerging from the one
  in the chest. Then another A emerges from that, and another —
  continuing in succession — until twenty-one syllables A have
  appeared,  
   rising one above the other.

- At the end of the exhalation, hold the breath out.

- During inhalation, visualise each syllable ཨ (A) dissolving into the
  one below it —  
  one into the next, then into the next — until all dissolve back into
  the main syllable ཨ (A). At the end of inhalation, hold the breath
  inside.

- If you practise in this way for seven days,  
   your body will become light,  
   your speech will become clear,  
   and your mind will rest in nonconceptual samādhi.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 3. Meditation on the Shape of Air

- Sit in the same posture. Focus your mind as well as your eyes on the
  bright crystal-like (*shel sgong*) spherical *bindu* that holds the
  union of the vital air and the consciousness at the spot between your
  eyebrows (where one of the upper tips of the two branches of the
  central channel remains).

- As you breathe out, visualise the *bindu* also moving away. At the
  same time, visualise that your body is also moving away (along with
  the breath and the *bindu*). Visualise your mind also completely
  disappearing in the space like a mist.

- Then visualise the *bindu* moving towards you and resting on your
  forehead as you inhale.

- If you feel cold, imagine the *bindu* (coming towards you) as hot, and
  if you feel hot, imagine the *bindu* as cold. This will bring balance
  to the meeting (of *bindu* and body).

- The outer signs of the accomplishment of this practice are that you
  don't feel that you have a body, the body becomes free from insect
  (infestation), and the mind becomes undistracted by other objects.

- The inner sign of the accomplishment of this practice is that it will
  pacify all kinds of diseases.

- The secret signs of accomplishment are that when you visualise your
  body moving away with the *bindu*, (as a result) your actual
  (physical) body also rises. By meditating on heat and cold, diseases
  are pacified and you don't need clothes. You won't have any problem
  with sweating even in a hot region.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 4. **Meditating on the colour of the breath**

- Visualise a skull cup (*kapāla*) at the navel — white on the outside,
  and red on the inside. Inside the skull cup, visualise a blue vajra,
  the size of a grain.

- As you breathe out, visualise the blue vajra moving outward along with
  a stream of bluish breath, going very far away.

- As you inhale, visualise the vajra returning to its place along its
  previous course, into the skull cup — just as before.

- As a result of this practice, when the breath goes out, it feels as
  though the mind moves — like a feather lifted by the air. When you
  breathe in, it feels as if there is a real vajra resting in your
  belly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 5. **Meditating on the breath entering the pot**

- Visualise a blue-green syllable Ha at the navel.

- Hold the union of upper and lower breath, and visualise the syllable
  Ha gradually filling with fire.

- As the breath is released, visualise it flowing out like a stream of
  blue smoke,  
  vanishing into space. At the same time, visualise your mind also
  fading away.

- As a result of inhaling and holding the breath, the physical body
  becomes free from food, the mind releases into bliss-emptiness, and
  Rigpa becomes free from the conceptual mind.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
