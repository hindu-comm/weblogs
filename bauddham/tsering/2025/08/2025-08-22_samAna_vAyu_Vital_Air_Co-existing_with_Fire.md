# TRANSLATION

## II. Samāna Vāyu

- **Name:** Vital Air Co-existing with Fire (Tib. མཉམ་གནས་ཀྱི་རླུང་ /
  མེ་མཉམ་གྱི་རླུང་ / མེ་དང་མཉམ་དུ་གནས་པའི་རླུང་)

- **Location:** Navel

- **Element:** Of the five elements, it is the air of earth.

- **Function:** Responsible for digesting food and drink. When it
  kindles into wisdom, it causes the lotus of knowledge to open — the
  knowledge of bliss-emptiness.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Adverse Effects

### **1. Fullness**

**Cause** — When practising Tummo, instead of entering the vital points (the mouth of the central channel), the vital air circulates in the navel region. As a result, the air fills up the belly.

**Symptoms** — Pain arises when the upper air is pressed down, but not when it is left unpressed. There is also discomfort in the abdomen.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Remedies

There are three:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

#### **(i) Body Posture**

Sit in the vajra cross-legged posture, and hold your knees with both hands.

#### **(ii) Object of Observation**

Visualise the flame of Tummo, the size of a needle, precisely at the point where the pain arises.

#### (iii) Breathing practice

Inhale a long, smooth breath, and imagine the air gently falling upon the flame, where the illness is burned away. When you can no longer hold the breath, exhale through the mouth, visualising the diseased air being expelled. After repeating this several times, massage the area of pain and expel the \[trapped\] air with a belch.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2. Paining

**Symptoms** — Pain radiating from the navel up to the breastbone, pain across the front and back of the upper body, and pain centered in the abdomen.

### Remedies

There are three:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

#### **(i) Body Posture**

Sit in the vajra cross-legged posture, placing your hands on your knees.

#### **(ii) Object of Observation**

Visualise the flame of Tummo, the size of a needle, at the exact spot where the pain is located.

#### (iii) Breathing practice

Fix the tongue to the palate and gently press the molar teeth together. Draw in a long, smooth breath through the mouth and visualise the air moving softly onto the flame. Hold the breath to your capacity. When you can no longer retain it, release the breath with the sound *Hi*, imagining all illness leaving with the air.

Then, while keeping the vajra cross-legged posture, hold the knees with both hands. Expand and raise the torso, twisting the chest and shoulders forward and backward, while holding the breath to your capacity. When you cannot hold it any longer, exhale again with the sound *Hi*, visualising the illness being expelled. After repeating this several times, belch repeatedly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **3. Lump / Growth**

**Symptoms** — Swelling of the belly; extreme pain when eating food or drinking fluids; a sensation of fullness in the abdomen even without eating; at the end, it develops into a tumour-like growth that can be felt by hand.

### Remedies

There are three:  
(i) **Body Posture**  
(ii) **Object of Observation**  
(iii) **Breathing Practice**

#### **(i) Body Posture**

Sit in the vajra cross-legged posture, and hold your knees with both hands.

#### **(ii) Object of Observation**

- **Piercing like a spear** — Visualise striking the tumour with a
  spear. Imagine the disease being pierced and shattered into pieces.

#### (iii) Breathing practice

Inhale a long, smooth breath and hold the air to your capacity. When you can no longer retain it, exhale forcefully through the nose. All these breathing practices should be done in one breath.
