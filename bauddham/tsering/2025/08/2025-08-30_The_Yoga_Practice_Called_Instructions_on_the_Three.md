I bow in reverence to the Yoginī, the Mother.

Having received the guru’s instruction,  
To realise the meaning of emptiness,  
The teaching of the triple svastika is explained,  
As taught by Kanakhalā and Mekhalā, \[the severed-headed sisters\].

Beginning from the crown down to the navel,  
\[Visualise\] one’s body as empty within,  
Except for the three channels—white, red, and yellow—  
All of them likewise hollow within.

At the right channel, visualise \[a red bindu\],  
the size of a needle-point, radiant in nature.  
At the left channel, visualise the lunar bindu.

The central channel is hollow within;  
place the mind there, free of \[the duality of\] outer \[object\] and inner \[mind\].  
If distraction arises, then steady mind  
by focusing on the two bindus of the right and left \[channels\].

Then, upon the severed-head central channel \[at the neck\],  
visualise a svastika arising from the syllable Hṛīḥ,  
turning with the colour of fire.  
On its four arms, visualise the seed-syllables Oṃ, Āḥ, Hūṃ, and Phaṭ.

At the right side, upon the Rasa channel,  
visualise a lunar svastika turning,  
arising from the syllable A,  
which stops the bodhicitta, the vāyu of water.

At the left side, upon the Lalana channel,  
visualise a red svastika turning,  
arising from the syllable Oṃ,  
which stops the vāyu of fire.

the vāyu rises upward and reaches the throat.  
In the central channel, as the vāyu of Tathāgata flows,  
it exits twelve finger-widths \[above the throat, cranial aperture\] from \[the severed central\] channel.

On the right and left sides, the air of water and fire,  
exhaling from the sun and moon channels.  
The two goddesses,(Vairocanī and Varṇanī),  
inhale them through their mouths.

The \[force of\] air pierce through the two side channels.  
Rising upward, it hits the two svastikas,  
Encircles the central \[channel or figure Chinnamasta\],  
Two side svastikas turn—one to the left, one to the right.

This kindles the fire of pristine wisdom,  
Melting slowly from throat to the soles of the body.  
At last, rest the mind upon the nondual bindu  
Within the boundless dharmadhātu.

By examination it is never attained,  
Its nature insubstantial, free from all characteristics of perceptual object.

> །གཡུང་དྲུང་འཁྱིལ་པ་གསུམ་གྱི་ཞལ་གདམས་ཡུམ་ཀངྐ་ལ་མེ་ཀ་ལས་གསུངས་པ་རྫོགས་སོ།།

Thus concludes the oral instructions on the three spiraling svastikas,  
spoken by the Mother \[Mahasiddha\] Kanakhalā and Mekhalā.
