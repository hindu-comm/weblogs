### **Intro**

In this post I present two different texts and translations, both written in the form of prayers describing the practice of the six limbs of Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga. The first text, mainly associated with the Jonang tradition, appears in Tāranātha’s collected works and in the Jonang collection of prayers, though its precise authorship is uncertain.

The second text was composed by Jamgön Amnye Zhab after he received teachings on the Kālacakra Tantra from Ngawang Kunga Dhondup (16th century). To make the translation of this text more accessible to general readers, I have taken some interpretive freedom, as a literal, word-for-word rendering would be difficult to follow. The Tibetan text is provided alongside the English translation, together with brief translation notes.

### Notes on Translation

- **བྱེད་བཅིངས (*****Karaṇa*****-*****bandhana*****):** One of the main
  aims of the *Pratyāhāra* practice of *Ṣaḍaṅga* *yoga,*is to see the
  image of emptiness (*Śūnyābimba*/སྟོང་པ་ཉིད་ཀྱི་གཟུགས་བརྙན་). This is done
  mainly by binding or restraining the vital air of the five senses
  (through the key postures and eye position), by binding the upper and
  lower air (*prana* and *apana vayu)*; this helps to subdue the mind
  and ultimately helps to see the image of emptiness. The Tibetan yogic
  phrase used for this practice is བྱེད་བཅིངས (*Karaṇa*-*bandhana*)་, བྱེད་
  (*Karaṇa*) refers to བྱེད་པ་དབང་པོ་ལྔ་ (the doer or instrument of the five
  senses), and བཅིངས (*bandhana*) refers to སྟེང་འོག་གི་རླུང་གཉིས་བཅིངས་པ་
  (binding or uniting the two upper (*prāṇa vāyu*) and lower air (*apāna
  vāyu)*. So the Tibetan term བྱེད་བཅིངས means binding the vital air of the
  five senses and the two upper and lower airs.

- མི་གཡོ་གསུམ་ལྡན་ (Unwavering body, speech and mind): **Unwavering body**:
  Sit motionless with legs crossed, spine straight, hands on knees or in
  dhyana mudra, neck slightly bent. Focus your eyes on the sky twelve or
  sixteen inches above the level of your nostrils with your eyes rolled
  up without blinking or moving your eyes. **Unwavering breath:** Do not
  deliberately hold air and do not deliberately exhale air. Let
  breathing be natural, without correction. **Unwavering mind:** Without
  correcting your mind, the mind in its innate present state,
  single-pointed but free and relaxed, abides in samadhi without
  distraction or thinking anything. The following three techniques help
  to maintain this unwavering mind (as told by Taranatha).

- སེམས་ཀྱི་བཞག་ཐབས་བཞི་ (Four methods of settling your mind): See my earlier
  post for details: *[Four meditation techniques used in Pratyahara
  \[Sense withdrawal\] to conquer
  thoughts](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/four-meditation-techniques-used-in?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=false)*

- Each of the six limbs of Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga is traditionally grouped into
  three categories: body, speech, and mind. Pratyāhāra and Dhyāna belong
  to the category of body; Prāṇāyāma and Dhāraṇā fall under speech (or
  breath); and Anusmṛti together with Samādhi belong to mind. In this
  presentation, however, Samādhi is set apart as primordial wisdom.

- དུས་མེ (*kālāgni*-fire at the end of the world) is also known as *rāhu*
  (སྒྲ་གཅན).

- The term *phyag rgya gsum* (the “three mudrās”) here refers to
  Karma-mudrā, Jñāna-mudrā, and Mahāmudrā. For practitioners of lower
  potential, Karma-mudrā involves working with one’s own body through
  posture, gaze, and *trul khor* practices, as well as with the body of
  a consort. Those of middling potential engage in the practice of inner
  heat (*tummo*), in which the blazing of the fire and the dripping of
  bindu give rise to the experience of the four joys. For practitioners
  of superior potential, the mudrā of secret instruction.

Focus the vajra-mind in open space;  
Cut the connection between the senses and their objects;  
May I cultivate ***pratyāhāra***,  
Directly perceiving the emptiness (*śūnyatā)*.

To stabilise the *śūnyatābimba* (image of emptiness),  
Practise the five factors of *dhyāna*—  
*ekāgratā, vitarka, vicāra, pīti,* and *akṣara-sukha.*  
May the ***dhyāna**–aṅga* fully manifest.

Stopping the two pathways of sun and moon on right and left,  
Guide the breath into the path of the central channel;  
Achieving complete victory over untimely death (*kālamaraṇa*),  
May the ***prāṇāyāma***–*aṅga* fully manifest.

In the bindu of the navel, heart, throat,  
And the forehead *nāḍi*–*cakra*,  
Hold the *prāṇa*, stopping its flow in or out;  
May the ***dhāraṇā***–*aṅga* fully manifest.

As the primordial wisdom-fire of *tummo* blazes,  
One sees oneself as the *iṣṭadeva*, free from concepts of the ordinary body;  
From that body, light radiates and spreads throughout the three worlds;  
May the ***anusmṛti***–*aṅga* fully manifest.

Remaining single-pointedly in the union of unchanging bliss (*akṣarasukha*)  
And emptiness free from subject–object duality,  
Attained through deep loving-passion (*anurakta*) for the *iṣṭadevatā* (and consort),  
May the ***samādhi***–*aṅga* fully manifest.

### I. Body (channels)

**Pratyāhāra**  
Assuming the proper posture and the gaze,  
With unwavering body, speech, and mind,  
Settle into meditation, applying the four methods of placing mind;  
Through day-and-night yoga,  
May the six senses withdraw from their six objects.

**Dhyāna**  
By remaining in the uṣṇīṣa–cakravartin–dṛṣṭi gaze,  
Practise single-pointedly the five factors of dhyāna,  
To stabilise the vision of the śūnyatābimba (image of emptiness);  
Thus may I perfect the **body’s vajra yoga**.

### II. Speech (Breath)

**Prāṇāyāma**  
By vajra-breathing, cleanse the passages of the nāḍīs;  
By gentle and forceful breathing method,  
Direct and bind the vāyu within the central channel;  
By blocking the pathways of sun and moon with force,  
May vāyu and mind enter the seat of Rāhu.

**Dhāraṇā**  
As you attain the stability of prāṇāyāma’s kumbhaka,  
Practise the kumbhaka of union of prāṇa and apāna-vāyu  
At the centre of the five nāḍi-cakras,  
Holding the vāyu within the unchanging bindu.  
Thus may I perfect the **vajra yoga of breath** (speech).

### III. Mind (Bindu)

**Anusmṛti**  
Guide down and hold the bodhicitta–bindu:  
The lower potential through Karma-mudrā,  
The middling through Jñāna-mudrā,  
And the supreme potential through Mahāmudrā.  
Beholding one’s body as a deity emanating light,  
Recollecting the śūnyatābimba—  
Thus may I perfect the **mind’s vajra yoga**.

### IV. Primordial Wisdom

**Samādhi**  
Without letting the bodhicitta-bindu fall,  
Draw it upward, realising the wisdom of the four joys;  
Culminating at the crown-cakra in the samādhi of bliss—  
Thus may I perfect the **vajra yoga of primordial wisdom**.
