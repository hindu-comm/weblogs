### Note

All my previous Substack posts have been chiefly focused on *dzogrim*, the completion-stage practices. To underline the vital importance of the preliminary foundations and the phase of generation, I’d like to share here the general preliminary practices in the context of Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga. These instructions are drawn from a long and detailed text on Ṣaḍaṅgayoga composed by the Jonang master Tārānātha, whose tradition is also known as *sadangayogi* or *sbyor-drug-pa*. I have translated the preliminary section of this text for readers who have an interest in it.

## General Preliminaries

There are two systems: one presenting three rounds of practice and another presenting five. The latter system is followed here.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### I. General preliminaries with three rounds of practice

1.  Preliminaries for all Dharma practices: *taking refuge and
    generating bodhicitta*.

2.  To purify negative deeds and obscurations: *the visualisation and
    recitation of Vajrasattva*.

3.  To accumulate merit and gain blessings: *the mandala offering and
    guru-yoga*.

    ------------------------------------------------------------------------

### II. General preliminaries with five rounds of practice

1.  The root of all Buddhist schools: taking refuge.

2.  The root of the Mahāyāna: generating bodhicitta.

3.  To purify misdeeds and obscurations: the Hundred-Syllable Mantra of
    Vajrasattva.

4.  To accumulate merit: the mandala offering.

5.  The root of blessings: guru-yoga.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **1. Taking Refuge in the Three Jewels (*****Triratna*****)**

In a solitary place, seated on a comfortable cushion, assume a customary posture and let the mind settle at ease. Wherever one abides, visualise that place as a great pure realm—vast and spacious. At its centre stands a beautiful house made of precious substances, a celestial mansion (*vimāna*) without limit or boundary. At its centre rises a wish-fulfilling tree (*kalpavṛkṣa*), its branches, leaves, flowers, and fruits flourishing.

Upon the top of the tree rests a throne of precious substances supported by lions, with lotus, sun, and moon seat-discs stacked upon one another. Upon this lofty and vast throne sits the root guru in the form of Vajradhara, surrounded by the lineage masters and the entire assembly of deities of the mandalas of the five Buddhas. At the base of the tree sit the vast hosts of Dharma protectors—*ḍākinīs* and guardian protectors of the Dharma—endowed with the eyes of wisdom, abiding in the manner of protecting me.

In the four directions around the wish-fulfilling tree, upon the ground within the celestial palace, visualise a lofty and vast lion-throne in each direction, each with seat-discs of lotus, sun, and moon. In the direction before the image of the guru, visualise the *nirmāṇakāya* Buddhas of the ten directions and the three times, such as the Śākyamuni. In the right-hand direction of the guru, visualise the *saṅgha* of bodhisattvas, such as Mañjuśrī. In the left-hand direction, visualise the saṅgha of śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas, such as Śāriputra. Behind the guru is the Dharma-jewel (*dharma*-*ratna*), abiding in the form of scriptures (you can also visualise it in the front).

Then envision yourself, together with all the limitless sentient beings, with fervent devotion taking refuge to the vast host of objects of refuge—the guru and the Three Jewels. Inspired by great compassion, especially towards all sentient beings, arouse the wish: “May these beings be protected from the sufferings of saṃsāra.” Then say this prayer:

> “These limitless sentient beings, from this moment onward and until
> they attain enlightenment:  
> I take refuge in the noble gurus.  
> I take refuge in the hosts of deities of the maṇḍala of the personal
> deity (iṣṭa-devatā).  
> I take refuge in the fully accomplished and perfect Buddhas
> (samyaksaṃbuddha).  
> I take refuge in the holy Dharma.  
> I take refuge in the noble Saṅgha.  
> I take refuge in the ḍākinīs and Dharma protectors, endowed with the
> eyes of wisdom.”

Then make this supplication three times:  
“May the guru and the Three Jewels bestow their blessings upon me and all sentient beings.”

Visualise the objects of refuge (the guru and the Three Jewels) dissolving into light and merging into yourself. Then dedicate the merits of taking refuge:

> “Through this virtue,  
> may all beings perfect the accumulations of merit (puṇya) and wisdom
> (jñāna),  
> and through the merit and wisdom thus attained,  
> may they realise the dharmakāya and rūpakāya.”

For the sleeping yoga, envision yourself lying upon a lotus–moon seat on the ground before the image of your guru. Enter sleep while meditating with devotion on the objects of refuge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 2. Generating Bodhicitta

Let your mind reflect upon the limitless world-systems of the ten directions. However far one travels in any of the ten directions, just as space is without limit, so too are the beings of the six realms spread throughout these world-systems. All these beings have been our kind parents. They all desire happiness, yet although they long for happiness, they do not know the methods for attaining it. Lacking such understanding, they engage only in actions that bring suffering—how truly worthy of compassion they are.

May I be able to draw them out of the sufferings of saṃsāra and swiftly lead them to the supreme nirvāṇa, the level of a samyaksaṃbuddha. Since I do not yet possess such power, may I swiftly attain Buddhahood endowed with that very power. For this purpose, I shall practise the profound path—vajra Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga.

> “May all sentient beings possess happiness and the causes of
> happiness.  
> May they be free from suffering and the causes of suffering.  
> May they never be parted from the happiness that is free from
> suffering.  
> May they abide in boundless equanimity, free from attachment to those
> who are close and aversion towards those who are distant.”

Recite these four immeasurables prayer and contemplate their meaning.

At the end of the session, perform the dedication prayer as described in the section on taking refuge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 3. Purifying bad deeds and obscurations:

After meditating on the Four Immeasurables, visualise at your crown a lotus arising from the syllable *paṃ*, and a moon-disc arising from the syllable *a*. Upon these, from the syllable *hūṃ*, there arises a white five-pronged vajra. At its centre is marked a *hūṃ*. From this, light radiates, paying homage to the bodhisattvas and purifying the bad deeds and obscurations of all sentient beings. The light then draws back and sinks into the *hūṃ*, which itself dissolves into light.

From this there appears the Bhagavān Vajrasattva, white in colour, with one face and two arms. With his right hand he holds a vajra, and with his left hand a bell; he embraces the consort. The mother, Dorjé Nyenma (Vajratopā), white in colour, holds a curved knife and a skull-cup, and is in union with him. Both are adorned with bone ornaments and precious jewelled ornaments. The father sits in the vajra cross-legged posture and the mother in the lotus cross-legged posture.

At the crown is *oṃ*, at the throat *āḥ*, at the heart *hūṃ*, and at the navel *ho*. From the *hūṃ* at the heart, light radiates, inviting blessings in the form of the nectar of wisdom.  
*jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ!* They dissolve into you, becoming inseparably one.

Say this prayer and recite the vajrasattva hundred-syllable mantra as many time as you can. Bhagavān, I pray: purify and cleanse all the bad deeds, obscurations, transgressions, and defilements that I and all sentient beings have amassed throughout beginningless saṃsāra.

*oṃ vajra heruka samayam/ anupālaya/ vajra heruka tvenopatiṣṭha/ dṛḍho me bhava/ sutoṣyo me bhava/ supoṣyo me bhava/ anurakto me bhava/ sarva siddhiṃ me prayaccha/ sarva karma su ca me/ cittaṃ śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ/ ha ha ha ha hoḥ/ bhagavan vajra heruka māme muñca/ heruko bhava/ mahā samayasattva āḥ (hūṃ phaṭ)*

> I am ignorant and deluded,  
> having violated and breached the samaya.  
> O guru and protector, grant me refuge!
>
> You, the supreme Vajradhara,  
> the embodiment of great compassion—  
> to you, chief of all beings, I turn for refuge.

All the violations and breaches of the samayas of body, speech, and mind, both principal and subsidiary—  
I confess them openly.

May all the negative deeds, obscurations, transgressions, and defilements that have been accumulated throughout beginningless saṃsāra be purified and cleansed. Then visualise Vajrasattva and consort dissolving into light and merging into yourself.

Then dedicate the merit with this prayer:

> “Through this merit,  
> may I swiftly accomplish the state of Vajrasattva.  
> Without leaving even a single being behind,  
> may I establish them all on that very level.”

One should spend one or two months engaged in this visualisation and recitation practice, until signs of the purification of negative deeds and obscurations appear. These signs include dreams of flying in the sky, bathing, crossing a great river, or expelling impurities such as vomiting, diarrhoea, or the body discharging pus, blood, and the like—arising as indications of purification in the form of experiential signs, or as very clear dreams occurring repeatedly.

**Supreme signs of purification:** As a result of this purification practice, if you actually experience your mind becoming clearer than before, your visualisation more vivid, your body lighter, and your faith, compassion, and renunciation growing stronger—while sleepiness, dullness, laziness, attachment, aversion, and the like diminish—these are the supreme signs of successful purification.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 4. To accumulate merit: the mandala offering and so forth

First carry out the practice of taking refuge and generating bodhicitta as explained above. Then, in the open space before you, clearly visualise the guru, the yidam, the buddhas, and the bodhisattvas, just as in the earlier refuge practice. After that, make prostrations, take refuge, and offer maṇḍalas.

**Prostrations:** Recite this and make prostrations.

*Gang gi trin gyi dechen nyi  
Kechik nyi la charwa gang  
Lama rinchen tapü ku  
Dorjé cen zhap pé-la dü*

Recite this and make prostrations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Taking refuge:** Recite this and take refuge.

> You are the father and you are the mother.  
> You are the guru of all beings;  
> you are my relatives and good friends.
>
> You are also the protector;  
> you are the one who carries out all that is beneficial  
> and the remover of evil.
>
> You are the supreme and ultimate refuge,  
> the sole abiding place of the highest qualities;  
> the one who destroys all faults is you.
>
> You are the protector of the lowly,  
> the wish-fulfilling jewel,  
> the lord of the Victorious Ones.  
> To you I go for refuge.

Or, in brief, recite this:  
“To the glorious holy gurus,  
the precious Buddha of the three times,  
I go for refuge.”

**Offering mandala**

Physically arrange the maṇḍala with heaps of grains and precious substances. Mentally visualise the universal base, the sphere of the four elements upon which the four continents rest, together with Mount Meru and the encircling mountains. Above this, visualise the entire universe—from the summit of saṃsāra down through the whole of the three worlds—piled high with the excellent offerings of gods and humans, such as the seven precious objects. Then recite this supplication: “I offer this to the guru and the Three Jewels. May I swiftly attain Buddhahood, and may the yogic experiences and realisation of the profound path, the Vajra Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga, blossom.”

***oṃ vajra bhūmi āḥ hūṃ***  
Upon the perfectly pure basis—the supremely mighty golden earth.

***oṃ vajra rekhé āḥ hūṃ***  
In the centre of the encircling ring of mountains stands the *hūṃ*, Mount Meru, king of mountains.

To the east lies *Pūrvavidehaḥ*,  
to the south *Dakṣiṇajambūdvīpaḥ*,  
to the west *Aparagodānīyaḥ*,  
and to the north *Uttarakuruḥ*.

Rāhu, the sun, the moon, and the fire of time are present;  
and in the centre is the excellent wealth of gods and humans, lacking nothing.

All of this—together with the gurus, both root and lineage,  
the yidam and its retinue of deities,  
and the entire assembly of buddhas and bodhisattvas—  
I offer as a maṇḍala. Out of compassion, please accept this offering  
and bestow your blessings.

The ground is anointed with fragrant water and strewn with flowers.  
Mount Meru and the four continents, adorned with the sun and moon—  
I offer this, visualising it as a Buddha-field.  
Through this offering, may all beings enjoy the pure realm. Recite these words and offer the maṇḍala to the best of your ability.

At the conclusion of the session, visualise the objects of offering dissolving into light and merging into yourself, becoming one with you. Then dedicate the merit just as in the section on taking refuge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 5. Guru yoga

I supplicate the precious guru, the embodiment of the four kāyas.  
I supplicate the precious guru, unrivalled supreme refuge.  
I supplicate the precious guru, unrivalled supreme guide.  
I supplicate the precious guru, revealer of the path of liberation.  
I supplicate the precious guru, source of siddhi.  
I supplicate the precious guru who dispels the darkness of ignorance.

Grant me empowerment and bless me, I pray.

Bless me so that I may practise the sādhana single-pointedly, and that no obstacles arise in my sādhana.

Bless me so that I may experience the very essence of the sādhana .

Bless me so that I may bring the sādhana to its supreme and final completion.

Bless me so that I may develop loving-kindness, compassion, and bodhicitta.

Bless me so that, within my mind-stream, I may attain the extraordinary experience and realisation of mastering the samādhi of union of śamatha and vipaśyanā.

Bless me so that I may progress through and complete the stages of the profound path, the Vajra Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga.

Bless me so that in this very life I may realise the supreme siddhi of the Great Seal (Mahāmudrā).

**Four empowerments**

Visualise **oṃ** at the guru’s crown, **āḥ** at the throat, **hūṃ** at the heart, and **hoḥ** at the navel. Into these syllables enters the vajra essence of the body, speech, mind, and pristine wisdom of all the Buddhas.

From **oṃ**, white light-rays radiate and dissolve into your crown, purifying the negative deeds and obscurations of the body. You receive the vase empowerment, and the blessings of the guru’s body enter you.

From **āḥ**, red light-rays radiate and dissolve into your throat, purifying the negative deeds and obscurations of speech. You receive the secret empowerment, and the blessings of the guru’s speech enter you.

From **hūṃ**, dark-blue light-rays radiate and dissolve into your heart, purifying the negative deeds and obscurations of mind. You receive the wisdom-knowledge empowerment, and the blessings of the guru’s mind enter you.

From **hoḥ**, yellow light-rays radiate and dissolve into your navel, purifying the karmic imprints of attachment. You receive the fourth empowerment—the pristine-wisdom empowerment.

Once again, with heartfelt devotion, make the supplication. Visualise the Buddhas and bodhisattvas seated around the guru dissolving into the guru. The guru, in a state of great joy, arrives at the crown of your head and then dissolves into you in the form of light or nectar. Remain for a short while in meditative equipoise, in which your guru and your own mind become inseparably one. Continue this meditation repeatedly, as deeply as you can.

Then, as before, dedicate the merits.
