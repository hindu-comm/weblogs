### **Notes to Readers**

*Dhāraṇā* is the fourth *aṅga* of *Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga*. In this practice, through the *kumbhaka* of the six *nāḍī-chakras*, the *prāṇa* (and the mind that rides on the vital air) is merged into the *bindu* within the central channel. As a result, the yogin perceives the image of emptiness (*śūnyatā-bimba*) with greater clarity and vividness, directly integrated with experience.

For the *dhāraṇā* section of Tāranātha’s *Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga* text, see “*[Dhāraṇā: Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga Text by Tāranātha](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/dharana-sadanga-yoga-text-by-taranatha?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=false)*.” For Tāranātha’s explanation of the *kumbhaka* practices related to the five *nāḍī-chakras* and their corresponding yogic postures, read “*[Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga’s Dhāraṇā Practice: Kumbhaka of Five Nāḍī-Chakras](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/sadanga-yogas-dharana-practice-kumbhaka?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=false)*.”

Both Tāranātha and Drikung Chungwa Chökyi Drakpa, in their instructions on *dhāraṇā*, describe a specific practice in which the yogin “milks” the *amṛta* from the small tongue using the tongue itself — a method closely resembling the *khecarī mudrā* known in the Hindu *Haṭha Yoga* tradition.

Here is an excerpt from Tāranātha’s text:

> Place the hands in a ‘earth-touching’ mudra, otherwise rest of the
> body postures are same. Visualise a white ‘ham’ (ཧཾ) bindu at the
> forehead circled by sixteen vowels. If you can not visualise the
> syllables clearly, it is ok to visualise just white bindu. As you draw
> the apāna vāyu upwards, the air, which is naturally warm, goes up
> through the abdomen and the chest. And when the warm air touches the
> white bindu, the Amrita begins to fall down like a stream of milk.
> Then with the ‘shu’ sound, draw in the air through the mouth, bringing
> the air over the tongue. And then swallow the air along with the
> saliva. Then roll your tongue into the cavity, touching the little
> tongue. Having filled the air equally in both the upper and lower
> body, a stream of nectar will flow down and imagine it filling the
> nāḍīs throughout the body. If you practise this again and again. One
> day the nectar will flow naturally from the little tongue.

A similar practice is mentioned in the Sakya *Lamdré* texts, though not specifically in connection with *Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga*. The text pays homage to Virūpa. For more on this, see my earlier post: [“Extracting Amṛta: Vajrayāna Version of Khecarī Mudrā!?”](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/extracting-amrta-vajrayana-version?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=false)

# TRANSLATION

## Ṣaḍaṅga Yoga’s *Dhāraṇā* 

#### **I. Preliminary — Mixing the** ***Prāṇa*** **and** ***Apāna Vāyu***

When the breath flows evenly through both nostrils, proceed; if not, regulate it through the practice known as *straightening the crooked channels*. Exhale long, expelling the prāṇa with the sound *Hum*. Then, without inhaling, make a short *Hum* sound and draw the apāna vāyu upward with force, contracting the abdomen towards the spine. Repeat this several times. As you continue the practice, a growling sound will arise in the lower abdomen — a sign of gaining control over the apāna vāyu. At that moment, draw the prāṇa vāyu downward and train in uniting \[the upper and lower airs\] for an extended period.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **II. Actual Dhāraṇā Practice — Holding Kumbhaka at the Six Nāḍi-Chakras**

**Visualisation of the Nāḍi-Chakra System**

Visualise yourself in the form of a deity. Within the body runs the central channel. Its upper end rises to the crown of the head, bends forward, and remains pierced at the point between the eyebrows. Its lower end descends to the perineum, curves forward, and remains pierced at the tip of the *vajra* (penis), through which the white element flows. The upper ends of the right and left channels are pierced at the two nostrils. From just below the navel, the right channel turns forward towards the *vajra*, where the urine flows; the left channel turns backward, where the faeces flows.

At the navel, where the three channels meet, visualise the *nāḍi-chakra* of the earth element, with sixty-four *nāḍi*-petals. At the heart centre, where the three channels meet, is the *nāḍi-chakra* of the water element, with an inner circle of four *nāḍi*-petals and an outer circle of eight petals. At the throat is the *nāḍi-chakra* of the fire element, with an inner circle of four *nāḍi*-petals, a middle circle of sixteen, and an outermost circle of thirty-two petals.

At the forehead is the *nāḍi-chakra* of the air element, with an inner circle of four *nāḍi*-petals, an outer circle of eight, and an outermost circle of sixteen petals. At the crown of the head, about an inch below the cranial aperture, is the *nāḍi-chakra* of the space element, with four *nāḍi*-petals. Finally, visualise the *nāḍi-chakra* of primordial wisdom at the perineum, with an inner circle of ten *nāḍi*-petals and an outer circle of sixteen petals.

#### **1. Navel Kumbhaka (ལྟེ་བའི་བུམ་ཅན/*****nābhi*****-*****kumbhaka*****)**

With a long *Hum* sound, exhale and expel the *prāṇa vāyu*. Then, before inhaling, draw the *apāna vāyu* upward with a short *Hum* sound. Next, inhale slowly, filling the body with *prāṇa vāyu*. Press the tongue against the palate and roll the eyes upward, keeping them wide open.

In this way, hold the *kumbhaka* according to your capacity, from three up to one hundred *kumbhakas*. While holding the breath, focus the mind on the *nāḍi-chakra* at the navel. Finally, visualise the navel *nāḍi-chakra* dissolving into light, and that light merging into the *nāḍi-chakra* of the heart. This is known as Navel Kumbhaka.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **2. Heart Kumbhaka (སྙིང་གའི་བུམ་ཅན/*****hṛd-kumbhaka*****)**

Inhaling and filling the *prāṇa vāyu* with a long *Hum* sound, and drawing up the *apāna vāyu* with a short *Hum*, are done as before. Here, the *kumbhaka* is held at the heart centre. At the conclusion, visualise the petals of the heart *nāḍi-chakra* dissolving into light, and that light merging into the *nāḍi-chakra* of the throat. This is known as Heart Kumbhaka.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **3. Throat Kumbhaka (*****Kaṇṭha-kumbhaka*****)**

With the same breathing technique, hold the *kumbhaka* at the throat centre. Finally, visualise the throat *nāḍi-chakra* dissolving into light, and that light merging into the *nāḍi-chakra* of the forehead. This is known as Throat Kumbhaka.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **4. Forehead Kumbhaka (*****Lalāṭa-kumbhaka***

With the same breathing technique, hold the *kumbhaka* at the forehead centre. Finally, visualise the forehead *nāḍi-chakra* dissolving into light, and that light merging into the *nāḍi-chakra* of the crown. This is known as Forehead Kumbhaka.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **5. Crown Kumbhaka (uṣṇīṣa-*****kumbhaka)***

With the same breathing technique, hold the *kumbhaka* at the crown centre. Finally, visualise the crown *nāḍi-chakra* dissolving into light, and that light merging into the *nāḍi-chakra* of the perineum. This is known as Crown Kumbhaka.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **6. Perineum Kumbhaka (*****guhya/yoni-kumbhaka)***

With the same breathing technique, hold the *kumbhaka* at the perineum centre. Then visualise the *nāḍi-chakra* at the perineum dissolving into light and transforming into a black syllable *Hum*, which shoots upward through the central channel. When it reaches the heart centre, it transforms into five coloured lights.

At that moment, your entire body together with the three channels blazes with radiant light — focus your mind upon that. After holding the *kumbhaka* for as long as you can, finally rest the mind in objectless awareness, as if the five coloured lights have vanished like a rainbow.

### **III. Supreme Rasāyana — Drinking the Inner Amṛta**

Sit in the same posture as in *pratyāhāra*. At the cranial aperture, within the cranial membrane (*mastiṣka-tvak*), visualise a *candra-maṇḍala* (orb of the moon) the size of a thumb. At its centre is the *bodhicitta-bindu*, shaped like a nipple on the verge of releasing *amṛta* (nectar), and encircled by sixteen vowels.

With that visualisation, exhale the *prāṇa* with a long *Hum*, and with a short *Hum* draw the *apāna* upward. Then inhale *prāṇa* through both the nose and the mouth, filling the body. As a result, the warm air of the self-born *tummo* stimulates the white element, and from the nipple-like *bindu* descends the white *amṛta*, flowing down through the “small tongue” of the palate.

As you gently draw through the small tongue, visualise the whole body becoming filled with *amṛta*, quenched, and intoxicated with inexhaustible bliss. As you continue to activate and draw through the small tongue, at first saliva will begin to descend, and finally a sweet taste will arise. This is the supreme form of *Rasāyana*.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **IV. Signs of Accomplishment of Holding Kumbhaka at the Six Chakras**

The image of emptiness (*śūnyatā-bimba*) that was previously perceived during *pratyāhāra*, *dhyāna*, and *prāṇāyāma* now appears clearer, more vivid, and integrated with direct experience. One is able to engage with the invincible (*anāhata*) form, sound, smell, taste, and touch.

As a result of the merging of *prāṇa* and *apāna vāyu*, the qualities of *tummo* of primordial wisdom manifest. Even if one eats food as vast as Mount Meru, it will be effortlessly digested; and even if one does not eat even a grain of sesame, no sense of hunger or thirst will arise.
