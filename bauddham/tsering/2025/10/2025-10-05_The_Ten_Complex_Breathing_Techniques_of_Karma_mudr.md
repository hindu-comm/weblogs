## **Translator’s Note**

This text is taken from Jonang Taranatha’s ‘Instruction on the Six Transmission Lineages of the Indian Master Shanti-Gupta’. It is a compilation of six practices transmitted in Tibet through Indian Siddhas’. The six practices are Stage of Generation (བསྐྱེད་རིམ), *Mahāmudrā* (ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཆེན་པོ), Tummo (གཏུམ་མོ), Clear Light (འོད་གསལ), Karma-mudra (ལས་རྒྱ) and Miscellaneous Instructions. The following English translation of the ten breathing methods (རླུང་སྦྱོར) is part of the Karma Mudra section of Jonang Taranatha’s ‘Instruction on the Six Transmission Lineages of the Indian Masters’. The text says that before doing these physical yoga and breathing practices, one should first learn the [four breathing techniques](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/tummos-four-breathing-methods-jonang?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web) \[click the text\] mentioned in the Tummo section of this text.

These ten breathing methods is followed by number of physical yoga practices that I will try to translate into the future post. I have shared the instructions at the end of this post, what to do after the breathing practices and before the commence of physical yoga practices. Some of the breathing techniques are given specific names, while others are left unnamed in the source text. I have translated the named techniques as closely as possible to their intended meaning, while preserving the phrasing and nuance of the original Tibetan where clarity allows.

The leg posture for breathing methods seven, eight, and nine is as follows: press one heel firmly against the perineum, and place the other heel on top of the first, resting on the talus while maintaining pressure at the perineum.

# Ten Breathing Methods

### **I. Supreme Drawing-Breath** 

*(Exhaling in Three Segments and Inhaling in Three Segments)*

Assume the correct posture and clear away the residual \[harmful\] air. Then exhale slowly. At the end of the exhalation, cut off the flow and hold it out briefly. Exhale once more, cut off the flow, and hold it out briefly again. Exhale a third time, and then inhale slowly.

At the end of the inhalation, cut off the flow and hold it in briefly. Inhale again, cut off the flow, hold it, and then press the upper air forcefully downward toward the lower abdomen \[?\]. At the same time, draw the lower air upward toward the back, contracting the waist firmly. Hold the air as long as you are able.

When you can no longer hold it, exhale in the manner described above: dividing one out-breath into three short segments. In the same way, divide one in-breath into three segments. Practise this cycle of exhalation, inhalation, and holding again and again.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **II. Primary Drawing-Breath**

Assume the correct posture and clear away the residual harmful air several times. Then inhale straight in and press the upper air down towards the navel. Next, draw the lower air upward toward the navel with force, and hold the union of upper and lower airs there.

Without losing the force that presses the upper air down, pull the union of airs upward strongly. Likewise, without losing the force that draws the lower air upward, with the anus closed firmly, and push the union of the two airs downward with strength towards the lower abdomen.

Practise this sequence about three times, and finally let the air out.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **III. Secondary Drawing-Breath**

Assume the correct posture and clear away the residual harmful air several times. Then exhale fully and hold the breath out to the limit of your capacity. Forcefully draw the lower air upward by pressing the belly against the spine, and raise the inner force of the breath within the abdomen upwards about three times.

While still holding the breath out, gently loosen the anus and press the inner force of the air downward. When discomfort arises, inhale. Practise this repeatedly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### IV. Fully-Rotating Breathing Technique

Sit either in the Jackal or in the Elephant Pose.

- Jackal Pose: Sit in a sattva cross-legged posture, grasp the legs just
  above the ankles, and bend the body forward.

- Elephant Pose: Place both knees and hands firmly on the ground, and
  bend the upper body downward.

With either of these two postures established, exhale the air fully and hold the breath out to the limit of your capacity. In that posture and breathing state, rotate the force-of-void within the abdomen clockwise and counter-clockwise. Then inhale, unite the upper and lower airs, and turn them clockwise and counter-clockwise.

Rotate the force-of-void for a longer duration while holding the breath out than when holding the breath in.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **V. Sixfold Transformation**

The sixfold transformations of the body are as follows:

1.  Sit cross-legged with the knees upright, crossing the arms and
    placing the hands beneath the opposite armpits.

2.  Tighten the muscles of the arms and legs.

3.  Turn the gaze upward.

4.  Press the tongue against the palate.

5.  Bend the throat.

6.  Draw the abdomen inward, fixing the belly to the spine.

First, assume the first five postures. With the throat bent, draw the *apāna-vāyu* upward, and simultaneously exhale with the sound of the syllable Hum, contracting the abdomen toward the spine — this is the sixth transformation. Hold the breath out. If discomfort arises, then inhale and repeat the practice.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **VI. Lengthened** ***Hum*** **Sound \[Breathing\] Technique**

\[Sit with\] both legs and arms extended forward. In this position, exhale the breath with the tone of twenty-one *Hum* syllables, emptying the air completely, and hold the breath out briefly. Then, fixing the abdomen to the spine, draw the *apāna-vāyu* upward. Next, as you inhale, grasp your legs by the feet and draw the breath in fully.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **VII. Natural Movement of Breath Stopped**

Do not perform the preliminary practice of clearing the residual air. Completely cut off the flow of breath. Press the inner force within the abdomen downward, and strive to release the *apāna-vāyu* as fully as possible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **VIII. \[Unnamed Breathing Technique\]**

Clear away the residual harmful air. Then inhale, drawing in the upper air, press it downward, and release the *apāna-vāyu*.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **IX. \[Unnamed Breathing Technique\]**

Clear away the residual harmful air. Draw the air inside \[inhale\] and draw the *apāna-vāyu* upward. Then thrust the force of the upper air downward toward the perineum, as if pounding with a pestle, five or six times, until the body trembles.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **X. \[Unnamed Breathing Technique\]**

Clear away the residual harmful air. Without drawing air in through the nose, press the inner force within the chest and abdomen downward toward the perineum with strength. Then let it rest naturally, and draw the *apāna-vāyu* upward with force. Next, inflate the belly, and open and close the anus three times.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Follow-up Instruction After the Breathing Practices

**(Preparation for the Physical Yoga Practices)**

After practising these breathing techniques (*rlung sbyor*) over an extended period, wash the entire body with warm water for a day or two. Then sunbathe and massage the body.

After that, bathe again with scented water and massage the body. Finally, wash with the juice of the *dhattūra* or *thang-phrom* seed and massage once more.

After that, take the ashes remaining from burning cow dung and mix them with clean water until a smooth paste is formed. Apply this soft, purified mixture—free from stones and impurities—to the entire body except for the sense organs. Then again massage the body in the sun. Finally, wash the body with water.

Afterward, anoint the body with the juice of three medicinal fruits (*aa-ru-ra/ba-ru-ra/kyu-ru-ra*) and the extract of the three hot medicinal plants (nga-skya. pi-pi-ling, pho-ba-ris), rubbing and massaging well.

These methods will make the skin-hair (pores), and channels of the body soft and pliant, thereby removing obstacles and hindrances \[in order to performing the next physical yoga practices\].
