## Notes for the Readers

- Various Tibetan Buddhist texts describe several complex non-seated
  yoga postures—such as the *Rooster Pose* and headstand postures—though
  they do not use the same names found in the Hindu yoga texts. However,
  this is the only text in which the author employs the same Sanskrit
  name and description of the non-seated posture *Kāpālāsana* as found
  in the *Haṭhayoga* tradition. In this context, the headstand posture
  is prescribed as a remedy for disturbances arising in the
  *Vyāna-vāyu*.

- The following yogic methods are applied when disturbances occur in the
  *vāyus* as a result of practices such as *kumbhaka* and
  completion-stage yoga.

&nbsp;

- This passage is drawn from **Jonang Tāranātha’s** *Instruction on the
  Six Transmission Lineages of the Indian Master Śānti Gupta*.  
  The text is a compilation of six systems of practice transmitted in
  Tibet through Indian *mahāsiddhas*:

1.  **Stage of Generation** (བསྐྱེད་རིམ)

2.  **Mahāmudrā** (ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཆེན་པོ)

3.  **Tummo** (གཏུམ་མོ)

4.  **Clear Light** (འོད་གསལ)

5.  **Karma-mudrā** (ལས་རྒྱ)

6.  **Miscellaneous Instructions**

The following English translation—**Methods for Dispelling the Disorders of the Five Primary Vāyus**—is part of the *Miscellaneous Instructions*.  
The five secondary *vāyus* have not been included here.

## Remedies for the Disorders of the Five Primary Vāyus

### I. Prāṇa-vāyu

**Symptoms:**  
When a disturbance arises in the *prāṇa-vāyu*, symptoms such as pain in the upper torso, discomfort in the heart, or mental restlessness may occur.

**Breathing Remedy:**  
At such times, in order to dispel these disturbances, one should focus the mind intensely at the tip of the vajra jewel (penis). If one has previously trained in *kumbhaka* or similar breathing practices, one should draw the *apāna-vāyu* upward.  
If not, then press the upper-air downward toward the perineum, while meditating that the lower-air dissolves at the vajra jewel.  
By doing so, the disturbance will be cleared away.

**Physical Substance Remedy:**  
Pouring hotspring water or lukewarm water over the upper torso also helps to relieve the disturbance.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### II. Samāna Vāyu (མེ་མཉམ་གྱི་རླུང་)

**Symptoms:**  
When a disturbance arises in the *Samāna-vāyu*, symptoms such as swelling and rumbling in the abdomen, belching, or retching may occur.

**Breathing Remedy:**  
At such times, one should focus the mind at the throat, then circulate the middle-air located in the lower abdomen. And then by rotating at the upper abdomen, these disturbances will be cleared away.

**Physical Substance Remedy:**  
To assist, apply *chang* (Tibetan beer) upon the stomach first, and then place warm ashes or any similar substance that counteracts cold.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### III. **Apāna Vāyu (ཐུར་སེལ་གྱི་རླུང་)**

**Symptoms:**  
When a disturbance arises in the *Apāna-vāyu*, symptoms such as pain in the urinary and excretory channels, retention or blockage of urine or stool, or inability to expel waste may occur.

**Remedy:**  
At such times, one should focus the mind at the crown of the head.  
By massaging the regions of the urinary and excretory channels, and gently tapping the buttocks with the fists, these disturbances will be cleared away.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **IV. Udāna Vāyu (གྱེན་འགྱུའི་རླུང་)**

**Symptoms:**  
When a disturbance arises in the *Udāna-vāyu*, symptoms such as constriction of the throat, a fleshy growth within, burning sensations, or irritation in the nostrils and vocal cords may occur.

**Remedy:**  
Inhale forcefully, hold the breath, and rotate the head.  
Then exhale forcefully and again rotate the head.  
Rotating the head while maintaining *kumbhaka* at the throat is the supreme method for dispelling this disorder.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### V. **Vyāna Vāyu (ཁྱབ་བྱེད་ཀྱི་རླུང་)**

**Symptoms:**  
When a disturbance arises in the *Vyāna-vāyu*, symptoms such as pain throughout the entire body, numbness, or localized pain in specific parts of the body may occur.

**Physical Remedy:**  
Shake all the limbs and let the whole body tremble.

**Substance Remedy:**  
Apply a mixture of oil and *gyerma* (a kind of pepper) over the body.

**Physical Yoga Remedy:**  
Assume the *Kāpāla-āsana* posture—place the crown of the head on the ground and raise both legs upward toward the sky.  
Practicing this posture will clear away the disorder.
