### Notes

This is a translation of a very short Tibetan text called འཆི་མེད་ཀྱི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་ \[Attainment of Immortality's Trulkhor\]. It is very difficult to say whether this Trulkhor practice is related to Virupa's Amrtasiddhi teaching. According to the colophon of the text, it was written by a person called the Panchen Lama \[who may not be the Panchen Lama of the Geluk tradition\]. I found this text in the Drikung Dharma Treasury text in the Rechung Nyengyud section. Interestingly, the name of the trulkhor \[འཆི་མེད་ཀྱི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་\] and the thirty-two numbers of trulkhors are the same as the Shangpa Kagyu Amrtasiddhi thirty-two trulkhor text. It is obvious that the Shangpa Kagyu Amrtasiddhi trulkhor practice is an extension of Virupa's Amrtasiddhi teachings developed within the Shangpa tradition. However, readers will notice the differences between the thirty-two Shangpa Kagyu trulkhors and this text. I have left the text without translation where the writing is unclear and difficult to understand. The instructions for each of the Trulkhors in this text are very short, and they are not explained. The words in brackets are my own interpretation.

#### 1.

First, subdue/train the body.

#### 2.

Second, shake your right and left hands.

#### 3.

Third, stand up and turn/rotate your hands \[from the wrists\].

#### 4.

Fourth, shake your legs.

#### 5.

Fifth, draw your legs \[like an\] arrow.

#### 6.

Sixth, touch your mouth to your knees.

#### 7.

Seventh, draw a bow.

#### 8.

Eighth, raise your hands.

#### 9.

Ninth, turn your legs inside out.

#### 10.

Tenth is Saraha.

#### 11.

Eleventh, intertwine the knee joints \[the calves\].

#### 12.

Twelfth, cross the legs over the head.

#### 13.

Thirteenth is Ghorakthali.

#### 14.

Fourteenth, bring the ankle joint area and the knee together.

#### 15.

Fifteenth, place the foot on the abdomen.

#### 16 & 17.

Sixteenth and seventeenth is the sitting posture of Avalokiteshvara.

#### 18.

Eighteenth, draw \[pull\].

#### 19.

Nineteenth, place the feet on the palms of the hands.

#### 20.

Twenty, touch the knees with the mouth.

#### 21.

Twenty-first, hold the big toes from behind \[with the opposite hands\].

#### 22.

Twenty-second, in the vajra posture \[full lotus\], sit on the vajra fists \[hands\].

#### 23.

Twenty-third, inside the vajra crossed legs, \[pass the hands\] and hold the head.

#### 24.

Twenty-fourth, make crossed legs on the hands.

#### 25.

Twenty-fifth, place the knees on the elbows.

#### 26.

Twenty-sixth, stretch out both the arms and the legs together.

#### 27.

Twenty-seventh, place the legs next to each other and stretch them up to the sky.

#### 28.

Twenty-eighth, put the head under the heel.

#### 29.

Twenty-ninth is the \[trulkhor\] the way the tigress vomit.

#### 30.

Thirtieth, \[the writing on the scripture is not clear\].

#### 31.

Thirty-first, \[words indistinct\].

#### 32.

Thirty-two, yogic drop, shake \[your body\] and settle in the objectless state of mind.
