## Introduction

Today's post contains the full English translation of the Tibetan Amrtasiddhi text entitled 'Commentary on Amrtasiddhi', composed by the Indian yogi and pandita E deva aka Dhurkhropa \[Black Charnel Ground Dweller\]. It appears that the author of this text was most likely the third lineage master of the Amrtasiddhi teaching, Dhurkhroe Nagpopa \[Black Charnel Ground Dweller\]. One of the colophons to the text states that this particular teaching was requested by Virupa's disciple, the second lineage master of the Amrtasiddhi, who is Krishna Vipra \[Black Brahmin\].

This text exists in two different Tibetan sources. There are slight differences in the title, the colophon and some minor spelling differences between these two texts. In the Tibetan Tengyur collection this text has an unusual title, in English it could be translated as 'Commentary on Amrtasiddhi, Attainment of the Swastika'. According to the text's colophon, this text was written and translated into Tibetan by the Indian yogi E Deba himself. In the Shangpa teaching cycle this text is entitled 'Commentary on Amrtasiddhi'. According to the colophon to this text, this text was translated into Tibetan by a Tibetan translator, rMa Lotsawa, who heard it from the Indian yogi E Deva in the Tete Charnel ground. Both texts, however, are entitled Amrtasiddhivṛtti in Sanskrit. This text is quite similar to the opening parts of Madhavachandra's Sanskrit Amrtasiddhi text, especially the viveka parts. However, this Tibetan text is shorter and some of the vivekas are missing.

### The *Vāyu* (air) Section

All beings come from the air. So does the wisdom body. It is the essence of all \[existence\]. I pay homage to Lord vāyu (Air).
