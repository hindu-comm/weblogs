### Taranatha's commentary on the praises of Chakrasamvara

> སྙིང་ཁ་ཆོས་ཀྱི་འཁོར་ལོའི་རྩ་འདབ་བརྒྱད་པོ་ལུས་དཀྱིལ་གྱི་བདེ་ཆེན་འཁོར་ལོ་ཡིན་ལ།

The set of the eight nāḍī petals of the heart \[chest\] dharma chakra is the great bliss chakra[1](#footnote-1) of the body mandala.

> ཆོས་འཁོར་གྱི་ལྟེ་བའི་དཔྱིད་དང། ལྟེ་བའི་རཀྚའི་དྭངས་མ་གཉིས་ཡབ་ཡུམ་གྱི་བསྐྱེད་གཞི་ཡིན་ལ།

Vasanta[2](#footnote-2) \[the nectar\] in the centre of the heart chakra and Rakta \[the blood\] in the centre of the navel chakra are the basis of the generation of the Father \[the Chief Male Deity\] and the Mother \[the Female Deity\] respectively.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Lacw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe64961c2-509a-4a64-95b9-a6291f8f6884_640x650.png)

> ཡབ་ཡུམ་ཞལ་སྦྱོར་དུ་བསྒོམས་པས་འདུ་འཕྲོད་སྙིང་ཁར་འབྱུང་བ་མཚོན།

When you meditate on the union of father and mother, the union symbolises that the union \[of white virile bodhicitta and red feminine wisdom\] occurs in the heart chakra.

> ལྷ་མོ་བཞིའི་བསྐྱེད་གཞི་ནི་རྩ་བའི་རླུང་བཞིའི་ངོ་བོ་ཕྱོགས་བཞི་ནས་འབབ་པ་དེ་ཡིན་ལ།

The basis of the generation of the four goddesses \[on the four petals of the nāḍī\] is the nature of the four primary vayus travelling down through the four directions \[of the heart chakra\].

> ཐོད་པ་བཞིའི་ནི་མཚམས་ཀྱི་འདབ་མ་ནས་བདུད་རྩི་ལྔའི་དྭངས་མ་འབབ་པའི་རྩ་དང་ཁམས་སོ།

The skulls on the four nāḍī petals, between each of the nāḍī petals in the four directions \[with the goddeses\], represent the refined fluids of the five nectars flowing down through these nāḍī petals.

This is one of the eight charnel grounds that are depicted at the edge of the mandala.

> སྙིང་ཁ་ཆོས་འཁོར་གྱི་འཕྱིའི་རྩ་བརྒྱད་ཀྱི་སྣེ་བུ་ག་བརྒྱད་ལ་ཟུག་པ་ཡིན།

The ends of the eight outer nāḍīs of the heart chakra remain pierced to the eight cavities of the body.

> དུར་ཁྲོད་བརྒྱད་ལ་སྦྱར་བའི་བུ་ག་བརྒྱད་ནི།
> ཡོངས་སུ་གྲགས་པའི་བུ་ག་དགུ་ལ།སྣ་བུག་གཉིས་གཅིག་ཏུ་སྡོམས་པར་བཤད།

The eight charnel grounds drawn in the edge of the mandala represent the eight cavities of the body. The eight cavities of the body actually refer to the nine openings of the body after counting two nostrils as one. They are: two eyes, two ears, mouth, genitals, anus and nose.

### Sakya Lamdre text

> ནང་གི་དུར་ཁྲོད་ནི་ནང་གི་བུ་ག་དགུའི་སྣ་བུག་གཉིས་གཅིག་ཏུ་སྡོམས་པའི་བརྒྱད་པོ་ཡིན་ཞིང།

The inner eight charnel grounds are the eight cavities of the body. These are the nine openings of the body, counting two nostrils as one.

> བུ་ག་བརྒྱད་པོའི་རྩ་བརྒྱད་ནི་ཤིང་བརྒྱད།རྩ་དེ་དང་འབྲེལ་བའི་རྣམ་ཤེས་བརྒྱད་ཕྱོགས་སྐྱོང་བརྒྱད།རླུང་བརྒྱད་ཞིང་སྐྱོང་བརྒྱད།གསང་བ་ནི་འཇིག་རྟེན་ཆོས་བརྒྱད་ཀྱི་རྣམ་རྟོག་སྟེ་དེ་དག་སྦྱང་གཞི་ཡིན་ལ།

The eight trees in the eight charnel grounds represent the eight channels of the eight orifices of the body. The eight guardians of the eight directions \[dikpālaḥ\] in the charnel ground represent the eight consciousnesses associated with the eight channels. The eight guardians of the sacred places in the charnel grounds represent the eight vayus moving through the eight channels. In the secret practice, the eight charnel grounds represent the conceptual minds of the eight worldly dharmas \[aṣṭau lokadharmāḥ\]. These channels, the consciousness and vital air of the channels, and the conceptual mind are the basis of purification. The fruit of purification is the attainment of the eight liberations \[Aṣṭa-vimokṣa\], achieved by purifying the conceptual mind of the eight worldly dharmas and realising the meaning of the eight examples of illusions.

[1](#footnote-anchor-1)

The crown chakra is usually known as the great bliss chakra, but here in Chakrasamvara's body mandala, the heart chakra is called the great bliss chakra.

[2](#footnote-anchor-2)

The meaning of the Sanskrit term vasanta and the Tibetan term *dpyid* is spring season. In the context of Vajrayana, vasanta refers to nectar, also known as white bodhicitta or semen. According to Krishnacharya's text Vasanta Tilaka \[tilaka here means red fiery thigle at the navel\], the reason why the white bodhicitta bindu is called Vasanta is that just as spring brings joy to animals and humans, the white bodhicitta bindu also brings joy to the heart of the yogi.
