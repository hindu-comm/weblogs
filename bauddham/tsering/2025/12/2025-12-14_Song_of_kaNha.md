Kanha, the Black one. Image 16th century from Tibet, Rubin Museum of Arts (HAR)

Kanha, or Kṛṣṇacaryāvrata (བརྟུལ་ཞུགས་སྤྱོད་པ་ནག་པོ་པ), is known for his arrogance in yogic power; no teacher was able to tame him. In a moment of anger, he misused his yogic power against a young woman. The girl was no ordinary being, and through her black magic he lost his life. His body died, yet it is believed that Kanha himself is still alive somewhere.

This *doha* tells that the body, like a lotus born from water and mud, is born from past karma and afflictive emotions (*kleśa*). Though a body born of karma and *kleśa* may wither and vanish, this impure body is not Kanha. So why does the world say that Kanha is dead?

This is not an ordinary death. With the exhaustion of karma and *kleśa*, there is ordinarily the cessation of the aggregates and saṃsāra. In the case of Kanha, however, the causal *kleśa* and the resultant body, senses, and consciousness are extinguished into the state of innate pristine consciousness. The *doha* calls this body *indra-jāla*—a jugglery, an illusory display.

Kanha practising **tummo**:

- Eating: the fire of *tummo* consumed karmic impurities

- Drinking: melts bodhicitta bindu and drinks amrita.

- Abiding: Blazing (of tummo) and dripping (of amrita) abides in
  one-taste (eka-rasa) at the centre of the nadi-chakra.

- Breaking branches: breaking the channel-knots (nadi-grantha)

- Depart: in the form of mere pristine consciousness where karmic vayus
  and bindus has ceased.

**Note:** This *doha* is taken from the Tibetan *Tengyur* and is attributed to Kanha, or Kṛṣṇacaryāvrata. It was translated into Tibetan by the yogeśvara Vairocana Vajra.

``` text When the water dries up, the lotus in the swamp withers; its nectar fades away, gone without a trace.

Fire consumes the lotus—leaves and roots. Kanha laughs: “Look at the jugglery of the cosmic play!” Eating, drinking, abiding, And departs, snapping the branches.

Kye ho! This action of mine is like tummo: a perpetual frost, scorching the lotus. One wrong action washes away a hundred virtues.

As the ocean empties, so too the lotus withers. From the ten gates, smoke begins to rise.

The world proclaims that Kanha does not exist; yet Kanha lives in the deep forest of Mahāyāna.

The mind, being void, culminates in the innate state. There is no remorse, even if the aggregates (skandha) become a joke.

As one cannot see the butter in milk, there is Buddha though the world cannot see. It has no birth or death whatsoever.

Such as this is the practice of the Dark Yogi. Even if the parts of my body, like a lotus, wither away, why does the world say that Kanha is dead? ```
