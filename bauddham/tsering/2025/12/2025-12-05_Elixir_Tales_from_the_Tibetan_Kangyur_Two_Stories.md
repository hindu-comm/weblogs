### I.

There was a sage named Traksha (Daksha). He performed a great *yajña* for all the gods and the rishis. However, Mahādeva was invited at the end of all the others. The wrathful Mahādeva grew angry, and an eye appeared upon his forehead. Through the hand of the Wind-god (*Vāyu-deva*), he scattered diseases of wind, bile, and phlegm among the rishis and gods, and likewise released infectious diseases among human beings. The gods and the rishis became ignorant, senseless, and intoxicated. It grew and spread everywhere, and even the rishis came to honour it.

This great “sheep’s-eye” flower is the queen of *rāsāyana*. Boil it with cow’s milk. Drinking a single dose will cause the hairs of the skin to rise; with two doses, the benefit is that all infectious diseases are cleared. The benefit of drinking three doses is that the veins of the eyes become red and the complexion becomes youthful. Dry the *phye-ma* in the shade and mix it with the juice of *matola*. If you give this drink to your enemies, they will become intoxicated, and not even the gods will be able to protect them from death.

### II.

The wild Rishi Kumāra, bearing Vishnu’s horn-bow and holding five kinds of weapons in his hands, steals the amṛita, primordial elixirs produced at the creation of the universe. From his mouth two drops fell upon the earth. From these, the supreme sacred amṛita, the later cause of *rasāyana*, took form as a beautiful maiden. She exists in the sandy plain of the river Gaṅgā, and is known as *Nye’u-shing,* or *Nye’u.*

It should be sought in late autumn and in mid-spring, on the the full moon. The method for obtaining it is as follows. A child of six or seven years of age, having bathed and dressed, should face the eastern direction and make three prostrations to the goddess. Then the following prayer is to be recited:

“To you, O supreme amṛita, I bow. I bow to the Mother who arises from the amṛita and who increases strength (*bala*) and majesty (*tejas*). By the words of the self-arisen Buddha you are endowed with power. O elixir, O supreme *rasāyana*, you who are so close to the highest *siddhi*, grant me long life and strength, and make all wisdom shine forth clearly.”

Remove the bark and fibre. (……..) Pound the core/essence and squeeze out the juice in a clean, fresh cloth. Having washed one’s body and put on fine clothing, then engage in mantra recitation (*japa*) to transform it into a medicine. Recite the mantra one thousand times, or one hundred thousand times, or as many times as one is able.

When eating or drinking it, (some kind of) cow’s milk, or else take it in powdered form. This is the mantra to be recited while eating or drinking: *nama jara agajatu svāhā*. The benefit of eating it either each day or with each meal is that the faculties of body, speech, and mind become clear. Whoever drinks this amṛita experiences, for humans, what is like immortality for the gods; like a medicine for the nāgas; like water for the plants; and like the light of the moon for the flowers. One will live for five hundred years with the youthful complexion of a sixteen-year-old, will be free from illness, and will remain in the prime of life. One’s strength becomes like that of an elephant.
