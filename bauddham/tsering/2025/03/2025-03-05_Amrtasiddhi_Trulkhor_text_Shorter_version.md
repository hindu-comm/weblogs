### Translator’s Note

This is a translation of a shorter version of the text *Amrtasiddhi Trulkhor* from the Shangpa teaching cycle. Translating this text is challenging without referring to the longer version, *Amrtasiddhi Thirty-Two Trulkhor*, composed by Tsundue Singhe (Virya Simha-13th century). The words in brackets in the Tibetan text are my corrections, while those in the English translation have been added to clarify the reader's understanding by referring to the *Amrtasiddhi Thirty-Two Trulkhor* text.

The *Amrtasiddhi Trulkhor* text of the Shangpa Kagyu tradition is significant because it illustrates how Virupa's *Amrtasiddhi* yoga practice from India was incorporated into the Tibetan tradition. It also shows how Hathayogic practices such as Mahamudra, Mahabandha, and Mahavedha were adopted and practiced in Tibet. In *Amrtasiddhi Trulkhor* texts composed by Tibetan masters, the Hathayogic Mahamudra technique appears to be referred to as *Am-grol*. It is evident that this shorter version of the *Amrtasiddhi Trulkhor* text teaches the thirty-two *trulkhors* of *Amrtasiddhi*, which include one preliminary (*jhi-ta karma*), twelve main *trulkhors*, and nineteen secondary *trulkhors*.

# Text and Translation

> འཆི་མེད་གྲུབ་པའི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་བཤད།

Amrtasiddhi’s Trulkhor is explained.

## I. Preliminary

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> དང་པོ་ཡན་ལག་ལྔ་སྦྱོང་བྱ།
> དེ་ནས་སྟེང་འོག་ཀུན་ཏུ་འཕང༎
> ```

First, to remove the problem of \[immobile air in the body, massage the whole body with oil, especially\] the five limbs: head, arms and legs\] of the body. Then fling \[your arms and legs\] in all \[four directions by stretching and bending your arms\] up and \[stretching your legs\] downwards. \[This is known as Jhi-ta-karma\]

## II. Twelve main Trullkhors

### (I) Four outer Trulkhors (Hathayogic mudras)

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> ཕྱིའི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་རྣམ་བཞི་ནི།
> ཨཾ་གྲོལ་རྐང་ཐེབ་བཟུང་ལ་སྡིག་༼བསྡིག༽།
> བྷེ་དྷ་འཕོངས་རྡིག་༼རྟིང༽་དྲག་ཏུ་སྡིག་༼བསྡིག༽
> །བྷན་དྷ་བཤང་ལམ་གྱེན་ལ་ཟློག།
> ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཆེན་པོ་ཙོག་པུ་བྱ༎།
> ```

**1. Am-grol (Hathayogic Mahamudra)**: Hold the big toe \[of the extended leg\] and thrust \[the union of upper and lower air downward below the navel\]

**2. Vedha (Hathayogic Mahavedha):** \[From a kneeling posture, lift\] the buttocks and strike them firmly against the heels.

**3. Bandha (Hathayogic Mahabandha):** \[From a standing position, bend forward and\] position the anal \[chakra\] facing upward.

**4. Mahamudra (Non-Hathayogic mahamudra):** Sit in a squatting posture \[with legs crossed and knees upright\].

### (II) Four Inner Trulkhors

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> ནང་གི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་རྣམ་བཞི་ནི།
> ཨཾ་གྲོལ་རྐང་ངར་བཟུང་ལ་བསྡིག།
> བྷེ་ཏ་འཕོངས་སྡིག་༼རྟིང༽་རེ་མོས་བསྡིག།
> བྷན་དྷ་གླང་ཆེན་སྣ་ལྟར་བཀུག།
> ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཆེ་ལ་མཉམ་བཞག་བྱ༎
> ```

**1. Am-grol (Hathayogic Mahamudra):** \[From a cross-legged posture,\] grasp your shins with opposite hands and thrust \[the air downward as before\].

**2. Vedha (Hathayogic Mahavedha):** \[With legs slightly extended, heels touching, and knees raised, hold the big toes with each hand. Lift\] the buttocks and heels alternately and strike them against the ground.

**3. Bandha (Hathayogic Mahabandha):** \[From a standing position,\] bend your upper body downward like an elephant's trunk, \[crossing your legs and holding the big toes with opposite hands\].

**4. Mahamudra (Non-Hathayogic mahamudra):** Sit in a meditative posture. \[Straighten the spine, cross the legs, and place your hands in the dhyana mudra below the navel. Extend both shoulders like the wings of a swan, hold the breath for a while, and remain in a state of non-mentation\].

### (III) Four secret Trulkhors

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> ཨམ་གྲོལ་རྒྱབ་ཏུ་ལག་གཉིས་རྩེག།
> བྷེ་ཏ་འཕོངས་སྡིག་༼རྟིང༽་དལ་གྱིས་བཞག།
> བྷན་དྷ་རོ་སྟོད་གཡས་གཡོན་གཅུ།
> ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཆེ་ལ་མཆོག་སྦྱིན་བྱ༎
> ```

**1. Am-grol (Hathayogic Mahamudra):** \[In a cross-legged posture,\] place the right hand on top of the left hand behind your back \[and follow the same breathing technique\].

**2. Vedha (Hathayogic Mahavedha):** \[In a kneeling posture, lift\] the buttocks and gently lower them onto the heels.

**3. Bandha (Hathayogic Mahabandha):** \[In a cross-legged posture,\] twist the upper body forcefully to the right and left \[while keeping the chin firmly pressed against the chest bone\].

**4. Mahamudra (Non-Hathayogic mahamudra):** \[In a Vajra cross-legged posture with hands on the knees,\] form the supreme offering hand gesture.

## III. Nineteen Secondary Trulkhor Practices

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> ད་ནི་ཡན་ལག་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་བཤད།
> ཁ་སྦྱོར་ཨཾ་གྲོལ་ལྟ་བུ་ལ།
> ཐིག་ལེ་སྒྲ་གཅན་གྱེན་ལ་ཟློག།
> གདུང་(བརྡུང་)དང་དཀྲུག་པས་དཀར་པོ་དགྲམ༎
> ```

1.  In Samputa Yoga, \[the breathing technique is\] similar to Am-grol,
    helps the growth of white bindu.

2.  Reverse the bindu and rahu upwards.

3.  Beating \[the base of the spine with your hands\] and shaking hands
    \[in the front\] helps to spread the white bindu.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Four Head Trulkhors

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> མགོ་བོའི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་རྣམ་བཞི་ནི།
> དང་པོ་མགོ་བོ་བྱ་སྙེས་༼བྱད་མཉེད༽་ཞིང།
> གཉིས་པ་གདངས་༼སྡང༽་མིག་མིག་གཉིས་བསྐོར།
> གསུམ་པ་མགོ་བསྐོར་མིག་གཉིས་བསྡམས།
> བཞི་པ་ཕྱོགས་བཞི་དག་ནས་རྔུབ༎
> ```

4.  First, rub and massage the head, face, \[and back of the neck. This
    helps clear headaches, reduce gray hair and wrinkles, and release
    channel knots in the head\].

5.  Second, looking with an angry gaze, rotate the eyes \[and rub them
    with the palms of your hands. This alleviates eye ailments, releases
    channel knots of the eyes, clears watery eyes and blurriness.

6.  Rotate the head and press your eyes firmly with your hands. \[This
    practice clears grogginess and mental fog, alleviates throat
    ailments, and releases the throat's channel knots.\]

7.  Inhale \[and move your head like a lizard—forward, to the right, to
    the left, and backward—\] in all four directions. This practice
    alleviates neck ailments and releases channel knots.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Three upper-body Trulkhors

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> རོ་སྟོད་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་རྣམ་གསུམ་ནི།
> དང་པོ་རོ་སྟོད་གཡས་སུ་གཅུ།
> གཉིས་པ་ཁུ་ཚུར་ཐེངས་དགུ་རྡེག།
> གསུམ་པ་བརྐྱང་བསྐུམ་དག་ཏུ་བྱ༎
> ```

8.  \[In a Sattva cross-legged posture,\] twist the upper body to the
    right \[and left three times\].

9.  \[Interlace the fingers, keeping the middle fingers extended, and
    stretch out your arms.\] Strike the hands \[to the right, left, and
    center of the chest\] nine times, \[three on each side\].

10. \[With the same hand posture,\] bend and stretch your arms \[to
    strike the hands on the right knee, left knee, and between the knees
    nine times, three on each side\].

    These three upper-body Trulkhors help release channel knots,
    alleviate upper-body ailments, and specifically clear wind-related
    heart/chest conditions such as drowsiness.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Four Arm-Trulkhor

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> ལག་པའི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་རྣམ་བཞི་ནི།
> དང་པོ་རོ་སྟོད་༼དཔུང་པ༽་ཕྱི་ནང་བསྐོར།
> གཉིས་པ་ཕྱག་རྡེག་རྐྱང་ཞིང་དཀྲུག།
> གསུམ་པ་དཔུང་སྟེག་རེ་མོས་སྟེག།
> བཞི་པ་སོར་རྩེར་བལྟ་བའོ༎
> ```

11. \[In a vajra cross-legged posture\] rotate the shoulders clockwise
    and counterclockwise. \[This alleviate ailments in the shoulder
    blade area and release channel knots\]

12. Curl the fingers of both hands \[into fists\], stretch them out, and
    then shake off the hands.

13. \[Bend the arm with the hand\] touching the shoulder, then stretch
    it out one after the other.

14. \[With the right arm stretched out and fingers spread,\] touch your
    head to your shoulder and fix your gaze on the fingertips \[and do
    the same with the left hand\].

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Three abdomen Trulkhors

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> འཕོ་བའི་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་རྣམ་གསུམ་ནི།
> དང་པོ་ལངས་ལ་འཕོ་བ་མཉེ།
> གཉིས་པ་ས་གནོན་དྲག་ཏུ་བསྐོར།
> གསུམ་པ་ལག་མགོ་སྟེག་པར་བྱ༎
> ```

15. In a standing position, \[firmly\] massage the abdomen \[with the
    palms of your hands\].

16. \[In a cross-legged posture with\] the hands in an earth-pressing
    mudra, forcefully rotate \[the belly\].

17. \[Interlace the fingers,\] raise your hands above the head, \[and
    strike the palms of the hands on the abdomen\].

These three stomach Trulkhors release abdomen’s channel knots, alleviate stomach ailments—especially stomach pain—and aid digestion.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Two Leg-Trulkhors

> དང་པོ་ལངས་ལ་རེ་མོས་སྐྱོང་༼སྤྲུག༽།

> ```

18. In a standing position, shake the legs one at a time.

19. In a sitting position, stretch out each leg one after the other.

> ``` text
> དེ་དག་འགྲོ་བའི་དོན་དུ་སེམས་བསྐྱེད་པར།

> རླུང་མ་བསྒོམས་པར་འཁྲུལ་འཁོར་མི་བྱ།
> ཅེས་ཆོས་རྗེ་སྟོན་པས་གསུངས་པ་ཡིན་ནོ༎
> ```

\[Before beginning the practice,\] by arousing bodhicitta for the welfare of all sentient beings, supplicate to your guru. Do not perform the breathing practice without first making a supplication, and do not practice Trulkhor without first doing proper breathing practice. This is taught by Choe-je Toenpa \[Tsundue Singhe\].
