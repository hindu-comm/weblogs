## Translator’s Note

This Trulkhor practice is popularly known as 'Na-ro Druk-skor' in Tibetan. There are several different versions of Naropa's six Trulkhors instructions across various Tibetan traditions. The root instructions shown in the following image are from the Shangpa Kagyu tradition, while the commentary in the translation comes from the Drukpa Kagyu tradition. The commentary was written by Mipham Tenpay Nyima.

# The six main Trulkhors of Naropa

### I.

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> སྐེད་པ་གཡས་བསྐོར་གཡོན་བསྐོར་གྱི།
> ལྟེ་བའི་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ་བ་ཡིན༎
> ```

In a sattva cross-legged posture, place your vajra-fists on your knees. After blowing out the harmful residual air, inhale while planting your fists on your mid-thighs. Keeping your elbows relaxed, rotate your waist three times clockwise and three times counterclockwise. This practice alleviates abdominal ailments and releases the navel's channel knots.

### II.

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> མགྲིན་པ་༼མགོ་བོ༽་བསྐོར་ཞིང་དགྱེ་སྒུ་བྱ།
> སྤྱི་བོ་མགྲིན་པའི་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ༎
> ```

In a sattva cross-legged posture, plant your fists near your groin. Tighten your upper arms by raising your shoulders high. Then, rotate your head three times clockwise and three times counterclockwise. Next, bend your head backward three times and forward three times. This practice helps release the channel knots of the crown and throat.

### III.

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> རྐང་ལག་བརྐྱང་བསྐུམ་བྱས་པ་ཡིས།
> ཡན་ལག་རྩ་མདུད་རང་སར་གྲོལ༎
> ```

#### Arms

In a sattva cross-legged posture, inhale and slide your hands from your knees along your thighs. Press firmly on the side of your waist with your left hand. With your right hand make a fist, fling it forcefully to your right side with the fist facing outward. Then, bend your arm forcefully, striking the back of your fist against your shoulder ball. Repeat this movement of bending and extending your right arm three times. Similarly, perform the same motion with your left arm three times. Next, repeat the movement three more times with both arms together. During the final repetition, instead of bending your arms, unclench your fists, bring your arms slowly to the front, and release your breath.

#### Legs

(Continued)...At the end of the exhalation, simultaneously plant both hands beside your hips. Putting your weight on your hands, lift your buttocks off the ground and draw in the breath. Insert your left foot underneath, with the heel facing the buttocks. Then, lift your right leg slightly and extend it forward. Bend your right leg, striking the heel against the buttock. Repeat this movement of bending and extending your right leg three times. Next changing your legs position, perform the same with your left leg three times. Finally, move your hands — on which you have been supporting your body — toward shoulder level, striking your legs and buttocks on the ground forming a sattva cross-legged posture.

#### Benefits

This will release the channel knots of the limbs.

### IV.

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> རོ་སྟོད་གཡས་གཡོན་གཅུས་པ་ཡིས།
> སྟོད་ཀྱི་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ་བར་བྱེད།
> ```

Sit in a sattva cross-legged posture and place your fists on your knees. Maintaining your posture, start by twisting your left shoulder forward while simultaneously twisting your right shoulder backward. Then, twist your right shoulder forward as you move your left shoulder backward. Finally, gently draw both shoulders back together. Repeat the sequence, this time starting with your right shoulder.

#### Benefit

This will release the channel knots of the upper body.

### V.

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> གྱེན་དུ་འཕར་ཞིང་འོག་ཏུ་རྡེབ།
> ཚིགས་རྣམས་ཀུན་གྱི་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ༎
> ```

#### Small yogic drop

Stretch your arms forward side by side with your palms facing outward. Simultaneously \[from sattva cross-legged posture\] raise your knees upward and plant your heels on the ground. Lift your buttocks slightly off the seat, then perform a small yogic drop into the sattva cross-legged posture with your hands placed in the dhyana mudra. Do this three times.

#### Mid-height yogic drop

With the same arm movement, spring up a cubit's height from your seat and perform a yogic drop as before. Repeat this medium yogic drop three times.

#### Big yogic drop

Perform the big yogic drop three times, assuming the sattva cross-legged posture with your hands crossed over your chest.

#### Benefits

This will help release the channel knots in all the joints (the spinal joints!?).

### VI.

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> ལུས་སྤྲུག་ཕུར་སུབ་བྱས་པ་ཡིས།
> རྩ་རྣམས་ཐམས་ཅད་གྲོལ་བའོ༎
> ```

#### Shake your body

At the end of the yogic drop, place your hands on your knees. As you exhale through both your mouth and nose, simultaneously shake your entire body and head, starting by moving your right side forward first. Repeat this motion three times each, beginning with your left side and the midsection first.

#### Rub and massage your body

In the Sattva cross-legged posture, slide your hands firmly upward along your body from your knees. When your hands reach your chest, cross your arms by placing your right arm over your left, with your right hand resting on your left shoulder and your left hand under your right armpit. Using your hands, rub your body along your left shoulder and right armpit back and forth. Then switch positions, crossing your left arm over your right, and repeat the motion by rubbing your right shoulder and left armpit back and forth.

With your arms still crossed and your hands on your shoulders and armpits, slide your hands down along your body. When your hands reach your thighs, uncross your arms. Next, rub your knees with the palms of your hands in a circular motion. Then, clench your hands into fists and, as if pressing your knees down, strike them firmly toward the ground.

#### Benefit

This will help to release all the body's channels.
