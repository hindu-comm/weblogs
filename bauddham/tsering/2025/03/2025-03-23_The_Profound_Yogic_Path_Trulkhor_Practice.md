### Translator’s Note

This Trulkhor practice is associated with the Drukpa Kagyu tradition and is supported by both a root text and a commentary. The Tibetan text presented in this post is the root text, while the accompanying English translation is based on the commentary. Trulkhor instructions are quite complex and challenging to understand, and as such, there may be some inaccuracies in the translation. These posts are intended for educational and informational purposes only; please do not use this translation as a practice manual. Words enclosed in brackets within the translation are sourced from the commentary.

## Text and Translation

### I. Head

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> རྒྱལ་མཚན་འཁྲུད་དང་གཡས་གཡོན་འཕངས།
> ཆུ་མིག་བསྐོར་དང་རྒྱབ་མདུན་བསྒྲོད།
> མགོ་བོའི་ནད་སེལ་བདེ་ཆེན་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ༎
> ```

1.  \[From the sattva cross-legged posture, bring your hands from your
    knees, placing your right hand at the back of your head and your
    left hand on your forehead.\] Rub your head three times.

2.  \[Maintaining the same posture, with your hands planted near the
    groin,\] bend your head to the right and left \[three times on each
    side\].

3.  \[Maintaining the same posture as before,\] rotate your eyes
    clockwise and counterclockwise \[three times each\]. Then, bend your
    head forward and backward \[three times each\].

**Benefits**: This practice helps clear head ailments and releases the channel knots of the crown Mahāsukha chakra.

### II. Neck

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> རྐེ་མཇིང་བཤུད་དང་གཡས་གཡོན་འཐེན།
> ཨོལ་མདུད་ལྟག་ཤ་མཉེད་དང་ཕྱི་ནང་བསྐོར།
> མགྲིན་པའི་ནད་སེལ་ལོངས་སྤྱོད་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ༎
> ```

1.  \[Sit in a sattva cross-legged posture.\] Rub the front and back of
    your neck with your hands.

2.  \[Remaining in the same posture, keep your arms straight with your
    hands planted near your groin\]. Then, draw \[your chin from your
    chest\] to your right \[shoulder\]. Repeat the same movement to your
    left shoulder, three times on each side.

3.  \[In the same posture as above,\] massage the throat and the muscles
    at the back of your neck \[by bending your head forward and backward
    three times\].

4.  \[Remaining in the same posture,\] rotate \[your head\] clockwise
    and counterclockwise \[three times each\].

**Benefits:** This practice helps clear throat ailments and releases the channel knots of the throat Sambhoga chakra.

### III. Chest

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> རོ་སྟོད་གཅུ་དང་མེ་ལོང་ཕྱི་ནང་བསྐོར།
> རྒྱབ་དགྱེ་མདུན་དུ་བསྡུ་བ་དང།
> ལི་ཁྲི་གཅུད་པས་གཡས་གཡོན་འཐེན།
> སྟོད་ཀྱི་ནད་སེལ་ཆོས་འཁོར་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ༎
> ```

1.  \[Sit in a sattva cross-legged posture and place your hands on your
    knees. Straighten your back and, keeping your forearms upright,\]
    twist your upper body \[to the right and left three times each\].

2.  \[Maintaining the same posture,\] rotate your shoulders clockwise
    and counterclockwise \[three times each\].

3.  \[In a sattva cross-legged posture,\] place your right hand on your
    left shoulder ball and your left hand over your right. With your
    elbows stacked on top of each other, raise them upwards, then push
    the right \[shoulder\], the left \[shoulder\], and both together.

4.  \[In a sattva cross-legged posture,\] cross your arms, holding your
    arms with the opposite hands. Raise them above your head and pull
    \[the right side\] upward, then pull \[the left side\] upward,
    followed by pulling both sides upward.

**Benefits**: This practice helps clear upper body ailments and releases the channel knots of the heart Dharma chakra.

### IV. Abdomen/waist

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> སྐེད་པ་བསྐོར་དང་མཁལ་རྩ་བཤུད།
> རྒྱ་མཚོ་ལོང་རྡེབ་ཕོ་བ་གྲིལ།
> སྐེད་པའི་ནད་སེལ་སྤྲུལ་པའི་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ༎
> ```

1.  \[Sit in a sattva cross-legged posture, placing your hands on your
    thighs.\] Rotate your waist clockwise and counterclockwise \[three
    times each\].

2.  \[Maintaining the same posture, position your hands below your right
    armpit in a fighting-claw position and\] rub the channels connected
    to the kidney. \[Repeat the same movement on the left side.\]

3.  \[Sit in a sattva cross-legged posture with your hands on your
    knees.\] Then surge your belly from the right side toward the left
    \[three times. Repeat the movement from the left side to the right
    three times\].

4.  \[Maintaining the above posture, draw the breath towards the belly
    and\] rotate the belly \[clockwise and counterclockwise three times
    each\].

**Benefits:** This practice helps clear ailments of the waist/abdomen, and releases the channel knots of the Nirmāṇa chakra.

### V. Pelvic area

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> དལ་མོ་བསྐོར་དང་དཔྱི་མིག་འཐེན།
> སྲིན་ཞལ་འབྱེད་འཛུམ་རྒྱབ་མདུན་བཀན།
> སྨད་ཀྱི་ནད་སེལ་བདེ་སྐྱོང་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ༎
> ```

1.  \[Sit in a sattva cross-legged posture with your hands on your
    knees, then\] slowly rotate \[your waist clockwise and
    counterclockwise three times each\].

2.  \[Maintaining the same posture,\] pull \[your right\] hip \[to the
    left and your left hip to the right three times each\].

3.  Contract and release your anus. \[Maintaining the same posture as
    above,\] draw the lower air upward. With your face and gaze directed
    upward, draw up and push down the lower air three times.\]

4.  \[Maintaining the same posture, hold the air in your belly and\]
    push it toward the back and then toward the front \[three times
    each\].

**Benefits:** This practice helps alleviate ailments in the lower abdomen/pelvic area, while also releasing the channel knots of the Bliss-sustaining chakra.

### VI. Four Limbs (Arms and Legs)

> Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when
> published
>
> ``` text
> སྟེང་འོག་འཕངས་དང་འདོམ་གཞལ་རྡེབ་པར་བྱ།
> མདའ་གཞུ་འགེངས་དང་སྟེང་འོག་བརྐྱང་བར་བྱ།
> རྐང་གཉིས་སྤྲུག་དང་ཆུ་སྲིང་བརྐྱང་བསྐུམ་དང།
> ལྗོན་ཤིང་ཕྱི་ནང་གཡས་གཡོན་རེ་མོས་བསྐོར།
> ཡན་ལག་ནད་སེལ་ལྟེ་བའི་རྩ་མདུད་གྲོལ༎
> ```

#### Arms:

1.  Fling your \[arms\] up and down: \[Stand with your heels together on
    the ground, lift your hands from ground level, and strike your hands
    against your thighs. Then raise your fists upward, stretching your
    body. As you bring your arms down, strike your fists on your
    shoulder balls, and finally place your hands beside your hips.\]

2.  \[Stand up, lifting your hands from ground level, and strike them
    against your thighs. With your fingers extended, place your hands
    over your chest with your fingertips touching each other, and spread
    your elbows outward. Finally, stretch out your arms to the sides\]
    as if measuring your arm span.

3.  \[Maintaining the same posture as above, extend your arms and\]
    simulate the motion of drawing a bowstring with an arrow. \[Repeat
    this movement three times\]

4.  \[Stand up, lifting your hands as fists from ground level, and
    strike them against your thighs. Next,\] extend your right arm
    upward and your left arm downward. \[As you lower your right arm,
    strike your fist on your shoulder ball before extending it beside
    your hip. Repeat this movement three times, alternating arms\].

#### Legs:

1.  \[In a standing position, place your hands on your waist and\] shake
    your legs \[in all four directions\].

2.  \[Placing the weight of your body on your buttocks, plant your hands
    inside the knee-pits with your arms upright. Then, alternately\]
    bend and stretch your right and left legs.

3.  \[From the sattva cross-legged posture, bring your right hand in a
    fist to your shoulder. As you extend your right leg, simultaneously
    hold your right foot with your left hand. Lower your right hand to
    your right knee and firmly rub the calf up and down. Next, hold your
    foot with your right hand, place your left hand on your knee, and
    rotate your right leg clockwise three times. Repeat the movement
    with your left leg, rotating it counterclockwise three times.

**Benefits:** This helps alleviate ailments in the four limbs and releases the channel knots of the navel chakra.
