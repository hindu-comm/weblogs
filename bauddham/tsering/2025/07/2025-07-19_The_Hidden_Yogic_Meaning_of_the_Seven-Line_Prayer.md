## Translator’s Note

This short translation is based on Ju Mipham’s (*'Ju Mi pham*, 1846–1912) commentary on the Seven-Line Prayer to Guru Rinpoche, titled *White Lotus*. In this work, Ju Mipham interprets the Seven-Line Prayer through the lens of tantric *Dzogrim* (completion stage) practice.

# Translation

> **ཧཱུྃ༔** — ***Hūṃ***

*Hūṃ* symbolizes the innate, self-existing primordial wisdom.

> ཨོ་རྒྱན་ཡུལ་ — **The land of Urgyen (*****Oḍḍiyāna)***

The land of Oḍḍiyāna refers generally to the body and specifically to the heart — the unique support for realization in Vajrayāna.

> ནུབ་བྱང་མཚམས — **North-West border**

In the inner symbolism, *north* and *west* correspond to the right and left subtle channels, respectively. The right red channel, *Ro ma*, associated with the sun, carries vital air of sun that diminishes the vital essences. The left white channel, *Kyang ma*, associated with the moon, conducts vital air of moon that supports the growth of vital essence. The *border* refers to the centre between these two channels.

> པདྨ་གེ་སར་སྡོང་པོ་ལ — **On the lotus stem bearing the lotus flower and
> stamens**

The *Padma* (lotus) here refers to the Heart Chakra, which is endowed with eight *nāḍī*-petals. *Gesar* (stamens) refers to the *bindu* — the aggregation of the five vital essences. The lotus stem symbolizes the central channel (*uma*), through which flows the vital air of eclipse (rahu), also known as the vital air of primordial wisdom.

> ཡ་མཚན་ — **Marvelous**

Just as camphor and its fragrance are naturally and inseparably present, so too, within the vajra body composed of channels, air, and *bindus*, the undefiled great bliss of the *bodhicitta bindu* is spontaneously present. This is what makes it truly *marvelous*.

> མཆོག་གི་དངོས་གྲུབ་བརྙེས་ — **Attains the Supreme Siddhi**

> པདྨ་འབྱུང་གནས་ཞེས་སུ་གྲགས — **known as Padmasambhava**

The supreme siddhi — (the union of bliss and emptiness in the form of ) the great Vajradhara — which is spontaneously present and primordially accomplished (within all beings), is known as the self-born true Padmasambhava.

> **འཁོར་དུ་མཁའ་འགྲོ་མང་པོས་བསྐོར༔** — **Encircled by many accompanying**
> ***Ḍākinīs***  

This self-born *bindu* of primordial wisdom — the Padmasambhava — through its manifesting power, is accompanied (*'khor du*) by a retinue consisting of the vital airs and vital essences (*rlung* and *bindu*), which move (*'gro*) through the empty pathways and spaces (*mkha’*) of the central channel and its secondary channels. These winds and essences that move through the encircling channels and *nāḍī*-*cakras* are called the inner body’s *retinue of Ḍākinīs*, because when they are intergrated with the method aspect — the *bodhicitta bindu* — they assist in the manifestation of the primordial wisdom of great bliss.

> ཁྱེད་ཀྱི་རྗེས་སུ་བདག་བསྒྲུབ་ཀྱི — **May I accomplish that very primordial wisdom
> which you embody**

After gaining an understanding of the subtle body system of the vajra body, one applies the yogic methods of *Dzogrim* (completion stage) that strike the vital points — the channels (*nāḍī*), vital airs (*prāṇa*), and vital essences (*bindu*). These include practices such as *Tummo*, in which bliss is generated by melting the *bodhicitta bindu* through the integration of physical *trulkhor* practices, breath control, and mind focussed on the subtle *bindu*; the method of directing the vital winds into the central channel; and the ensuing advanced practices such as the illusory body, clear light, dream yoga, and others. By the power of these profound *Dzogrim* practices of tantra — as mentioned above — this aspiration is made: *“May I accomplish that very primordial wisdom which you embody.”*

> བྱིན་གྱིས་བརླབ་ཕྱིར་ — **To bestow your blessing**

Through the skillful methods of tantra that rely on the subtle vajra body, the refined vital essence of primordial wisdom is strengthened, while the impure vital essences are purified within the expanse of bliss. As a result, all of existence becomes purified and abides in undefiled great bliss. Thus, it is said, *“Bestow your blessing”* — so that the being’s body, speech, and mind may be transformed into the mandala of vajra body, vajra speech, and vajra mind.

> གཤེགས་སུ་གསོལ — **please come**

The entire perception of the innumerable appearances of *saṃsāra* — all of which are encompassed within the truth of suffering and the origin of suffering — arises from the imprints left by the movement of karmic airs. When these karmic airs are brought to a stop within the central channel — where the vital air of primordial wisdom flows — and when both the air and the mind are restrained within the unchanging *bindu*, perfect enlightenment is attained. From that point onward, for as long as space remains, there is no return to *saṃsāra*. In order to attain the vajra-like body of great bliss, (the prayer) calls upon the expanse of realization of all the Buddhas — the ground expanse, the great *dharmakāya*.
