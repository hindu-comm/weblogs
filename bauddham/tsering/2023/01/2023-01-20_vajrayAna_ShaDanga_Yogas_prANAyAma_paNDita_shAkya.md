*Prāṇāyāma* (སྲོག་རྩོལ་) is one of the six limbs of *Ṣaḍaṅga* *yoga* (རྣལ་འབྱོར་ཡན་ལག་དྲུག་). (*Vajrayāna* follows the *Ṣaḍaṅga* *yoga* system, not the aṣṭāṅgayoga *yoga*). The *Ṣaḍaṅga* *yoga* or six limbs of yoga are *Pratyāhāra*, *dhyāna*, *Prāṇāyāma*, *dhāraṇā*, *anusmṛti* and *samādhi*. In *Vajrayāna* Buddhism, *Ṣaḍaṅga yoga* practice mainly comes from the *Kālacakra* tantra teachings. *Kālacakra*’s *Ṣaḍaṅga* *yoga* is a common practice in various Tibetan Buddhist schools. However, Jonangpa, one of the eight practice lineages of Tibet, is an ardent practitioner of *Ṣaḍaṅga* *yoga*. So, this lineage holder is also known as *Ṣaḍaṅga-yogi* (སྦྱོར་དྲུག་པ་). The translation in this article is from Jonang Taranatha’s text on *Ṣaḍaṅga yoga*.

There are two main existing traditions of *Kālacakra*, the Ra (Ra Choerab Lotsawa) རྭ་ལུགས་ and Dro ( Droton Lotsawa) འབྲོ་ལུགས་translator lineages. There are also two slightly different practices of *Prāṇāyāma* that came down from these two traditions. Dro translator’s *Prāṇāyāma* practice is based on the teachings of *Paṇḍita* *Śākya Śri* and *Paṇḍita* *Sōmanātha*. Venerable *Samanta* *Śri’*s teaching is known as Ra’s tradition. In this article, I will present the translation of *Paṇḍita* *Śākya Śri*’s method of *Prāṇāyāma* practice as written by Taranatha. Before that, I will first briefly explain what *Prāṇāyāma* is.

According to Taranatha, the meaning of the Sanskrit term *Prāṇāyāma* is, *prāṇa* (སྲོག་) refers to (*prāṇa*) *vāyu* (air), and *āyāma* means effort, stopping and lengthening. So, *Prāṇāyāma* is stopping the *prāṇa* (*vāyu*) at the central channel and lengthening it. *Prāṇa* and *āyāma* sometimes also refer to inhalation and exhalation respectively. In *Ṣaḍaṅga* *yoga*, *Prāṇāyāma* is the method to stop (transform) the impure (*aśuddhi*) *prāṇa* (air) that moves through the sun (right) and moon (left) channel into the *prāṇa* of pristine wisdom.

The *prāṇa* (air) of pristine wisdom and *Śūnyābimba*-*Mahāmudrā* (སྟོང་གཟུགས་ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཆེན་མོ་) are of the same nature. Yogi before the practice fails to recognise the pure essence of air (*prāṇa*) as the image/form of emptiness (*Śūnyā-bimba*). Now the Yogi focuses on such an entity of image of emptiness and uses the techniques such as *kumbhaka* to stop the movement of the sun and moon’s air (right and left channel) again and again. Prior to the practice, the air of the sun and moon act as a hindrance to seeing the true nature (*dharmata*) of air, and now as these two air are slightly stopped and merges into space again and again, the yogi experiences the image of emptiness in the form of ultimate nature of air. The image of emptiness and the air also appears as mingled into one composite in the yogi’s mind. As the mind and the image of emptiness are merged into one, wherever the mind directs its intention, will manifest the image of emptiness. When the air (*prāṇa*) transforms into that form (image of emptiness), it is called *prāṇa*-*bimba* (སྲོག་གཟུགས་) is attained. Once this is attained, the yogi is able to achieve all the collection of *kriya*/*karma*.

***Paṇḍita*** ***Śākya Śri*****’s method consists of:**

1.  **Cleansing the** ***nāḍī*** **passages**: breathing technique using
    the sound *Ōṃ (*ༀ), *Huṃ (*ཧཱུྃ) and *Aḥ (*ཨཱཿ) simultaneously with
    inhalation, retention and exhalation respectively.

2.  **Guiding** ***prāṇa*** ***vāyu*** **inside the central channel
    after cleansing the** ***nāḍī***: By applying a gentle breathing
    technique of filling in the air (breath) and releasing the air
    (breath).

3.  **When the** ***prāṇa*** **enters the central channel, bind or
    confine the** ***prāṇa*** **inside the central channel**: With
    *Kumbhaka* rough breathing technique.

4.  **If the** ***prāṇa*** **couldn’t enter or bind inside the central
    channel:** Apply *Haṭhayoga* (yoga of coercive method) བཙན་ཐབས་སྦྱོར་བ་
    by forcefully blocking the passage of the sun and moon (right and
    left channels). Once they are blocked, the lower openings are
    forcibly stopped and lead to liberation through the upward way.

    ------------------------------------------------------------------------

I. ***Vajra Breathing*** **to cleanse the channels**:

- **Eyes position is** ***Amṛta*** **whorl gaze
  (*****Amṛta*****-*****Kuṇḍali*****) or wrathful gaze**: Look slightly
  above the level of the eyes, gazing sharply at the spot between the
  eyebrows.

- **Body posture**: Vajra cross-legged, spine straight, tongue touching
  the palate, neck slightly bent, tighten the arm and calf muscles, toes
  are drawn in, hands in vajra fist sitting on the root of the thighs
  with arms straight.

- Expel the waste or stale air three times.

- First focus the mind on the air coming in, remaining and going out
  with a steady mind.

- **Visualise the three channels inside the body**: The central channel,
  blue in colour, thickness about the stalk of a middling reed, the
  upper tip fixed to the crown of the head and the lower tip lies at the
  navel. On the right side of the central channel is the red colour
  *Roma* and on the left side is the white *Kyangma* channel. Both the
  right and left channels are much finer than the central channel and
  these three channels are lying closely side by side.

- **At the time of breathing out**: Imagine the air moving from the
  navel area through the right and the central channel, air coming out
  from every part of these two channels in the region between the throat
  and crown of the head, and finally leaving through the nostrils.

- **At the time of breathing in**: The air enters the nostrils, imagine
  the air moving through every part of the left and the central channel
  of the region between the throat and the crown of the head and heading
  towards the navel area through the left and the central channel.

- **At the time of retention**: Imagine the air filling the central
  channel alone.

- In the beginning, the duration of the inhalation, retention and
  exhalation are kept uniformly. The retention will then gradually
  increase with practice.

- Practice inhalation, retention and exhalation with the visualisation
  of air as red light rays if the air is moving only through the right
  nostril, as white light rays if there is movement only through the
  left nostril, and as blue or green light rays if the air is evenly
  moving through both nostrils.

- Then during exhalation, air as a light ray goes out with the sound ཨཱཿ
  (*Ah*) and visualises a syllable ཨཱཿ (*Ah*) at the end of the breath.
  When breathing in, air (light) coming in with the sound ༀ (*Ōṃ*),
  visualise ༀ (*Ōṃ*) at the end of the breath. Visualise the syllable
  ཨཱཿ and ༀ going out and coming in not through the left and the right
  channels, but only through the central channel. During breathe
  retention, the air stays inside the central channel with the sound ཧཱུྃ
  (*hum*), and place the mind on the syllable ཧཱུྃ (*hum*) at the navel.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

II. **Guiding** ***prāṇa*** ***vāyu*** **inside the central channel after cleansing the** ***nāḍī***:

- First, examine through which nostril the air is moving.

- If the air is moving from the left nostril, clasp the right armpit and
  the breast with the palm of the right hand. Clear away (expel) the
  waste/stale air. Block the right nostril with the left index finger.
  Then inhale a long and powerful air from the left nostril, press it
  down at the navel and bind (lock) it for a while. If there arises any
  uncomfortable sensation, block the left nostril with the left index
  finger. Turn the face to the right, and expel the air forcefully from
  the right nostril. Then again block the right nostril, inhale from the
  left and repeat it again as earlier. The lower air should be
  fastened/bind at its own place. This will shift the movement of air to
  the right nostril or will move evenly through both nostrils.

- If the air is moving through the right nostril, then follow the above
  practice in reverse order. So that the movement of air shifts to the
  other nostril and the air evenly flows through both nostrils.

- The moment the air flows evenly, it is a good time to practice
  *Pūraka* and *kumbhaka*. At this point, it will be good to include the
  *Pūraka* breathing practice. This practice has been highly praised for
  attaining a long life.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

III. **Confining the** ***prāṇa*** ***vāyu*** **inside the central channel with** ***Kumbhaka*** **rough breathing practice**:

- It will be good to start with the breath flowing evenly through the
  nostrils. If it is crooked then make it evenly by above mentioned
  practice.

- Expel the waste/stale air slowly, forcefully and very forcefully, each
  three times, nine in total from the left, right and middle. This time
  keep drawing the *Apāna* *vāyu* (ཐུར་སེལ་གྱི་རླུང་).

- Then quickly inhale a little bit of air, press the air at the navel
  and at the same time ingest saliva.

- After that, draw the *Apāna* *vāyu* (to the navel) and hold the union
  (of upper and lower air) at the navel region at one’s capacity.

- If one couldn’t hold it any longer, then take three short inhalations
  and press it down.

- If one couldn’t hold it anymore, then release the air slowly and in
  the end expel the air completely.

- Here key points of observation are, if there arises clear light, sign
  and vision inside the body, then focus one’s mind on the form/image of
  emptiness that has arisen at the navel. Otherwise, focus one’s mind on
  the place where the image of emptiness has appeared.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

IV. **Yoga of coercive method (*****hathayoga*****) by forcefully blocking the passage of the sun and moon (right and left channels) when the practitioner is unable to usher and confine** ***prāṇa*** **inside the central channel**:

- After clearing away the waste/stale air, retain the breathing in its
  natural flow.

- If the veins are good, press the throat’s pulsating veins (sides of
  the throat) with the thumb and index finger of either the right or
  left hand. If the veins are weak then press the right pulsating vein
  with the right thumb, press the left pulsating vein with the left
  thumb, keep the rest of the fingers around the nape and squeeze
  adequately. The best result the practitioner achieve is the experience
  of bliss-emptiness and the experience of clear light. The intermediate
  result is, the conceptual thought will cease and will arise some new
  accomplishment signs and visions. The lowest benefit is, only a little
  bit of conceptual thought will stop.
