*Haṃsa* *Pakṣa* or the swans' wing posture is part of a trulkhor practice mentioned in the Sakya Lamdre text. The following Tibetan text is taken from Jamgon Amnye’s collected works (attributed to mahāsiddha Buddha Shri). The description of this yoga posture is almost identical to the famous *Kukkuṭāsana* (Rooster Pose) of the Hindu tradition which is considered one of the earliest non seated *āsana*. The description of the same posture is also mentioned in the Tibetan *Amṛtasiddhi* text by *Amoghavajra*, but is not named. In this article I have presented the text and the instructions for this posture with an English translation.

### Translation

In *Haṃsa Pakṣa*, after inserting the hands into the legs, the legs are bent and the weight of the body is placed on the hands. This is the swan-wing trulkhor.

### Translation

In the vajra cross-legged posture, cross the arms with the fists over the chest. Then bring the hands with the fists close together inside the thighs and place them on the ground. Straighten the elbows and put the weight of the body on them. Raise the crossed legs almost to the elbows and sit like this.
