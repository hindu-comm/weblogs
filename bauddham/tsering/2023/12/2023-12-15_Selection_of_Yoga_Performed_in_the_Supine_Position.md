#### Translator’s Note

Virupa's Amrtasiddhi texts are known for teaching some of the main practices of Hatha Yoga such as *mahāmudrā*, *mahābandha* and *mahāvedha*. What about the *āsana*? The Amrtasiddhi texts do not teach *āsana* in the way that Hatha Yoga texts do. However, there are many yoga postures which have no name and which have some similarity to *āsanas* in the *Nātha* and Hatha yoga traditions. Many yoga instructions from Amoghavajra's Amrtasiddhi texts (attributed to Virupa) end in a particular posture, like some Hathayogic *āsanas*. In this article I have presented a selection of all the yoga practices that are performed in the supine position that are found in Amoghavajra's Amrtasiddhi texts on physical yoga. I have also tried to include the benefits of each practice where they are available.

In Vajrayana Tibetan Buddhism, tulkhor (body-oriented yoga practice) generally consists of body posture (ལུས་གནད་), breathing technique (རླུང་གི་གནད་) and mental observation (དམིགས་པའི་གནད་). The following texts (taken from Virupa's Amrtasiddhi texts written by Amoghavajra) are mainly posture-oriented yoga. Amoghavajra calls these physical yoga practices ལུས་གནད་ in Tibetan, which means body posture. This may be because posture plays an important role in this yoga practice. The breathing techniques are mentioned only in the main practice of its three phases of yoga (initial, main and final). The object of observation is not mentioned in the texts, but he explains it in his commentary.

Draw in the lower air forcefully and press down the upper air forcefully. After making a fist in your hands, touch it to your forehead. Gather strength and practise.
