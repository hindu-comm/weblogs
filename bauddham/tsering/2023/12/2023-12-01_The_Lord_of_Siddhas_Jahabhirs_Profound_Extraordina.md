#### **Translator’s Note:**

This text is from the Drikung Kagyu Great Dharma Treasury. Images of this text are from the British Library. This is another lineage of *Jaharpīr* yogic practice which came down to Drikungpa Rinchen Phuntsok from *Jaharpīr’s* disciple *Vajranātha* (whereas Jamyang Khyentse Wangchug received it from *Jaharpīr* or his disciple *Manikanātha*). According to the text's colophon, the author of this text is a person named Ratna, who appears to be Rinchen Phuntsok, who received this teaching from *Vajranātha* when he was twenty-four years old.

This text is difficult to translate and quite long for a single post. So I have presented the first part of the text here and may post the rest in the future. Although Virupa's Amrtasiddhi and *Jaharpīr’s* Yogic Practice both mention attaining the deathless state, their approach and methods are quite different. One technique mentioned in both texts is the 'blocking of the nine gates of the body', though not with the fingers in the case of Amrtasiddhi.

### TRANSLATION

*Uḍḍiyāna* Guru (*Padmasambhāva*), *Mahānātha* and *Jaharpīr*, all these Gurus attained *siddhi* through the rainbow body. Even now, any fortunate one can see them in person and receive their teachings. My guru is *Vajranātha*, the disciple of *Mahāsiddha* *Jaharpīr*. I met him when I was twenty-four and received all these teachings. This teaching that has come through them contains preliminary, main and postliminary practices.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### First, the preliminary practice

In order to attain the enlightenment in one body and in one life, first the preliminary practice are:

- The secret instruction of Mantra-Protection to shield against
  obstacles.

- The secret instruction for the attainment of longevity by means of
  (sustenance on) water.

- For protection against food poisoning, the secret instruction of
  neutralising the potency of *viṣa* (poison).

- The secret instruction of warding off and eliminating the power of the
  knowledge mantra (*vidyāmantra*) of others.

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Second, the main practice

Second, the main practices are:

1.  The secret instruction of daily visualisation of eating the vital
    air of the five elements as food, in order to attain the rainbow
    body by cutting the attachment to food.

2.  Central channel manifestation (by) breathing in properly; Trulkhor
    of cleansing the passage of channel and turning central channel; and
    the breathing technique instruction.

3.  Activation of the bliss essence (*bindu*), and focusing on the white
    and red bindu as enhancement practice.

4.  The secret instruction of closing the channels and the air by
    guarding against the hindrance of *Kunda* (white *bindu* falling
    down) and holding it.

5.  The secret instruction of breathing technique and trulkhor to draw
    the *bindu* upwards and spread it throughout the body.

6.  The secret instruction of turning the focus to the attainment of the
    rainbow body (nirvana) without remainder of the *skandhas*
    (aggregates).

    ------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### I. Attainment of the rainbow body by cutting the attachment to food.

1\. Body posture. 2. Guru yoga. 3. To visualise the colour of five vital air. 4. Eating vital air gently. 5. Eating vital roughly. 6. Eating vital air forcefully.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 1. Body Posture

Commit yourself to a strict retreat in a solitary place and abstain from the defilement. Sit on a mat with a raised back. Press your left heel against your perineum. Place the right heel on the hairline of the pubes, resting the toe on the ground. Place the palms of your hands on your knees. Keep your body completely straight and your throat bent. Settle your eyes forward about the length of a yoke (*yugamātraprekṣī*).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 2. Guru Yoga (Unification with the guru)

Recite *Oṃ namo namo namo*/ *Jaharpīr Jaharpīr Jaharpīr*/ (ཨོཾ་ནམོ་ནམོ་ནམོ། ཛ་ཧར་པཱིར་ཛ་ཧར་པཱིར་ཛ་ཧར་པཱིར།). Wet your right ring finger with saliva and apply to the spot between your eyebrows (*ūrṇā*).

Visualise *Jaharpīr* at the spot between your eyebrows, dressed in a monk's robe. He is sitting in a posture with his palms together above his head and the soles of his feet touching each other. Then have a clear vision of the yellow air of earth, the white air of water, the red air of fire, the green air of air, and the blue air of sky, which are the true nature of the entire appearance of external things. During the inhalation, practise as if you were inhaling and filling (your body) with air of the five colours.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 3. Eating the vital air gently

Using the same body posture as above, blow out the (unhealthy) residual air outward. Then breath in after it has cleared. Swallow the saliva over and over again until it becomes filled with air. The purpose is to avoid the hindrances (*vighna*) of the air and gain mastery.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 4. Eating the vital air roughly

Body posture and the object of observation are the same as above. Blow out the residual air with sound. Suck in the air with sound, close your mouth, fill the air and swallow the saliva with sound again and again as above.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 5. Eating the vital air forcefully

Blow out the residual air with sound, squeeze the navel and twist the abdomen. With the gullet and throat stretched forward, immediately draw in the air from the throat with a sound. When the air is filled inside, press down slightly.

During the practice of eating the vital air gently, roughly and forcefully, retain the posterior vital air, don’t discard it. The object of observation is same in all the three practices. These are the daily practice and should be practise continuously.
