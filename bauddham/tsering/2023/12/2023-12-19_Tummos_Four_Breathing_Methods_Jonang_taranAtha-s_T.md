This text is taken from Jonang Taranatha's 'Instruction on the Six Transmission Lineages of the Indian Master Shanti-Gupta'. It is a compilation of six practices transmitted in Tibet through Indian Siddhas’. The six practices are Stage of Generation (བསྐྱེད་རིམ), *Mahāmudrā* (ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཆེན་པོ), Tummo (གཏུམ་མོ), Clear Light (འོད་གསལ), Karma-mudra (ལས་རྒྱ) and Miscellaneous Instructions. For anyone interested in the Tummo practice, I am translating the Tummo section of this text. In this post I have presented the translation of the first part of the Tummo practice, the breathing practice, and will post the rest of the translation next.

Regarding the Sanskrit terms of the breathing technique, I haven't found any Sanskrit equivalent to the Tibetan term རྡུལ་མཐའ་ཅན་ (the first breathing technique). However, many Tibetan texts, including Taranatha himself, give the Sanskrit term *recaka* for རྡུལ་མཐའ་ཅན་. However, *recaka* is also widely used as a term for mere exhalation in Vajrayana. Taranatha says རེ་ཙ་ཀ་སྟེ་དབྱུང་བའམ།རྡུལ་མཐའ་ཅན་ (*Recaka* is exhalation or *rdul mtha' can*). The Tibetan term *rdul mtha' can* literally means dust/particle-ending. Taranatha also explains in another text the meaning of the term of this breathing method, 'exhaling slowly and gently so that even the dust in the air doesn't move'.

In the Sanskrit text The Tantra of *Caṇḍamahāroṣaṇa* Controlling *Prāṇa* (*Caṇḍamahāroṣaṇatantram*), the Sanskrit term for the fourth breathing technique རེངས་པ་ཅན་ is *stambhaka*. The texts also use the names of the other three breathing techniques, *recaka*, *pūraka* and *kumbhaka* ([ap22.8](https://read.84000.co/translation/UT22084-080-015.html?part=UT22084-080-015-chapter-22#appendix)).

In the body-oriented yoga practices that prevailed in Tibet from the 10th-11th century, the *Kumbhaka* method of breathing usually involves holding the union of two airs (usually the upper and lower airs at the navel region).

### TEXT AND TRANSLATION

> གཏུམ་མོའི་དངོས་གཞི་ལ་ རླུང་ལ་བསླབ་པ་དང། གཏུམ་མོའི་མེ་དམིགས་ལ་བསླབ་པ་དང།
> ཐིག་ལེའི་རྣལ་འབྱོར་ལ་བསླབ་པ་དང། རྡོ་རྗེའི་བཟླས་པ་ལ་བསླབ་པའོ༎

In Tummo's actual practice there is training in

1\. Four breathing techniques. 2. Visualisation of Tummo's fire. 3. Yoga of the bindu and. 4. Vajra recitation (Breathing practice of the air coming in, staying in and going out with the sound of the syllable Om, hum and ah respectively, which is practised for cleansing the passage of channels).

### I. Training in four breathing techniques.

#### 1. རྡུལ་མཐའ་ཅན་/*Recaka* 

#### (exhaling slowly and gently so that even the dust in the air doesn't move)

> རྡུལ་མཐའ་ཅན་ནི་ རླུང་རོ་བསལ། རླུང་ནང་དུ་རྔུབས་ལ་རྔུབ་པ་རྫོགས་པ་ན།
> དལ་བུས་ཕྱིར་བུས་ལ།མིག་སྣ་རྩེ་ལ་བསྟད་ཅིང། ཕྱིར་འབུད་པ་རྫོགས་པ་ན།
> ལུས་སྟོང་སང་སྟེ་སོང་བར་བསམ་མོ༎

In this breathing (out) method, (first) clear away the residual air (in your body). Then inhale the air and at the end of the inhalation, let the air out slowly and gently. Direct your gaze at the tip of your nose. At the end of the exhalation, imagine your body becoming completely empty.

####  2. གང་བ་ཅན་ / *Pūraka* / Filling up

(Breathe in and press the air down a little at the belly)

> དགང་བ་ཅན་ནི། རླུང་ཕྱིར་སངས་གྱིས་བུས་ལ་ནང་དུ་རྔུབ་པ་ན་དལ་བུས་རྔུབ་ལ། རྔུབ་པ་རྫོགས་པ་ན།
> རླུང་ལྟོ་བར་ནན་ཙམ་བྱའོ༎

Blow out the (residual) air. When inhaling, inhale slowly and gently. At the end of the inhalation, press the air a little at the belly.

#### 3. བུམ་པ་ཅན་ / *Kumbhaka*

(Holding the union of upper and lower air at the belly)

> རླུང་ནང་དུ་ཤར་གྱིས་རྔུབ་ལ། འོག་རླུང་འཐེན་ལ་རླུང་གཉིས་ལྟོ་བར་ཁ་སྦྱོར་དུ་བཟུང་ཞིང།
> ཁ་ནང་གི་རླུང་མིད་ལ་ཐུབ་ཚད་བཟུང། མི་བདེ་བ་སྙམ་བྱེད་ན་གཞིལ་བར་ཡང་བྱ་ཞིང།
> མཐར་སངས་ཀྱིས་བཏང་ངོ༎

Inhale straight in. Draw the lower air up and hold the union of the upper and lower air in your belly. Ingest the air inside your mouth down (with saliva) and hold the air (in the abdomen) as long as you can. If there is any discomfort, you can also do the zhilwa breathing technique (short inhalation and pushing the air down into the central channel). Finally, let the air out completely.

#### 4. རེངས་པ་ཅན་ / *Stambhaka*

(Breath suspension after exhalation)

> རེངས་པ་ཅན་ནི་ རླུང་ཕྱིར་བུས་ལ་ཕྱིར་ཐུབ་ཚད་དུ་བཟུང་ངོ༎

Breathe out and hold the air out to your capacity.
