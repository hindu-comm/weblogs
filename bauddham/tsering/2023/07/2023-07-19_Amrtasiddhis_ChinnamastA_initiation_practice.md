Homage to Amrita Mahasukha.

First, when a student who is a suitable recipient of this teaching, requests a teacher for the complete Amrtasiddhi instruction, make a triangular-shaped source of existence (*dharmodaya* mandala) either from a red sandal paste or from a strained vermillion, arrayed with three clusters of blossoms, the edge surrounded by offerings, and lay down the ritual instruments. (After performing the preliminary ritual ceremony and recitation of mantras) the spiritual instructor oneself should perform the Zogrim practice and also recite mantras for few times. Afterwards, the pupil performs the mandala offering and makes an offering to avert the hindrances.

Jonang tradition’s Vajrayogini Chinnamasta Mandala

Draw (instructor) a triangular-shaped *dharmodaya* (mandala) on the earth with wine and let the disciple stand upon that. Let the disciple meditate a reddish-yellow Buddha Dakini holding a hooked knife, a skull cup and a *khatvanga*. Let the disciple meditate on the syllable *Om ah hum* at the corners of the *dharmodaya* triangle located below the navel, the verticle stacked mantras of the three yoginis inside the triangle, and the triangle is turning in counter-clockwise motion from one gate to another. Let the disciple settle their mind on the body and mind becoming filled with bliss. The spiritual instructor invokes innumerable yoginis and he visualizes them dissolving into the disciple. Play the musical instruments either with a long, high pitch or first louder and then gradually gentler sound.

Central figure Chinnamasta (Chhindamunda) Buddha Dakini, right Vajra Vairocani and right Vajra Varnani.

Instruct the student to tighten the arms and calf muscles and expel the air clearly through the nostrils a few times while focusing on that. Meditate the root guru and chief vajrayogini and her two retinue yoginis on the crown of the head. Meditate *śrīmati dārikā* on your left and Black (dark) Virupa on your right side. Many other Vira (hero) and yoginis are beating musical instruments, hands in a dancing pose, with their vajra melodious voice, sitting in a manner to bestow blessings on you. With the sound of gentle music, (the instructor) sings,

> Emaho! Hey child! Come hither, come to my side!
>
> The whole body and speech are the mind itself.
>
> Emaho! There is no body and speech other than the mind.
>
> The mind is devoid of birth and death.
>
> Emaho! There is no death to thy body.
>
> Hey Child, thou are immortal and intrinsically free.
>
> Emaho! Buddha Dakini, Sri Yogini, Varahi, dark Virupaksha,
>
> Please make your appearance here.
>
> And bless this noble child.
>
> Bless this child to attain the rainbow vajra body.
>
> May this child gain a power in his speech,
>
> May his speech attain the immortality.
>
> May the primordial wisdom shine in his mind,
>
> And attain the immortaility of the mind.

The chief yogini (Chinnamasta) and her two retinue yoginis in the sky dissolve into syllable HRI inside the *dharmodaya* triangle at their respective navel. This force will suddenly lift the body up. The Goddess śrīmati dārikā and Virupa from the right and left present flowers like a billowing cloud with adoration. Along with the various musical sound, imagine yourself moving into the infinite space above, and at the end in that state, let your mind settle without any object/support. Chant the mantra *Om ah hri hum hum ha* and at the same time play musical instruments first slowly and then more intensely. It is said that if the primordial wisdom arrives on this occasion, one will quickly attain the siddhis and the blessings are immeasurable. The rise of body hair and even being able to draw back conceptual thoughts slightly will have great blessings. A joyful heart and the mere clarity of an object will have middling blessings. If there is no sign or experience then will have a little blessing.
