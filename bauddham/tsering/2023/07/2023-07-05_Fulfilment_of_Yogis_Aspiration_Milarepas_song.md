(This Gur or song was addressed to his sister Peta)

མི་མེད་ལུང་པའི་བྲག་ཕུག་ཏུ།             mi mé lung pé drak puk tu སྤྲང་པོ་འཆི་བའི་སྨོན་ལམ་འདི             trang po chi bé mön lam di འགྲོ་བའི་དོན་དུ་ཐེབས་པར་ཤོག།          dro bé dön du tep par shok ཐེབས་ན་བསམ་པ་རྫོགས་པ་ཡིན།         tep na sam pa dzok pa yin ```

TRANSLATION

``` text I pray to the excellent guru, May this beggar remain in a mountain cave. Relatives won't notice my happiness, Enemies won't notice my grief. If the yogi can die in the mountain cave, Then his wishes will be fulfilled.

Friends won't see me growing old, Sister won't see me falling ill. If the yogi can die in the mountain cave, Then his wishes will be fulfilled.

People won't see my death, The birds won't see my rotting body, If the yogi can die in the mountain cave, Then his wishes will be fulfilled.

Flies will suck on my rotting flesh, And the worms will feed on the sinews, If the yogi can die in the mountain cave, Then his wishes will be fulfilled.

If the yogi can die in the mountain cave, Where there are no human footprints at the entrance, and no traces of blood inside, Then his wishes are fulfilled.

If the yogi can die in the mountain cave, Where no one gathers around your corpse, And no one weeps at your death, Then his wishes are fulfilled.

If the yogi can die in the mountain cave, Where no one asks where I am, And nowhere to show where I have gone, Then his wishes will be fulfilled.

This beggar's prayer to die, In an unpeopled solitary rocky cave, May it be fulfilled for the benefit of sentient beings, If it does then the yogi's wishes are fulfilled. ```
