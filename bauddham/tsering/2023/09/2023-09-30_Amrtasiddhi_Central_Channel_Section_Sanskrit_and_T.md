The Sanskrit text and its English translation are taken from **The Amṛtasiddhi and Amṛtasiddhimūla: the Earliest Texts of the Haṭhayoga Tradition. Critically edited and translated by James Mallinson and Péter-Dániel Szántó**. The Sanskrit Amrtasiddhi text (attributed to Virupa) is written by Madhavacandra. The Tibetan Amrtasiddhi text (attributed to Virupa) is written by E deva and the English translation is mine.

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: meru saṃveṣṭya sarvatra vyavahāraḥ pravartate Activity takes place all around [Mount] Meru.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: ལྷུན་པོ་རྣམ་པར་འཁྱམ་པ་ཡིས་།  འགྲོ་བ་ཀུན་ལ་ཁྱབ་པ་ཡིན༎ As the Meru wanders, it pervades all the beings. ```

II\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: madhyety anupamo mārgo meruṃ saṃvedhya tiṣṭhati A peerless pathway called the Goddess of the Centre is located entirely inside [Mount] Meru.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: དབུས་མ་དཔེ་མེད་ལམ་ཞུགས་ནས།  ལྷུན་པོ་རྣམ་པར་ཕུག་ནས་གནས༎ One who is in the middle (the central channel), having entered the excellent path, remains pierced in Meru. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

III\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: tatra dvāra dvayaṃ puṇyam uparyadho vibhāgataḥ 
 It has two sacred doors, in its upper and lower parts.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: བསོད་ནམས་སྒོ་ནི་གཉིས་སུ་གནས།  སྟེང་དང་འོག་གི་ཆ་རུ་གནས༎ The doors of meritorious deeds are of two, remains at the top and the bottom parts. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

IV\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: adho dvārasya saṃbhedaḥ sṛṣṭi mṛtyvoḥ svayaṃ bhavet The splitting of the lower door happens spontaneously during [pro]creation and death.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: འོག་གི་སྒོ་རུ་ཞུགས་པ་ཡིས།  སྐྱེ་དང་མི་རྟག་རང་བཞིན་ནོ༎ Entering the lower door, birth and impermanence is nature. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

V.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: ye dhanyā jñānino martye vīrya vanto mahābalāḥ 
 Those fortunate ones on earth who have knowledge, vigour and great strength

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: བསོད་ནམས་ཡེ་ཤེས་ལྡན་པ་དང།  བརྩོན་འགྲུས་སྟོབས་དང་རབ་ལྡན་པས༎ One who is endowed with merit, knowledge, diligence and strength ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

VI\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: sṛṣṭi dvāreṇa saṃviśya mukti dvāraṃ vrajanti te enter by way of the door of [pro]creation and proceed to the door of liberation.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: ཉམས་པའི་ལམ་ནས་ཞུགས་ནས་ནི།  རྣམ་གྲོལ་ལམ་དུ་འགྲོ་བར་འགྱུར༎ Enters through the lower path and goes to the path of complete liberation. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

VII\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: eṣā devī mahāvidyā devānāmpi durlabhā This goddess, the great knowledge (mahāvidyā), is hard for even the gods to obtain

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: འདི་ནི་ལྷ་མོ་རིག་པ་ཆེ།  ལྷ་རྣམས་ཀྱིས་ཀྱང་རྙེད་པར་དཀའ༎ This is a great wisdom/knowledge (mahāvidyā) goddess, difficult to find even for the gods. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

VIII\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: sarveśām jananī prokta ajñānasya Kṣayaṃkari She is said to be the creator of all [beings] [and] the destroyer of ignorance (ajñānasya).

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: འགྲོ་བ་ཀུན་གྱི་མ་ཡིན་ནོ།་འཆི་བ་ངེས་པར་ཟད་པར་བྱེད༎ (She is) the Mother of all beings who puts an absolute end to death. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

IX\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: sṛṣṭi dvāre sthitāś cāsyāḥ sarvā devyo mahābalāḥ All the mighty goddesses are located at her door of [pro]creation.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: དེ་ནི་སྐྱེ་བའི་གནས་ན་གནས།  ལྷ་མོ་ཀུན་ལ་སྟོབས་མོ་ཆེ༎ It resides in the place of birth (womb), she is the goddess powerful to all. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

X.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: sakalo niṣkalo nātho mukti dvāre vyavasthitaḥ The lord, with parts (sakalaḥ) and whole (niṣkalaḥ), is situated at the door of liberation.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: སྡིག་པ་ཐམས་ཅད་འཇོམས་པའི་མགོ།  གྲོལ་བའི་ལམ་དུ་རྣམ་པར་གནས༎ It is the head of destruction of all the sins, remains at the path of liberation. ```

XI\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: avadhūtī padaṃ kecic chmaśānaṃ mahāpathaṃ Some call [her] the place of Avadhūtī, Śmaśāna (the cremation ground) and the Great Pathway;

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: ཁ་ཅིག་ཨ་བ་དྷུ་ཏི་ཟེར།་ཁ་ཅིག་དུར་ཁྲོད་ཞེས་ཀྱང་ཟེར༎ Some call her avadhūtī. Some even call her charnel ground. ```

XII

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: kecid vadanti ādhārāṃ suṣumnāṃ ca sarasvatīm Some call her the Substrate (ādhārām), Suṣumnā and Sarasvatī

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: སུ་སོ་ཕྲ་ན་ཕྲ་གཟུགས་ཟེར།  དབྱནས་ཅན་ཞེས་ཀྱང་རྣམ་པར་འདོགས༎ Some call her sūkṣmarūpā (ཕྲ་གཟུགས). Some give her the name Sarasvatī. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

XIII\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: nāmni matāntare bhedo nityaikā jñāna cakṣuṣām In different doctrines she has different names, [but] she is one and eternal to those who have the eye of knowledge.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: སྣ་ཚོགས་དུ་མའི་དབྱེ་བ་ཡིས།  ཡེ་ཤེས་སྤྱན་ལྡན་རྣམས་ཀྱིས་བཏགས༎ All these different types (of names) are given by those who are endowed with eyes of knowledge. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

XIV\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: enām āśritya tattvāni  vasanti nṛkalevare All elements in the human body depend on her.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: འདིའི་མིང་ལ་བརྟེན་ནས་སུ།  ལུས་ནི་རྟེན་པར་འགྱུར་བ་ཡིན༎ Relying on (her with) these names, the body is sustained. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

XV\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: gaṅgā yamunā madhye vahatyekā sukha anvitā She flows between Gaṅgā and Yamunā, happy on her own.

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: གཡས་གཡོན་གཉིས་ཀྱི་དབུས་སུ་ནི།  བདེ་ཆེན་གཅིག་པུ་འབབ་པར་འགྱུར༎ In the middle of duo right and left flows the great bliss alone. ```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

XVI\.

Text within this block will maintain its original spacing when published

``` text Amrtasiddhi Sanskrit text: āsāṃ tu saṅgme snātvā dhanyā yānti parām gatim After bathing in the confluence of those [three channels] the fortunate ones go to the ultimate destination

Amrtasiddhi Tibetan text: གསུམ་པོ་གཅིག་པུ་གྱུར་པ་ཡི།  སྐྱེས་བུ་དེ་ནི་མཆོག་ཏུ་འགྱུར༎ when the three of them (channels) becomes one, that person will become the best. ```
