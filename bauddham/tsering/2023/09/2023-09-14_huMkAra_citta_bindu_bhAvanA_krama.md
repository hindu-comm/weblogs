Title

Sanskrit: *Huṃkāra citta bindu Bhāvanā krama*

Tibetan: ཧཱུྃ་ཡིག་སེམས་ཉིད་ཐིག་ལེ་བསྒོམ་པའི་རིམ་པ་

English: The stages of meditation on the Heart’s essence Syllable ཧཱུཾ

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xw9-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6bb7f893-fc20-41b6-b91e-1ec6127a5513_1730x295.png)

The one who seizes the bindu of sun and moon, shukra and rakta, is the Rahu.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_GI4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae7e5d05-7d21-48e9-aacd-21b6403dfbaa_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_GI4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae7e5d05-7d21-48e9-aacd-21b6403dfbaa_1730x295.png)

How do you purify the mind itself, the bindu? And where does the bindu reside?

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xEMZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3101a2fa-e570-48ae-8b3e-ef2060d8a74a_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xEMZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3101a2fa-e570-48ae-8b3e-ef2060d8a74a_1730x295.png)

In the centre of the four nāḍī-chakras, in the centre of the eight-petalled lotus, the bindu of the union of the sun and the moon is adorned with the nāda.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GBFg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4fb86ed3-90eb-40ad-9b10-726d832a6709_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GBFg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4fb86ed3-90eb-40ad-9b10-726d832a6709_1730x295.png)

Meditate on a very fine syllable hūṃ abiding there, the size of a mustard seed.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Fxql!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0af89085-6a2f-4d11-8d58-d6868658dfc6_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Fxql!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0af89085-6a2f-4d11-8d58-d6868658dfc6_1730x295.png)

Imagine four bindus radiating from the syllable hūṃ to the four petals.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!br_t!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6905c930-7384-467a-9501-c69aaf476729_333x333.jpeg)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!br_t!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6905c930-7384-467a-9501-c69aaf476729_333x333.jpeg)

Four goddesses on the four petals with vajra varahi in the centre.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OgQF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7ef2ab65-b65b-45d3-9e08-981c4cb8da26_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OgQF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7ef2ab65-b65b-45d3-9e08-981c4cb8da26_1730x295.png)

The bindu on the eastern petal is the Ḍākinī whose nature is urine (nectar). The bindu on the western petal is the Lāmā whose nature is faeces (nectar). Khaṇḍarohā radiates to the Bodhicitta bindu (semen) on the northern petal. Rūpinī is on the southern petal, meditate a red bindu (blood).

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DKmn!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65d7df12-6352-44ff-a4c1-877fbed96324_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DKmn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65d7df12-6352-44ff-a4c1-877fbed96324_1730x295.png)

When the nāḍī-chakras of the four regions are purified, there is purification of the four wombs (four modes of rebirth).

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Ye7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9dcef273-ae5d-4d32-9a6a-28d820c98780_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Ye7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9dcef273-ae5d-4d32-9a6a-28d820c98780_1730x295.png)

Imagine the radiance of light purifying the beings of the six realms.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gzqz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe27d01f8-c970-4a30-9c06-4150444a8071_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gzqz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe27d01f8-c970-4a30-9c06-4150444a8071_1730x295.png)

As the bindu dissolves again, one should know the union as follows.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dRxN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbde39f9c-5682-4045-8750-547970e1581f_513x454.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dRxN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbde39f9c-5682-4045-8750-547970e1581f_513x454.png)

Five parts of the syllable hum

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HzGl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcde39604-f699-4b9c-a227-216310879aa4_1730x295.png)

The four bindus dissolve into the syllable hūṃ and the syllable hūṃ dissolves into the crescent moon.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5Ls_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5d7fe58a-16fc-46d1-ba71-12471b83dbba_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5Ls_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5d7fe58a-16fc-46d1-ba71-12471b83dbba_1730x295.png)

The crescent moon dissolves into the space bindu and the space bindu dissolves into the empty space of the body.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!h5Pc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd177ba28-09de-46f1-857b-e5e6435cfa65_1730x295.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!h5Pc!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd177ba28-09de-46f1-857b-e5e6435cfa65_1730x295.png)

The yogi should meditate on the bindu of the union of the sun and the moon, which is luminous without form or colour.

**The text is from Tibetan Tengyur text, attributed to Kanhapa or Krishna, written by Dharmapa.**
