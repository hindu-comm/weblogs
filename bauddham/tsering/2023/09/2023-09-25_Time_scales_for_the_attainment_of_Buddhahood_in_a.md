**Pratyāhāra and Dhyāna** (one year practice)

It is sense direct perception (indriyapratyakṣam), sees the image of emptiness (śūnyatābimbam) directly through the eyes of flesh (māṃsacakṣuḥ)

**Prānāyāma and Dharaṇa** (Practise six months or four years and two months)

This is mental direct perception (mānasapratyakṣam) as a result of confining the vital air in the bindu of the central channel.

**Anusmṛti** (practise for three years forty-five days)

This is yogic direct perception (yogipratyakṣam) as a result of four bliss (catvāra ānandāḥ) experiences through the flow of nectar melted by the blazing candali (tummo) of primal wisdom in the navel.

**Samādhi** (practice for three years forty-five days)

This is a direct perception of intrinsic awareness (svasaṃvedanapratyakṣam) that experiences the supreme immutable bliss (paramākṣarasukha).

By holding bodhicitta bindu at the tip of the penis the practitioner attains the moment of unchanging bliss. After practising samadhi for three years and forty-five days, eight hundred thousand breath stops and attains eighteen thousand unchanging bliss.

Boddhicitta bindu rises upward and fills the half of the perineum nadi-chakra and attains the siddhi of mahamudra, the first bhumi.

The bodhicitta bindu flourishes and fills the perineum, attaining the second bhumi. Here the three thousand six hundred breath stops. The rest of the breath becomes smoother and longer. The movement of the breath becomes less noticeable.

The bodhicitta bindu rises upward and fills the navel, attaining the third and fourth bhumi. Three thousand six hundred breath stops.

The bodhicitta bindu fills the heart, attaining the fifth and sixth bhumi and stops another three thousand six hundred breaths.

The bodhicitta bindu rises and fills the throat, attaining the seventh and eighth bhumi and again stops three thousand six hundred breaths.

The bodhicitta bindu rises and fills the forehead, attaining the ninth and tenth bhumi, stops three thousand six hundred breaths.

The bodhicitta bindu rises and fills the crown, finally attaining the eleventh and twelfth bhumi and the person has attained buddhahood.
