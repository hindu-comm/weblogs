#### Translator’s Note

There are different views among Tibetan Buddhist practitioners as to whether or not the central channel exists conventionally. Some believe that it does not exist physically, but exists in the form of light, which disappears at the time of death. However, the majority maintain that it should exist in a physical form, like the other channels of the body, otherwise there is no basis for practising the Vajrayāna *dzogrim* (*rtsa rlung thigle*) practice, which is fundamentally based on the central channel.

Jamgon Amnye says the central channel with its right and left channel are located within the *srog pa’i rtsa*. I have translated the Tibetan term *srog pa rtsa* as life-channel which I couldn’t identify. He says life-channel which carries the central channel and two side channels are located within the spine or backbone. So, during *dzogrim* practices, (according to Jamgon Amnye and other texts) when the practitioner visualises the three channels and six *nāḍī*-chakra, for example the heart *nāḍī*-chakra, the practitioner has to visualise the heart *nāḍī*-chakra not at the front part of the body, but at the back of the middle of the chest towards the spine.

#### TRANSLATION

There are many opinions about the identification of the central channel. Some identify the central channel as the spinal marrow (Tibetan: *rgyungs pa*) or spinal cord. Some believe that it is within the spinal cord. Some say that the central channel is the life channel. But they all contradict the treatises of Tantra. *Mahāmudrā* *Tilaka* *Tantra* says that the central channel is in the life-channel. The life-channel has different names, such as *Brahmā's* rod and the rod of life. So, the central channel is within the life-channel/Brahma’s rod/rod of life. The example that is given in the Tantra treatise is that of a snake in a tree of sandalwood.

Some say that *Brahmā's* rod is the spine or backbone. Since *Brahmā's* rod extends to the cranial aperture (*Brahmarandhra*), *Brahmā's* rod cannot be the spine (*Brahmā's* rod according to Jamgon Amnye is the life-channel which carries the central channel). And also, the bones of *Brahmā's* rod and *Brahmā's* rod are not the same because the bones of *Brahmā's* rod is part of the backbone. Life-channel which carries the central channel and two side channels are located inside the spine.

The central channel runs from the crown of the head to the tip of the penis. The left and right channels also lie within the life-channel, forming six knots. At each of the six knots, the secondary channels pierce the life-channel and branch out. The shape of the six knots are- crown (triangular *dharmodaya*), throat (like a breast of a young woman), heart (like a cow’s eye), navel (like a lotus), perineum the root of the penis (crossed vajra), and tip of the penis (like sesame flower).
