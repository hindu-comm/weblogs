The Sanskrit term *Jālandhara* in the text's title has been transliterated into Tibetan. It has two ways of translation in Tibetan, net-holder (དྲ་བ་འཛིན་པ་) and retaining fire or flame holder (འབར་བ་འཛིན་པ་). The later translation seems more appropriate and I think it may not be related to the Mahasiddha Jālandhara. As the second Dalai lama explains,

> ་ཛྭ་ལ་ནི་འབར་བ་སྟེ་མེ་དང་དྷ་ར་ནི་འཛིན་པ་སྟེ *jvala* is blazing which is fire and
> *dhara* is to hold/retain.
>
> གསང་གནས་ཀྱི་འཁོར་ལོ་ལ་ཛཱ་ལ་དྷ་ར་ཞེས་གསུངས་ Thus the secret abode chakra (four
> inches below the navel) is called *jāladhara*. (*guhya-agni-chakra*)
>
> As it says in Samputta text ‘The fire of Brahma blazes at the
> cross-road of three channels’. Tummo the fire of Brahma blazes at the
> cross-road. So here the cross-road Secret chakra is called
> *jāladhara.*

### TRANSLATION:

**Jālandhara's[1](#footnote-1)** ***Cāṇḍālī*** **(Tummo) practice is taught in three parts:**

I. The downward moving air (Tib. ཐུར་སེལ་, Skt. *apāna*) kindles the source, the *Cāṇḍālī* (Tummo).

II. The life-bearing air (Tib. སྲོག་, Skt. *Prāṇa*) melts the seed of *amṛta* (syllable *Haṃ*).

III. And the result, *Cāṇḍālī*.

``` text I. The downward moving air (Tib. ཐུར་སེལ་, Skt. apāna) kindles the fire of Cāṇḍālī:

First, sit either in a vajra or sattva cross-legged posture. Attune the body, cupping the knees with both hands and stretching the elbow joints. Stick the belly to the backbones and straighten the vertebrae. Slightly bend the neck/throat. By joining the tongue to the palate, leave half an inch gap between the lips. At the four inches below the navel, inside the central channel, imagine a deep red in colour, glowing triangular shape dharmodaya (Tib. ཆོས་འབྱུང་གྲུ་གསུམ་པ་), in the form of four inches size Cāṇḍālī, adorned with the red syllable བཾ (Vaṃ) in the centre with an extremely fine flourish of Nāda. 

Holding the lower air that has reached the navel chakra (ལྟེ་བའི་འཁོར་ལོ) will force it to circle in a counter clockwise motion. Focus one’s mind single-pointedly on the uninterrupted circling motion, and this will bring a stability of mind. And then, the dharmodaya along with its syllable will blaze in a very fine and sharp fire. Focus single-pointely on the very fine flame blazing from the tip of the syllable བཾ (Vaṃ). And this is the causal stage of Cāṇḍālī, because when one is able to focus one’s mind on that, there will emerge a glowing, deep red, fierce, unbearably hot fire as fine as the vein (Tib. snal ma) of the lotus. 

As the fire slowly moves upwards, it pierces the navel chakra. Remain in a meditative equipoise without conceptual elaboration as the fire fills the entire Nāḍīs/channels of the navel chakra. And then the fine flame heads towards the chakra of heart, throat and the crown of the head. Attend one’s mind steadily on the fire filling up the Nāḍīs of each chakras, this will give rise to the Samadhi of free from conceptual proliferation.
```

``` text II. The upper air ( Skt. Prāṇa, Tib. སྲོག་རླུང་) melts the seed (syllable Haṃ) of the nectar (amṛta):

Keep the body posture the same as earlier. The difference is to look a little bit upwards. Slowly draw the lower air upwards. Let in the upper air (Prāṇa) so that its movement is just about undetectable. As the upward air passes through the right and left channels and diverts toward the lower end tips of the central channel, it meets with the lower air. And through the lower opening of the central channel (Avadhūtī), a very fine flame of fluttering Cāṇḍālī as a flash of light goes upward piercing the chakras of the throat, heart and the crown of the head with the light of unbearable pain. 

The syllable ཧཾ (Haṃ), which is the inmost nature of refined essence of all the elements, is located in the center of the great bliss chakra in an upside down manner, and its Bindu dripping down like a dew causing extreme pain. Bodhicitta, just about a size of the tip of a hair, drips down inside the Avadhūtī channel and falls down to the throat chakra giving rise to the wisdom of joy. While observing that, it falls to the heart chakra, and there arises the wisdom of supreme joy. And then, as it falls on the navel chakra, Bodhicitta bathes/cleanses the Tummo/Cāṇḍālī. Bodhicitta travels to the genital (Tib. rdo rje nor bu’i rtse), and is arrested at the tip without flowing out, one will then
```


**III. And the result,** ***Cāṇḍālī*****:**

When one experiences the co-emergent joy at the jewel (genital), *Cāṇḍālī* (flame) rises upward even more, and bodhicitta *Bindu* travels upward. Just as one places butter lamps on an evenly surfaced vessel filled with water and covers them with a pitcher, the water also rises upward because of the wind force from the upward burning flame.

What is resting on each of the chakras is, *Vajradhara* at the secret (perineum), *Vaircona* at the navel, Amitābhā at the heart, *Ratnasambhava* at the throat, *Amoghasiddhi* at the forehead, and *Akṣobhya* at the crown of the head. Before *Sahaja* (co-emergent) arises, the five elements dissolve into one another. When the earth element dissolves into the water element, one will see a mirage. When the water dissolves into the fire, one sees a smoke cloud. When the fire dissolves into the air, one will see a firefly. When the air dissolves into the sky, one will see a flame of a lamp. When the sky dissolves into consciousness, one will see a cloudless sky. When one sees all these signs, one attains the *Mahāmudrā*.
