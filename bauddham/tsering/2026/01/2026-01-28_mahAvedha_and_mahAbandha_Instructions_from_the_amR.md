### Notes

I have presented the instructions for Mahāvedha and Mahābandha from two different *Amṛtasiddhi* trulkhor texts from Shangpa teaching cycles. The instruction designated as number one is taken from an *Amṛtasiddhi* trulkhor text composed by Tsundue Singhe (also known as Vīrya Siṃha), a thirteenth-century Shangpa master and the second lineage holder of the *Amṛtasiddhi* teachings. This text may represent the earliest extant *Amṛtasiddhi* trulkhor manual.

The second *Amṛtasiddhi* trulkhor text, according to its colophon, was written down by the Nyingma master Minling Khrichen Pema Gyurme Gyatso (seventeenth–eighteenth century), in accordance with the wishes of his younger brother Yizhin Legdrup. The text states that these *Amṛtasiddhi* trulkhor teachings were received from Jagoe Wonpo Kunga Phuntsok though it is based on Tsundue Singhe’s text.

The Sanskrit terms Mahāvedha and Mahābandha are translated in the *Amṛtasiddhi* root texts into Tibetan as *bsdam pa chen po* and *’bigs pa chen po*, respectively. However, in the Shangpa tradition the Sanskrit terms are retained, appearing as Veta (vedha) and Bandha. Notably, the sequence of the three principal practices of the *Amṛtasiddhi* in the Shangpa tradition differs from that found elsewhere. In the Shangpa lineage, the order is Mahāmudrā, Mahāvedha, and then Mahābandha. Mahāmudrā in the Shangpa tradition is known as Am-grol. I have not included Mahāmudrā (Am-grol) in this post; it is discussed in my previous post.

In the *Amṛtasiddhi* trulkhor texts, Mahāvedha is performed either by striking the buttocks onto the heels in a squatting position, with the hands planted on the ground in *liṅga* mudrā, or by alternately striking the buttocks and heels on the ground in a seated position, with the soles touching each other and the big toes held (the latter method is not included here).

Mahābandha in the *Amṛtasiddhi* trulkhor texts is performed in a distinct posture in which the yogi reverses the cakra (*viparīta*) by bending forward from a standing position, with the buttocks raised and the head lowered, the arms wrapped around the legs and holding the big toes. Through this, the nāḍīs, vāyus, and bindus are aligned upward toward the head.

In the second *Amṛtasiddhi* trulkhor text, *beps* appear to have been later addition at the end of each of the three principal practices—Mahāmudrā, Bandha, and Vedha. In this text, Mahāvedha concludes with a great bep \[*‘bebs chen*\], performed by controlled dropping onto the buttocks and thighs with the arms extended downward, the fingers spread and the palms turned outward at the thighs, ending with the hands in *dhyāna mudrā*. Mahābandha concludes with an open-wide bep \[*‘grad ‘bebs*\], performed by dropping from a standing position onto the buttocks and thighs, ending with the hands in fists crossed across the chest.

Having lowered the body \[into a squatting position\], place the feet next to each other, leaving a space of about five finger-breadths between them. Plant the balls of the feet and the toes firmly and evenly on the ground. Bind the *liṅga* with the hands, symbolising the \[right hand\] method aspect and the \[left hand\] wisdom aspect, by pressing \[squeezing\] the right thumb with the left hand. \[Then place the hands in this position on the ground\], keeping a distance of one finger-span between the hands and the feet. Then bend the head down \[between the arms\]. Slowly draw \[the buttocks\] upward and strike the buttocks and thighs firmly and forcefully on the heels and calves.

#### II.

> སེམས་སྐྱིལ་བརླ་བཞག་ལས་བུམ་ཅན།
> དེ་ནས་ཀག་གིས་ལང་ལ་ལག་པ་གཡོན་གྱི་སོར་མོ་རྣམས་ལས་མཐེ་བོང་མ་གཏོགས་པ་གཡས་སོར་གྱིས་བཟུང་སྟེ་རྗེས་རྐང་པ་གཉིས་པུས་མོ་མ་གུག་པར་ཆུན་བཙལ་ཏེ་སྲང་བ་དང་ལྷན་ཅིག་ལག་པའི་བཅའ་ཐབས་སྔར་བཞིན་བྱས་པ་སར་བཞག་ལ་མགོ་བོ་གུག།
> དེ་མཐར་འཕོངས་རྟིང་སྤྲད་དེ་ས་ལ་རྡེབ་པ་ལན་གསུམ་མཐར་འབེབས་ཆེན་ནོ།

\[Perform\] *kumbhaka* in a *sattva* cross-legged posture, with the hands placed on the thighs. Then quickly stand up \[on the feet\], and with the right hand grasp all the fingers of the left hand except the thumb. Thereafter, bend \[the body and\] the head down, place the hands in the same position on the ground, and straighten the legs to the utmost, without bending the knees. Finally, bringing the buttocks onto the heels, strike them firmly on the ground three times. At the end, perform a great *bep.*

#### Mahābandha \[བྷན་དྷ\]

#### I.

> བྷན་དྷ་དམ་ཚིག་ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ཡི་། །རྐང་པའི་བར་དུ་ཁྲུ་གང་གཞལ་། །ལག་གཉིས་རྐང་པའི་ནང་ནས་ཕྱིར་།
> །འཁྱུད་པས་རྐང་པའི་མཐེབ་ཆེན་གཉིས་། །ལག་པའི་གུང་མཛུབ་མཐེ་པོས་བཟུང་།
> །ལངས་ནས་མགོ་བོ་དགུག་བྱ་སྟེ་། །ལུས་པོ་དྲག་ཏུ་བཅུད་ལ་བོར་། །བཤང་ལམ་འཁོར་ལོ་གྱེན་ལ་བཟློག།
> རྩ་དང་ཐིག་ལེ་གྱེན་ལ་ཕྱོགས།

Keeping a distance of one cubit between the legs, wrap the arms around the legs from the inside to the outside, and grasp the two big toes with the thumb, forefinger, and middle finger of each hand. In a standing position, bend the head downward \[directly towards the secret area/groin\], twist the body vigorously. Reverse the anal (secret) cakra upward \[by drawing the lower air forcefully\], causing the nāḍīs (*rtsa*) and bindus (*thig le*) to align upward.

#### II.

> བྷན་དྷ་གཤང་ལམ་གྱེན་ལ་ལྡོག། སེམས་སྐྱིལ་བརླ་བཞག་ལས་བུམ་ཅན།
> ཀག་གིས་ལངས་ལ་རྐང་པ་གཉིས་སར་བཙུགས་པའི་བར་དུ་བསྐུམ་ཁྲུ་ཙམ་བཞག་ལ་མགོ་བོ་བཀུག་སྟེ་ལག་གཉིས་རྐང་པའི་ནང་ནས་རྒྱབ་ཕྱོགས་སུ་ཁྱུད་དེ་ལག་སོར་གྱིས་རྐང་སོར་རྣམས་ལ་བཟུང་ལ་རོ་སྟོད་གཡས་གཡོན་དུ་ཅུང་ཟད་གཅུ།
> དེ་མཐར་འགྲད་འབེབས་སོ།

Bandha: Reverse the anus upward. \[Perform\] *kumbhaka* in a *sattva* cross-legged posture, with the hands placed on the thighs. Then quickly stand up \[on the feet\], leaving a distance of about one cubit between the feet. Bend the head downward, wrap the arms around the legs from the inside to the outside, and grasp the toes with the fingers. Then twist the upper body slightly to the right and left. At the end, perform an open-wide bep.
