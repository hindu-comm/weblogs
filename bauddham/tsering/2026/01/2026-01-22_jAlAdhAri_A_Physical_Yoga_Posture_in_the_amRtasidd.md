#### Translation

Gathering strength in the right leg, extend it and plant it firmly on the ground. Gathering strength in the left arm, lift the left foot upward and grasp the ankle with the elbow. Interlace the fingers of both hands to form the mudrā known as *rdo rje phyed mgo’i phyag rgya*, and place it at the crown of the head. Forcefully draw the lower-air upward and forcefully press the upper-air downward. By the method of filling the *jāla* \[the net of nāḍīs at the navel\] bend the chest forward. From this, intense bliss arises.

### Note

This physical yoga practice is called *Jāladhāri*, transliterated into Tibetan as *‘ja’ la d+ha ri* in the Tibetan *Amṛtasiddhi* text by Amoghavajra. It constitutes the seventeenth of the *Amṛtasiddhi*’s Seventeen Principal Physical Yoga Practices (attributed to Virūpa). In this text, the names of the physical yoga practices appear to be assigned in direct correspondence with their main phase of the practice. Each of the seventeen principal practices is structured in terms of initial, main, and final phases.

The posture associated with this physical yoga practice (*Jāladhāri*) is very similar to the posture shown in the image below, which is identified with the Mahāsiddha Jālandhara. The posture of the arms and legs is quite similar, with the hands placed in a mudrā on the head, although I could not identify the specific name of this hand mudrā. This resemblance made me think whether the name of this physical yoga practice may be associated with the yoga posture depicted in this image.

The Tibetan term *dra mig* in this verse corresponds to the Sanskrit word *jāla*, carrying the meaning of “net-like” or “lattice-like \[channels\].” According to the *Amṛtasiddhi* text by Amoghavajra, these net-like channels are associated with the nāḍīs of the navel nāḍī-cakra, which abide like a net. The text states:

> *sprul pa’i ’khor lo la rtsa dra mig ltar gnas pa’o*

(“At the navel nāḍī-cakra, the channels abide like a net.”)

Further, at the end of the verse relating to the breathing technique, the phrase

> *dra mig dgang ba’i sbyor ba*

appears to indicate that *Jāladhāri* refers to the method of filling or holding the vital air within the net-like channels at the navel cakra.
