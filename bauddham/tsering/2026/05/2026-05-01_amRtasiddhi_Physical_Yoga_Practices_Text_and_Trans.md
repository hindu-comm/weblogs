Amoghavajra wrote several *Amṛtasiddhi*-related texts, including the *Amṛtasiddhi* Root Text, its commentary, and the *Amṛtasiddhi* Miscellaneous Physical Yoga texts. These are all available in Tibetan. With the exception of the commentary, almost all of these texts are attributed to Virupa. Amoghavajra's *Amṛtasiddhi* texts (especially the root text) are very different from the rest of the *Amṛtasiddhi* texts. What makes Amoghavajra's *Amṛtasiddhi* corpus unique is the presence of instructions for a large number of physical yoga practices (which basically appear like a trulkhor) and several Hathayogic-style postures. The present text is quite long, so I plan to post the translation in sections.
