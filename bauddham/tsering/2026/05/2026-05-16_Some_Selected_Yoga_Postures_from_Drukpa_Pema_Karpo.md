# Some Selected Yoga Postures from Drukpa Pema Karpo's (16th century) Trulkhor text

#### 1. Supine

lying on the back, stretch both legs straight up to the sky, with the fingers interlocked, pull (the legs) from the knee-pits, stretching the joints of the back.

#### 2. Standing

Stand up, place the feet side by side, stretch out the arms upward and bend the body backwards.

#### 3. Stooping

Place both palms and soles of the feet on the ground, arch the back and bend the head.

#### 4. Seizing the Sun

Stand up, placing fists above the knees.

#### 5. Tortoise

Bend over, place the belly above the knees, bring forearms on the ground with fists in your hands, and bend your head forward.

#### 6. Expelling/Sending away

In prone position, hold the legs with the hands, stretch the abdomen without letting the front part of the lower body touch the ground.

#### 7. Ocean’s wave

In prone position, hold the legs from the thighs.

#### 8. Lifting Rabbit

With the buttocks on the ground, extend the legs forward, with the vajra fists, drag the forearms along the waist and the sides of the ribs, when the hands reach the chest, pull it up towards the sky, then pull the toes with the hands without bending the knees.

#### 9. lizard expanding the front body

In a prone position, leave a cubit between the legs stretched down, also one cubit between the hands stretched up, and then move slowly.

#### 10. Frog

In a face down position, spread the legs and arms in four directions, and take three steps without touching chest to the ground. At the end, fall on your chest and abdomen without touching your hands and feet to the ground.

Note: The names of the trulkhors/postures in the English language are translated from the Tibetan.
