## **Translator’s Note**

This post continues the translation of the text titled *Amṛtasiddhi’s Removing the Two Sets of Eighteen (Thirty-Six) Hindrances*. To read the physical yoga practices for removing the first five hindrances, \[[click here to read my previous post](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/amrtasiddhis-physical-yoga-practices?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=false)\]. To continue with the practices for removing hindrances six to ten, \[[click here to read that section](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/amrtasiddhis-physical-yoga-practices-a44?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=false)\]. In this post, I present a translation of the physical yoga practices corresponding to the Eleven to Fifteen hindrances from this text. In my view, the physical yoga practices described in the *Amṛtasiddhi* clearly demonstrate a synthesis of Vajrayāna Tibetan *Trulkhor* and Brahmanical āsana traditions.

The text is attributed to Virūpa and was written by the Indian yogi Amoghavajra. Each of the thirty-six physical yoga practices prescribed for the removal of hindrances includes three sequences: Initial, Main, and Final. Breathing techniques are incorporated only into the Main sequence of each practice. Altogether, the text contains 108 individual physical yoga practices.

Although the text distinguishes between two types of hindrances—outer and inner—these primarily refer to mental and spiritual obstacles. However, in the *Amṛtasiddhi’s Seventeen Principals* (also attributed to Virūpa), Amoghavajra outlines around 117 physical yoga practices and provides explanations of their physical benefits.

**Terminology Note**  
The phrase “bend the throat” refers to pressing the chin against the chest, a posture sometimes translated as “bend the neck.”  
The “lotus cross-legged posture” mentioned here does not correspond to the “full lotus” position of the Haṭha Yoga tradition.

# Translation

## XI.

### Initial sequence

Lie down on your back. Place the body's weight onto the upper third joint of the back. Extend your arms upward and grasp the big toes, with the legs bent upward \[toward the torso\]. Then, with strength, stretch \[the legs\] out.

### Main sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Raise both arms upward, with the back of the left hand facing the right palm, and interlock the fingers. Straighten the spine and straighten the throat. Then, forcefully draw the lower air upward and slightly press the upper air downward.

### Final sequence

Having firmly settled at the base of the spine, cross the ankle of the right bent leg behind the neck. Stand up, gathering strength in your left leg. Then bring the palms together at the chest. Repeat the same with the left leg.

## XII.

### Initial sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Interlace the ten fingers with the palms facing outward \[at the chest\] and the elbows raised. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Gathering all your strength, practise as if straightening \[the arms\].

### Main sequence

Having firmly settled at the base of the spine, cross the legs at the ankles and sit \[with the knees\] upright. Grasp the arms \[with the opposite hands\] and insert the knees inside the arms. Plant the edges of the soles on the ground, straighten the spine, and bend the throat. Forcefully press down the upper air and slightly draw up the lower air.

### Final sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Gather strength in the arms and make fists. Then, gathering all your strength, \[extend the arms\] as if pushing Mount Meru. Straighten the chest as well.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## XIII.

### Initial sequence

By the method of crossing the legs, plant the talus bones on the ground and bind the buttocks with the heels. Interlace the fingers and turn the palms outward. Plant the hands on the ground, pressing them beneath the shin bones.

### Main sequence

After firmly settling at the base of the spine, interlace the fingers and plant the hands firmly on the ground. With the soles of the crossed legs, stretch out the wrists. Forcefully draw up the lower air and slightly press down the upper air.

### Final sequence

After firmly settling at the base of the spine, by the method of crossing the legs, extend the right leg to the left and the left leg to the right. Then, gathering strength in the arms, bind the toes with the palms of the hands. With the help of the abdomen, slowly twist to the right. Repeat the same to the left.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## XIV.

### Initial sequence

\[In a standing position,\] place the feet side by side and plant them firmly on the ground. Place the tips of the toes against the palms of the hands, bind them tightly, and practise as if stretching out.

### Main sequence

After firmly settling at the base of the spine, with the legs crossed, bind the sides of the hips with the heels. Measuring a distance of one handspan and four fingers' width from the position of the ankles, plant the right fist firmly on the ground and bind the wrist tightly with the palm of the left hand. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Forcefully draw up the lower air and forcefully press down the upper air. Gather strength and practise it.

### Final sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Gathering strength in the arms, plant the hands firmly on the ground on the left and right sides. Then lift the crossed legs upward, floating fully in mid-air.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## XV.

### Initial sequence

After firmly settling at the base of the spine, extend the legs, keeping them side by side. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Form fists with the hands and bind the abdomen with the arm muscles.

### Main sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Tilt the crossed legs upward, settling firmly at the base of the spine. Then, gathering strength in the arms, pass the hands through the knee-pits and plant the fists firmly on the ground. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Forcefully press down the upper air and forcefully draw up the lower air. Gather strength and practise it.

### Final sequence

In a kneeling position, with the legs side by side, plant the shins on the ground. Holding fists in the hands, gather strength and plant them on the ground. Bind the buttocks with the heels of the feet. Then extend the knees outward.
