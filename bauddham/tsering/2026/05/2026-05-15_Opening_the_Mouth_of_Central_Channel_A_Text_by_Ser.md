This text is a method for opening the mouth of the central channel in order to bring the subtle air \[and the mind that is devoid of duality which rides on the air\] into the central channel by stopping the subtle air moving through the right and left channels, which arouses the image of the external grasped object and the internal mental conceptual thought, respectively.

### I. Preliminary: Breathing Practice

1.  Cleansing the *Nāḍī*

2.  *Kumbhaka*

#### 1. Cleansing the *Nāḍī*

- Visualise yourself in the form of *Vajrayoginī*, empty \[of bone and
  flesh\] within.

- Then visualise the central channel running from the centre of the
  body, the navel, to the crown of your head, which has three
  characteristic features: straight as a cane/reed \[*vetraḥ*\], red as
  lac \[*lākṣā*\], and bright as a lamp.

- Visualise the lower end of the central channel touching the circle of
  the moon \[at the navel\] and its upper mouth is covered by the upside
  down syllable ཧཾ at the crown.

- On the circle of the moon \[at the navel\], where the lower end of the
  central channel is, visualise five *bindus* the size of a mustard
  seed. The *bindu* in the centre is essentially the air of the sky and
  has the colour of the sky, blue. The *bindu* in the east is the air of
  air, colour green. The *bindu* in the south is the air of the earth,
  yellow in colour. The *bindu* in the west is the air of fire, colour
  red. And the *bindu* in the north is the air of water, white colour.

- When breathing out, imagine the air emerging from the five *bindus*
  with five different colours like smoke and coming out \[finally\]
  through the nose.

- When breathing in, imagine the air of five different colours coming in
  and dissolving into the five *bindus*.

- As you practise this over and over again, it has the benefit of
  removing the residual harmful air \[out of the channels\] and greater
  benefit in controlling the air.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 2. Practising *Kumbhaka* breathing 

- Visualise the body, the \[central\] channel and the *bindus* clearly
  as before.

- Now inhale the air slowly through the nostrils.

- Imagine the air dissolving into the navel *bindus* like water being
  poured on sand, which takes time to fill. When you feel that it is now
  filled, draw the lower air upwards. Swallow saliva without making a
  sound and push the upper air downwards below the breastbone, inflating
  the belly.

- Hold the air as long as possible. If you can't, let the air out
  slowly.

- Focus your mind on the space in the mid-air where your mind and the
  space become inseparable.

- Then inhale and repeat as before. You should practice this over and
  over again.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### II. Main practice

1.  Setting the fire

2.  Opening the mouth of the central channel

#### 1. Setting the fire

- Visualise yourself in the form of *Vajrayoginī*. Visualise the central
  channel with three distinct characteristics, as before with the left
  and right channels.

- At the base of the three channels, within the central channel, imagine
  a triangular \[A-shad\] A-stroke as if it were painted with
  vermillion.

- The upper tips of the right and left channels go around the back of
  the ears and reach the nostrils.

- Visualise a dark red syllable, ཀྵ, upside down, below the navel.

- On each ankle, visualise a blue syllable ཛྷཾ.

- The legs in the vajra cross-legged posture \[full lotus\] squeeze the
  toes inwards, this helps the air to set the syllable ཛྷཾ on fire at the
  ankles.

- This sends the sparks of the fire through the calves of the legs, and
  with a burning sensation, they travel rapidly through the great
  channels of the thighs. When it touches the syllable ཀྵ \[below the
  navel\], the syllable ཀྵ burns with fire and the fire enters the bottom
  of the Roma \[right\] and Kyangma \[left\] channels. It travels
  upwards in flashes with a burning sensation through the right and left
  channels. When it reaches the back of the throat, draw in the air
  slowly \[inhale\], visualising the fire going down where the three
  channels meet \[at the navel\].

- When you think the air is sufficiently filled, draw the lower air
  upwards. As a result, visualise the syllable ཀྵ blazing more violently
  with fire. Then push down the upper air with swallowing saliva without
  sound. As a result, imagine the fire from the left and right channels
  blazing in the lower opening of the central channel.

- Hold the air as long as possible. If you can't, let the air out
  slowly. Focus your mind on the space in the mid-air where your mind
  and the space become inseparable. Remain in this meditative state.

- When the thirst for air has subsided, repeat the whole process again.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 2. Opening the mouth of the central channel

- From your earlier visualised image of the fire burning \[at the lower
  opening of the central channel\], visualise the heat/rays of fire
  entering the central channel, the triangular \[A-shad\] tummo A-stroke
  \[within the central channel\] also ablaze with fire, and the
  heat/rays of fire moving upwards \[through the central channel\].

- Now visualise an inverted dark red syllable ཀྵྱུཾ the size of a mustard
  seed coming out of the bindu of the inverted white syllable ཧཾ in the
  crown of your head, and the syllable ཀྵྱུཾ also begins to blaze with fire.

- Visualise these two fires \[the fire of Tummo A-shad and the fire of
  the syllable ཀྵྱུཾ\] clashing violently. If the force of the fire is more
  powerful from below, visualise the fire clashing at the heart
  \[chest\] or throat. If the force of the fire is more powerful from
  above, visualise the fire clashing four inches above the navel, don't
  visualise it at the heart, there are more obstacles.

- Now draw in the air slowly \[inhale\], when you think the air is
  sufficiently filled, draw the lower air upwards, swallow saliva
  without making a sound and press the upper air downward below the
  breastbone. With that hold air of *kumbhaka* as long as possible.

- As you exhale, let the air out slowly and focus your eyes on the space
  in the air so that your mind and the space become inseparable. This
  will bring about the samadhi of emptiness.

- The final change of the object of the mind is to do the rest of the
  visualisation as before, except don't visualise the blazing of the
  syllable ཀྵྱུཾ \[now\]. From the fire burning below \[at the navel\], the
  syllable ཧཾ is activated above, and from the syllable ཧཾ bodhicitta
  falls down. As the bodhicitta *bindu* falls, it drives the fire down
  and touches the fire like a drop of oil falling on a fire. At this
  point, focus your mind on the blazing of the fire and the dripping of
  bodhicitta.

- Then again, as before, hold the breath of *Kumbhaka*. Let the air out
  and unite your mind with the space as before.

- If you are young and it is summer, visualise the Bodhicitta *bindu*
  falling in a drop from the syllable ཧཾ. If you are older and it is
  winter, visualise the Bodhicitta *bindu* falling in a stream. Practise
  this over and over again.
