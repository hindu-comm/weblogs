## Translator’s Note

This post continues the translation of the text titled *Amṛtasiddhi’s Removing the Two Sets of Eighteen (Thirty-Six) Hindrances*. To read the physical yoga practices for removing the first five hindrances, \[[click here to read my previous post](https://open.substack.com/pub/tsering/p/amrtasiddhis-physical-yoga-practices?r=19pwkm&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=false)\]. In this post, I present a translation of the physical yoga practices corresponding to the Six to ten hindrances from this text. I have not included a translation of the 'initial sequence' from the ninth practice, as I was unable to fully understand the instructions.

The text is attributed to Virūpa and was written by the Indian yogi Amoghavajra. Each of the thirty-six physical yoga practices prescribed for the removal of hindrances includes three sequences: Initial, Main, and Final. Breathing techniques are incorporated only into the Main sequence of each practice. Altogether, the text contains 108 individual physical yoga practices.

Although the text distinguishes between two types of hindrances—outer and inner—these primarily refer to mental and spiritual obstacles. However, in the *Amṛtasiddhi’s Seventeen Principals* (also attributed to Virūpa), Amoghavajra outlines around 117 physical yoga practices and provides explanations of their physical benefits.

**Terminology Note**  
The phrase “bend the throat” refers to pressing the chin against the chest, a posture sometimes translated as “bend the neck.”  
The “lotus cross-legged posture” mentioned here does not correspond to the “full lotus” position of the Haṭha Yoga tradition.

# Translation

## VI.

#### Initial sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Gather strength in your arms, pass your hands between the crossed legs, and plant them firmly on the ground. Straighten your spine and \[slightly\] bend the throat. Keeping both arms upright, use the chest to shake \[the body\] seven times.

#### Main sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture with the knees touching the ground. Measure a distance of two hand spans plus four finger widths from the ankles, and place your interlocked fingers on the ground with the palms facing outward. Then, straighten the spine and bend the throat \[slightly\]. In that position, forcefully draw up the lower air and press down the upper air, rotate \[the abdomen at\] the navel.

#### Final sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Cross your arms behind your back and tighten your arm muscles. Then lie on your back, raising the crossed legs upward. Support the weight \[of the body\] on the forearms, placing them firmly on the ground at the level of the upper seventh joint of the back. Generating strength, bend both the upper and lower \[body\] upward nine times.

## VII.

#### Initial sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture and press the thighs with the palms of your hands. Using the method of straightening the spine, draw the secret \[nadi chakra\] upwards. Then, by bending the throat (Tib. *dra-mig*, Skt. *Jāla*), raise it upwards seven times.

#### Main sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture and place your hands in the Vairocana mudra. Then, by forcefully drawing the lower air upward and pressing the upper air downward, practise as if rising up.

#### Final sequence

\[In a standing position,\] plant the right foot firmly on the ground. Wrap the left leg around the right leg, pressing the left heel against the right talus \[bone\]. Interlace all ten fingers and press them firmly on the upper part of the left knee. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Then repeat the same on the opposite side.

## VIII.

#### Initial sequence

\[In a seated position,\] place the legs side by side and stretch them fully forward. Interlock the thumbs, straighten the spine, and bend the throat. Then, with strength, extend both arms fully forward.

#### Main sequence

Having firmly grounded yourself on the base of the spine, place the feet together. Rest the calves on the elbows and interlace the ten fingers to clasp the ankles. Then, with strength, stretch the legs and simultaneously pull them inward using your hands. Forcefully draw the lower air upward and press the upper air downward. With strength, practise in this way.

#### Final sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Gathering strength in your arms, plant the palms of your hands firmly on the ground to your right and left. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Then rotate the chest fully.

## IX.

#### Initial sequence

#### Main sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Tilt the crossed legs upward and settle firmly on the base of the spine. Clasp the wrists with \[the opposite\] hands, insert the crossed legs between the arms, and bind them. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Then forcefully draw the lower air upward and press the upper air downward.

#### Final sequence

Plant the shins firmly on the ground. Press the heels firmly between the genitals and the anus. Cross the arms and bind the armpits tightly with the opposite hands. Press the thighs using the forearms. Then, gathering all your strength, bend down the first limb—the head.

## X.

#### Initial sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Extend the right arm forward. Gathering strength in the right arm, form a fist and rotate the wrist twenty times. Then shake out the hand completely. Repeat the same movement with the left arm.

#### Main sequence

Bind the yoni \[perineum\] firmly with the left heel. Bend the right leg and press the right ankle against the left thigh. Bend the left arm outward and bind \[hook\] the right talus bone with the left elbow. Bend the right arm behind the back and form a fist. With strength, use the left hand to bind the right wrist. Then forcefully draw the lower air upward and press the upper air downward. Practise this with strength.

#### Final sequence

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Straighten the spine and bend the throat. Interlace the ten fingers and raise the arms upwards. Using the method of evenly \[stretching\] the channels, rotate the upper body.
