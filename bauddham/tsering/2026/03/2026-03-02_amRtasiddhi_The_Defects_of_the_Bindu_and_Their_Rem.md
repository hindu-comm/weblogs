### **Note and Context**

According to the colophon of the text, this *Amṛtasiddhi* yoga manual is attributed to Virūpa (Birwapa, སློབ་དཔོན་ཆེན་པོ་བིར་ཝ་པ) and was written down by Amoghavajra. The text discusses the functions of the bindu, its beneficial qualities, and its defects.

The present text focuses specifically on the defects of the bindu and their remedies, which are classified under *lus gnad* (physical yogic techniques employing specific bodily postures), medicinal substances, and blessings. In this post, only the *lus gnad* section is included.

In Vajrayāna Buddhism, the subtle body’s bindu (*thig le*) does not refer exclusively to seminal drops. The two primary bindus are the white element: the refined quintessence of semen which abides as *haṃ* at the crown, and the red element: the refined quintessence of blood, also known as karmacaṇḍālī which abides below the navel.

These two bindus are regarded as the generative bases of the multitude of bindus throughout the body. They are also sometimes described as the bodily essence (*khams*; Skt. *dhātu*) that flows through the channels. Certain systems classify twelve refined fluids or clear bodily essences and twenty-four impure bodily essences, corresponding to the twenty-four nāḍīs and sacred sites as shown in previous post. These are said to support physiological processes such as the growth of teeth and nails. In Amoghavajra’s *Amṛtasiddhi*, sixteen distinct types of bindu are described, located in various parts of the body, including the back of the ears and the hips.

**What are the “upper air” and the “lower air”?**

The *Amṛtasiddhi* frequently employs the phrases“upper air” and “lower air” in its yogic instructions.

The three principal channels: right, left, and central channels are functionally divided into upper and lower segments. The point of demarcation is the *guhya-cakra* (the secret chakra), located approximately eight inches below the navel. Above this point are the three upper channels; below it, the three lower channels.

Through the three upper channels flows the prāṇa-vāyu, which has a natural upward tendency. For this reason, in practice it is pressed downward. Through the three lower channels flows the apāna-vāyu, which naturally moves downward and is therefore drawn upward during yogic practice. Thus, in the terminology of the *Amṛtasiddhi*, prāṇa-vāyu corresponds to the “upper air,” and apāna-vāyu to the “lower air.”

**Note**: The first remedy (*lus gnad*) corresponds to the physical \[hathayogic\] Mahāmudrā technique.

# TRANSLATION

### Function of the Bindu (*thigle*)

When the thigle is clear, it generates strength in the body.  
When the thigle is unclear, it weakens the body.  
When the thigle leaks or escapes, the body declines.  
When the thigle is retained, the body flourishes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Defects of the *thigle* & Remedy

#### I. Thigle leaking inside the torso of the body

**Cause:** It results from excessive exertion and fatigue.

**Symptom**: There is a loss of lustre in the complexion.

**Remedy**: Perform this *lus gnad*

#### II. Dispersion of thigle to the joints

**Cause**: It arises from laziness or lethargy in one’s activities.

**Symptom**: There is swelling and protrusion of the joints.

**Remedy**: Rotate the joints.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### III. Thigle dripping like tears

**Cause**: It arises from intense joy and intense anguish.

**Symptom**: Thick flow of tears.

**Remedy**: Seated in the vajra cross-legged posture, with the hands in dhyāna-mudrā, turn or roll the eyes upward.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### IV. Thigle dripping like saliva

**Cause**: It arises from uncleanliness and from the ingress of impure particles.

**Symptom**: There is profuse flow of saliva.

**Remedy**: Seated in the lotus cross-legged posture, with the hands in dhyāna-mudrā, draw in a cooling breath \[through the mouth\].

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### V. Thigle dripping like mucus

**Cause**: It arises from cold and hunger.

**Symptom**: Mucus flows profusely.

**Remedy**: Seated in the vajra cross-legged posture, press the thighs with the palms of the hands. Straighten the spine, bend the throat (bringing the chin towards the chest), and slowly rotate the neck. Place the tongue against the palate, and cause the amṛta to descend from the throat.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### VI. Dripping or discharge of the actual thigle

**Cause**: It is discharged due to malevolent influences, illness, \[sexual\] desire, and intense engagement.

**Remedy**: Perform this *lus gnad*: press or draw the abdomen back towards the spine.
