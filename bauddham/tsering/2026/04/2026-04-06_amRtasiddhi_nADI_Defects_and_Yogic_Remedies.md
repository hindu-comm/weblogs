### **Introduction**

This material forms part of the *Amṛtasiddhi* corpus attributed to Virūpa and written down by Amoghavajra. What is particularly distinctive about this corpus is that, unlike other *Amṛtasiddhi* sources, it contains a large number of physical yogic practices. It not only describes the constituent subtle elements of the body, such as the channels nāḍī, vāyu, bindu, sun, and moon, but also includes a dedicated section on their defects and the methods for remedying them.

Another unusual feature of this corpus is that, rather than presenting the well-known system of three principal channels (right, left, and central), it speaks of five principal channels. Although Amoghavajra does not explicitly define these five, his other writings suggest a possible framework. The system appears to be based on the distinction between upper and lower channels, demarcated at the secret chakra, located approximately eight inches below the navel. The three channels above this point are referred to as the upper channels, while the three below are the lower channels. On this basis, the five principal channels may be understood as the right and left channels above, the right and left channels below, together with the single central channel.

This post presents a translation of a detailed account of the defects of the nāḍīs, explaining how they arise through diet, behaviour, and physical strain, how they manifest in the body and mind, and how they may be corrected through specific postural techniques (*lus gnad*) and related methods.

# TRANSLATION

### I. Numbers of Channels

In general, the *nāḍīs* (channels) in the human body are said to be too numerous to count. For the sake of presentation, however, they are traditionally described as numbering seventy-two thousand. This is then condensed into smaller groupings: from 72,000 to 360, then to 141, to 72, to 32, to 9, and finally to the five principal channels.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **II. Formation of Channels**

The channels are described as abiding in various forms: like a central column (*srog shing* / *iṣikā*), like the mesh of a net (*dra mig* / *gava-akṣa*), like woven threads, like the trunk of a tree, like a bundle of snakes, like *nāḍī*-petals, and like knots, and so forth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **III. Functions of Channels**

As for their functions, they bring about clarity and obscuration, cause \[the mind\] to move or to settle, and enable it to engage \[with their objects\].

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **IV. Defects of Channels**

As for their defects, the channels may become impaired, tangled, stiff, cracked, split, shrunken, and so forth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **V. Methods for Removing Defects**

As for the methods of remedying these defects, there are four: postural technique (*lus gnad*), mental support (*dmigs pa*), substances (*rdzas*), and blessings (*byin rlabs*).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **Defects of the** Channels **& Remedy**

#### 1. Impaired channels

**Cause:** Its causes are sleep, women \[sexual activity\], and alcohol.

**Symptom**: Its symptoms are emaciation, frequent yawning, and loss of appetite, and others.

**Remedy (Postural Technique,** ***lus gnad*****):**

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Cross the arms over the chest and clasp the armpits with the palms of the opposite hands. Generating strength, rotate the abdomen (*kukṣi*) thoroughly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 2. Entangled channels

**Cause:** The causes are coldness (*śīta*), disturbing or agitated dreams, and intense fear (*bhīta*).

**Symptom**: The symptoms include fainting and a fixed, unblinking gaze.

**Remedy (Postural Technique,** ***lus gnad*****):**

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Draw the belly (the “ocean”) inward towards the spine (Meru), straighten the spine, and bend the throat. Then massage the abdomen with both hands.

#### 3. Rigid channels

**Cause:** This arises from an imbalance in the temperature of food.

**Symptom**: The symptoms include twisting or coiling of the lower intestines (*rgyu zhabs*), crying out in distress, and difficulty in breathing.

**Remedy (Postural Technique,** ***lus gnad*****):**

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Pass the hands through the inside of the knees (knee pits), and bring the palms together at the point between the eyebrows.

#### 4. Ruptured channels

**Cause:** The causes are over-satiation from food, excessive passage of food, and the failure of food to reach its proper place.

**Symptom**: The symptoms are bloating and pain in the stomach or intestines.

**Remedy (Postural Technique,** ***lus gnad*****):**

Assume a cross-legged posture. Cross the arms \[over the chest\], straighten the spine, and bend the throat fully. Focus the mind on the navel and rotate \[the abdomen\] thoroughly.

#### 5. Severed channels

**Cause:** The causes include jumping, lifting heavy objects, carrying heavy loads, and drawing a stiff bow, among others.

**Symptom**: The symptoms are sharp, shooting pains that arise when standing up suddenly or during physical activity.

**Remedy (Postural Technique,** ***lus gnad*****):**

Assume a lotus cross-legged posture. Firmly clasp each arm with the opposite hand, and use the forearms to press down on the crown of the head. Straighten the spine, bend the throat, extend the whole body, and rotate it.

#### 6. Shrunken channels

**Cause:** This arises from keeping the body bent or contracted for too long, and from sitting in one position for extended periods.

**Symptom**: The symptom is a crooked or bent gait, especially affecting the areas of the body that have been held in that position.

**Remedy (Postural Technique,** ***lus gnad*****):**

Gently and slowly extend and straighten all parts of the body that have become contracted.
