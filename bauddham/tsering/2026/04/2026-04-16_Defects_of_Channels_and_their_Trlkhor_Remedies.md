### **Notes and Context**

I have not been able to identify the name or title of this Tibetan text. It is drawn from the *Rinchen Terzö Chenmo* (Schechen Monastery collection).

The descriptions of defects in the channels are similar to those found in the *Amṛtasiddhi*, which I have recently posted, though the methods of remedy differ. The remedies involve both trülkhor and medicinal treatments; here, I have included only the trülkhor section.

Trülkhor as in general consists of the coordinated application of bodily posture, breathing technique, and mental support.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### TRANSLATION

In general, when the channels become dry, the body also becomes emaciated; when the channels become impaired, the body likewise becomes impaired. Thus, whatever faults arise in the channels give rise to corresponding ailments in the body. Therefore, if the faults of the channels are not cleared, the vital airs within the channels, and the mind that rides upon them, will not function properly.

The causes of defects arising in the channels are: forceful or violent physical activity; falling from heights; practising trulkhor without proper breath control; and improper eating, drinking, and conduct.

The signs or symptoms of faults arising in the channels are: sensations of numbness in the body; heat or boiling sensations; itching; burning; stabbing pain; and pain throughout the body. The experiential signs include disturbances caused by non-human entities in dreams. There also arise excrescences, abscesses, warts, paralysis, and many other undesirable conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **Remedies**

The remedies are trülkhor and medicine.

### **Defects of the channels and their trülkhor remedy**

#### **1. Severed channels:** 

**Trülkhor remedy:** Wherever a channel is severed, whether in the upper or lower part of the body, assume the sevenfold posture of Vairocana. With the breath properly controlled, apply the visualisation of the channels and channel-wheels (nāḍī and nāḍī-cakra). Then rub and massage the area wherever there is discomfort. If you cannot reach it with your own hands, have a companion perform the massage.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **2. Withered channels**

**Symptom:** When the channels become dry or withered, the body develops itching and the limbs become emaciated.

**Trülkhor remedy:** Sit \[body\] in a cross-legged posture, with the mind resting in meditative equipoise, and the breath properly held. First, rub the palms together until they become warm. Then rub from the crown of the head down to the soles of the feet, spreading warmth throughout the whole body. Next, rub again from the soles of the feet up to the crown of the head. When the hands reach the crown, expel the residual air \[within the channels\] through the nose and gently shake the entire body.

This clears the defects of dried channels and increases the strength of the body.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **3. Twisted channels**

**Trülkhor remedy:** First, while lying down, expel the residual air from within the body several times. Then, \[sitting up in a cross-legged posture\], by holding a gentle breath twist the body to the right and left repeatedly. Finally, allow the body and mind to settle in equipoise without movement.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **4. Agitated channels**

**Symptom:** When the channels become agitated, the body and arms begin to shake.

**Trülkhor remedy:** First, place the body in a comfortable position, then rub and massage the entire body. Next, lie on your back and, with both arms, alternately make a movement like drawing a firm bow, three times each. The same should be done with the legs. Then extend both arms and legs upward towards the sky and draw them in towards the chest. At first do this slowly, then increasingly more forcefully, twenty-one times, and then relax. Finally, expel the residual air within the body with a long “ha” exhalation. This clears the ailments.

All activities should be performed in a relaxed manner. When sitting, assume the sevenfold posture of Vairocana and maintain gentle breathing. When lying down, adopt the Buddha’s sleeping posture, and avoid improper sitting or sleeping positions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### **5. Rigid and impaired channels**

**Trülkhor remedy:** Retain the breath in kumbhaka (vase breathing). Apply a piece of oil-soaked felt, warmed over a fire, repeatedly to the affected area. This is beneficial.
