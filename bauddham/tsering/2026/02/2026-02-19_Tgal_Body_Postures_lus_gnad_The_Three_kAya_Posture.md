### Notes and Context

These bodily postures (*lus gnad*) are practised during tögal after khregs chöd, in order to obtain the visions of primordial wisdom. Two key elements are involved in these postures: the posture itself and the gaze. I have not included the translation of the gaze instructions here, in order to keep the presentation less complex. The text emphasises the importance of these postures:

> The inherent radiance of primordial wisdom—the power of natural
> expression—abides within the nāḍī-petals \[channels\]. Without
> perfecting these postures, one will be unable to activate this
> inherent radiance, and the visions of primordial wisdom will not
> appear in the sky. Moreover, without the proper gaze, one will be
> unable to see them.

These sets of bodily postures are not selected from different texts or from different parts of a larger work. Rather, they come from a text devoted specifically to bodily postures, accompanied by illustrations and drawings. The first three postures are the well-known “three kāya sitting postures”: the lion, elephant, and ṛṣi postures, whose respective visual targets are the sun, moon, and lamp.

By gazing at these targets with the eyes slightly closed, the practitioner is said to perceive visions at the level of the brows. First appears the vision of the “lamp of pure space,” which resembles a rainbow or a peacock’s feather. Next, within that, appears the “lamp of the empty sphere,” like ripples on a pond when a stone is thrown. Finally, within that, arises the vision of the “vajra chains of rigpa,” resembling a fine horsehair with knots.

Image from Karma Khenchen’s text: visions of the lamp of pure space, the lamp of the empty sphere (bindu), and the vajra chains of rigpa (illustrated on the right side).

These three postures are said to be applied continuously \[during tögal practice\]. The next two: numbers four and five serve as supplements to the first three and are used as forms of resting (and the rest of the postures as well). The following five postures: numbers six, seven, eight, nine, and ten are related to the five primordial wisdoms. The final seven are presented as secondary postures. Throughout this system of bodily postures, the gaze appears to be of crucial importance.

First three images are from Lhasa Lukhang mural.

# TRANSLATION

#### 1. Dharmakāya Lion’s Pose

Sit like a lion at the mouth of its den. Place the soles of the feet firmly on the ground in a squatting position. Form vajra-fists and place the hands down near the big toes. Extend the torso like a lion, drawing the neck slightly back.

#### 2. Sambhogakāya Elephant’s Pose

Lie like an elephant, with the front of the body facing downward. Fold the legs back with the toes extended behind, and lie prone with the knees drawn in towards the chest. Plant the elbows on the ground and support the chin with the hands. Draw the neck slightly backward.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PE7m!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8578063-d593-4306-866d-36c4a297c5f4_722x561.png)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PE7m!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8578063-d593-4306-866d-36c4a297c5f4_722x561.png)

#### 3. Nirmanakaya Rishi’s Pose

Sit like a rishi squatting. Place soles of the feet on the ground. Bring the ankles close together, pressing the knees against the chest. Wrap the arms around the knees. Straighten the spine and slightly loosen the neck.

#### 4. Abhisambodhikāya Vulture’s Pose

Sit with the soles of the feet planted on the ground, without allowing the buttocks to touch the ground. Thrust the torso forward and extend the arms behind along the sides of the body like a bird’s wings, placing the hands upon the buttocks.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!o1Yu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9b615a4f-461a-45a4-acca-4c1a407bf899_536x436.png)

#### 5. Vajrakāya Vajra Pose

Join the soles of the feet together and **set** the heels against the perineum. Then join the palms together and place them at the crown of the head.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ruGH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F271242f9-a01c-48b5-847d-54c745912e32_542x471.png)

#### 6. Dharmadhātu-jñāna Sevenfold Posture

Sit with the legs in a cross-legged posture. Place the hands in *dhyāna* mudrā. Rest the gaze on the tip of the nose. Keep the spine straight. Place the tongue against the palate. Let the teeth and lips remain gently parted, without fully touching. Breathe gently.

#### 7. Ādarśa-jñāna sitting posture

Lean the body down on the right side, planting the right elbow on the ground. Rest the cheek on the right hand. Place the left hand at the right armpit, \[with the legs folded back\] and the toes pointing backward.

Lie supine and extend the arms and legs.

#### 9. Pratyavekṣā-jñāna Posture

#### 10. Kṛtyānuṣṭhāna-jñāna Posture

\[Stand with\] the right leg extended and the left leg bent, with the hands in a threatening mudrā \[gesture\].

#### 11. Garuḍa Pose

\[Sitting\] bring the soles of the feet together in front. Form vajra-fists and place them upside down on the thighs. Bend the neck slightly.

Bring the soles of the feet together in front. Place the hands \[on the ground\] near the groin and bend the neck slightly.

#### 13. Man’s Pose

From a rishi seated posture, wrap the arms around the legs and interlace the fingers beneath the thighs.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mf1x!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F06ba49fc-a0f6-40b6-a8fb-9566386dcb1f_497x448.png)

#### 14. Fox’s Pose

\[From seated\] Place the soles of the feet on the ground, with the ankles side by side. Form a vajra-fist in each hand, with the thumbs tucked in, and place the hands on the ground beside each foot. Draw the shoulders upward and straighten the spine.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UYL3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Facfe5d23-e26e-414b-9689-3b9c45ffd701_523x452.png)

#### 15. Vulture’s Pose

Place the legs as in vajra pose \[heels pointing towards the perineum\], but extend them slightly outward. Form a vajra-fist in each hand and plant them on the ground behind, beside the hips. Stretch the channels at the back of the neck \[drawing the chin closer to the chest\] and straighten the spine.

#### 16. Malla (Wrestler’s) Pose

Sit in a \[vajra\] cross-legged posture. Plant the elbows on the thighs and support the jaw with the hands.

Sit in a \[vajra\] cross-legged posture. Place the hands on the knees. Draw the back of the neck slightly backward.
