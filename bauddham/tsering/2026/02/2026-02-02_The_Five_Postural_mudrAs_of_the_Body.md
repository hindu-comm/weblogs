#### **Note**

“Five mudrās of the body,” or “five postural seals of the body,” is another form of *lus gnad* (body posture), comparable to the sevenfold posture of Vairocana and the sevenfold posture of dhyāna, though it is not as widely used as these more common systems. The term “five mudrās” seems to be derived from the five symbolic ornaments worn by deities—namely the wheel, earrings, bracelets or anklets, necklace, and girdle—as explained in the text below.

This passage is taken from *Embodiment of the Sugatas* *trulkhor* text, composed by Minling Lochen Dharma Shri (associated with Nyingma tradition). The posture described here is specifically connected with *trulkhor* practice concerned with holding the *bindu*, which likely explains the inclusion of *kumbhaka* (vase breathing) in this context. In other presentations of the five postural mudrās, however, the instructions instead emphasise keeping the spine straight and placing the hands in the *dhyāna mudrā*, without reference to vase breathing.

#### The five postural seals

1.  Vajra cross-legged posture. The hands are crossed in vajra-fists at
    the chest;  
    the muscles of the arms and legs are held with firmness,  
    and the fingers and toes are firmly drawn in.

2.  The eyes are opened wide,  
    the gaze directed upward.

3.  The tongue is fixed to the palate.

4.  Throat bent.

5.  The waist is engaged,  
    and the abdomen is expanded outward.

#### Five ornamental *mudrās* corresponding with five postural *mudrās*

> ལུས་ཀྱི་ཕྱག་རྒྱ་ལྔས་བཟུང་བ་ནི།

Maintaining the posture through the five *mudrās*.

#### 1. Wheel \[*cakrī*\]

> མིག་གཉིས་བགྲད་ནས་གྱེན་ལ་ལྡོག་སྟེ་སྤྱི་བོའི་ནམ་མཁའ་ལ་བལྟའ་བ་སྤྱི་བོའི་འཁོར་ལོ།

With both eyes opened wide, turned upward, and directed into the space above the crown, this is the crown wheel.

#### 2. Earrings \[*kuṇḍalaḥ*\]

> ལྕེ་ནང་དུ་སྒྲིལ་ནས་རྐན་ལ་སྦྱར་ཞིང་ལྕེ་ལྡོག་པའི་སྟོབས་ཀྱིས་རྣ་བའི་རྩ་གཉིས་ཀྱང་ཁྱུར་གྱིས་འོང་བའི་རྣ་བ་སྡམས་ཏེ་རྣ་ཆ།

Rolling the tongue inward and placing it against the palate; by the force of the tongue being drawn back, the ears and their two descending channels are sealed, this constitutes the earrings.

#### 3. Neck ornament \[*kaṇṭhī*\]

> མགྲིན་པ་དགུག་པས་མགུལ་རྒྱན།

Bending the throat constitutes the neck ornament.

#### 4. Bracelets and Anklets \[*kaṭaka*\]

> རྐང་པ་རྡོར་སྐྱིལ་དང་ལག་གཉིས་ཁུ་ཚུར་སྙིང་གར་བསྣོལ་ནས་ཉྭ་བཞི་སྒྲིམས་ཤིང་སོར་མོ་རྣམས་དྲག་ཏུ་བསྐུམ་པ་ནི་གདུབ་བུ།

The legs in vajra cross-legged posture and the hands in vajra-fists crossed at the chest, the muscles of the four limbs are held with firmness and the fingers and toes are tightly drawn in, this \[represents\] the bracelets and anklets.

#### 5. Girdle/belt \[*mekhalā*\]

> སྐེད་པ་བསྐོར་ཞིང་ལྟོ་བ་ཕྱིར་བསྐྱེད་པ་འོག་དཔག་སྟེ་སྟེང་རླུང་བུམ་ཅན་ནོ།

Turning the waist and expanding the abdomen outward, the upper air \[is pressed down\] with kumbhaka, this constitutes the girdle.
