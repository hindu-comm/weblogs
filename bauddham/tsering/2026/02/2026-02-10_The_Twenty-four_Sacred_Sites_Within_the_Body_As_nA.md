### Notes and Context

Vajrayāna, or Buddhist tantra, is an embodied system of practice. It teaches a swift path to Buddhahood in this very life, or in the *bardo*, by using the body itself as the path. This is accomplished through working with the body’s *rtsa* (*nāḍīs*), *rlung* (*vāyu*), and *thig le* (*bindu*). This emphasis is distinctive when compared with non-tantric Mahāyāna presentations. In tantra, the body is sacred and indispensable, and is regarded as the abode of deities and buddhas. Tārānātha’s commentary on the *Vasanta-tilakā* states:

> རང་ལུས་ཉིད་ལ་སངས་རྒྱས་གནས།  
> གཞན་ནི་གང་ནའང་ཡོད་མ་ཡིན།  
> ལུས་ལས་གཞན་དུ་སངས་རྒྱས་ཉིད།  
> མི་ཤེས་པ་ཡིས་བསྒྲིབས་པའི་ལུགས།
>
> *The Buddha abides in one’s own body*  
> *and does not exist anywhere else.*  
> *To seek the Buddha outside the body*  
> *is the doctrine of ignorance.*

As Tārānātha explains, the meaning of this verse is that, in general Mahāyāna Buddhism, a sentient being’s nature has been buddha from primordial time. It is only a matter of clearing away temporary defilements; apart from that, there is no buddha to be attained that is newly produced. And in Vajrayāna, the principal cause for the attainment of Buddhahood is the purification of one’s own body’s nāḍī, vāyu, and bindu. Apart from working with one’s own nāḍī, vāyu, and bindu, there is no other means of attaining Buddhahood.

In the Vajrayāna vajra-subtle-body system, the nāḍīs have a feminine aspect and abide in the form of yoginīs or ḍākinīs. The respective dhātu (the constituent element of the body) that flows within each nāḍī is male-aspected, and abides in the form of ḍākas and vīras (*dpa’ bo*). Thus, in this configuration of twenty-four channels and their respective dhātus, each is represented by a specific male and female consort in union. The following is an excerpt from Tārānātha’s commentary on the *Vasanta-tilakā* by Kṛṣṇācārya.

> ཉི་ཞུ་རྩ་བཞིའི་དབྱེ་བ་ཡིས། རྩ་རྣམས་ཀུན་གྱི་གནས་དག་ནི། ཀུན་ནས་ཡང་དག་བཤད་པ་ཡིས།
> གནས་འདི་རྣམས་སུ་མཁའ་འགྲོ་མ། རྩའི་གཟུགས་ཀྱིས་ཡང་དག་གནས།

Through division into twenty-four,  
the twenty-four abodes of the nāḍīs are taught.  
In these twenty-four locations,  
the ḍākinīs abide in the form of nāḍīs.

> མི་ཕྱེད་མ་ལ་སོགས་པའི་རྩ་ཉེར་བཞི་ནི། རབ་ཏུ་གཏུམ་མ་ལ་སོགས་པ་རྣལ་འབྱོར་མ་ཉེར་བཞི་དང།
> སོ་སེན་སྐྱེད་པའི་རྒྱུའི་ཐིག་ལེ་ལ་སོགས་པ་ཁམས་ཉེར་བཞི་ནི།
> ཁཎྜྲ་ཀ་པ་ལ་སོགས་པ་དཔའ་བོ་ཉེར་བཞིའི་རང་བཞིན་དུ་གནས་པ་ཡིན་ནོ།

The twenty-four nāḍīs, such as the Abhedyā nāḍī, abide in the nature of the twenty-four yoginīs, such as Pracaṇḍā. Likewise, the twenty-four dhātus such as the bindu that gives rise to teeth and nails are of the nature of the twenty-four vīras (male deities), such as Khaṇḍakapāla.

### Twenty-four nāḍīs designated with the names of the twenty-four sacred sites (*pīṭha*) and yoginīs

**Nāḍī function:** Through this nāḍī flows the constituent element of nails and teeth.  
**Yoginī designation:** Pracaṇḍā (Rab gtum ma) **Nāḍī name:** *Abhedyā* (mi phyed ma)
