IN THE TRADITIONAL ANNALS of Sanatana Dharma, **Akshaya-Tritya** is a highly auspicious festival signifying charity and philanthropy, which in turn will lead to increased all-round prosperity. The traditional method of celebrating *Akshaya-Tritiya* is by visiting temples, offering sugarcane, donating food and provisions and funding the education of poor people.

It is to this sacred and profound spirit that The Dharma Dispatch would like to make a heartfelt appeal: since its inception, we have envisioned our work as a form of continuing cultural education at all levels and all age groups.

And so, if you have found **inspiration, joy, and value** in the content published on The Dharma Dispatch, **please consider supporting it financially**.

We run this publication out of a sense of sacred civilisational duty to Sanatana Dharma and as a labour of love and commitment. A lot of effort goes on behind the scenes. It takes us hundreds of hours each month to research, travel, write, shoot, interview, edit, and it takes significant costs to sustain this publication.

Since our founding, we’ve made a conscious decision to *not* accept corporate funding or advertising so that our work remains independent *forever.* We would like to keep it that way so that no compromise comes in the way of our sustained fight against Asuric forces and for the restoration of Sanatana Dharma in troubled times.
