This is not their own history.

Till date, the largest volume of primary sources of the history of medieval Muslim rule in India are chronicles, inscriptions, and official records written by Muslim historians, record-keepers, and chroniclers.

## **A Fundamental Failure of Hindu History**

The repeated defeats of Hindu kings and the inevitable horrors that followed owes to two pre-eminent failings on the part of Hindus. Actually, these are two faces of the same coin.

In general, it is a failure on the part of the Hindu scholarly class to study the core doctrines of Islam and to study Muslim records of their own time —specifically, *how* a Muslim victory was described, *why* Hindu subjects were treated as *Zimmis* even in peacetime and *why* Jizya and pilgrimage tax was levied.

Perhaps the earliest, but hazy attempt in this direction was made by Vidyaranya Swami, the spiritual founder of the Vijayanagara Empire. Although he did not study the Islamic scripture, he intuitively knew that there was something extremely lethal in its core that motivated such horrific violence.

As a result of this failure to inspect the Islamic scripture, by the time of the monarchy of Devaraya II, we see him place the Quran on a pedestal equaling his own throne. About 120 years after his death, the magnificent Vijayanagara Empire was annihilated in the terminal Battle of Talikota which was won by the Bahamani confederacy in which the treachery of the Muslim contingent of the Vijayanagara army played a significant role.

Thus, such a study of primary Muslim chronicles is highly revealing, extremely disturbing, and profoundly sickening. We get a firsthand and detailed look at the kind of mindset that operates behind these chronicles. The scale and extent of the diabolism and depravity that characterises all such accounts lies in a realm lower than barbarism.

Which brings us to the charge often levelled against Hindus which until recently was accepted almost as a settled truth: that Hindus lacked a sense of history. Anybody acquainted with even the basic facts of the history of Sanatana culture and civilisation will immediately recognise this as an ignorant, sweeping generalisation or an agenda-driven drivel.

## **Hindus as the World’s Greatest History Writers**

In reality, Hindus are the only people who wrote prodigious epics and Puranas running into *lakhs of pages* in a highly sophisticated language like Sanskrit using ornate verses.

Most of these fall in the category of *Itihasa* (loosely, history). Should we seriously believe that such people were incapable of writing a straightforward, linear narrative as we understand the term history today?

This point becomes even more pronounced when we observe the mountainous corpus of *caritras* (literally, biography) authored in various Indian languages. Are we to believe that no chronicles and official records were maintained by mighty empires in the prolonged and rich history spanning hundreds of years?

The fact that even in the face of extant inscriptions, epigraphic, and literary evidence, which narrate lineages, battle descriptions, grants, charities, etc., the claim that Hindus have no sense of history is made should count as a spectacular con job.

THE WIDELY-CITED example of Kalhana, the 12^(th) century Kashmir historian (roughly, in the modern mould) reveals — or conceals — another important fact. Kalhana’s history writing didn’t materialize in thin air. He inherited, nurtured and enriched an ancient, existing *parampara* or tradition of such history writing similar to what Panini, Abhinavagupta, Kalidasa, Sudraka *et al,* did in their respective realms.

By ancestry, tradition, and basic cultural nature, history-writing is in the very DNA of Hindus. When the inhabitants of a famous village or town take pride in their *sthalapuranas*, they’re actually narrating history. It is easy, lazy and arrogant to dismiss these *sthalapuranas* as “local legend,” “folklore” and “fable,” a vile tactic that has been done with enormous success.

But there are two parts to this.

This dismissal is done because it doesn’t fit into “accepted” notions of what is regarded as “real” history — i.e., what is approved by the colonial West. When such discarding is done over a long enough period, we lose both the *sthalapurana* and the real history.

Secondly, the Sanatana method of narrating histories both in conception and practice is to regard it as a *value*, and not merely as fact. D.V. Gundappa puts this in a different but evocative fashion:

> **History, if it should serve its purpose of stirring emotion,
> instigating inquiry and directing thought, must first of all be
> exciting. Is it impossible to be both truthful and warm-hearted, both
> factual and moving? Are imagination and conscience necessarily enemies
> to each other? In reconciling them is the art of the true historian.**

> 

Thus, if the history of the Hoysala Empire should be immortalised, its greatest *celebutantes* must become personal to us, they must materialise before our vision in full splendour and dignity of character, they should touch our active consciousness and conscience and they should reveal their flaws as well.

We have lost the emotional content in our *sthalapuranas* and *caritras* and the “history” that has survived is inert, arid, and distant; they are also a mountain of lies and imbued with full of hatred for Hindus if you read the Marxist mythology of calumny.

## **Concealed Historical Realities**

It is equally true that Hindus largely did not write their side of the medieval Muslim period. This was for a straightforward reason, which Jadunath Sarkar provides with his typical unapologetic candour.

**…when a class of men is publicly depressed and harassed (as under Muslim rule) it merely contents itself with dragging on an animal existence. The Hindus could not be expected to produce the utmost of what they were capable. Amidst such social conditions, the human hand and the human mind cannot achieve their best; the human soul cannot soar to its highest pitch. The barrenness of the Hindu intellect is just one more bestowal of inheritance of Muslim rule in India.**

> 

The other related reason is the fact that every Muslim conquest was a war of total liquidation. We’re aware only of the more infamous instances of library burning and destruction of splendid centres of learning like Nalanda, Odantapuri, Vikramashila etc. The number of such centres easily run into thousands, large and small. Should we seriously argue that these centres didn’t contain any historical documents?

The tragic and cruel fact is that over the last 150 or more years, innumerable books of Indian “history” have been written precisely based on such spurious arguments.

Because most questions of history are also questions of comparison, this backdrop is essential to fully understand the innate nature and psyche of the medieval Muslim chroniclers of India.
