Vaṇśaṭhakōpadayā

![](https://vansatakopadaya.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/image-1.png)

![](https://vansatakopadaya.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/parasara-bhattar.jpg)

श्रीपराशरभट्टार्य: श्रीरङ्गेशपुरोहित: ।

श्रीवत्साङ्कसुत: श्रीमान् श्रेयसे मेऽस्तु भूयसे ॥

शुक्लाम्बरधरं विष्णुं शशिवर्णं चतुर्भुजम् ।

प्रसन्नवदनं ध्यायेत् सर्वविघ्नोपशान्तये ॥

वष॑ट् ते विष्णवा॒स आ कृ॑णोमि॒ तन्मे॑ जुषस्व शिपिविष्ट ह॒व्यम् ।

वर्ध॑न्तु त्वा सुष्टु॒तयो॒ गिरो॑ मे यू॒यं पा॑त स्व॒स्तिभि॒: सदा॑ नः ॥

### **Introduction:**

The viṣṇu sahasranāma (The Thousand Names of viṣṇu) is a seminal chapter of the anuśāsana parva within the *Mahābhārata*. Its preeminence is not merely traditional but is rooted in the foundational authority of the Vedas.

In his bhāṣya (commentary) on the sahasranāma, śrī ādiśaṅkara cites the ṛgveda (1.156.3) to demonstrate that the glorification of viṣṇu’s names is a prescribed path for attaining spiritual realization

> तमु॑ स्तोतारः पू॒र्व्यं यथा॑ वि॒द ऋ॒तस्य॒ गर्भं॑ ज॒नुषा॑ पिपर्तन ।
>
> आस्य॑ जा॒नन्तो॒ नाम॑ चिद्विवक्तन म॒हस्ते॑ विष्णो सुम॒तिं भ॑जामहे ॥(ṛgveda 1.156.3)

śrī ādiśaṅkara elucidates that the chanting of viṣṇu’s names is ordained for the attainment of saṃyak jñāna (perfect knowledge of Brahman). He explains:

> “..इत्यादि श्रुतिभिर्विष्णोर्नामसङ्कीर्तनं सम्यग्ज्ञानप्राप्तये विहितम् । तमेव स्तोतार:
> पुराणं यथाज्ञानेन सत्यस्य गर्भं जन्मसमाप्तिं कुरुत । जानन्त: आस्य विष्णो: नामापि आवदत
> अन्ये वदन्तु मा वा हे विष्णो ! वयं ते सुमतिं शोभनं मह: भजामहे । ”

> “The aforementioned Vedic revelation establishes that the
> nāmasaṅkīrtana of viṣṇu is the prescribed means for obtaining the
> perfect knowledge of the Absolute. Oh reciters of hymns! Realize the
> ancient Truth, the essence of all reality, and thereby terminate the
> cycle of birth and death. Acknowledge and recite the names of viṣṇu;
> regardless of whether others join in, Oh viṣṇu, we shall perpetually
> worship Your auspicious glory and benevolent nature.”

Śrī parāśarabhaṭṭa, in his commentary bhagavad guṇadarpaṇa, provides six foundational reasons why this particular hymn is considered the most excellent among all scriptures

> महाभारतसारत्वात् ऋषिभि: परिगानत: ।
>
> वेदाचार्यसमाहारत् भीष्मोत्कृष्टमतत्वत: ॥
>
> परिग्रहातिशयतो गीताद्यैकार्थ्यतश्च न: ।
>
> सहस्रनामाध्याय उपादेयतमो मत: ॥

### **The essence of mahābhārata:**

The *Mahābhārata* is revered as one of the two primary itihāsas and is celebrated across Vedic literature as the Fifth Veda. Its supreme status is not merely a matter of tradition but is explicitly confirmed by the purāṇas and grammatical principles.

The purāṇas consistently describe the *Mahābhārata* as a divine illumination derived from the very essence of the Vedas.

**vāyu and pādma purāṇa**: Both texts proclaim that the *Mahābhārata* is like the moon, churned from the vast ocean of the Vedas for the welfare of the world.

> “मतिमन्थानमाविध्य येनासौ श्रुतिसागरात् ।
>
> जगद्धिताय जनितो महाभारतचन्द्रमा: ॥”(vāyu purāṇa 1.1.44-45, pādma purāṇa
> 1.1.41)

**mārkaṇḍeya** **purāṇa:** It establishes the epic’s superiority through a series of profound analogies, comparing its rank among scriptures to viṣṇu’s rank among the gods.

> त्रिदशानां यथा विष्णुः द्विपदां ब्राह्मणो यथा ।
>
> भूषणानां च सर्वेषां यथा चूडामणिर्वरः ॥
>
> यथायुधानां कुलिशमिन्द्रियाणां यथा मनः ।
>
> तथेह सर्वशास्त्रणां महाभारतमुत्तमम् ॥ (mārkaṇḍeya purāṇa 1.1.4,5)

It further notes that the words of vyāsa have purified the earth, sweeping away crooked logic like a mountain stream.

**mātsya and viṣṇu purāṇas:** These texts emphasize that the author, kṛṣṇa dvaipāyana vyāsa, is an incarnation of Lord Narayana Himself.

The mātsya purāṇa states:

> यस्य द्वैपायनः पुत्रः स्वयं विष्णुरजायत ।
>
> प्रकाशो जनितो येन महाभारतचन्द्रमाः ।।(mātsya purāṇa 201.31,32)

The viṣṇu purāṇa too talks of the greatness of *Mahābhārata*:

> कृ‍ष्णं द्वैपायनं व्यासं विद्धि नारायणं प्रभुम् ।
>
> को ह्यन्यो भुवि मैत्रेय महाभारतकृद्भवेत् ।।(viṣṇu purāṇa 3.4.5)

Furthermore, the itihāsas are said to be superior over purāṇas by the following shloka and upanishad passage:

> इतिहासपुराणाभ्यां वेदं समुपबृंहयेत् ।
>
> बिभेत्यल्पश्रुताद्वेद: मामयं प्रतरिष्यति ॥(*Mahābhārata* Adi Parva 1.1.293),
>
> इतिहासपुराणं पंचमं वेदानां वेदं भगवोऽध्येमि(Chandogya Upanishad 7.1.2),

As per the rules of grammar अल्पाच्तरम्(aṣṭādhyāyī 2.2.34) – the word with lesser syllables must come 1st – hence here it should be purāṇetihāsau. However there is an apavāda to this general rule that those words which carry more importance would come 1st even if they have more syllables (अभ्यर्हितं च). Therefore itihāsas carry more weight than purāṇas from a grammatical perspective too.

Furthermore the purāṇas have mixed views due to the presence of triguṇas – sāttvika purāṇas carrying more weight than rājasa and tāmasa purāṇas.

> अग्नेश्शिवस्य माहात्म्यं तामसेषु प्रकीर्त्यते ।
>
> राजसेषु च माहात्म्यम् अधिकं ब्रह्मणो विदु: ॥
>
> यस्मिन् कल्पे तु यत् प्रोक्तं पुराणं ब्रह्मणा पुरा ।
>
> तस्य तस्य तु माहात्म्यं तत्स्वरूपेण वर्ण्यते ॥
>
> सात्त्विकेष्वथ कल्पेषु माहात्म्यं अधिकं हरे: ।
>
> तेष्वेव योगसंसिद्धा: गमिष्यन्ति परां गतिम् ॥ (mātsya purāṇa 290.14-17)

This is in accordance with the maitrāyaṇīya upaniṣat which declares that viṣṇu is the sāttvika form among the trimūrtis:

> प्रजापतिस्तस्य प्रोक्ता अग्र्यास्तनवो ब्रह्मा रुद्रो विष्णुरित्यथ  
> यो ह खलु वावास्य राजसोंऽशोऽसौ स योऽयं ब्रह्माथ यो ह  
> खलु वावास्य तामसोंऽशोऽसौ स योऽयं रुद्रोऽथ यो ह  
> खलु वावास्य सात्विकोंऽशोऽसौ स एव विष्णुः

It is said in the bhāradhvāja saṃhitā –

> नाधीयेत पुराणादीन् राजसांस्तामसांस्तथा ।
>
> अनीशानां परेशत्वं वृथा यत्रोपवर्ण्यते ॥

Unlike the purāṇas, which may present mixed views based on these qualities, the *Mahābhārata* is considered free of such limitations. This internal consistency and overarching authority place the epic—and specifically the viṣṇu sahasranāma found within its anuśāsana parva—on a vastly superior footing compared to any other hymn or scripture.

### **Being sung by** **ṛṣis:**

Bhīṣma himself later glorifies the viṣṇu sahasranāma as that which was sung by the ṛṣis such as nārada, śuka, śaunaka, sanaka – “ऋषिभि: परिगीतानि तानि वक्ष्यामि भूतये”

![](https://vansatakopadaya.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/image-4.png?w=799)

### **Having been authored by veda** **vyāsa:**

The viṣṇu sahasranāma has been sung by none other than veda vyāsa who is known as an incarnation of nArAyaNa in the famous verse:

> व्यासाय विष्णुरूपाय व्यासरूपाय विष्णवे ।
>
> नमो वै ब्रह्मनिधये वासिष्ठाय नमो नम: ॥

This veda vyāsa is none other than the son of parāśara – who is said to have gotten a boon from pulastya for knowing the tattva as it is. He is also the great grandson of vasiṣṭha who by a word of his ennobled viśvāmitra who was a kshatriya into a brahmarṣi.Finally he is the father of sage shuka – who gave to the world the nectar of bhāgavata purāṇa. All this is summarized in another shloka:

> व्यासं वसिष्ठनप्तारं शक्ते: पौत्रमकल्मषम् ।
>
> पराशरात्मजं वन्दे शुकतातं तपोनिधिम् ॥

![](https://vansatakopadaya.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/image-3.png?w=1024)

### **Being recited by** **bhīṣma** **himself:**

veda vyāsa had exhorted yudhiṣṭhira to learn all subtleties of dharma from bhīṣma himself. He tells:

> “यस्य ब्रह्मर्षय: पुण्या: नित्यमासन् सभासद: ।
>
> यस्य नाविदितं किञ्चित् ज्ञानं ज्ञेयेषु विद्यते ॥
>
> स ते वक्ष्यामि धर्मज्ञो धर्मान् सूक्ष्मार्थतत्त्ववित् ॥”(Mbh śāntiparva 12.35.15-16)

“In all assemblies where bhīṣma was present – there were always found to be present holy ṛṣis. There is nothing that has to be known which is not known to him. He, the great knower of dharmas and all subtle truths, will instruct you in all dharmas.”

Kṛṣṇa himself blessed bhīṣma who was distressed due to arrows that his mind will not be clouded and will be rooted in sattva – which is ideal for clarifying all the questions of yudhiṣṭhira without any bias.

> “यद्धि भूतं भविष्यच्च भवच्च भरतर्षभ ।
>
> सर्वं तत् ज्ञानवृद्धस्य तव पाणाविवाहितम् ॥
>
> अहं च त्वाऽभिजानामि स्वयं पुरुषसत्तम !
>
> तपसा हि भवान् शक्त: स्रष्टुं लोकांश्चराचरान् ॥(*Mahābhārata* śānti parva
> 12.49.27,30)
>
> न ते ग्लानिर्न ते मूर्छा..
>
> सत्त्वस्थं च मनो नित्यं तव भीष्म ! भविष्यति ।
>
> रजस्तमोभ्याम् अस्पृष्टं घनैर्मुक्त इवोडुराट् ॥” (Mbh śāntiparva 12.51.16-18).

yudhiṣṭhira furthermore opens up to such a bhīṣma in the sahasranAma adhyAya with generic questions without any reference to a particular deity thus showing true jijnAsa without a biased mind:

> “किमेकं दैवतं लोके किं वाप्येकं परायणं…”

This again makes the viṣṇu sahasranāma much different from sahasranāmas of other devatas which start directly with a specific question in reference to a deity. This shows their biased opinion and hence are not as authoritative as the former with regards to ultimate enquiry about the truth. For example, the adhyāyas in anuśāsana parva leading to śiva sahasranāma starts as follows:

> पितामह महेशाय नामान्याचक्ष्व शंभवे ।
>
> विदुषे विश्वमायाय महाभाग्यं च तत्त्वत: ॥(*Mahābhārata* 13.45.1)

The start itself is biased – here bhīṣma recuses himself out and asks kṛṣṇa to recite a story relating to the same.

A similar thing can be found in the sūrya sahasranāma of bhaviṣyat purāṇa:

> केन मन्त्रेण जप्तेन दर्शनं भगवान्व्रजेत् ।
>
> स्तोत्रेण वापि सविता तन्मे कथय सुव्रत ॥

The sūrya sahasranāma of skānda purāṇa too begins similarly:

> मण्डलस्य च विस्तारं नमस्कारविधिं ततः ।
>
> पूजाक्रमविशेषेण श्रुत्वा पुनरभाषत ।
>
> सर्यवर्चा उवाच –
>
> भगवन् सर्वतत्त्वज्ञ सर्वकर्मप्रवर्तक ।
>
> नाम्नां सहस्रं दिव्यानां श्रोतुमिच्छाम्यहं रवेः ॥

We can see that the work again starts with a specific question and gets a specific answer – unlike the questions posed before viṣṇu sahasranāma which are generic.

### **Universal acceptance of** **viṣṇu sahasranāma**:

The viṣṇu sahasranāma is famous and well known in ancient circles even in Ayurveda and among poets of yore apart from internal references in the *Mahābhārata*. That viṣṇu was unequivocally known as the one with 1000 names is known from the sabhA parvA:

> सहस्रबाहु: सर्वज्ञो देवो नामसहस्रवान् ।(sabhā parva – 2.43.11)

The charaka samhita of Ayurveda tells the solution for removing fevers:

> “विष्णुं सहस्रमूर्धानं चराचरपतिं विभुम् ।
>
> स्तुवन् नामसहस्रेण ज्वरान् सर्वान् व्यपोहति ॥”(caraka saṃhitā 3.311-312)

In case of being affected by maleficent planets also the recitation of viṣṇu sahasranāma is prescribed:

> आसत्मतं मरुत्सूक्तं विष्णोर्नामसहस्रकम्

In kādambarī, the poet bhaṭṭabāṇa describing the maternity ward mentions it as a place where the 1000 names are recited incessantly.

> “अविच्छिन्न पठ्यमान नामसहस्रम्”

śrī śaṅkara in his bhaja govindam recommends the singing of the 1000 names of viṣṇu. His views on the

> गेयं गीता नामसहस्रं ध्येयं श्रीपतिरूपमजस्रम्

Therefore we can see that people of diverse backgrounds have accepted the validity of the sahasranāma. This stands in stark contrast to other *sahasranāmas*, such as the one dedicated to Śiva within the *Mahābhārata*, whose authenticity within the *itihāsa* has been historically contested. Prominent ancient commentators, including Arjuna Miśra, have questioned its legitimacy citing its absence in numerous authoritative manuscripts. Furthermore, its exclusion from Vidyāraṇya’s *Bhāratasaṅgraha*—a definitive compendium of the epic’s core—suggests it was not universally recognized as part of the original tradition. In a similar vein, the *sahasranāmas* of other deities found across various *purāṇas* remain philologically doubtful, largely due to the frequent propensity for interpolation within these lesser-known recensions.

### **Conformity with the gītā and other scriptures**

Drawing from the foundational pillars of the *Mahābhārata*, it is evident that the Viṣṇu Sahasranāma serves as the crystalline essence of the entire epic. By incorporating the theological depth of the Nārāyaṇīya, the diplomatic majesty of the Bhagavad-Yāna, the ritualistic sovereignty of the Agrapūjā, and the ethical culmination of the Anuśāsana Parva—where it is declared the ‘Supreme Dharma’—this hymn transcends mere liturgy.

In alignment with the Lord’s own declarations in the Bhagavad Gita regarding the constancy of Kīrtana

> सततं कीर्तयन्तो मां यतन्तश्च दृढव्रता:(bhagavadgītā 9.14)

and the sanctity of Japa Yajña,

> यज्ञानां जपयज्ञोऽस्मि (bhagavadgītā 10.25)

the Viṣṇu Sahasranāma emerges as the most accessible and excellent scripture for the modern age. It stands as a universal spiritual legacy, open to all seekers regardless of caste, creed, or gender. As echoed in the ṛgveda—ध्रु॒वासो॑ अस्य की॒रयो॒ जना॑स:—those who anchor themselves in the chanting of His glories attain eternal immutability. Thus, its daily recitation is not merely a practice, but the ultimate spiritual safeguard for all humanity.
