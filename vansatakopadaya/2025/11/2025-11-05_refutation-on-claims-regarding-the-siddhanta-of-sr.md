Of late, certain individuals with vested interests have been targeting the works of revered **pūrvācāryas** like śrīrāmānuja and Madhva. Among their claims are that vaikuṇṭha is not an eternal abode, that it is situated within Satya Loka, and that Veda Vyasa refuted the pañcarātra tradition explicitly.

With due respect to the opponent, we start by pointing out some glaring errors in the opponent’s arguments.It is clear from the outset that the respected person making these claims has a poor understanding of śrīrāmānuja’s **siddhānta**, beginning with the fundamental error of claiming that the **śrībhāṣya** is a commentary on the bhagavad gītā. While we generally refrain from engaging in inter-philosophical debates, the increasing traction of these narratives obliges us to provide a clarification and refutation.

### The pañcarātra Tradition: A Vedic Authority

The speaker begins with a significant blunder by claiming that Veda Vyasa mentions “pañcarātra” by name in the **Brahmasūtras**. This alone exposes a lack of familiarity with even śaṅkāra’s commentary. The relevant **sūtra** in the **pañcarātra-adhikaraṇa** are:

> – उत्पत्त्यसंभवात् (2.2.42)
>
> – न च कर्तु: करणम् (2.2.43)

The speaker confuses the **vyākhyāna** (commentary) with the actual **sūtra**, revealing his level of scholarship.

Regarding the claim that **pañcarātra** is non-Vedic, even teachers from his own philosophical tradition have declared in their works that it is trustworthy, as it was revealed by the Lord Himself. Amalānanda, in his commentary, states:

> “तत्र – ईशोक्तं न पुराणेषु व्यामोहार्थमितीरितम् । पञ्चरात्रमतो जीवो विकार इति
> मीयते ॥”
>
> “पञ्चरात्रकर्तुर्वासुदेवस्य वेदादेव सर्वज्ञत्वावगमात् कपिलपतञ्जल्यादीनां च जीवत्वात्,
> पञ्चरात्रस्य च पुराणेषु बुद्धादिदर्शनवत् व्यामोहार्थमीश्वरप्रणीतत्वाश्रवणात्”

He further explains that the **jīvotpatti** (creation of the individual soul) mentioned in pañcarātra is to be taken figuratively:

> “बुद्धिपूर्वकृतिस्तन्त्रं ब्रह्मनि:श्वसितं श्रुति:। तेन जीवजनिस्तत्र सिद्धा गौणी नियम्यते
> ॥”

### The Eternal Nature of vaikuṇṭha

Now, for the claims regarding vaikuṇṭha, let us consider the **kauṣītakī brāhmaṇa**, which describes the path of the liberated soul:

> स एतं देवयानं पन्थानमासाद्याग्निलोकमागच्छति स वायुलोकं स वरुणलोकं स आदित्यलोकं स
> इन्द्रलोकं स प्रजापतिलोकं स ब्रह्मलोकं…

Having passed through the path of light (**archirādi**), the **jīva** attains the worlds of agni, vāyu, indra and prajāpati. After this, it goes to **Brahmaloka**. Note that a **Brahmaloka** is mentioned above **prajāpati loka**. That **Brahmaloka** is none other than the **paramapada** of viṣṇu, as proclaimed in the mahābhārata’s Vanaparva.

The same **shruti** also mentions freedom from birth and death for those who meditate on the **aṣṭākṣara**:

> यो ह वै नारायणस्याष्टाक्षरं पदमध्येति । अनपब्रुवस्सर्वमायुरेति । विन्दते प्राजापत्यꣳ
> रायस्पोषं गौपत्यम् । ततोऽमृतत्वमश्नुते ततोऽमृतत्वमश्नुत इति । य एवं वेद ॥

And finally, it identifies the destination of muktas freed from saṃsāra as vaikuṇṭha:

> यमुक्त्वा मुच्यते योगी जन्मसंसारबन्धनात् …वैकुण्ठभुवनलोकं गमिष्यति ।
>
> तदिदं परं पुण्डरीकं विज्ञानघनम् । तस्मात्तटिदाभमात्रम् ।

Next we look at a set of mahābhārata verses. This comes in the context of a **devadūta** describing to mudgala the various **lokas**. Just before this, the **devadūta** explicitly declares that all **lokas** from the abode of downwards are filled with flaws:

mahābhārata , Vanaparva (3.262.33):

> आब्रह्मभवनादेते दोषा मौद्गल्य दारुणाः। नाकलोकेसुकृतिनां गुणास्त्वयुतशो नृणाम् ॥

When Mudgala asks which place is free from these flaws, the **devadūta** answers that it is the loka above that of Chaturmukha brahmā:

mahābhārata , Vanaparva (3.262.37):

> महान्तस्तु अमी दोषास्त्वया स्वर्गस्य कीर्तिताः। निर्दोष एव यस्त्यन्यो लोकं तं प्रवदस्व
> मे ॥

The **devaduta** replies:

mahābhārata , Vanaparva (3.262.38-40):

> ब्रह्मणः सदनादूर्ध्वं तद्विष्णोः परमं पदम्। शुद्धं सनातनं ज्योतिः परं ब्रह्मेति यद्विदुः ॥
> (38)
>
> न तत्र विप्र गच्छन्ति पुरुषा विषयात्मकाः। दम्भलोभमहाक्रोधमोहद्रोहैरभिद्रुताः ॥ (39)
>
> निर्ममा निरहंकारा निर्द्विन्द्वाः संयतेन्द्रियाः। ध्यानयोगपराश्चैव तत्र गच्छन्ति
> मानवाः ॥ (40)

“Above the abode of Brahmā is the supreme place of viṣṇu, pure, eternal, and effulgent, which is known as **Parabrahman**. People who engage in carnal pleasures, overcome by hypocrisy, greed, great anger, delusion, and malice, do not go there. Only those without feelings of ‘I’ or ‘mine,’ who have controlled their sense organs and are ever engaged in meditation, reach this place.”

Elsewhere in the same Vanaparva(3.164.17,18,23,24,25) the same is mentioned.

This is a direct supporting statement for the Bhagavad Gita verse (**ābrahma-bhuvanāl lokāḥ…**) and demonstrates the existence of an eternal **paramapadam** of viṣṇu above Brahma’s residence.

That **Brahmaloka** can refer to vaikuṇṭha is accepted by Advaitic scholars as well, such as Māheshvara Tīrtha. While commenting on a verse in bālakāṇḍa of rāmāyaṇa:

> दशवर्षसहस्राणि दशवर्षशतानि च । रामो राज्यमुपासित्वा ब्रह्मलोकं प्रयास्यति॥

“Brahmaloka itself is the abode of the qualified Brahman, viṣṇu, known as vaikuṇṭha.”

Elsewhere while explaining a verse in ayodhyākāṇḍa, the commentator explicitly identifies the residence of viṣṇu with the shruti referring to ayodhyā. This points out to the supreme abode being called by multiple names.

The absence of any of one of these names in a text cannot mean total non-existence of the same. It is clear that this is the supreme abode of viṣṇu , referred to as the goal of knowers of Brahman, as per the Rig Vedic verse: **तद्विष्णो: परमं पदं सदा पश्यन्ति सूरय:**.

> ।। (4)
>
> मरुतां विश्वदेवानां साध्यानामश्विनोस्तथा । रुद्रादित्यवसूनां च तथान्येषां दिवौकसाम् ।।
> (5)
>
> एते वै निरयास्तात स्थानस्य परमात्मनः । आत्मा केवलतां प्राप्तस्तत्र गत्वा न शोचति।।
> (6)

“All these divine abodes of the immortal devas, with their celestial vehicles, assemblies, various pleasure gardens, and lotus ponds—belonging to the lokapālas, śukra, bṛhaspati,maruts,viśvedevas,sādhya, aśvins,rudras,ādityas and vasus—are, in fact, like places of deprivation (**niraya**) compared to the abode of the Supreme Self. The soul, having attained oneness, goes there and does not grieve.”

The above cannot refer to nirguna brahmaikya for the obvious reason that movement is referred to in the final shloka.

### **Conclusion**

All these scriptural evidences—from the **Brahmasūtras**, the mahābhārata , the viṣṇu Purana, and the **shruti**—conclusively prove the eternal and supreme nature of viṣṇu’s abode, vaikuṇṭha, and the authoritative status of the pañcarātra tradition. The claims made by the speaker are based on a superficial and fragmented understanding of these texts and are soundly refuted by a comprehensive and contextual study of our sacred literature.The claims regarding equality or sameness of all devas and the issue of rāma worshipping in rāmeśvaram will be examined in the next part.
