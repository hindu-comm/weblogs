I have something a little different for you today, something a little experimental. Google recently released its Artificial Intelligence Podcast Generator tool: [NotebookLM](https://lifehacker.com/tech/use-google-notebooklm-ai-to-turn-your-research-into-a-podcast#:~:text=NotebookLM%20from%20Google%20can%20use%20AI). Using the tool you can feed the AI different pieces of text and it will give you a natural sounding podcast discussion. Cleverly it distills down the text and is able to not only pick out the overall themes and wider questions, but also to bring in additional details and background context not found in the articles. It is still a work in progress and the analysis is a little shallow, but I would be curious to hear what you think?

I used four recent articles from September:

-   [Scottish Altar Stone at
    Stonehenge?](https://www.stoneageherbalist.com/p/the-scottish-altar-of-stonehenge)

-   [Ancient Contact Between Easter Island and South
    America?](https://www.stoneageherbalist.com/p/ancient-contact-between-easter-island)

-   [Deeply Isolated Neanderthal
    Groups?](https://www.stoneageherbalist.com/p/deeply-isolated-neanderthal-groups)

-   [Renaissance Poet Buried in
    Notre-Dame?](https://www.stoneageherbalist.com/p/renaissance-poet-discovered-in-notre)
