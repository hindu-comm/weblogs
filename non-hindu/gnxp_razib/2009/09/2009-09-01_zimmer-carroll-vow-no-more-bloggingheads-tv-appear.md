Zimmer & Carroll vow no more bloggingheads.tv appearances

Carl Zimmer is rather mild-mannered, but has expressed rather [strong sentiments](http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/loom/2009/09/01/bloggingheads-and-the-old-challenges-of-new-tools/) about what recently happened on bloggingheads.tv. Sean Carroll, not surprisingly, has [stronger opinions](http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/cosmicvariance/2009/08/31/bye-to-bloggingheads/). But they’re now both proactively dissociating themselves from bloggingheads.tv. The [McWhorter & Behe](http://bloggingheads.tv/diavlogs/22075) discussion is now back online. The issue is really simple. John McWhorter played up Michael Behe’s ideas as awesome, mind-blowing and revolutionary for an hour. Most scientists don’t consider Behe’s ideas controversial, they consider them crankery.
