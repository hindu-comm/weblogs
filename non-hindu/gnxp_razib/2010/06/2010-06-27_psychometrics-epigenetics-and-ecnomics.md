Psychometrics, epigenetics and economics

Two papers of interest. [IQ in the Production Function: Evidence from Immigrant Earnings](http://www3.interscience.wiley.com/journal/121684849/abstract?CRETRY=1&SRETRY=0) ([ungated](http://mason.gmu.edu/~gjonesb/Immigrant%20IQ)). And [Human Intelligence and Polymorphisms in the DNA Methyltransferase Genes Involved in Epigenetic Marking](http://www.plosone.org/article/info:doi/10.1371/journal.pone.0011329). My impression is that the focus on epigenetics has a higher-order social motive; even the sort of humanists who are involved with *N + 1* have asked me about the topic. But how many people know what [methylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylation#Epigenetics) is?
