Party like a Persian

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.gnxp.com/wp/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/persian_version.png?resize=202%2C152 "persian_version")![](https://i0.wp.com/www.gnxp.com/wp/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/persian_version.png?resize=202%2C152 "persian_version")](https://i0.wp.com/www.gnxp.com/wp/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/persian_version.png)[MTV Not Involved With ‘Jersey Shore’ Imitations](http://www.nytimes.com/2010/04/19/business/media/19shore.html?hp):

> For the show he calls the “Persian Version,” his casting company > wrote: “If you are at least 21 years old, appear younger than 30, and > are outrageous, outspoken and a proud Persian-American, then Doron > Ofir Casting and 495 Productions, the team who brought you ‘Jersey > Shore,’ are looking for you!”

On Jersey Shore all the cast members were at least nominal Catholics (though not all were Italian American), so there was a unanimity of religious presuppositions which stood in sharp contrast to the forced pluralism evident in *The Real World* series. This is going to be difficult for Persian Version if they get a representative group of [Iranian Americans](https://www.gnxp.com/wp/religion/only-a-minority-of-iranian-americans-are-muslims). Rather, perhaps they can appeal to a common shared value of the need for [Latoya Jackson face](http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/2008/04/nose-jobs-in-iran/) (the one [Nowruz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nowruz) I’ve attended had a lot of that).
