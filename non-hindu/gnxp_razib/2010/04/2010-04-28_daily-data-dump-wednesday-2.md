Daily Data Dump (Wednesday)

[Adaptation, Plasticity, and Extinction in a Changing Environment: Towards a Predictive Theory](http://www.plosbiology.org/article/info:doi/10.1371/journal.pbio.1000357). It’s beneficial to have a little give.

[Enculturation and Wall Street](http://scienceblogs.com/cortex/2010/04/enculturation_and_wall_street.php). Rich, irrational herds? I think you can’t ignore the [Magnetar strategy either](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetar_Capital#ProPublica_.2F_NPR_.2F_This_American_Life_report). What may be rational for a firm or individual may result in the shrinking of the aggregate pie.

[Peppers May Increase Energy Expenditure in People Trying to Lose Weight](http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2010/04/100427190934.htm). No wonder so many people who like bland food are fat.

[Y chromosomes of Northwest China](https://dienekes.blogspot.com/2010/04/y-chromosomes-of-northwest-china.html). No surprise if you’ve read [*Empires of the Silk Road*](https://www.gnxp.com/blog/2009/09/whos-barbarian-now-empires-of-silk-road.php).

[Crossing the Wallace Line](http://agro.biodiver.se/2010/04/crossing-the-wallace-line/). Patterns across species.
