Ant fiction

[Steve](https://isteve.blogspot.com/2010/01/save-ants.html) points me to an except from E. O. Wilson’s new [ant novel](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0393071197/geneexpressio-20/) in [The New Yorker](http://www.newyorker.com/fiction/features/2010/01/25/100125fi_fiction_wilson?printable=true). In the late 1990s I read [Empire of the Ants](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0553573527/geneexpressio-20/), which had a significant ant-centric aspect. A friend who later went on to do graduate work in entemology borrowed it from me, and she must have liked it because I never did get it back. I have a feeling that Wilson’s [Anthill](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0393071197/geneexpressio-20/) isn’t going to become a classic like [Watership Down](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0743277708/geneexpressio-20/), but I’d be happy to be proven wrong….
