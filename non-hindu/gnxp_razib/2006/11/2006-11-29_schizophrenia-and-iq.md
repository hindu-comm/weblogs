Schizophrenia and IQ

[](https://www.gnxp.com/blog/uploaded_images/schizophrenia-736593.jpg)  
Low IQ is a risk factor [for developing schizophrenia](http://journals.cambridge.org/action/displayAbstract?fromPage=online&aid=25559), though the mechanism behind this association is somewhat unclear. A [new study](http://www.nature.com/neuro/journal/v9/n12/abs/nn1795.html) sheds a little light on this subject, and suggests the link might be genetic. The gene in questions is neuregulin 1, about which little is known. They find, first, that a regulatory SNP is associated with the development of psychotic symptoms in a particularly at-risk population (see part a aboveâ€“ each bar is the percentage of subjects developing symptoms for a given genotype). They also find lower levels of activity in certain part of the brain in the patients with the TT genotype (see parts b and c above).

Further, here are the means and standard deviations of the IQ distributions of the different genotypes:

CC: 101.9 (8.4)  
CT: 100.4 (9.4)  
TT: 94.3 (6.9)

So this regulatory polymorphism could explain some of the natural variation in IQ.
