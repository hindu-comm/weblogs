Imprinting articles

The [March 2006 issue of Cytogenetic and Genome Research](https://content.karger.com/ProdukteDB/produkte.asp?Aktion=Ausgabe&ProduktNr=224037&Ausgabe=231627) was entirely dedicated to genomic imprinting. I know [some of y’all cats](https://www.gnxp.com/blog/2006/06/autism-and-imprinted-brain.php) are [into this business](http://scienceblogs.com/gnxp/2006/05/kinship_theory_and_genomic_imp.php). There’s one by [Haig](https://www.gnxp.com/blog/2006/06/10-questions-for-david-haig.php) entitled [Intragenomic Politics](https://content.karger.com/ProdukteDB/produkte.asp?Aktion=ShowAbstract&ProduktNr=224037&Ausgabe=231627&ArtikelNr=90816), and several on regulatory mechanisms which are more my speed, including one about [small non-coding RNAs](https://content.karger.com/ProdukteDB/produkte.asp?Aktion=ShowAbstract&ProduktNr=224037&Ausgabe=231627&ArtikelNr=90820).

Of course, the articles aren’t free, but perhaps arrangements could be made for the really important ones.
