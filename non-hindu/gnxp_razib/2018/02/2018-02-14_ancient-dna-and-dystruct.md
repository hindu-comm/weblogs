Ancient DNA and Dystruct

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.gnxp.com/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/dystruct_ad.jpg?resize=625%2C547&ssl=1)![](https://i0.wp.com/www.gnxp.com/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/dystruct_ad.jpg?resize=625%2C547&ssl=1)](https://i0.wp.com/www.gnxp.com/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/dystruct_admixture.png?ssl=1)  
There’s a new preprint, [Inference of population structure from ancient DNA](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/early/2018/02/07/261131), which uses explicit demographic models to make inferences about ancestry. I haven’t dug into the guts of the math, but, the outputs are quite interesting.

**What seems to be obvious is that Western Eurasia has a much richer set of models to choose from than elsewhere.** European, Middle Eastern and South Asian populations exhibit the greatest difference between Dystruct and Admixture.
