The dead hand of the law

Want to make analytic philosophy papers the exemplars of lively and clear prose? Just read some articles from [The Harvard Law Review](http://www.harvardlawreview.org/recentissues.shtml). My own personal experience with lawyers is that most of them know the law as well as a heating & cooling engineer knows the temperature systems of the typical modern building. That being said, heating & cooling engineers don’t presume to have insights into the human condition, while many smart lawyers seem to think they do have such general knowledge. Lawyers are the potentates of process, and I give them their due, but they should leave conversations of substance to those who actually know facts which see the light of day outside of the pages of briefs.
