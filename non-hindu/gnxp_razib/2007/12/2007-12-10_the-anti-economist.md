The anti-economist

Reading [The Early Chinese Empires: Qin and Han](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/067402477X/geneexpressio-20), and I was struck by passages extracted from [The Book of Lord Shang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Book_of_Lord_Shang), a work which supposedly encapsulates the thought of the [Legalist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legalism_%28Chinese_philosophy%29) philosopher & statesmen [Shang Yang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shang_Yang). In short, Shang Yang extols the virtues of the [Malthusian Trap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malthusian_catastrophe) in perpetuating a stable & well ordered state! He believes that surplus only encourages mischief and social disorder, and any excess production should be burned away by wars of attrition so that the population is driven back to the margins of subsistence. Any surprise that Legalism was anathema for most of Chinese history despite the reality that the [Qin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_Dynasty) state and its ruling philosophy had a significant practical impact upon how the Imperial system was structured?
