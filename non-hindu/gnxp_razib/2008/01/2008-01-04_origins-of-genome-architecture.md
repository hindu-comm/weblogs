Origins of genome architecture???

There’s been some talk about the dynamics of genome architecture on the comments, so I thought I’d point to Mike Lynch’s [The origins of eukaryotic gene structure](http://mbe.oxfordjournals.org/cgi/content/full/23/2/450) (Open Access). The paper is a reduction of the major themes presented in the book of the [similar name](https://www.gnxp.com/blog/2007/11/origins-of-genome-architecture.php). This is of course only Lynch’s view; but by making a positive argument he sketches out implicitly objections and counterpoints to his contentions. You can find many more of his [papers](http://www.indiana.edu/%7Elynchlab/LynchPublications.htm) on his [website](http://www.indiana.edu/%7Elynchlab/LynchPublications.htm); his ideas on genome architecture go way back (at least in terms of the “post-genomic era”).
