The spread of agriculture, part n

If agriculture, and the social and cultural revolutions triggered by this new form of extracting economic productivity from land, was a major variable in triggering [recent human evolution](http://scienceblogs.com/gnxp/2007/09/important_papers_on_recent_hum.php) it is important to know when it swept over a particular region. With the big ranges given for when selection pressures began to reshape a genomic region the narrower values from archaeology might be very useful. So here’s another map of the spread of agriculture in Europe….

[Dienekes](http://dienekes.50webs.com/blog/archives/000411.html) has an [old post](http://dienekes.50webs.com/blog/archives/000411.html) with similar numbers. The recent [results](http://www.sciencemag.org/cgi/content/summary/316/5823/364a) which imply that *SLC24A5* (responsible for 1/3 of the European vs. African skin color difference) might have only started rising in frequency 6-12 thousand years ago would be constrained if we are to assume that changes triggered by agriculture were necessary; as farming only spread to northern Europe around 7,000 years ago.

([source](http://www.nerc.ac.uk/publications/planetearth/2007/winter/win07-asia.pdf))
