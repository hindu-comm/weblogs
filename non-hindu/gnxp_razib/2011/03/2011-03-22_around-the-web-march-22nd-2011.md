Around the Web – March 22nd, 2011

[Monuments to Clan Life Are Losing Their Appeal](http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/23/world/asia/23yongding.html?_r=1&partner=rss&emc=rss). A rule of thumb is that the Chinese tend to emphasize permanent architecture less than other societies, probably due to the tendency not to use durable materials.

[The Next Bubble: Farmland](http://www.slate.com/id/2289095/). Did not know: “And large-scale farmland bubbles are quite rare: There was only one in the United States in the entire 20th century, during the great population scare of the 1970s.”

[Tortoise and Hare, in a Laboratory Flask](http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/22/science/22evolve.html?pagewanted=print). Carl on the [new paper](http://www.sciencemag.org/content/331/6023/1433.abstract) out of the Lenski lab.

[Unknown Animals Nearly Invisible Yet There](http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2011/03/110322105740.htm?utm_source=feedburner&utm_medium=feed&utm_campaign=Feed%3A+sciencedaily+%28ScienceDaily%3A+Latest+Science+News%29). There aren’t enough labor hours to catalog the tree of life.

[A Proud ‘Lobbyist’ and ‘Southerner’ Weighs ‘President’](http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/22/us/politics/22barbour.html). We haven’t had a fat president in nearly 100 years.

  
[Europe’s Rift Over Energy Is Widened by France](http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/22/world/europe/22iht-energy22.html?partner=rss&emc=rss). Go France!

[Less Wrong NYC: Case Study of a Successful Rationalist Chapter](http://lesswrong.com/lw/4ul/less_wrong_nyc_case_study_of_a_successful/). Brass tacks: what’s the rate of drop of proportion of virgins over the course of the past year?

[Law of Averages](http://www.slate.com/id/2288751/). Finally, people are responding to the disincentives of low-tier law school attendance!

[Vaccine to Cure Asthma Brought on by House Dust Mite Allergies?](http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2011/03/110321134500.htm?utm_source=feedburner&utm_medium=feed&utm_campaign=Feed%3A+sciencedaily+%28ScienceDaily%3A+Latest+Science+News%29)

[Canine Genetic Wrinkle Has Human Potential](http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/22/science/22obdog.html?hpw=&pagewanted=print). Dog genetics yields important insights for humans. Not *too* surprising. Dogs and humans exhibit a lot of convergences from different sections of the mammalian bush.

[The skull that proves those New Stone Age farmers weren’t so peaceful after all…](http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-1364489/85-New-Stone-Age-skulls-head-injuries-Orkneys-Tomb-Eagles.html?ITO=1490) My null model is that intra-specific competition results in violence once at the Malthusian limit.

[Economics and Evolution Help Scientists Identify New Strategy to Control Antibiotic Resistance](http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2011/03/110318153325.htm?utm_source=feedburner&utm_medium=feed&utm_campaign=Feed%3A+sciencedaily+%28ScienceDaily%3A+Latest+Science+News%29). This is the sort of thing that’s potentially the most potent antidote to Creationism.

[Sizing Up Kinship: Larger Groups Win](http://genealogyreligion.net/sizing-up-kinship-larger-groups-win). Review of the [Kim Hill](http://www.sciencemag.org/content/331/6022/1286.abstract?sid=35ef3a7e-956c-4615-87ee-60985cabb83b) paper.

[Hunter-gatherer kinship and band composition](http://johnhawks.net/weblog/topics/population_structure/kin-co-residence-hill-2011.html). John Hawks on the Kim Hill paper.

[Population-Based Resequencing of Experimentally Evolved Populations Reveals the Genetic Basis of Body Size Variation in Drosophila melanogaster](http://www.plosgenetics.org/article/info%3Adoi%2F10.1371%2Fjournal.pgen.1001336?utm_source=feedburner&utm_medium=feed&utm_campaign=Feed%3A+plosgenetics%2FNewArticles+%28Ambra+-+Genetics+New+Articles%29).

[A Molecular Phylogeny of Living Primates](http://www.plosgenetics.org/article/info%3Adoi%2F10.1371%2Fjournal.pgen.1001342?utm_source=feedburner&utm_medium=feed&utm_campaign=Feed%3A+plosgenetics%2FNewArticles+%28Ambra+-+Genetics+New+Articles%29).

[A Faded Industrial Town Is Feeling Britain’s Cuts](http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/18/business/global/18welfare.html). Half the workers in the public sector? This is not a sustainable ecosystem.

[Public Jobs as the Ticket to Middle Class Status](http://www.nationalreview.com/agenda/262320/public-jobs-ticket-middle-class-status-reihan-salam). Reihan Salam offers some skepticism.

[Molecular Evolution, Mutation Size and Gene Pleiotropy: A Geometric Reexamination](http://www.genetics.org/cgi/content/short/187/3/877?rss=1).

[Return of the Sailer Strategy](http://theamericanscene.com/2011/03/16/return-of-the-sailer-strategy). I’m feelin’ more and more right-wing of late. But no pinker.

[Wildlife defies Chernobyl radiation](http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4923342.stm). Similar to the phenomenon of wildlife refuges created by land mines.
