Cousin marriage can reduce I.Q. a lot

In light of [the previous post](http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/2012/07/inbred-shorter-people/) I was curious about the literature on inbreeding depression of IQ. A literature search led me to conclude two things:

– This is not a sexy field. A lot of the results are *old*.

– The range in depression for first cousin marriages seems to be on the order of 2.5 to 10 IQ points. In other words \~0.15 to \~0.65 standard deviation units of decline in intelligence.

The most extreme case was this paper from 1993, [Inbreeding depression and intelligence quotient among north Indian children](http://www.springerlink.com/content/f608480457n22667/). The authors compared the children of first cousin marriages, and non-bred in individuals, from a sample of Muslims in Uttar Pradesh of comparable socioeconomic status (though the authors note that inbreeding has a positive correlation with socioeconomic status in this community).
