Continuing the search for Indo-Europeans

[Dienekes P.](https://dienekes.blogspot.com/) is often rather laconic in commentary on the papers he links to, but of late he has “come out of his shell.” He has two posts which are important “weekend reading”:

– [Population strata in the West Siberian plain (Baraba forest steppe)](https://dienekes.blogspot.com/2012/07/population-strata-in-west-siberian.html)

– [Hints of East/Central Asian admixture in Northern Europe](https://dienekes.blogspot.com/2012/07/hints-of-eastcentral-asian-admixture-in.html)

I freely admit that much of the conjecture here is above my pay-grade in terms of evaluation. But I do think it’s important think through. My “gut” tends to lean toward a “revenge of the Mesolithic” scenario promoted by some of Dienekes’ critics, but I don’t have a strong position.
