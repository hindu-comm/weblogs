The Hobbit, part 1

I went and saw [The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0903624/) yesterday with some friends. It’s been 20 years since I last *read* [The Hobbit](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B0079KT81G/geneexpressio-20), and even longer since I watched[the television film](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2dQ5c5SIYnc) from the late 1970s. So I really didn’t notice all the [differences](http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2012/12/14/the-hobbit-19-changes-from-j-r-r-tolkien-s-novel-to-peter-jackson-s-movie.html) between the three hour film and the original novel. Two quick comments:

1\) I didn’t pick up on all the big technological changes. I suspect this is something movie reviewers are going to focus on, because they have such a good grasp of the technical element. But for the average person it’s not as obvious. Some of the 3D was well done, but much of it was a little excessive for me.

2\) I wasn’t too bored, but a two hour film would have been more than sufficient. Someone behind me literally fell asleep, judging by the persistent snoring.

I’d give the film a B-. This wasn’t in [Jar Jar Binks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jar_Jar_Binks) territory.
