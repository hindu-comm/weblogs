Haldane on the future, and perversion

[Cosma](http://cscs.umich.edu/%7Ecrshalizi/weblog/) has transcriped this [old 1923 paper](http://cscs.umich.edu/%7Ecrshalizi/Daedalus.html) by J.B.S. Haldane on science and the future, with a biofocus. Haldane really is way too optimistic about the progress of biological engineering, for example, he says “It was in 1951 that Dupont and Schwarz produced the first ectogenetic \[outside the womb\] child.” But the essay is worth this great line: “The biological invention then tends to begin as a perversion and end as a ritual supported by unquestioned beliefs and prejudices.”
