Neutral diffusion on HIV

[The Case for Selection at CCR5-Î”32](http://biology.plosjournals.org/eor/10.1371_journal.pbio.0030378-S.pdf) isn’t as strong as some would have you believe according to this [article](http://biology.plosjournals.org/eor/10.1371_journal.pbio.0030378-S.pdf). They don’t rebut that selection could have resulted in the spread of this allele, but they contend that a [neutral](http://www.biochem.northwestern.edu/holmgren/Glossary/Definitions/Def-N/neutral_gene_hypothesis.html) hypothesis can not be rejected by the data on hand.
