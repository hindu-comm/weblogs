Gay men's maps

According to news reports today, e.g. [here](http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/main.jhtml?xml=/news/2005/02/25/nmap25.xml&sSheet=/news/2005/02/25/ixhome.html), gay men read maps in a way more like women than men. For example, they make more use of landmarks and less of compass directions.

I have no idea if this is a sound finding. The research was carried out at the University of East London, which is not – how shall I put this – a world centre of academic excellence. But it should be easy enough to replicate it if it is true.
