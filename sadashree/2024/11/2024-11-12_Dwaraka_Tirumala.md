Dwaraka Tirumala often known as Chinna Tirupathi, is the next most prominent Venkateswara temple in Andhra Pradesh, after Tirumala. The Venkateswara Swamy Temple here is located on the Sheshadri Hill. As per the Sthala Purana, the temple dates back to the time of Dasharatha, the father of Shri Rama, and gets itâ€™s name from Dwaraka, a Rishi who did severe penance to Vishnu, covered with an ant hill, to be blessed by worshipping at the feet of Vishnu.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2f42b4bd-12ed-4674-be22-c7a92b93a74d_800x958.jpeg "undefined")

</div>

For devotees who could not visit Tirumala, going to this temple is an equivalent. As per the Brahma Purana, Aja the grandfather of Rama, is believed to have worshipped Venkateswara during his wedding. Apparently on the way to the Swayamvar of Indumati, he had passed by this temple, but had not worshipped the Lord, in his haste. While he managed to win the hand of Indumati, he had to fight an intense battle with other suitors at the Swayamvar. Realizing that the conflict was due to him forgetting to worship Shri Venkateswara, he proceeded to the temple, and did the Puja there.

One of the features of this temple is to see two main deities under one Vimana Sikharam. One of the deities is a Swayambhu believed to have been discovered by Dwaraka Rishi himself, and shows only the upper part, with his feet hidden.

However other saints felt that the worship of Shri Venkateswara, would not be complete without doing the seva to his feet, and hence installed a full murthi of the Lord behind the first one. Itâ€™s believed that the smaller deity represents Moksha, while the larger one represents Dharma, Artha and Kama. The Kalyanotsavam is celebrated twice a year here, for the Swayambhu deity, its in the month of Vaishaka, while for the larger deity itâ€™s in the month of Aswayuja.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc1bbc1a2-d503-4498-908d-2568333e56e7_260x351.jpeg)

</div>

Facing the sanctum sanctorum on both sides of the main entrance, are two statues of Dwaraka Rishi and Annamayya, while on the top of the main door, you find sculptures of the Saptarishis. Around the wall of the sanctum sanctorum, you have the sculptures of the 12 Alwars carved into it, and there is a Deeparadhana Mandap in the Pradakshina path. There are smaller shrines for Anjaneya and Garuda in the temple complex behind the Dwajastambham.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2436a04c-45db-41ef-b8e9-bc381425d4b0_968x648.jpeg)

</div>

Four gali gopurams flank the temple in 4 directions, the Southern gali gopuram is the tallest of the lot, with 5 stories. The hill known as Sheshadri is shaped in the form of a huge snake, itâ€™s believed that a Naga ruler Ananta, carried Mallikarjuna Swamy to the top of the hill, and Shri Venkateswara is located on his tail.

Most of the current temple was built between 1762-1827 by the local chieftain Dharma Appa Rao, while the golden jewelry, silver vahanas were donated by Rani Chinamma of Mylavaram royal estate. There are steps leading up the hill to the temple, which have the Dasavataram sculptures flanking them. The Sudarshana Pushkarini is located in the Southern end, that has two bathing ghats Chakra Theertham, Snana Theertham.

</div>

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5f011cb-954e-46c2-9970-4fe578a21b08_448x298.jpeg)

</div>

The temple can be reached by road from Rajahmundry, Eluru, Tadepalligudem, while the nearest Railway Station is at Bhimadole around 15 km away, located on the Vizag-Vijayawada route. The temple is worth a visit, with itâ€™s towering gopurams, exquisite sculptures and pleasant surroundings.
