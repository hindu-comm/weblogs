![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YUlC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F420edaa0-7b72-43fb-9b3a-b1e2ade1f21f_1000x600.jpeg "Mogalarajapuram Caves (Vijayawada) History")

</div>

While Kanaka Durga temple is the most famous landmark of Vijayawada, the city has 3 famous cave temple in it’s vicinity, the Mogalrajpuram caves within the city precincts, the Akkanna Madanna Caves at the foothills of Indrakeeladri and Vundavalli caves around 10 km from the city, falling in the Capital region.

The Mogalrajpuram caves are basically five rock cut cave temple groups, mostly dedicated to Shiva built during the Eastern Chalukya/Vishnukundin regime, dating to about 7th century. The caves and the surrounding area are believed to have got their name from a Rishi Mogalaraju, who meditated here. The caves per se don’t have a name, rather they are numbered from I to V, with cave no II being the most architecturally rich.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vu0U!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac8e50d9-aa6c-4239-b702-c4193d20f217_840x425.jpeg "Mogalrajapuram Caves: 7th-Century Marvels In Andhra Pradesh - TripXL")

</div>

Basically caves III, IV and V are excavated from the same rocky hill, with Caves III, IV close to each other while V is on northern side of the same hill. Cave II is located 500m away from this group, while Cave I is around 150 m west of Cave II.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6QKi!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F72c8a6b6-4fd5-410e-a73c-7f5e6c7361a8_300x204.jpeg "Mogalrajapuram caves – ecoheritage.cpreec.org")

</div>

Cave I has a very simple facade with two pillars and pilasters, between which it has a notch. The pillars are square, decorated with Bhuta Gana sculptures, and the kapota exterior merging into the ceiling. The interior has a square plan with 3 mandapas-mukha mandapa( entrance hall), maha mandapa(main hall) and ardha mandapa(devotional), each having it’s own *vajana* frame, with *ganas* and *hamsa* malavahakas motifs.

The sanctum here is carved from a single rock, that has two dwarapalas in front, which seem to have been destroyed by invaders.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iHyj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ad3d44e-335d-45e8-a9c2-f5b306206941_2048x1232.jpeg "Mogalrajapuram Caves - Wikipedia")

</div>

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sb2G!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4098388a-2ced-4539-98cd-153541a27a90_1024x576.jpeg "Cave II (Brahma-Shiva-Vishnu trimurti)")

</div>

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_Olt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8bb2bcd7-a551-48d2-a642-0e6105252224_800x533.jpeg "pillars and mandapa, Cave II")

</div>

Cave 2 which is the most famous of all, located on the south side, has an elaborate front court carved out of 9 meters of rock. Again like Cave 1, the facade has two pillars and pilasters, with a rectangular mandapa inside with four pillars and two pilasters that leads to three sanctums.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CFy_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F02d6bc32-7bd6-40a4-99f2-2881f429a435_320x570.jpeg "A dvarapala, Cave II")

</div>

The front is flanked by two dwarapalas, both of them damaged, in tribhanga pose, and square pillars with octagonal bottom in between. At the top of the rock face is Shiva in Tandava pose, though damaged, the damru, parasu and trisulam can be identified.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wwQ5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff41ec79b-e880-447f-b78e-b60b3a56034f_800x533.jpeg "One of the small sanctums")

</div>

The facade pillars are notable for upper shadurams with 7th century Vaishnava artwork, one depicting Krishna on Kaliya. The three sanctums here are dedicated to Brahma, Vishnu and Maheswara, though right now you can just see a monolithic Shiva Linga, the other two sanctums are empty.

Cave III and IV are basically small sanctum shrines, both having a facade with two pillars flanked by two pilasters, and a square sanctum. Though Cave III has auxiliary shrines, and a bas relief of Mahishasura Mardini, again mostly damaged. Cave IV has a square platform for a Shiva linga( missing) and a padma pitha for Vishnu with four arms and Brahma.

Cave V located on the north side is not yet fully excavated, there are 3 small shrines, with mandapa pillars having a square cross section, while pilasters have a series of animal friezes.

The Mogalarajapuram caves can be visited between 9-6 AM, nearest stop is Labbipeta in Vijayawada, and are worth a visit, even many of the sculptures here have been damaged or destroyed by Muslim invaders.
