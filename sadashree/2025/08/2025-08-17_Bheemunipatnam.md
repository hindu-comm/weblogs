Bheemunipatnam, more popularly known as Bhimili,a sleepy seaside town, often eclipsed by Vizag‚Äôs bustle, yet once a thriving Dutch outpost. In the 17th century, Bhimili was a key Dutch trading port. Its lanes still echo with colonial footprints‚Äîold cemeteries, weathered churches, and fading forts.

Named after Bhima who is believed to have founded this place. There is a small temple complex, tucked away within the lanes, that is dedicated to the Pandavas, not many know about it.

Pavurallakonda, also known as Narasimhaswami Konda, holds traces of Buddhist habitation from the 3rd century BCE. Excavations revealed relic caskets, Brahmi inscriptions, votive stupas, and monastic foundations.Both Hinayana and Mahayana schools flourished here, radiating wisdom across the seas via the River Gosthani.

From this ancient port, Buddhist teachings sailed eastward‚Äîtoward Burma, Java, and beyond. Today, Pavurallakonda stands weathered but proud. Sixteen rock-cut cisterns still whisper of monks who once gathered rainwater and silence.

Apart from Pavurallokonda some of the other prominent Buddhist sites nearby are Bojjannakonda, Bavikonda, Lingalakonda, Pavurallakonda, Salihundam and Thotlakonda, which formed an arc of sorts.

On the eastern flank of Pavurallakonda, facing the ocean, stands a 14th-century Narasimha temple built by the Mindi kings. n the heart of town lie two ancient temples‚ÄîBheemeswaralayam and Choleswaralayam‚Äîdating back to the Chola dynasty. Their architecture whispers of a time when Bhimili was a spiritual waypoint.

The Jagannatha Temple in the town is equally famous, nestled in the heart of Bhimili, is a beacon of Vaishnavite devotion. Every year, the temple hosts a vibrant **Rathotsavam**, echoing the grandeur of Puri. The chariot rolls through Bhimili‚Äôs lanes, carrying centuries of faith and festivity.

Alongside Jagannatha Mandir, Bhimili houses the **Bheemeswaralayam**, **Choleswaralayam**, and the **Narasimha Temple** on Pavurallakonda‚Äîeach a testament to its spiritual plurality.

In the 17th century, it became a major Dutch East India Company trading post, exporting rice, jute, and textiles. The town even housed a **Dutch currency mint**, producing coins for trade across the eastern seas.A rare privilege, marking it as a commercial hub of global exchange.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z9ob!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb49ab54-796d-4efa-a16a-b5dfd89331b1_1024x768.jpeg "Dutch cemetery in Bhimli |")

</div>

Two Christian cemeteries‚Äî**Kummaripalem** (1661) and **Flagstaff Cemetery** (1762)‚Äîhold Dutch and British tombs. Skull-and-crossbones motifs hint at pirate legends and maritime lore.The Portuguese, too, visited Bhimili during the same period, adding to its cosmopolitan character.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SMPT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb2520184-2ee4-4e26-93d9-6cd74456ea04_800x533.jpeg "Vintage Old Bheemili S St. Peter S Church Which Was Called As Red Church  Stock Image - Image of green, religion: 301932795")

</div>

Built in 1864, **St. Peter‚Äôs Church** is a Gothic marvel with stained glass from London.  
Its acoustics were designed to amplify sermon, echoing faith across centuries.

During the Colonial conflicts, the Dutch East India Company, sought permission to build a fort to protect its trading post.Military engineer **Coenraad Pieter Keller** was commissioned to design the fort. His plans were detailed, even accounting for water wells, cremation grounds, and a temple that doubled as a lighthouse.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!G9aY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb8dfee07-ef3f-435c-be99-a8033d55db16_360x360.jpeg "Top Forts in Bheemili, Visakhapatnam near me - Justdial")

</div>

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xPUt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0a372d91-f739-469c-bcaf-5ef9c4a40405_295x171.jpeg "Mana Bheemili - Fifteen decades of glory A treasure house of cultural and  monumental heritage, Bheemunipatnam Municipality celebrates 150 years of  its existence Standing tall St. Peter's Church at Bheemunipatnam A treasure")

</div>

The fort collapsed **twice**‚Äîfirst in 1759 due to monsoon rains and unstable foundations. Keller was summoned to **Batavia** to defend his reputation before VOC superiors. Though eventually pardoned, Keller‚Äôs career never recovered. His maps survive today as relics of ambition, failure, and colonial complexity.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Va7f!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F773688bc-3ef9-4d90-9410-9df2d7bdfea3_1200x1800.jpeg "File:Clock tower on Bheemili main road.jpg - Wikimedia Commons")

</div>

After the Dutch presence collapsed, Bheemunipatnam became the **main trading base** of British East India Company on the east coastThe **British-built clock tower** still stands in the town square‚Äîa monument to colonial precision and imperial permanence.

From Bhimili‚Äôs shores and warehouses, **passenger vessels sailed to Madras and Calcutta**, ferrying **perfumes, textiles, and fine goods**, officials, and stories across the Bay of Bengal.The scent of trade lingered in the salty air. A **vast coco plantation** stretched across Bhimili‚Äôs beaches. Coconuts were harvested, processed, and shipped‚Äîan empire of palms swaying to the rhythm of commerce.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FVJd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd23e8f19-0b2e-4843-85e3-4f30a0049efa_2560x1920.jpeg "File:Front view of Bheemili Lighthouse.jpg - Wikimedia Commons")

</div>

In 1933, the British inaugurated **Visakhapatnam Port**, citing strategic safety and deeper waters. Bhimili was sidelined‚Äîbut never forgotten. However the British lighthouse at Bhimili built in 1868 still stands‚Äîweathered, silent, and proud.It witnessed Dutch maps, British cargo, and the fading of sails.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NUmd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F10809580-b3ad-47e0-9508-5badff278fad_800x600.jpeg "River Gosthani at Bheemili")

</div>

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1_Tw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F987abae6-8716-4c33-baf9-ec5da635202a_1000x667.jpeg "Explore Bheemili beach for water sports and fascinating Dutch architecture  | TimesTravel")

</div>

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-vWg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2aca310b-c86c-42df-8573-e4462305afbc_700x400.jpeg "Places To Visit Near Bheemili Beach Top Picks For Your Trip")

</div>

Bheemili Beach is located at the confluence of the River Gosthani with Bay of Bengal, the cemetery, light house are all located in it‚Äôs vicinity. The Beach is a nice place to relax with views of the sea and a park, and happens to be less crowded too. There are couple of resorts here including Haritha, Novotel.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m6mo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa9087c8e-5816-4408-87f7-60ad7a1e0b5c_554x300.jpeg "Weekend trip to Bheemili Beach | MyTravaly")

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GkQP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc20dbd61-7b8a-4272-9f16-cd054626bf13_1200x692.jpeg "Vizag's Erra Matti Dibbalu cries for attention - The Hindu")

The 50 km road journey from Vizag to Bheemili is itself a lovely experience, with hills on one side, sea on the other side. You get to pass Rushikonda, the large Hanuman statute at Ramadri, the Erra Matti Dibbalu, and the panoramic view of Bhimili town.
