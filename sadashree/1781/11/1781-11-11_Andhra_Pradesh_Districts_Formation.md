On the occasion of Andhra Pradesh formation day, taking a look here at the formation of each district and their significance.

Nellore district also known as Simhapuri in ancient times, was ruled by the Nawabs of Arcot, is the oldest district of the satte. The last Nawab Azim-Ud-Daula handed over the Nellore to the British in 1781 and it was founded as a district in 1792. Famous for it's rice fields, beaches, Sriharikota, Pulicat Lake, and the Sriranganatha Temple in Nellore.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd987d23b-4a05-46ab-a064-1bfd44f94721_630x680.jpeg "Image")

</div>

Kadapa and Kurnool district both formed in 1808, would be among the oldest districts in Andhra Pradesh. It was originally under Nizam rule, who ceded it to the British in 1800 as per the Treaty of Srirangapatnam. The district was formed in 1808, when the British split it into Kadapa and Bellary. The district headquaters was Siddavatam originally, but shifted to Kadapa in 1812.

Kadapa gets it's name from Devuni Gadapa(Threshold of God), referring to the Venkateswara Temple here, that was a starting point to many pilgrims going to Tirumala. Also famous for the Gandikota fort and Canyon, Pushpagiri Temple complex, Vontimitta Ramalayam and Brahmamgari Matam.

Kurnool district was founded in 1808, and it included Bellary then too. After formation of Andhra State in 1953, the Kannada speaking areas consisting of Bellary were transferred to Mysore state, while Telugu speaking areas came under Kurnool district, and some parts to Anantapur district.

</div>

Yemmiganur, Alur, Adoni became part of Kurnool district, while Kanekallu, Bommanahallu went to Anantapuram. One of the Dwadasa Jyotirlingams Srisailam is located here, as well as the Belum caves, Ahobilam Narasimha Kshetra, Mantralayam, Yaganti and Mahanandi.

Krishna district was initially called as Machilipatnam district. It was formed in 1859 and gets it’s name from the Krishna river flowing through it. Andhra Pradesh's second largest city Vijayawada is in Krishna district, as well as Machilipatnam one of the oldest muncipalities in India. The Kanaka Durga Temple is one of the most famous in the state, and it also has one of the largest railway junctions in India.

Kuchipudi classical dance originated here, and the district is an economic, cultural, literary hub.Designer of our national flag Pingali Venkayya is from Machilipatnam, as are many movie stars, media magnates, writers, freedom fighters, businessmen.

</div>

Anantapur district was formed in 1882 from Bellary district. The place gets it's name from a tank Anantasagaramu built during Vijayanagara era. The district is famous for the Lepakshi temple, Puttaparthy Sai Baba Ashram, Penugonda, Gooty forts, Kadiri Narasimha Temple, Tadipatri Bugga Ramalingeswara Temple and Guntakal Railway Junction.

</div>

</div>

Guntur famous for it's chillies, spicy food, hot weather, was formed from Krishna district in 1904. It was the hub of the freedom struggle in AP, famous for the Palnadu Satyagraha, Chirala Satyagraha.

Some of the great freedom fighters of Andhra Pradesh like Kanneganti Hanumanthu, Duggirala Gopalakrishnayya, Acharya N.G.Ranga were from Guntur, the district is also a commercial, educational hub, and many Telugu cinema artists, directors come from there. The district is also famous for Amaravati temple, Vundavalli caves, Kondavidu fort, Narasimha Temple at Mangalagiri.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6a618ac6-0269-4f06-9b26-9858fe4d7d07_680x680.jpeg "Image")

</div>

Chittor district was formed in 1911, with Chittor, Palamaneru,Tirupati from then N.Arcot dt of Madras, Madanapalle, Vayalpadu from Kadapa dt, and the Zamindari estates of Pileru, Sri Kalahasti, Punganur. Later in 1960, some villages in Chittor dt were merged with Chingleput, Thiruvallur districts of TN, while Satyavedu and Kuppam were transferred from Chingleput dt. Chittor to date still has a mixed Telugu-Tamil culture.

The district is known for the Shri Venkateswara Temple at Tirumala, Srikalahasti one of the Panchabhoota Kshetrams, Horsley Hills, Kanipakam Vinayaka, Talakona forests, Nagulapuram temple and Chandragiri, erstwhile capital of Vijayanagara Empire.

Godavari districts were originally a single Rajahmundry district created in 1823, that included Krishna too. In 1859, it was bifurcated into Godavari, Krishna districts. And in 1925 it was split into East with Kakinada as HQ and West with Eluru as HQ.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8651d036-5fc4-447e-adf5-6a868a541b9b_680x510.jpeg "Image")

</div>

Vishakapatnam was formed as a revenue district by the British in 1802, that incorporated Srikakulam then part of Ganjam. When Odisha province was formed in 1936, areas like Rayagada, Jeypore, Koraput, and whole of Ganjam, except Srikakulam were transferred to Odisha.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feca86610-3804-4131-bb1e-6662d33b3b1b_680x451.jpeg "Image")

</div>

Vizag still has a strong Odiya connect, the Science Department in AU University was funded by the Maharajah of Jeypore. Daspalla Hills, gets it's name from the erstwhile princely state in Odisha, whose land it was.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9c9c2388-25bc-4f76-b594-0377bf2fec99_680x451.png "Image")

</div>

Srikakulam district was formed in 1950 from Vishakapatnam dt. The district bordering Odisha was part of the erstwhile Kalinga region, famous for Arasavalli, one of the few Sun temples in India, as well as Sri Kurmam, where Vishnu is worshipped as Kurmavatara and Srimukhalingam temple complex.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1e5a1791-ac7e-44f5-b28b-821051e90ac2_1200x770.jpeg "Arasavalli temple wears a festive look - The Hindu")

</div>

Vizianagaram dt was formed in 1979, from Srikakulam district and parts of Vizag dt. It is famous for Bobbilli where the epic battle was fought against French, as well as the town of Vizianagaram, known for it's education, as well as Ramatheertham, one of the oldest Rama Temples in the state.

Prakasam dt was formed in 1970 with taluqs from Guntur(Addanki, Chirala, Ongole), Nellore(Kandukur, Kanigiri, Darsi) and Kurnool(Markapur, Giddaluru) districts. Named after the great freedom fighter Andhra Kesari Tanguturi Prakasam.

<div class="captioned-image-container">

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fceb27314-cfb5-4e02-be30-d592fe8c1f67_276x360.png "Image")

</div>

The district is famous for the Ongole Gitta, one of the finest bull breeds, temples at Tripurantakam, Bhairavakona, beaches at Vodarevu.

The Agency area, covering Vizag, Vizianagaram, Srikakulam, Godavari districts, known for it’s thick forests, valleys, flanking the Eastern Ghats, was formed as a separate district in 2022 named after the legendary revolutionary Alluri Sitarama Raju, and has the beautiful Araku Valley, Borra Caves and Anantagiri plantations from where you get the Araku Coffee.
