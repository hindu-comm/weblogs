# India is not poor, We love to appear poor.

For decades, India was framed internationally through poverty and underdevelopment, partly because of colonial legacies and partly because of how aid organizations and media chose to highlight it. At times, emphasizing struggle has been used to rally solidarity, justify policy, or attract aid and sympathy.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iN08!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fad48bf8d-a91f-41c4-8cbb-adab9a74c6b7_275x183.jpeg)

![From Pancake to Pyramid in India Part I: When Building a City Requires Better Property Rights](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iN08!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fad48bf8d-a91f-41c4-8cbb-adab9a74c6b7_275x183.jpeg "From Pancake to Pyramid in India Part I: When Building a City Requires  Better Property Rights")

Now once an image forms, it takes quite some time to shift, even if the reality changes. Consider this , we are the 5th largest economy in the world, with a booming middle class, an IT, Pharma hub, we have sent satellites into space, globally our soft power is growing through movies, culture. Yet the image persists of us being a backward nation.

Now the first impression as they say is the best impression, for an average visitor from abroad, what they see first is when they exit from the airport. And I am afraid, it’s not a very pretty sight.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EC9q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcbe081f3-6003-47a7-ae6e-893c2220c21c_1600x900.jpeg)

![Top 10 dirtiest city in India 2025: Bengaluru, Chennai among the worst as smaller towns outshine metros](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EC9q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcbe081f3-6003-47a7-ae6e-893c2220c21c_1600x900.jpeg "Top 10 dirtiest city in India 2025: Bengaluru, Chennai among the worst as  smaller towns outshine metros")

Slums, haphazard constructions, buildings stacked up one on another randomly, broken roads, potholes, garbage dumps, overflowing sewage water on the roads and the stink. And it’s not that our cities are poor, Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai are all economic hubs, have immense wealth, yet look even worse than cities in war torn Somalia or Iraq.

Now every city in the world has it’s ugly, sleazy side, but it’s hidden away, you would need to wander into back alleys, industrial zones, or neglected suburbs to see it. But in India, the contradictions are **front and center**, layered right on the main roads, visible from the airport taxi window.

Many countries manicure airport corridors and tourist routes, our cities don’t even bother covering up. Luxury malls, tech parks, and five-star hotels exist side by side with broken drains, garbage heaps, and street vendors.

The so called “posh” areas are no better than slums, just that they have better looking homes. Even gated communities often have crumbling access roads.Waste management is inconsistent, so trash piles up right outside luxury apartments.

High-rise towers jammed together, with no breathing space, create the same claustrophobia as slums. Fancy homes surrounded by tangled wires, open drains, and chaotic traffic.

Ok our cities are ugly, fucked up, but we still have beautiful mountains, beaches, outdoor spaces, rivers, yes we do, but they are turning out to be as ugly as our cities.

Mountains, once pristine and beautiful, are now scarred by unregulated construction, litter from tourism, and landslides worsened by deforestation. Beaches have been ruined by plastic waste, sewage discharge, and overcrowding turn what should be idyllic coastlines into dumping grounds.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dwen!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65f1a531-f354-4499-8805-b3b496b16960_1200x675.png)

![List of Most Polluted Rivers in India 2025](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dwen!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65f1a531-f354-4499-8805-b3b496b16960_1200x675.png "List of Most Polluted Rivers in India 2025")

We treat our rivers as sacred, yet they are choked by industrial effluents, untreated sewage, and ritual offerings choke them.Parks and lakes often suffer from encroachment, garbage, and neglect, becoming extensions of urban dysfunction.

India has some of the world’s most stunning landscapes—Himalayas, Kerala backwaters, Goa beaches, Sundarbans mangroves. Yet instead of being protected as treasures, they’re treated like open-access dumping sites. It’s as if the urban mindset of neglect and chaos has spilled into nature, erasing the line between city and wilderness.

Once upon a time while our cities were were chaotic, the hills, rivers, and beaches offered relief. Tourism, overbuilding, plastic waste, and unchecked crowds have dragged the same dysfunction into nature. There is no quiet, clean place to escape to, the outdoors are now an extension of the cities.

We are not a poor and backward country, materially, but somehow we have this fetish to look poor and backward. Maybe it’s some kinda colonial hangover, which has conditioned us to equate India with poverty, but why is it that countries in Africa, equally ravaged by colonialism, has better looking cities.

We have some kinda pride in appearing shabby, equating it with “simple living”, no that simple living does not mean you appear like a slob. We are not poor, but all that wealth is used for our homes, weddings, functions, but not into public infrastructure where it actually matters.
