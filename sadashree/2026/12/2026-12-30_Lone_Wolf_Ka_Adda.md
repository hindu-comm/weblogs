*Adda* is a term that refers to informal, often leisurely group conversations or hangouts, typically filled with storytelling, debate, humor, and companionship. Though associated with Bengal, it is widespread all over India in different forms. The long night chats at Irani cafes in Mumbai, late night debates at the Dhabas of Delhi, chai-bisket hangouts at Irani cafes in Hyderabad. While the name and setting vary, the basic spirit and ethos remains the same.

**So why Adda?**

The very concept of Adda is that you can sit and chat on everything under the sun, you could start from Sachin Tendulkar move on to Amitabh Bacchan, end up discussing Swami Vivekananda’s philosophy, with some bits on geopolitics, world history, like whatever you fancy.

And that has been me, though I post mostly on Indian history, that is not the only thing that interests me. I could start up with Rana Pratap, Mewar move to Oscars, go on to the Bhagwad Geeta, try figuring out Ramanujan’s equations. No I don’t consider myself a polymath or an intellectual per se, I don’t even know what I am to date.

Basically I hate being labelled, I am not a box or something in Walmart, where I am put in one category, and have a tag attached. I am just a human being with my own thoughts, good, bad or whatever. I would go into depression if I had to think in the same mode all the time, reason why I go from very sublime thoughts to absolutely ridiculous one.

Of course in the world of social media, where you are considered Left Wing, Right Wing or whatever wing, where you have labels attached on you, that would be blasphemy. I used to think about that, but of late have honestly stopped caring. What is the point of seeking validation from strangers, whom you would never meet in life.

I honestly have stopped caring about Left and Right Wing, only thing that matters to me is Chicken Wings. Don’t care if I am whitepilled or blackpilled, there are only 2 types of pills I know, one you take for sleeping, another you take to sleep around with someone.

I am very much Right Wing on issues like National Security, Defense, in fact down right hawkish. On Economy and Social Issues more centrist. I support industries, liberalization, yet not at the cost of environment and pollution. I am a proud Hindu, have nothing against Muslims, Christians, or any non Hindus, for that matter, but not gonna accept any crap being spoken against Hinduism.

Yet at the same time, I find it hard to be with the Right Wing, for their utterly boorish behaviour, misogyny, lack of empathy. Not to mention their self serving, opportunistic attitudes. There is no genuine Right Wing space in India, just a bunch of grifters and hangers on.

Every year teaches new lessons in life, some pleasant, some harsh, and so has this year. We just learn from those lessons and keep moving on. So to all my subscribers and followers, wishing you a Happy New Year.
