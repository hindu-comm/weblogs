Growing up in Andhra Pradesh, during the pre Internet era, one of the memories was the network of libraries in the state, both public and private. Even a very small town would have a library with a reading room, most with a decent collection of books.

The network of libraries all across erstwhile Andhra Pradesh, was due to the Library movement that was the brainchild of 3 great men- Ayyanki Venkata Ramanayya, Gadicherla Hari Sarvottama Rao, Kalaprapurna Paturi Nagabhushanam.

Ayyanki Venkata Ramanayya, freedom fighter, scholar, called the father of the Library movement in Andhra Pradesh, hailing from Biccavolu in East Godavari district. Founded a magazine called Grandhalaya Sarvasvamu. He set up the Rammohan Grandhalayam in Vijayawada in 1911, as well as establishing the first ever Library Association in India in 1914, also started an English magazine Indian Library Journal.

Between 1934-48, Venkata Ramanayya set up libraries in every nook and corner of the Godavari,Krishna and Guntur districts. He was honored with the Padmasri in 1972 for his yeoman service.

The other pioneer of the Library movement was Gadicherla Hari Sarvottama Rao, from Kurnool, he in fact was the one who coined the word Rayalaseema as opposed to Ceded, that was used by the British. He was the first political prisoner, when he was jailed by the British in 1908, for writing an article Kruramaina Videsi Puli( Cruel Foreign Tiger), after the Tirunelveli incident where 3 protesters died in police firing.

Heading the Andhra Pradesh Grandhalaya Association, he played a major role in the spread of the libraries across the state, was also the first editor of Andhra Patrika, and actively fought for establishment of separate Andhra state.

Kalaprapurna Paturi Nagabhushanam, took active part in the freedom struggle, went underground during the Quit India movement, took part in the Salt Satyagraha, also set up the Chemical factory at Nidadavolu.

His biggest contribution though would be the boat libraries, by which he took books to very remote villages. In those days for most villages in Krishna-Guntur districts, boats were the only means of transport with no roads available yet. He travelled from village to village by boat, taking books for the villagers to read, a sort of mobile library. You can read about it in detail here.

https://t.co/pKoMOluV2f?amp=1

The first All India Library Conference was organized in Vijayawada on April 10, 1914, it's first president was Mocherla Ramachandra Rao Pantulu, and the session was presided by the famous writer Chilakamarthi Lakshmi Narasimham. The objectives of the movement were to spread Literacy among the Telugu people, inculcate the reading habit, awaken the consciousness and a spirit of reasoning among the masses.

The movement had many achievements to it's credit, apart from being the first of it's kind, also began training many in library science, providing livelihood to them, as also educating the people. The Andhra Library movement would also play a role in setting up Library associations in Bengal, Madras provinces. They started a book stall in 1944 to ensure the libraries had access to the best quality.

The Sarvottama Library was among the first major ones to be set up in Vijayawada in 1944, which also operates mobile libraries, one of the well known libraries in the state.

However with the advent of smartphones, TV, Social Media, the reading habit has taken a hit, and most of these libraries right now struggle due to lack of patronage sadly. These libraries and reading rooms were a valuable resource of news, knowledge and information, especially so in the pre Internet, Social Media era. As well as being a meeting point for many. And for many Telugu people, preparing for competitive examinations, these libraries once again proved to be quite valuable for getting the requisite knowledge.
