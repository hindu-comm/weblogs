This Valentine’s Day (which must be around now since they have those raspberry and white chocolate cookies back at the Co-op again), straight men around the world are buying their girlfriends a packet of After Eights from the gas station to make a show of pretending to care about things like Valentine’s Day. Or their girlfriends. Even though they’ve been told like five times or something already that After Eights taste like cheap toothpaste, which just makes them look like they don’t give a shit even more. But while they're busy doing the absolute bare minimum for their girlfriends, are they also doing the bare minimum in fulfilling their moral obligations to their fellow man (literally)? I believe the disturbing truth is that they are, and that there are devastating arguments to this effect.

One of the most hoary philosophical arguments used by defenders of heterosexual conduct is that it is in line with the natural law, as heterosexual sex is essentially oriented towards the fulfilment of the functional teloi for which the reproductive organs are maintained, that being reproduction. While this position may have assuaged the consciences of many a heterosexual throughout history, does it actually withstand scrutiny?

The answer, of course, is no. As we all know, humans are perfectly capable of possessing faculties capable of appropriating physiological functions for teloi distinct from those for which the aforesaid functions were originally maintained. What better example of this is speech, which appropriates human dental structures for articulatory processes, temporarily frustrating their telos (that is, mastication) in service of enunciating fricatives. As Etienne Gilson says:

> *The appropriate function of teeth is to masticate, that of the palate
> is to taste; yet language superimposes on their natural functions that
> of contributing to the articulation of speech. One could truly say
> that a transcendent power “uses” the body for ends of a transcendent
> order akin to itself.
> [1](https://williamjames17.substack.com/p/the-natural-law-argument-against#footnote-1-157219644)*

No one, of course, denies the liceity of speech, since it allows for the satisfaction of an independent measure of the good while not permanently compromising the teloi for which the appropriated functions are maintained in themselves. Likewise for homosexuality, since, as Plato teaches us in his *Symposium*, it is the form of love most conducive to progression in the contemplation of the good without permanently frustrating the immediate teloi of the reproductive organs, as evinced by the time-honoured use of the turkey baster. Given this, the truth behind the illiceity of heterosexuality becomes painfully clear: it offers no benefits with regards to adherence to natural law (nothing a good old turkey baster can’t do) while offering clear disadvantages with regards to ascending the Ladder of Love and attaining the appreciation of the good necessary to attain a flourishing life. Given the widespread attainment of human flourishing is obviously a greater good than just avoiding a turkey baster, the moral calculus becomes startlingly clear. This basically means that raising your child heterosexual is sort of like not teaching them their ABCs for fear they’ll have to stop chewing at the dinner table to talk sometimes. That’s how silly it is.

This article is short, because, the matter, unfortunately, is not that complicated when viewed truthfully and with no ulterior motives or biases whatsoever in the way, as I have done so here. However, to any struggling heterosexuals reading this: I’m here for you, at any time or any place, to offer any help I can. Unless you’re fat, a woman, Scouse, or over 44, in which case I’m super sorry but I’ve just got too many other things to do right now. Stay blessed.
