40,000 BP footprints in Mexico?

[40,000-year-old footprint of first Americans](http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/main.jhtml?xml=/news/2005/07/05/wfoot05.xml&sSheet=/news/2005/07/05/ixworld.html). I don’t really believe it, and the “island hopping” thesis via Polynesia seems ludicrous to me (if such a thing occurred phylogeneticists should find a previous extinction or bottleneck event prior to the arrival of Polynesians for many species I would think). But that this research gets any play is an indicator as to the unsettled nature of New World paleoanthropology. [Nature](http://www.nature.com/news/2005/050704/full/050704-4.html) has some voices of skeptics in their article….
