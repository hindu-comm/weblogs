The Facebook search plateau

I check on this every 6 months or so. Here’s the search trend for Facebook:

[![](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2012/06/fb1.jpg?resize=588%2C294)![](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2012/06/fb1.jpg?resize=588%2C294)](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2012/06/fb1.jpg)

Everyone basically knows about Facebook now. Contrast this with Twitter:

  
[![](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2012/06/twit1.jpg?resize=588%2C292)![](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2012/06/twit1.jpg?resize=588%2C292)](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2012/06/twit1.jpg)

All that being said, Twitter has such a smaller footprint compared to Facebook that it seems obvious that it will “peak” far below Facebook in both mindshare and public utilization. I do find it interesting that Facebook peaked literally months about *The Social Network*.
