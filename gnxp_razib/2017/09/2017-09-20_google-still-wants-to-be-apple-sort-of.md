Google still wants to be Apple (sort of)

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.gnxp.com/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/htc.jpg?resize=150%2C294)![](https://i0.wp.com/www.gnxp.com/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/htc.jpg?resize=150%2C294)](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B00UCALTN0/geneexpressio-20)[Google Is Buying HTC’s Smartphone Expertise for \$1.1 Billion](https://www.nytimes.com/2017/09/20/technology/google-htc-smartphones.html?_r=0). This, after Google has already [bought and sold Motorola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motorola_Mobility#Under_Google_ownership). Remember when [Microsoft bought part of Nokia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Mobile)?

The problem is that Apple and Samsung are starting to create a duopoly. And though most phones run Android, iPhones are much more profitable. There’s a reason many companies develop for iOS but not Android. A friend at Google years ago bemoaned how much more profitable iPhone owners were compared to those bought Android phones.

With all that being said the Apple launch and comments on this blog have convinced me I’m not going iPhone. I don’t know if I’ll go for an [HTC](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B00UCALTN0/geneexpressio-20), [Motorola](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B01NBRN2YI/geneexpressio-20) or [Samsung](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B01CJU8FV4/geneexpressio-20). But for me a phone is functional, not an accessory. Perhaps that explains some of the psychological reasons that iPhone owners spend so much more money on apps….
