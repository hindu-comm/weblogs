Best American Science Writing, no physics?

[Chad](http://scienceblogs.com/principles/2009/02/physics_gets_no_respect.php) is complaining that [The Best American Science Writing 2008](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0061340413/geneexpressio-20/) is too focused on biomedical science. He finds it especially lame that there’s no physics when this was the year of the [LHC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_Hadron_Collider). Here’s what I found in the contents….

  
Amy Harmon, [Facing Life with a Lethal Gene](http://www.nytimes.com/2007/03/18/health/18huntington.html)  
Richard Preston, [An Error in the Code](http://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2007/08/13/070813fa_fact_preston)  
Thomas Goetz, [23anMe Will Decode Your DNA for \$1,000. Welcome to the Age of Genomics](http://www.wired.com/medtech/genetics/magazine/15-12/ff_genomics)  
Carl Zimmer, [Evolved for Cancer](http://www.sciamdigital.com/index.cfm?fa=Products.ViewIssuePreview&ARTICLEID_CHAR=634610CD-2B35-221B-6741D56AB263FFE2)  
Tara Parker-Pope, [How NIH Misread Hormone Study in 2002](http://online.wsj.com/public/article/SB118394176612760522-hNh92GVPMoyJO_a5hNvC_QTJfQk_20070807.html?mod=tff_main_tff_top)  
Gardiner Harris, Benedict Carey, and Janet Roberts, [Psychiatrists, Children and Drug Industry’s Role](http://www.nytimes.com/2007/05/10/health/10psyche.html)  
Daniel Carlat, [Dr. Drug Rep](http://www.nytimes.com/2007/11/25/magazine/25memoir-t.html)  
Tina Rosenberg, [When Is a Pain Doctor a Drug Pusher](http://www.nytimes.com/2007/06/17/magazine/17pain-t.html)  
Jerome Groopman, [What’s Normal](http://www.jeromegroopman.com/articles/whats-the-trouble.html)  
Sally Satel, [Supply, Demand, and Kidney Transplants](http://www.hoover.org/publications/policyreview/8818392.html)  
Oliver Sacks, [The Abyss](http://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2007/09/24/070924fa_fact_sacks)  
Ben McGrath, [Muscle Memory](http://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2007/07/30/070730fa_fact_mcgrath)  
Margaret Talbot, [Duped](http://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2007/07/02/070702fa_fact_talbot)  
Stephen S. Hall, [The Older-and-Wiser Hypothesis](http://www.nytimes.com/2007/05/06/magazine/06Wisdom-t.html)  
Al Gore, [Moving Beyond Kyoto](http://www.nytimes.com/2007/07/01/opinion/01gore.html?pagewanted=print)  
Jim Yardley, [Beneath Booming Cities, China’s Future Is Drying Up](http://www.nytimes.com/2007/09/28/world/asia/28water.html)  
Joseph Kahn, [In China, a Lake’s Champion Imperils Himself](http://www.nytimes.com/2007/10/14/world/asia/14china.html)  
John Seabrook, [Sowing for Apocalypse](http://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2007/08/27/070827fa_fact_seabrook)  
I see only one physical science pure play in the list. Looks like Chad has a point.
