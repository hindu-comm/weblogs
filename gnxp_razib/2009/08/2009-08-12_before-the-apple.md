Before the apple

To those with more accrued years of wisdom, or a greater knowledge of intellectual history, what was social science like in the pre-computer era? E. O. Wilson once commented on Charlie Rose’s show that social science hasn’t discovered anything of note (I know [Robin Hanson](http://www.overcomingbias.com/) disagrees). Has the introduction of computation changed much?
