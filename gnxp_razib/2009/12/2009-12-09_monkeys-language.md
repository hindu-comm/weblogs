Monkeys & language

The paper is out, [Campbell’s monkeys concatenate vocalizations into context-specific call sequences](http://www.pnas.org/content/early/2009/12/08/0908118106.short?rss=1). [Nicholas Wade](http://www.nytimes.com/2009/12/08/science/08monkey.html?pagewanted=print) and [Ed Yong](http://scienceblogs.com/notrocketscience/2009/12/campbells_monkeys_have_a_rich_vocabulary_based_on_combinatio.php) review the evidence. One of the issues is that chimps don’t seem to have syntax, so how can a monkey? But since domesticated dogs have better human-comprehensible theory of mind than chimps I don’t think genetic distance or general intelligence should weight *that* highly.
