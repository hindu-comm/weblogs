Pigmentation variation in humans

It was only six short years ago that [Greg Barsh](http://barshlab.stanford.edu/) wrote [an “unsolved mystery” review in PLoS Biology](http://biology.plosjournals.org/perlserv/?request=get-document&doi=10.1371/journal.pbio.0000027&ct=1) asking, “What Controls Variation in Human Skin Color?”

A recent review provides [a nice summary](http://hmg.oxfordjournals.org/cgi/content/full/18/R1/R9?etoc) of the developments since then–in short, pigmentation is now probably one of the best understood (at a genetic level) phenotypes in humans. A pretty impressive story.
