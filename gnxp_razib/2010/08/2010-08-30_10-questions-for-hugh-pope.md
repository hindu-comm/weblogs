![popehugh](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2010/08/popehugh.png?resize=100%2C134)![popehugh](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2010/08/popehugh.png?resize=100%2C134)[Hugh Pope](http://hughpope.wordpress.com/) is the [author](https://www.amazon.com/Hugh-Pope/e/B001K8EWKA/ref=sr_tc_img_2_0?qid=1283186564&sr=1-2-ent) of [Dining with al-Qaeda: Three Decades Exploring the Many Worlds of the Middle East](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0312383134/geneexpressio-20/), [Sons of the Conquerors: The Rise of the Turkic World](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/158567804X/geneexpressio-20/), and [Turkey Unveiled](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1585675814/geneexpressio-20/). He was a foreign correspondent in the Middle East for 25 years, most recently with *The Wall Street Journal*, and has a degree in Oriental Studies from Oxford. He currently works for the [International Crisis Group](http://www.crisisgroup.org/en/about/staff/field/europe/hugh-pope.aspx), focusing on issues of Turkey and Cyprus. Despite [similarities of](http://topnews.in/light/files/hugh-grant-67.jpg) [physiognomy](http://www.intl-institute.wisc.edu/images/Pope.jpg) and Oxford educations Hugh Pope is *not* [Hugh Grant](http://hughpope.wordpress.com/2010/05/05/dining-with-al-qaeda-book-tour-ii-washington-dc/).
