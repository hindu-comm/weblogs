Daily Data Dump

[The Apple Two: The iPad is Steve Jobs’ final victory over the company’s co-founder Steve Wozniak](http://www.slate.com/id/2249872/). Sounds totally plausible to me, though you can probably come to that inference just by observing 5 minutes of Woz vs. Jobs’ personal affect.

[Upbeat Signs Revive Consumers’ Mood for Spending](http://www.nytimes.com/2010/04/07/business/07shop.html?hp=&pagewanted=print). I remember reading as a kid in the early 90s about how the recession of the time was going to result in a major shift in American habits and values. That didn’t pan out, the latter half the decade saw the emergence of irrational exuberance which surpassed the 1980s. But this recession/depression has been quantitatively much deeper, so perhaps something will stick. For one, it seems that we’ve lost a lot of wealth and some of the current upsurge in spending is pent up demand for goods & services which we can put off only for so long.

[A Study of the Influence of Sex on Genome Wide Methylation](http://www.plosone.org/article/info:doi/10.1371/journal.pone.0010028). Digesting.

[Did world religions help bring about complex societies?](http://epiphenom.fieldofscience.com/2010/03/did-world-religions-help-bring-about.html) I lean toward Peter Turchin’s thesis that world religions were essential in generating “meta-ethnic” identities. In other words, perhaps not complex societies per se, but civilizations which span localities.

[Have the hunting habits of leopards shaped primate evolution](http://scienceblogs.com/laelaps/2010/04/spotting_leopards_-_the_import.php). When we were prey….
