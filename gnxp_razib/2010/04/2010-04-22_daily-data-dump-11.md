Daily Data Dump (Thursday)

[Cupp, unsupported](http://scienceblogs.com/tfk/2010/04/cupp_unsupported.php). S. E. Cupp, sellout, or really, really, confused. That’s how you describe an atheist who accepts evolution, and, who defends the teaching of Creationism in science classes (as a conservative I’m skeptical that she’s a down-the-line majoritarian).

[The Red Bias](http://lesswrong.com/lw/24k/the_red_bias/). Red as the color of success?

[Of Yeast and Men](https://neuroskeptic.blogspot.com/2010/04/of-yeast-and-men.html). Reviews the recent attempt to finding QTLs of small effect via “Extreme QTL Mapping.” What may be doable in yeast may be harder in men.

[Claim Jumper: World’s Unhealthiest Restaurant](http://eater.com/archives/2010/04/20/claim-jumper-unhealthiest-chain-restaurant.php). Is it me, or do these casual dining chains which are oriented toward value always brightly lit? And their food is always super-vivid in their coloring. Contrast with higher end steak houses.

[The Apple Secrecy Machine](http://www.slate.com/id/2251431/). Secrecy wouldn’t matter if the products weren’t useful.
