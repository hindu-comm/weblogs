Who da man?

[](https://www.gnxp.com/blog/uploaded_images/football28-718187.jpg)  
[Frank Ryan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Ryan_%28American_football%29): “**Frank Beall Ryan**, Ph.D. (born [July 12](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/July_12 "July 12"), [1936](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1936 "1936")) is a retired [American football](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_football "American football") [quarterback](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quarterback "Quarterback") who played for the [Los Angeles Rams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_Rams "Los Angeles Rams") ([1958](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1958 "1958")–[1961](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1961 "1961")), [Cleveland Browns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleveland_Browns "Cleveland Browns") ([1962](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1962 "1962")–[1968](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1968 "1968")) and [Washington Redskins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_Redskins "Washington Redskins") ([1969](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1969 "1969")–[1970](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1970 "1970")). Although he led the Browns to their last [National Football League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Football_League "National Football League") title in [1964](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1964 "1964"), Ryan is best remembered for being perhaps the only [Ph.D.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ph.D. "Ph.D.") in [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics") to play in the league.”
