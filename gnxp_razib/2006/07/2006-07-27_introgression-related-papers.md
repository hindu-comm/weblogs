Introgression related papers

I suspect the basic general process of introgression is clear to most of you, though I will get back to it soon. But here are some papers with candidate genes (click “related” for more references):  
[*RRM2P4*](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/query.fcgi?db=pubmed&cmd=Retrieve&dopt=AbstractPlus&list_uids=15483323&query_hl=3&itool=pubmed_docsum), \<a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/query.fcgi?db=pubmed&cmd=Retrieve&dopt=AbstractPlus&list_uids=14513410&query_hl=5&itool=pubmed_docsum"*dystrophin*, [*xp21.1*](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/query.fcgi?db=pubmed&cmd=Retrieve&dopt=AbstractPlus&list_uids=15937130&query_hl=1&itool=pubmed_docsum) and [*17q21.31*](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/query.fcgi?cmd=Search&db=pubmed&term=stefansson+inversion+17&tool=fuzzy&ot=stefannson+inversion+17). Also, I should add in the amusing [evidence](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/query.fcgi?CMD=search&DB=pubmed) that archaics and moderns had sexual intercourse (amusing, but scientific nonetheless). Finally, a more [theoretical paper](http://genetics.plosjournals.org/perlserv/?request=get-document&doi=10.1371/journal.pgen.0020105) that just came out.  
There are other possibilities, like MC1R, but that could also be frequency dependent selection. More to come *soon*, I promise….
