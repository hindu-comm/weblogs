Biology and the preprint revolution

Over the past decade now there has been a shift away from “gated” model of scientific publication. One of the models has been to imitate what fields such as physics, computer science, and economics, have been doing for a while now, and focus on preprints. See Joe Pickrell’s [posts](http://www.genomesunzipped.org/2011/07/why-publish-science-in-peer-reviewed-journals.php) [Genomes Unzipped](http://www.genomesunzipped.org/2012/08/the-first-steps-towards-a-modern-system-of-scientific-publication.php) for the general argument, and [Haldane’s Sieve](http://haldanessieve.org/) as a model of how it might play out. [Hopi Hoekstra](https://twitter.com/hopihoekstra/status/459563053080928256) pointed me to a trend on bioRxiv on who is putting up preprints:
