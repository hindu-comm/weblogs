Facebook: being wrong

![fb_icon_325x325](https://i0.wp.com/www.unz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/fb_icon_325x325-150x150.png?resize=150%2C150)![fb_icon_325x325](https://i0.wp.com/www.unz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/fb_icon_325x325-150x150.png?resize=150%2C150)Long time readers know that around 2010 I was a [Facebook skeptic](http://www.unz.com/gnxp/peak-facebook/). I would periodically check [Google Trends](https://www.google.com/trends/explore#q=facebook) results, and post them, to illustrate that the phase of exponential growth was ending (you saw that in user base too). [The Social Network](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B004HWT6DO/geneexpressio-20) film also seemed to herald the top of the cultural influence of Facebook. Well, whatever the search engine terms are, **as a matter of business viability it seems that I was wrong, Facebook has not peaked.** The fact that I stopped talking about Facebook is a clue to that, since I would no doubt be posting my vindication if I had been right. But I thought admitting I was wrong in public might be useful, in particular since [others are](http://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2014/07/facebook-is-eating-mobile/374943/).
