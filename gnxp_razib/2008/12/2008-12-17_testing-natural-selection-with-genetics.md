Testing natural selection with genetics

[H. Allen Orr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H._Allen_Orr) (one of the authors of the study I [mentioned recently](https://www.gnxp.com/blog/2008/12/speciation-genes.php)) has a fun [article in Scientific American](http://www.sciam.com/article.cfm?id=testing-natural-selection) on testing hypotheses about natural selection using genetic data. Orr has been one of the few people to try and formally model adaptation in a population genetics framework (I highly recommend [this review article](http://www.springerlink.com/content/u8664u817ukx5h37/) from 2005 for a well-written and accessible discussion of this issue), so his thoughts are worth a read.

And if you’re in the mood for a chuckle, check out [Larry Moran’s thoughts](https://sandwalk.blogspot.com/2008/12/testing-natural-selection-part-1.html) on the article.
