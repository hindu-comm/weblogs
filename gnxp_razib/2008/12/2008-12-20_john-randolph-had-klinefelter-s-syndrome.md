John Randolph had Klinefelter's syndrome?

Just a weird historical-genetic note, the radical decentralist Republican [John Randolph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Randolph_of_Roanoke) likely suffered from [Klinefelter’s syndrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Klinefelter's_syndrome) (XXY as opposed to XY). In [What Hath God Wrought: The Transformation of America, 1815-1848](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0195078942/geneexpressio-20) the author claims that Randolph’s rumored anatomical peculiarities were confirmed postmortem. I only make note of this because it seems strange to me (I don’t know why) that the paleoconservative [John Randolph Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Randolph_Club) would be named after a childless eccentric who was likely genetically abnormal. Then again, it also struck me as peculiar that a [conservative Christian college based out of Manhattan](http://www.nytimes.com/2008/12/20/nyregion/20metjournal.html?scp=1&sq=Evangelical%20King's%20College&st=cse) would name some of its fraternities and sororities after [avowed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susan_B._Anthony) [freethinkers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clara_Barton).
