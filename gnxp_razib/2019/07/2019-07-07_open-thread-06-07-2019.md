Balances textual analysis with social-historical context.

Some bots and crawlers seem to be hitting this website. I have throttled a lot of that so hopefully, that will help. The outages have only gotten worse over the past month or so. And this website has been around with lots of traffic using WordPress since 2010…so that’s not the issue.

[America’s Monopoly Crisis Hits the Military](https://www.theamericanconservative.com/articles/americas-monopoly-crisis-hits-the-military/). Why is Matt Stoller having to publish this in *The American Conservative*? (with respect to that publication!)

[Tiny Jag pulls out of AfroFuture Fest after learning white people would be charged a different price to attend](https://www.metrotimes.com/city-slang/archives/2019/07/04/tiny-jag-pulls-out-of-afrofuture-fest-after-learning-white-people-would-be-charged-a-different-price-to-attend). Personally, I am disturbed and offended. But, isn’t this the **logical** conclusion from a lot of stuff that is taken for granted on the Left? I saw a well-meaning person on Twitter analogize denying systemic racism to being against vaccination.

[Evidence for a unitary structure of spatial cognition beyond general intelligence](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/693275v1). Since I’ll be at the *Intelligence* meeting in Minneapolis I guess I should read this.

[Recent changes in candidate genes for domestication in humans in Europe: focusing on language](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/684621v1). Skeptical at “candidate.”

[Distinct patterns of selective sweep and polygenic adaptation](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/691840v1).

[Deep evolutionary origin of limb and fin regeneration](https://www.pnas.org/content/early/2019/07/02/1900475116).

[Genetic contributions to variation in human stature in prehistoric Europe](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/690545v1). You read this.

[Emerging technologies towards enhancing privacy in genomic data sharing](https://genomebiology.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s13059-019-1741-0).

[A missense variant in CREBRF is associated with taller stature in Samoans](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/690586v1).
