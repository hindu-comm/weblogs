Nature's Oracle: A Life of W. D. Hamilton

I just noticed that [Nature’s Oracle: A Life of W. D. Hamilton](https://www.amazon.com/Natures-Oracle-Life-W-Hamilton/dp/019860727X/ref=sr_1_1/002-9954258-8060061?ie=UTF8&s=books&amp;amp;amp;amp;qid=1176109204&sr=1-1) by Ullica Segerstrale is finally on Amazon’s website for pre-orders (expect it sometime in 2008). You can view the [table of contents online](http://www.oup.com/uk/catalogue/?ci=9780198607274). Seeing as how R.A. Fisher’s [biography](https://www.amazon.com/Fisher-Scientist-probability-mathematical-statistics/dp/0471093009/ref=sr_1_3/002-9954258-8060061?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1176109578&sr=1-3) (written by his daughter!) made the man seem something of an ass I’m a bit trepidatious about biographies of men of science, but [Defender’s of the Truth](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0192862154/geneexpressio-20/) treated Hamilton with some sensitivity, so I’m not that worried.
