Frank’s RedHot Original

![franks.jpg](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2007/05/franks.jpg?resize=300%2C264)![franks.jpg](https://i0.wp.com/blogs.discovermagazine.com/gnxp/files/2007/05/franks.jpg?resize=300%2C264)There’s a local “wrap” bistro  
where I often get my lunch, and they offer a wide selection of hot sauces for condiments. I always pick out [Frank’s RedHot Original](http://www.franksredhot.com/) because it’s the hottest of the bunch. By “hot” I’m talking on a relative scale, it’s a typical cayenne sauce, powerful enough to terrify civilians, but nothing intimidating for true soldiers. Nevertheless, I really enjoy its tangy flavor, it adds some extra dimensions of taste that nicely complement the heat (and compensate for its middle-row “spice” rating). It some ways it reminds me of the [Trappey’s Red Devil](https://www.amazon.com/Trappeys-Devil-Cayenne-Pepper-Sauce/dp/B00032KO6K/ref=sr_1_11/002-9954258-8060061?ie=UTF8&s=gourmet-food&qid=1178055736&sr=1-11) cayenne sauce, though the flavor is more subtle and richer.  
**PS:** If you want “extra hot” hot sauce blogging, you have to check out [Hot Sauce Blog](http://www.hotsauceblog.com/).
