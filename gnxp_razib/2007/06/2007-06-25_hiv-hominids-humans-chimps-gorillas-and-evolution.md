HIV, hominids, humans, chimps, gorillas and evolution

By now you’ve probably heard/read about the relationship of HIV resistance and hominoid evolutionary genetics. The original paper in *Science* that started it off is titled [Restriction of an Extinct Retrovirus by the Human TRIM5α Antiviral Protein](http://www.sciencemag.org/cgi/content/abstract/316/5832/1756); quite a mouthful. Lucky for us Carl Zimmer has an excellent [exposition](http://scienceblogs.com/loom/2007/06/21/pleistocene_medicine_for_battl.php) as well as a background primer, while John Hawks [offers further thoughts](http://johnhawks.net/weblog/2007/06/24#trim5alpha_ancient_retrovirus_chimpanzees_2007) looking at the various hypotheses through the lens of someone with a deep grasp of paleoanthropology.
