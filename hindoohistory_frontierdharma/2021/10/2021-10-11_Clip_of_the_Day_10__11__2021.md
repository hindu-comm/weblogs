From the Turner County Herald, 1912:

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F293e8c44-3ffe-4bd7-bd0a-9e9ddaf20bd3_502x559.jpeg "Image")

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7201b9fe-cfbd-46bd-83ed-47154f94f99f_233x395.jpeg "Image")

"Much as the English authorities would like to abolish the appalling Indian worship of Kali, the Goddess of Revolution, the popularity of her little shrine within easy reach of government house seems to increase year by year. And the strangest part of the whole thing is that this worship is not confined to the most ignorant of the Hindoos, but is participated in to an equal degree by those who have had the advantage of European residence and education"
