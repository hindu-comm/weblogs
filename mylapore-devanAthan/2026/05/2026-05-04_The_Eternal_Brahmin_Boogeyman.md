Tamil Nadu has produced many enduring cultural institutions.

Filter coffee.  
Rajinikanth intros.  
Engineering colleges every 4.7 kilometers.  
And, towering above them all, the single greatest franchise in state history:

> **The Aryan Brahmin Threat™**

Now entering its 97th consecutive year in theatres.

No flop.  
No reboot failure.  
No audience fatigue.  
Pure box-office longevity.

Every few months, like clockwork, somebody in the ruling ecosystem dramatically rushes to a microphone and announces:

> “Sanatana Dharma must be abolished!”

At this point, I no longer see this as politics.  
I see it as seasonal content updates.

Like IPL auctions.  
Or Apple launching the same iPhone with slightly different camera angles.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Inside Fort St. George: The Crisis Room

In my imagination, somewhere inside Fort St. George there exists a secret underground war room.

A giant digital screen flashes:

> **ARYAN THREAT LEVEL: CRITICALLY LOW**

Panic erupts.

An aide rushes in breathlessly:

> “Sir! Emergency situation!”

“What happened?”

> “Sir… most Brahmins have already left.”

“Dear God.”

> “The remaining ones are in New Jersey conducting Zoom upanayanams.”

“Impossible.”

> “One group is attending a Carnatic concert in Cleveland.”

“Activate the Assembly speech immediately!”

And thus begins another season of:

> *Keeping Up With The Aryans.*

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### The Villain Who Refused to Stay in Tamil Nadu

The funniest part of this entire drama is logistical.

The villain has largely emigrated.

Tamil Nadu politics still speaks as though a massive Brahmin conspiracy controls the state from hidden basements in Mylapore.

Reality, meanwhile, looks more like this:

- one uncle in Sunnyvale discussing Tesla stock,

- one mami in New Jersey making quinoa pongal,

- one IIT graduate in Seattle explaining AI to Americans,

- and one paati in West Mambalam forwarding “Good Morning” Krishna GIFs.

Entire agraharams now resemble:

> “Heritage architecture sponsored by NRI remittances.”

Yet the speeches continue with the intensity of a Marvel movie climax.

I increasingly suspect Tamil Nadu’s political class misses Brahmins more emotionally than Brahmins miss Tamil Nadu.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Neyveli: The Original Talent Export Processing Zone

For me, this comedy became obvious years ago in Neyveli.

Ah yes, Neyveli.

That surreal township where:

- every road looked identical,

- every father worked either in NLC or wanted his son to become an
  engineer,

- and every child was raised with two instructions:

  1.  Study.

  2.  Study harder.

I studied in Jawahar Senior Secondary School, which in retrospect functioned less like a school and more like a human export warehouse.

Every year, the brightest students vanished abroad with terrifying efficiency.

One went to IIT Madras. Several went to BITS Pilani mistaking it for a pilgrimage to Pazhani.  
Many disappeared into Silicon Valley.  
Some became semiconductor experts.  
Several entered healthcare administration in America.  
Another now one advises the governor of a US state while his class mates run multi national corporations out of the US.

Meanwhile, Tamil Nadu political discourse still talks as though these people are secretly occupying all government jobs from hidden Sanskrit bunkers.

The reality?

Most of us grew up in completely ordinary homes:

- steel almirahs,

- plastic water drums,

- Bajaj scooters,

- Sunday *The Hindu*,

- tuition notebooks wrapped in brown paper,

- and mothers treating exam marks like national security data.

No Aryan-Brahmin masterplan.  
Just terrifyingly competitive parenting.

Note: Replace Neyveli with Mylapore, Alwarpet, Triplicane and Jawahar School with PS Senior, Hindu High School and Vidya Mandir to you get the same filter coffee with the same colour of decoction.

### The Great Tamil Nadu Business Model

Tamil Nadu’s economic model increasingly appears to be:

##### Step 1:

Train highly skilled students.

##### Step 2:

Emotionally exhaust them with endless ideological theatre.

##### Step 3:

Export them abroad.

##### Step 4:

Claim their foreign success as “Tamil pride.”

This is like:

- training Virat Kohli,

- gifting him permanently to Australia,

- and then proudly tweeting whenever he scores a century.

America gets:

- engineers,

- researchers,

- startup founders,

- doctors,

- AI scientists.

Tamil Nadu gets:

- television debates,

- YouTube shouting matches,

- and one retired ideologue explaining how the *Mahabharata* caused
  urban flooding.

It is honestly one of the boldest reverse-outsourcing models in economic history.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Kollywood’s Cinematic Brahmin Extended Universe

Of course, none of this survives without cinema.

Tamil cinema has protected the Aryan Threat franchise with extraordinary commitment.

For decades, audiences have bravely endured:

- evil priests,

- oily Sanskrit-speaking villains,

- scheming temple trustees,

- and Brahmins so sinister they appear capable of causing cyclones
  through Vedic chanting.

At this point, the Tamil Movie Brahmin has evolved into a supernatural creature.

He no longer pays electricity bills.  
He simply appears:

- adjusting his sacred thread,

- plotting social domination,

- while background violins intensify.

I am personally waiting for:

> **PERIYAR: ENDGAME**

A 4-hour cinematic universe event featuring:

- seven monologues,

- twelve slogans,

- slow-motion sacred-thread cutting,

- and a final revelation that all societal problems were caused by
  somebody eating curd rice confidently in 1954.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Elite Socialism: Business Class Edition

My favorite part, however, remains elite hypocrisy.

The same ruling aristocracy that condemns “elitism” sends its own children to:

- international schools,

- London universities,

- Ivy League campuses,

- and luxury global careers.

Apparently:

> anti-elitism is for mass consumption only.

Ordinary families receive lectures about social justice.

Political families receive:

- SAT coaching,

- foreign admissions consultants,

- and LinkedIn internships.

It is socialism with airport lounge access.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### The Eternal Recharge Pack

And yet, despite everything, the ghost must survive.

Because the ghost is politically renewable energy.

A solved issue cannot sustain speeches.

A permanently angry voter base, however, is infinitely recyclable.

So every few months, the old screenplay returns:

- Aryans,

- Brahmins,

- Sanatana,

- oppression,

- danger,

- outrage,

- applause.

Meanwhile, the actual 2026 Tamil middle class is busy:

- paying EMIs,

- preparing for NEET,

- learning Python,

- applying for visas,

- and searching “best universities in Texas.”

The disconnect is almost poetic.

Tamil Nadu is producing AI engineers while parts of its political discourse remain emotionally trapped in black-and-white cinema.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### At Some Point, Even Satire Gets Tired

And beneath all the comedy lies one uncomfortable truth:

Tamil Nadu is genuinely brilliant.

I saw it in Neyveli.

Ordinary middle-class families produced extraordinary people through sheer discipline, ambition, and educational obsession.

Imagine what this society could achieve if even half the energy spent maintaining century-old political ghosts went into:

- research,

- manufacturing,

- innovation,

- universities,

- entrepreneurship,

- and institutional excellence.

Instead, we repeatedly reboot the same ideological franchise like a television channel that lost the remote control in 1978.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Time to Retire the Franchise

Every franchise eventually reaches exhaustion.

The audience changes.  
The world changes.  
The villain moves to California.

At some point, Tamil Nadu too must move on from its endless rerun politics.

Not because inequality never existed.  
Not because history was perfect.  
But because permanent outrage is a terrible development strategy.

The world has moved on.

The diaspora has moved on.

Even the supposed villain has mostly moved on.

Only the screenplay remains.

And increasingly, it feels less like revolutionary politics and more like an elderly director insisting his 97-year-old franchise still has “pan-India opening potential.”

P.S: You could substitute Brahmin with Reddiar, Chettiar, Pillai, Anglo Indian and the like and get similarly flavoured brews.
