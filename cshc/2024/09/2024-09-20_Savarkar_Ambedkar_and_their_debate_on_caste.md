_(In this letter, dated 18-2-1933, Dr. B.R. Ambedkar conveys his appreciation to Mr. Savarkar for his efforts in social reform, particularly for inviting him to open a temple to the Untouchables. Ambedkar acknowledges that true reform requires ending untouchability and dismantling the caste system (Chaturvarnya). He criticizes the continued use of the caste-based system, even if merit is considered.)

_(Savarkar indirectly suggested to Dr. Ambedkar that "charity should begin at home," meaning that rather than advocating for ideas like the annihilation of Chaturvarna, even if it is based on Guna, he should first focus on eliminating casteism within his own Dalit society.)

_(Free press Journal on 17-10-1931,published a letter to the editor written by S.V.Jaykar, where The letter accuses Dr. Ambedkar of being largely inactive and unknown before the Simon Commission, questioning his contributions to improving the conditions of the Untouchables. It criticizes him for not being involved in opening seminaries or working with those who have been aiding the Untouchables. The letter highlights specific issues, such as the refusal to let Untouchables cremate their dead at the centrally located Sonapur burning grounds and questions Ambedkar's efforts in building temples for the community, as Savarkar did. The author also accuses Ambedkar of lacking deeds to back his statements, questioning his credibility and sincerity in advocating for the Depressed Classes, especially in comparison to Gandhi's well-known efforts.)
