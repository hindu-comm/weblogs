This piece is inspired by the recent backlash against the UGC regulations, and other comments have been made on this issue. The first piece was also inspired by a schism on the Indian Right between Raitas(Politics first) and Trads(Traditionalists) on a variety of issues. This piece is inspired by their sudden unity on the UGC regulations, which is admirable.

I appreciate the General Category for the successful abeyance of UGC regulations 2026, as they were the ones who successfully protested and went to the Supreme Court. I also acknowledge right-minded OBCs and SC/STs for not protesting the abeyance of such neurodivergent regulations, except, of course, the ideologically anti Upper Caste groups, not to say that not protesting in support of such pieces of regulations is praiseworthy, but such is the state of the discourse. These regulations were a monumentally moronic piece of regulations that could have ever been implemented in universities, **as of yet**. I expected that such protests would take place during the announcement of the caste census, but like Lord Hanuman required assistance to remember his strength, so did the GCs, for the good of us all, they finally remembered their strength.

Coming to the regulations, it would have been disastrous for these simple reasons:

• It would have resulted in the breakdown of cohesion in college campuses along caste lines.

• It would empower bad-faith actors who could use the powers granted to them within these regulations to carry out personal vendettas.

• No punishment for the false cases being pushed against innocent students.

• The procedure for collecting evidence for the supposed discrimination against a student , and in what manner it was committed would lead to a bureaucratic nightmare which would stall the proper working of the university system due to the bureaucracy having to work on such cases, instead of performing their other functions.

• The equity squads being created which would function in every way like the Stasi

I would also like to state that certain provisions were good for students**, but it is similar to saying that war is good for population control;** even the fruitful discussion that could have been had around good provisions was drowned out.

#### Philosophy Behind the Regulations

The central issue with these regulations is not whether they are good or bad, but the philosophy that is guiding them, which can only lead to such hare-brained policies and regulations even in the future. The two ideological idioms that led to such regulations are those of equity(Rawlsian Justice) and Lohiaism.

#### Equity- a false hope

Equity is equality of outcomes, which is by far the greatest evil that can ever be justified under the veneer of virtue. If outcomes between siblings who come from the same family and clan cannot be the same even after having access to equal resources, similar values, socio-economic conditions, and upbringing, to believe that the State can somehow equalize outcomes between different sections of society is imbecilic. It is not only a bad joke because it is a burden on State resources due to utopian nonsense, but also because when the outcomes do not equalize, it breeds resentment within different sections of society.

The policies taken to overcome the difference in outcomes, such as reservations or positive discrimination as John Rawls defined it, will never lead to any positive outcomes for the larger society, as the government jobs where these reservations are implemented are minimal in both quantity and their qualitative outcome on societal inequality. John Rawls theory is based on the idea of fairness, but fairness is not a quantitative value but a qualitative one, which cannot be measured; it is similar to the privilege of an individual being used as an argument to dismiss valid arguments, as even privilege cannot be measured. To use such non-measurables to make State policy that leads to large-scale State-led intervention in society is asinine at best and malignant at worst. Robert Nozick’s argument against the Rawlsian theory of justice in his book, “Anarchy, State, and Utopia,” on the issue of justice is a welcome intervention, even though I have certain disagreements with his theory of justice, as in some places it contradicts what is considered natural justice around the idea of a minimal state.

#### Lohiaism-Arsonist’s Justice

Lohiaism as an ideology, philosophy, and policy framework deserves a set of articles; **there has never existed such a dumpster fire of an ideology** that not only contradicts its stated goals but exacerbates the issues it seeks to correct. It is what provided the ideological and philosophical bedrock for OBC and EWS reservations. It will astound some readers to see that I have equated OBC and EWS reservations, but this requires a certain level of understanding of the environment in which Lohiaism was born; it was born in Bihar, where poverty was very much a soul-crushing reality. EWS reservations were borne out of the discussions of Bharat Ratna KarpooriThakur and Shri Ram Manohar Lohia. Lohia’s philosophy was anti-elitist and Hegelian; he wanted to unite the Bahujans. The striking quality here is that his Bahujans included everyone except the upper class, upper caste, and rich English-speaking elites of New Delhi. He wanted to destroy their hegemony built around the Nehruvian ideology and the State. His Bahujans included even poor upper castes. His idea of OBC reservations for Shudras was based on the idea of them being poor and in a weirdly “socially backward” state. The myth of Shudras being socially backward as a whole came about due to a scriptural understanding of Hindu society, and not the ground reality, which was stated with evidence by GS Ghurye in his book, “Caste and Race in India,” and by MN Srinivas in his works, “Dominant Caste and Other Essays” and “The Remembered Village”. Them being economically backward was a reality, but that was the reality of most of India due to colonization and then, due to our mixed economy model, which became increasingly socialistic and sapped our economic strength. OBC’s full form is not*Other Backward Castes* but *Other Backward Classes*, this is why Muslims get reservations within them, like they do within EWS. OBC reservations are deployed not for correcting historical wrongs but to ensure that certain communities can be used as reliable vote banks in perpetuity. That is why Jats of Haryana do not get reservation within the Haryana State list for OBCs but they do in Uttar Pradesh’s state list as they are a valuable vote bank in Western Uttar Pradesh. The same is true for many Brahmin groups who get OBC reservations in certain Southern and Eastern States.

I would propose dissolving OBC reservations by 10% and adding the leftover percentages in EWS reservation, but that is not going to happen. I would also like to State that **OBC reservation is Savarna reservation**. Ideally, no reservation should exist as stated by veteran Hindu Dalit Mahasabhaiteleader, President of Berar Hindu Mahasabha, Ganesh AkkajiGavai, but in present condition only SC/ST reservation should exist as they are for representation which is required given the historical reality of untouchability and other impediments it led to for the larger community and the nature of large number of individuals towards their communities to ensure their civil liberties and rights are not taken away under conditions of a super majority until we reach a level of urbanization and industrialization which makes certain divisions redundant at a policy and societal level that rights and liberties of no community will come under threat of an anachronistic force.

The solution to most of these problems will come with:

• Industrialization

• Entrepreneurship

• Culturally Cohesive third spaces (Bhajan Clubbing has some problems but it is a workable example)

• Urbanization

I will now comment upon certain topics which are of special interest to me.

#### Inter Caste Marriage

State encouragement of ICM through policies should cease. This is not because I believe they have bad intentions, but it is disgusting, not because Inter-Caste married couples are evil (my parents are not evil), but because it is putting a price on a sacred institution. The same way Dowry is evil because it puts a price on marriage, so is this; whatever the intention, it results in practically the same thing, and given the history of certain anti-caste movements, this becomes even more disgusting. Making any man or woman of any community and their relation some sort of public good is dystopian, and such a policy should go lock, stock, and barrel. Such policies also make such couples’ existence and the existence of their progeny political, which is not acceptable. Protection against societal reprisal can be provided in the form of security or safe havens, but no policy that results in societal resentment against such couples due to monetization of the institution of marriage, even though such has been the history of such a sacred institution for centuries, but the State cannot be a party to it.

#### Caste based scholarships

They are much better than reservations as they help in ensuring the skilling of individuals and ensure a level playing field before examinations for certain educational admissions but such policies will be helpful even if they were based on economic standards given that poverty is higher within the lower castes. But at one point the State can only do so much and the work needs to be done within the larger Hindu community and even the specific communities which are both affected by economic degradation. At the same time, I have to remind people that all these policies will not be able to bring forth an ideal form of equality as it is just not an attainable ideal or reality.

#### OBCs- are they a community?

Given that the movement for this started in Bihar, Lorik Sena, Bhumi Sena and Kuer Sena were caste based militias while most of these militias came into existence to counter Naxals, they routinely engaged in targeted killings of other caste militias members. Most such militias were upper caste in nature, but clashes between them were also routine in certain places. Therefore, I would like to state that both intra GC and intra OBC unity exist only on the internet and not on the ground except for certain fleeting moments. While inter OBC and GC unity has existed on the ground for political reasons.

#### To OBCs- a way forward

If it is a belief among a certain section of OBCs (there is no OBCs, **the only community we rightly belong to is the Hindu community**, being honest) that to rise, somebody needs to be pulled down. That is not true. We will rise when India rises, and for that to happen, we do not need to pull others down but to ensure that we help our great civilization rise. By virtue of our being a majority and the structure of the Indian polity, it will lead to our rise. We need to let go of imagined resentment and strive towards excellence; that has always been the only way. Maa Saraswati has never abandoned us and never will. She is such a caring mother that within one generation, her worship and hard work based on her ideals change the destiny of whole clans, my clan being one of them. Focus on raising yourselves, not on pulling others down.

**Once again, I would like to congratulate the Hindu community and in particular to the General Category on the abeyance on UGC regulations, it should be continued until complete rollback and the implementation of UGC 2025 regulations which were more sane and did not add the regulations state by Indira Jaising.**
