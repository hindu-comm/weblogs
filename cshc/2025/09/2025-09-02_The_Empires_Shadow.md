The current fracas of the Indian American relationship has left many a people stunned. How is it that the oldest and largest “democracies” of the world are not aligned, one may ask. What drives them to behave the way they do? And why is it that Pakistan remains a cynosure of the Anglo Saxon Empire of the world despite its multiple double crosses?

Many memes, essays, arguments have come out, and word salads have been created. However, those who have bothered to look at the civilizational and historical aspects of the world will tell you that there is nothing new in this behaviour. In fact, one could argue, that it is foolish to think otherwise, since the soft corner of the Anglo Saxon, Christian empire towards Muslims has existed for long, and will continue to drive the way “unwashed idolator heathens” will be perceived, even if hidden inside the lens of “modernism”, “values”, “secularism” and what not. One has to remember of course that all these terms were responses of Christian societies to their own overreaches, and had nothing to do with “people like the damned heathens”.

During the 19^(th) century, as the British had started to understand India better to “govern it better”, a major encounter it had was with the question of the presence of Muslims in India. Of course, the Christian empires’ attempts to engage with Christianity. Of course, the love was not lost among each other, given the memory of the Crusades and the subsequent Ecumenical Council of Vienna (AD 1311-1312) decision to have "have an abundant number of Catholics well versed in the languages, especially in those of the infidels, so as to be able to instruct them in the sacred doctrine." This had led to the creation of chairs of Hebrew, Arabic, and Chaldaean at the Universities of Bologna, Oxford, Paris, and Salamanca, essentially laying the seeds for the Treaty of Westphalia.

A classic example of the hatred could be seen in the way the Christians traversing across India in the sixteenth century portrayed the country. [Sitaram Goel](https://voiceofindia.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/history-of-hindu-christian-encounters-ad-304-to-1996-sita-ram-goel.pdf) had highlighted the accounts of Italian traveller, Ludovico di Varthema, from Bologna in highlighting the perception of heathenism from a Christian perspective. During his time in South India between AD 1503 and 1508, Varthema documented an incident of the Raja of Calicut paying respect to a “devil” known as “Deumo”, who was “made of metal” and had “four horns and four teeth with a very large mouth, nose and most terrible eyes.” Its hands were “made like those of a flesh-hook and the feet like those of a cock.” Word spread around in Europe later, leading to figurines of the Devil coming up. This was followed by the Portuguese entry into India and infamous inquisitions of Goa and elsewhere across the Indian coastline. The objective was and continues to be driven by the belief of the Second Coming of Christ, which continues to remain impossible till “everyone is a Christian”.

Contrast this with the way the Christian society of the West encountered Islam. The Catholic empires, and then Anglo Saxon Empire essentially led by Protestant Missions, took upon themselves to seriously study Islam. For instance, the study by the orientalist and Church Minister David Samuel Margoliouth had chosen to look at Islam for a "tolerant" audience and chose to observe towards the prophet "the respectful attitude which his greatness deserves". What emerged out of this study was a grudging acknowledgment verging on admiration of this system. As [Ram Swaroop](https://voiceofindia.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/hindu-view-of-christianity-and-islam-ram-swarup.pdf) had quoted Rev. Charles Forster, a clergyman of the Church of England, and author of Mohammedanism Unveiled (1829) in his book, Islam began to be viewed, despite its licentious and devilish nature, "confessedly superior" to the gross idolatry of its predecessor, even having a place inthe divine scheme. Ram Swaroop noted the following words of Forster

> *"...pre-appointed scourge of heresy and heathenism, as cleansing the
> world from the gross pollutions of idolatry, and preparing the way for
> the reception of a purer faith \[Christianity\], it may well be
> regarded as a blessing."*

While other authors asked for an even liberal treatment towards Islamists, the Christian theologists discovered, much to their discomfort, the similarity of theology. For instance, theologist William Muir, who wrote the four volume seminal work “The Life of Mahomet” in 1861 clearly saw the parallels and noted that:

> *“...Mohammedanism acknowledges the divine original, and has borrowed
> so many of the weapons of Christianity, that it is so dangerous an
> adversary."*

The reasons for these were of course rather straightforward. The affinities being high, Missionary scholars felt that converting the Muslims to Christianity would be rather , and this had great supporters including the high and mighty like Sir Robert B. Edwardes, Commissioner and Governor-General's Agent at Peshawar.

These views continued well into the worldview of the Anglo Saxon Empire, and was very much the mainstream. Considering the Muslims as the “superior martial race”, the discredited Martial Race Theory sought to include them into the Imperial Indian Armed forces in big numbers. The Muslims were also not subject to social reform in the same manner as the Hindus by the British imperialists - there was no Sharda Act equivalent for Muslims because the affinity with the Christian theological principles implied that any criticism would open them to scrutiny as well. The British needed to work on Muslims in a genial manner was a rather mainstream thought in the post 1857 era. [In 1871, Sir William Hunter had in fact in his book “The Indian Musalmans](https://ia902800.us.archive.org/32/items/PeterHardyTheMuslimsOfBritishIndia/the-muslims-of-british-india_1.pdf)” had sought to demonstrate that the Muslims were not interested in overthrowing the British since their religious rights were protected, which influenced Lord Mayo, the then Governor General of India, to conduct an outreach towards Muslims. This position continued despite Lord Mayo’s assassination by a Muslim fanatic Shir Ali, even to the extent of sympathy and discrimination for hiring more Muslims across the bureaucracy, seeking to have a force “malleable” to their interests.

This malleability played well into the twentieth century. While the arguments of Winston Churchill are well known with respect to the Hindu versus the Muslim in India, the attitude of the British towards the Islamic world at large was certainly more sympathetic than towards India. For instance, Katherine Mayo in her pamphlet “Mother India” had left no stone unturned in seeing Muslims as superior and having a global empire. In [Mayo’s words](https://dn790001.ca.archive.org/0/items/motherindia035442mbp/motherindia035442mbp.pdf):

> *“The Muslim comprises but a bare quarter of the population of British
> India. But that percentage is growing. His gains indicate both
> superior fecundity and superior vitality. His brain is not quick, but
> he has often a gift of horse sense. He is beginning to see that he
> must go to school. Granted time, opportunity and a sense of security,
> he may wipe out his handicaps and fit himself for full participation
> in the administration of the country. Thrown into the arena today, he
> would see but one recourse the sword. And it should never for a moment
> be forgotten that when the Muslims of India draw the sword, it will
> not be as an isolated body but as the advance line of an energy now
> banked up, like the waters of a brimming reservoir, by the Frontier
> Defense of the Army.”*

In policy terms, Sir Arthur Balfour, as Foreign Secretary, had argued that the Arab states [should be independent and ‘live their own life in their own way](https://shs.cairn.info/revue-histoire-politique-2010-2-page-7?lang=fr)’. Balfour had also argued that that these ‘would be under the patronage, and for certain purposes under the control’ of the ‘superintending Powers’ in an indirect reference to the British overlordship, claiming that this overlordship would not be unusual or contrary ‘to the immemorial customs and traditions of this portion of the Eastern world’. The creation of Pakistan very much fit into this malleability paradigm as well, when we put into context [Narinder Singh Sarila’s argument](https://archive.org/details/shadowofgreatg00sari) of the Anglo Saxon empire creating a foot for itself in South Asia to keep an eye on then Soviet Union and the associated Communist threat.

It is these very attitudes that also had a strong influence on American attitudes towards “HIndu India”. The mainstreamed racism against the Hindus and their homeland was experienced firsthand by Swami Vivekananda, but it was not to be a once off story. The [1923 case of Bhagat Singh Thind](https://www.pbs.org/rootsinthesand/i_bhagat2.html)and the rejection of his naturalization remains a beacon of how Indians were viewed in the United States of America. However, even during this two decade period, the ideological clarity was there. Theodore Roosevelt in 1904 had legitimized British colonial rule of India by comparing it with American rule in the Philippines during a speech to the American Congress. [1907 saw major riots against “Hindoos” in Bellingham and other parts of America](https://www.infinityfoundation.com/mandala/h_es/h_es_korit_histical.htm), while [American newspapers of the time ran amok](https://www.opindia.com/2024/12/racist-attacks-hinduphobia-and-targeting-of-india-as-a-hindu-collectivity-how-the-debate-over-h-1b-visas-laid-bare-deep-seated-anti-hindu-sentiment-in-the-us/) with rhetoric that was meant to instigate violence against Hindus in general. These views persist to this day in the mainstream consciousness of American society - notice how in two recent podcasts, American media personality Tucker Carlson casually brought in racism about “Indian motel owners” to [hold them responsible](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DE2AVar-XNc) for the growth of the Chinese drug mafia in Maine. Contrast this with the way [he and a nun working in Palestine showed Hamas in a relatively positive light](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y79CfG2R_3g) despite having carried out a horrific incident in October of 2023. Similarly, it remains a fact that the rise of the mujahideen in Afghanistan and the way Al Nsura was used as a proxy in Syria is an extension of this thread of malleability. The idea that Israel is needed, as Tucker Carlson also highlighted, remains important for the Christian mission, given how it is an important first step for the return of Christ, particularly for many Evangelical denominations that are extremely active in the United States.
