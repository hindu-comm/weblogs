### An Interesting Study in India’s Economic and Industrial Scenario

Whenever I study the Indian economy and industrial scenario, the city that amuses me the most is Pune. Every day, you would hear about a firm opening its manufacturing unit or a company establishing its global capability centre (GCC) in Pune. The case of Pune is very interesting as it excels and leads in all 3 sectors of the economy, from agriculture, manufacturing, and services. You can call it the most 'versatile city of India' by looking at the number of industries where it has a dominant contribution.

So, let's dive in and understand Pune's economy sector by sector:

## **Manufacturing**

Pune is recognized as the manufacturing powerhouse of the country. It is a leader in heavy industries, including engineering goods (automobiles included), pharmaceuticals, food processing, defense, and aerospace. Additionally, Pune has a considerable industrial base in electronics manufacturing.

The reason behind Pune's dominance in these industries is the proactive policymaking and facilitation of industrial development by successive governments in Maharashtra and the central government. Their main objective has been the decentralization of sectors within the state and the decongestion of industries from Mumbai.

The seeds for Pune's industrialisation were sown in 1954 with the establishment of Hindustan Antibiotics Limited (HAL) in Pimpri-Chinchwad, which laid the foundation for today’s thriving pharmaceutical industry in Pune.

In the 1960s, the Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (MIDC) established multiple industrial estates and clusters around regions like Pimpri-Chinchwad and Bhosari. This initiative not only created a robust infrastructure for industries but also eliminated land acquisition hurdles, making land readily available for companies.

Today, Pune is home to eleven MIDCs, which possess thousands of hectares of land in the district, dedicated to industrial clusters for automobiles, electronics, pharmaceuticals, data centres, and integrated logistics parks. The MIDCs provide plug-and-play infrastructure, including built-up sheds, with uninterrupted water and power supply.

MIDC also features a single-window approval system that combats rent-seeking behaviour and bureaucratic delays, allowing for faster approvals and land acquisitions for industries. Furthermore, MIDC has nurtured robust ecosystems for micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs) by reserving areas in industrial estates for them and offering financial support and cheaper credit facilities.

MSMEs now form the backbone of the automobile and defense industries by establishing ancillary units, thereby creating a comprehensive supply chain and ecosystem.

Additionally, Pune is known as the "Oxford of the East" due to the large number of educational institutions located here. The presence of numerous engineering colleges has played a key role in transforming Pune into an industrial powerhouse and R&D hub by providing a skilled workforce for various industries.

## **Automobile**

Pune is often referred to as the "Detroit of the East" due to the sheer volume of automobile manufacturing units located in the city. It is home to a wide array of manufacturers, including global giants like Mercedes-Benz, Volkswagen, and Toyota. Pune has over 4,000 automobile manufacturing and ancillary units, contributing 15-20% of the country's automobile production and Gross Value Addition (GVA).

The automobile sector in Pune is highly diversified, producing various types of vehicles, including:

- Passenger Vehicles (Cars):- Mercedes-Benz, Land Rover, Mahindra &
  Mahindra

- Commercial Vehicles (Trucks, Autos, Vans):-Piaggio, Bajaj, Tata Motors

- Electric Vehicles (EVs):- Bajaj, Mahindra & Mahindra, Tata Motors

Pune features multiple Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (MIDC) areas, with Chakan, Talegaon, and Chinchwad being particularly well-known for their automobile manufacturing ecosystems. The Chakan-Talegaon belt is considered one of the densest automobile manufacturing hubs in the world. Today, Chakan is a preferred location for many automobile manufacturing companies, thanks to its advanced infrastructure, Grade A industrial spaces, and logistics parks.

These industrial parks are also home to a significant number of advanced auto component manufacturers and Tier-1 suppliers such as Tata Auto comp, Sona Comstar, Bharat Forge, and Samvardhana Matherson International, which provide essential auto components to Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) like Tata Motors and Mercedes-Benz. This comprehensive supply chain makes it increasingly attractive for companies to invest in Pune.

Currently, Pune boasts a fully functional ecosystem for the automobile industry, including a robust research and development (R&D) framework. It is home to the Automotive Research Association of India (ARAI), making it the largest automobile R&D hub in the country. Several automobile research institutes and labs are based in Pune, offering services such as design, product development, and testing. Notable R&D institutions in the area include the Auto Cluster Research and Development Institute (ACDRI) and the Automotive Research Association of India (ARAI).

Multiple multinational corporations (MNCs) and OEMs have also established their R&D facilities in Pune, focusing on innovation and specialized services in areas such as powertrain development, intelligent mobility, and software development. Examples include Mercedes-Benz, FEV, and almost all major OEM manufacturers in India.

As a result of this robust ecosystem, new-age industries like electric vehicles (EVs) are emerging in Pune, with companies such as Mahindra & Mahindra, Tata Motors, Valeo, and Volkswagen setting up EV and EV components manufacturing plants in the region.

The successive governments of Maharashtra have played a crucial role in transforming Pune into an automobile manufacturing hub, providing world-class infrastructure, fostering early growth and development in the industry, and implementing policies such as the Maharashtra Electric Vehicles Policy (2021) and the Maharashtra Industrial Policy (2019). These policies offer special incentives and subsidies to automobile companies investing in the state.

## **Machinery**

Pune has established itself as a significant hub for industrial machinery manufacturing, driven by a strong base in diverse sectors such as automobiles, aerospace, pharmaceuticals, and electronics. The city is known for producing CNC machinery, precision tools, and special-purpose machines that serve various industries, including textiles, pharmaceuticals, cranes, and healthcare.

Key machinery manufacturing clusters in Pune include Bhosari MIDC, Ranjangoan MIDC, and Talegaon MIDC. Some of the prominent machinery manufacturing companies in Pune are FEV, TRUMPF, and PVR Industries.

## **Defence**

Pune has had ordnance factories since the British era; however, the establishment of factories and research and development by Defence Public Sector Undertakings (DPSUs) has played a major role in making Pune a key defense manufacturing hub in India.

The creation of the Armament Research and Development Establishment (ARDE) under the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958 marked Pune as a significant defence R&D centre in the country. ARDE is a core DRDO laboratory dedicated to innovation, armament development, and design. Notable weapons developed here include the Pinaka multi-barrel missile launcher, the DRDO Advanced Towed Artillery Gun (ATAGS), and the INASAS (Indian Small Arms System), all of which have been deployed by the Indian Armed Forces in various operations and wars.

Several DPSUs are present in Pune, including:

- Munitions India Limited (MIL): - Headquartered in Pune, MIL focuses on
  ammunition manufacturing.

- Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL):- A prominent defense electronics
  manufacturing PSU, BEL has a factory in Pune dedicated to producing
  batteries, electronic fuses, and electro-optics. In addition to
  supplying the Indian Armed Forces with munitions, BEL also focuses on
  exports, primarily to Europe.

Private defense manufacturers also play a crucial role in establishing Pune as a defense manufacturing hub. Some of the prominent companies with facilities in Pune include:

- Tata Advanced Systems (TAS):- TAS has dedicated facilities for
  manufacturing optronic systems, combat vehicles, and light armoured
  vehicles in Pune.

- L&T Defence: - L&T has a strategic systems complex in Talegaon, Pune,
  which serves as a cutting-edge facility for manufacturing and
  designing strategic weapons platforms, sensors, and military
  engineering systems.

Pune also boasts a robust network of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs). Today, it is home to more than 500 defense MSMEs, the highest number in any city in India, with these enterprises supplying ordnance and weapon components to DPSUs and large private manufacturers, effectively creating a supply chain.

According to a survey conducted by the Maharashtra Chamber of Commerce, Industries, and Agriculture (MCCIA), as much as 81% of defense MSMEs are projecting an annual growth rate of over 20% in the coming fiscal year, indicating robust growth within the sector.

The main drivers of growth in the defense industry are proactive government policies, increased procurement from indigenous companies by the armed forces, and a high demand for Indian defense products and components worldwide.

One key policy that has fostered growth in the sector is the Maharashtra Aerospace and Defence Manufacturing Policy (2018), which provides incentives such as discounted land rates and service charge waivers for mega defense units. This policy also incentivizes investment in ancillary units and promotes competitiveness among defense MSMEs.

Discussions are ongoing about establishing a defence industrial corridor in Maharashtra, with one defence manufacturing cluster planned for Pune. As the defence industry continues to grow, with exports expected to double by 2030, Pune is set to make a significant contribution to the advancement of India's defence industry.

## Pharmaceuticals

Pune is one of the largest pharmaceutical hubs in the country. The foundation for pharmaceutical manufacturing in Pune was established in the 1950s with the creation of institutions like the National Institute of Virology and the National Chemical Laboratory. These institutions propelled Pune into a significant research and development hub for pharmaceuticals. Subsequently, public sector undertakings like Hindustan Antibiotics Limited, the first manufacturer of antibiotics such as penicillin in India, helped Pune become a leading exporter of pharmaceutical products.

Today, after engineering goods (including automobiles), pharmaceuticals represent the largest export from the district. Pune houses five prominent pharmaceutical manufacturing clusters, with Pimpri and Chinchwad being the most renowned. The city is home to many leading national companies such as Wockhardt, Nicholas, and Intas, as well as respected international firms like Novo Nordisk, Aventis, and Abbott, which manufacture active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) and drug formulations for a diverse range of treatments and diseases.

In addition to APIs and drug formulations, Pune is the largest exporter of vaccines in the country and is home to the world's largest vaccine manufacturer, the Serum Institute. Multiple global brands, including Lupin and Sun Pharma, have established their research and development centers in Pune.

The future of Pune in the pharmaceutical sector looks promising. The Indian pharmaceutical industry is growing at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13%. As companies increasingly focus on manufacturing raw materials and key intermediates, such as key starting materials and APIs, Pune will play a crucial role in this shift.

## Electronics

Pune's electronics manufacturing sector is quite diverse, encompassing companies that produce consumer, industrial, and defense electronics. Electronic goods rank as the third largest export category for the city.

A dedicated Electronics Manufacturing Cluster (EMC) is located in the Ranjangaon MIDC area, which has established Pune as a significant hub for Electronics Manufacturing Services (EMS). Companies in this sector offer solutions in circuit design, prototyping, and PCB assembly, serving various industries such as automotive, industrial automation, and consumer electronics.

Pune is also home to multiple global brands and product design firms specializing in electronics manufacturing across various domains. Bharat Electronics Limited operates a military electronics manufacturing facility in Pune that designs and produces electronic fuses for both the Indian Army and export purposes.

SFO Technologies provides original design manufacturing solutions for diverse industries, including defense and healthcare, by designing and manufacturing hardware. They are collaborating with DRDO to develop high-energy laser systems for the Indian Army.

Jabil, a certified supplier for Apple, operates two plants in Pune, where they manufacture plastic casings for AirPods. The Motherson Group has a facility in Pune that produces industrial and automotive electronic components for multiple OEMs. Additionally, Syrma SGS Technologies manufactures PCB boards specifically for the automotive industry. Pune’s electronics manufacturing sector is quite diverse, encompassing companies that produce consumer, industrial, and defense electronics. Electronic goods rank as the third-largest export category for the city.

## Logistics & Warehousing

The scale of manufacturing growth in Pune is evident from the city's status as having the second-highest warehousing stock in the country, trailing only the National Capital Region (NCR). Additionally, Pune recorded the highest absorption of leased industrial spaces for two consecutive years (2023-2024), with approximately 75% of these spaces meeting Grade A specifications, the highest in the nation.

The industrial real estate market in Pune is expanding at an impressive compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 20% and is projected to surpass 140 million square feet by 2030.

Under the logistics policy for 2024, the Maharashtra government has proposed the development of a smart logistics hub in the Pune-Purandar region, covering an area of 5,000 acres and connected to the upcoming Purandar Airport. This logistics hub will include mega logistics parks, warehousing, storage facilities for industries, truck terminals, common freight stations, testing facilities, and an innovation hub.

## IT

The IT industry is the second-largest growth engine of Pune, following manufacturing. Today, Pune is the third-largest IT hub in India, behind Bengaluru and Hyderabad.

Pune's journey to becoming an IT hub began in 1981 when Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) established the Tata Research and Design Centre (TRDDC) as its first software research facility in the city. The establishment of the first Centre for Development of Advanced Computing (C-DAC) in the country in 1988 further propelled this growth. The C-DAC Pune center is credited with indigenously developing India's first supercomputer, PARAM.

The creation of the Rajiv Gandhi Infotech Park in Hinjewadi in 1998 made this growth unstoppable. This park, spread over 2,800 acres, attracted multinational giants like Infosys and Wipro and currently hosts more than 100 companies. Today, Hinjewadi is undergoing its fourth expansion, while Pune has become home to multiple IT zones, with prominent areas including Kharadi, Cybercity, and SP Info City.

The IT industry in Pune alone employs over 400,000 individuals, and the city has impressive export figures of ₹1,05,818 crore for FY 2023-24, which is double the figures from 2019. This makes Pune's exports the third-highest in the country, slightly behind Hyderabad. Global giants like Microsoft, Google, and Deloitte have their campuses in Pune, and almost every major Indian IT company has a presence there.

Pune is the birthplace of IT companies such as Persistent Systems, Cybage Software, and Hexaware Technologies. The IT sector in Pune is now witnessing a shift towards emerging technologies like machine learning, blockchain, the Internet of Things (IoT), and cloud computing, driven by new startups and global capability centers of multinational companies.

In 2024, Pune emerged as the leading startup hub in the country, with startups raising \$4.7 billion in funding across 373 deals. This achievement made Pune the fourth-largest startup hub in India, surpassing Hyderabad. Almost every day, a new Global Capability Center (GCC) is inaugurated in Pune.

Currently, Pune is home to more than 360 GCCs spanning various industries, including IT, Banking, Financial Services, and Insurance (BFSI), automotive, research and development (R&D), and analytics, employing over 80,000 people. GCCs are attracted to Pune due to its large talent pool of over a million students, cost advantages (with lower rents and salaries than other tier-1 cities), and a robust base of industries such as automobiles, pharmaceuticals, and IT.

By 2025, Pune will have 72 million square feet of Grade A office space out of a total gross stock of 106 million square feet. Pune is now the second-fastest-growing GCC hub, with over 5 million square feet of office space absorbed in 2024 alone. The city recorded the highest office space leasing by flexible space operators in the country, with nearly 18% of total leasing in 2024 coming from this sector.

In the first quarter of FY 2025, Pune recorded office space leasing of 3 million square feet, making it the third-highest in the country. This data indicates that Pune has become a favoured choice for companies looking to expand their operations in India. The IT industry is the second-largest growth engine of Pune, following manufacturing. Today, Pune is the third-largest IT hub in India, behind Bengaluru and Hyderabad.

## Agriculture & Food Processing

Maharashtra is the horticulture powerhouse of India and the largest fruit exporter in the country, with Pune making a significant contribution to this status. Pune stands out as a major exporter of high-value crops such as grapes, mangoes, pomegranates, and bananas.

Cooperative societies, along with the Maharashtra government, have played a crucial role in this development. Organizations like the Grape Growers' Association, Maha Grapes, and the Mango Growers' Association operate Farmer Producer Companies (FPOs) that provide export facilitation, branding, and financial assistance to farmers, helping them build capacity and market their produce. Additionally, many cooperative societies run training centers for farmers, focusing on skill development and the integration of modern technology into agriculture.

Maharashtra leads India in the formation of FPOs, with 1,000 registered across the state. The Maharashtra government has been instrumental in promoting FPOs and cooperatives by offering equity grants and credit guarantee schemes. Pune also boasts robust logistics, cold storage, and packaging facilities for farmers, with 12 cold chains—the highest in any district of Maharashtra.

Under the Maharashtra Logistics Policy (2024), the government is establishing a smart logistics hub in the Purandar region of Pune. This logistics park will feature warehousing and cold storage facilities for agro-industries. Furthermore, the Maharashtra government has approved the MahaAgri-AI Policy (2025-29) to transform the agricultural sector, supporting key initiatives such as Agristack, Maha-Agritech, Mahavedh, CropSAPP, and Agmarknet.

Pune is home to a thriving food processing industry, featuring world-renowned companies like Parle, PepsiCo, Ferrero, Britannia, and Tetra Pak. MIDCs like Ranjangaon host dedicated food processing clusters.

The Maharashtra State Food Processing Policy (2017) provides capital subsidies of 30% of the project cost (with a ceiling of Rs 50 lakh), corporate tax exemptions for export-oriented facilities, and investment-linked incentives that allow for a 100% deduction of capital expenditure for establishing and operating cold chain and warehousing facilities.

The combination of sector-specific policy initiatives, incentives, a cooperative culture, robust infrastructure, and a developed food and Agri-processing industry has established Pune as a well-developed agro-industrial hub in the country.

## Conclusion

As India aims to become the third-largest economy in the world, cities like Pune will play a crucial role in this growth. Pune can serve as a role model for developing cities in the country, demonstrating how to cultivate a diversified economy with significant contributions from the service sector, a strong industrial base, and modernised agriculture
