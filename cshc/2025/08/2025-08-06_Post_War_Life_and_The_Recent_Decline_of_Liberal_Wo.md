Recently I was listening to the song “Bangla Desh” where the very first line “ my friend came to me with sadness in his eyes” has always raised a curiosity in me and bugged me with questions such as “who is this friend”, “ why george wrote this song” etc. So, finally, sitting and stopping myself from mindlessly consuming mindless twitter drama and obscure fights, actually using google for something worthwhile once. I found that this “mysterious” friend was none other than Bharat Ratna Ravi Shankar, the great musician and composer and the song primarily was written for creating awareness and raising enough funds to provide a humanitarian intervention for the East Pakistan people who were devastated by the Bhola Cyclone in 1970 which finally culminated into the call for an independent movement by the Bengalis on 25th March 1971. One peculiar aspect is that this concert underscores the ideology which was affected by several key events which shaped and mold it and primarily evolved in the 60s. We are going to survey the various reasons and their outcomes which developed a sense of morality which tried to unite people through the rhymes of music. How post war institutions played an important role in developing this idea and finally the present day decline of these institutions and awaiting challenges.

## **60s and Counterculture Movement**

End of the war, an era of hope begins.

“This is the day we have been waiting……the war is over” are the words of the US President Truman when he officially declared the end of the war on August 14th, 1945. Celebrations began in America, most famous of all, the Time Square crowd where young men and women gathered and kissed creating one of the most iconic photos of the time. For the people, it was ushering in a new world, a world of hope and prosperity. A hope for a lasting peace. Of course, this dream saw its first dent pretty quickly as the era of cold war & nuclear age began somewhere in the spring of 1947. Korean war erupted where the new entities called superpowers US and USSR had one of their first greatest showdown. conflicts in south east Asia, Africa emerged, yet, the hope and a desire for peace and love was still taking its roots in the subconscious minds of the people.

## 50s Prosperity and Transformation

The 50s as a decade is one of the most transformatory periods in post war history. While 60s-80s were revolutionary and disruptive, its seeds were laid in what can be called an era of prosperity. Humans as a group arguably saw an exponential rise in the comforts and general living standards. Western world with the backing of the Marshall plan rebuilt itself. Suburban culture emerged in the US, with exponential increase in disposable income, people spent more, desired more, consumed more. One of the best accounts of this time is given by David Farber in his book “ the age of great dreams: America in the 1960s” where he notes how “a nation of fewer than 50 million families almost 60 million automobiles were registered”. That even the poor people in America owned television and a car.

Advancement of technology and race for becoming better created more opportunities. The rise of corporations which fueled the rise of consumerism,notably in the US , started gripping society. In midst of this was a new generation, peculiar in itself, the boomers. Termed as such due to the sudden increase in the birth rates, boomers were the first generation who tasted the fruits of the post war prosperity. What made them unusual compared to their predecessors was the consumption of electronic media. Baby boomers were the first generation which saw the advent of radios and television. While television and radio fueled the content they consumed and created a new culture of its own. Up until the rise of radios and televisions, the information was controlled by the parents and elders of the society, who actively enforced their views and morality on children. Beliefs, cultural norms and values were respected and limitedly criticised.Even the criticism was largely confined to the academic and upper class spheres. Society largely was still following the time and tested norms. Radios and television changed that. Programs, surveys, talk shows conducted on them influenced the young minds. Individualism, self-expression became more and more prominent. Suddenly, a new sense of morality emerged which saw anything related to the already established norms as an outpost of establishment, bourgeois and upper class.

## 60s And Rise of Counterculture

With record breaking admissions in higher education, people became more aware about the various theories and critiques of the establishment, but what really revolutionised this generation was their sense of solidarity. Electronic media created culture that cut across the barriers of class, region, ethnicity, race. Favorite movies, artists, musicians became the binding knots for the children. They realised that their thoughts, ideas, beliefs aligned more with their peers rather than their own family members or the people belonging to their class. All these above circumstances had laid the groundwork for an explosion. People were boiling with new ideas, new sense of morality. Influence of socialism and marxism establishing a place in the academia, intellectuals creating new narratives and theories which were critically examining and deconstructing the society with new lens and their influence have touched upon every section of the curiosity driven young adults. Arts, literature, architecture have developed on these new ideas and have started creating their own new themes. Music which was once confined to the realm of emotions and occasional criticism has been transformed into a new entity. A new form of intellectual movement which fused music with the tradition of literature. Bob Dylan, one of the pioneers of this new tradition effectively fused the poetic aspects with the musical ones. All this needed was a spark. That spark came through the failed Bay of Pigs invasion, Cuban missile crisis, civil rights movement and the event which ignited the fire “ Vietnam war”. Arguably the first televised war, it changed the public perception of their government and elites. Growing dissatisfaction, a sense of betrayal by the elders of the social order finally paved the way for the explosion which now came to be known as the "counterculture movement”. Artists, musicians broke the barriers, openly became critical of the government betrayal. Campaigns, road shows, and awareness programs led by them were enthusiastically supported by the college, school going masses. Suddenly this integration gave rise to what later came to be known as “ Woodstock Nation”, a phrase coined by activist Abbie Hoffman which became a popular term for the people who were associated with the counterculture movement. Artists like Bob Dylan, bands like Rolling Stone, Creedence Clearwater Revival, Black Sabbath blasted the hypocrisy of the governments, elites and highlighted the meaninglessness of the war.Lyrics like “it ain’t me, it ain’t me, i am not a fortunate son” from Fortunate Son, "politicians hide themselves away, they only start the war” from war pigs directly attacked the establishment while songs like “Paint it Black” which talked about loss and grief resonated with the soldiers and the families. Anti-war protests brought spiritual and eastern religious influence on the western society at its peak. John Lennon’s “ Give peace a chance” became rallying cries.The Hippie movement whose ideals called upon people to reject mainstream and consumerist values for peace, love and freedom took its roots which further influenced the music and created a new genre "Psychedelic Rock” which became a new mainstream sensation. Bands like Pink Floyd, Beatles, singers like Jimi Hendrix, Janis Joplin became prominent names. David Bowie in “The man who sold the world” highlighted the selling self and values for materialist gains which later created the grounds for the loss of identity, a sense of meaninglessness. By the late 60s, music had been transformed from mere entertainment to an intellectual statement of expression.

## Bhola Cyclone, Ethiopian Famine and awareness, aid concerts & campaigns

In the previous section we walked through the transformation of music and how the circumstances surrounded it changed its nature. In the beginning of the 70s, music, notably rock music including its multiple genres, reached a new level of popularity. Singers became larger than life itself for people. Commanding fan following in millions cutting across culture, race, religion. This is where in my opinion, a new international phase of the music began where artists and singers unknowingly or knowingly became the voice of the marginalised nations and groups. Since the 50s, decolonisation has been going on and new nations emerged in the continents of Asia and Africa with their own unique challenges and struggles. One common area where they all converge was the lack of funds and necessary extractable resources or the technology to exploit them to kickstart the programs for lifting up their underprovished society. Political instability and their own government’s apathy complicated the entire process. With it came disasters and tragedies. One such being the “Bhola Cyclone” which hit then East Pakistan on 12th November 1970 and ravaged the entire region leaving with an estimate of 500,000 people dead and directly affected almost 3.6 million people. Apathy of the government and inadequate response worsened the crisis, political unrest and growing agitation which led to then the West Pakistan government launching Operation Searchlight gave way for a call for independence on 26th March, 1971 by Sheikh Mujibur Rehman. West Pakistani government cracked down was hard which cumulated into a genocide. While all this was happening, the majority of the established media and the western governments were silent. This silence was highlighted in the famous “blood telegram” where US government's own bureaucrats & officials who were posted in the Pakistan called out the moral bankruptcy of their government.

As the crisis kept worsening, Ravi Shankar, famous composer who was distressed with the state of affairs, asked his friend George Harrison to help raise awareness and funds. George, while accepting his inability to understand and feel this disaster wholly, agreed and in July released the song “Bangla Desh” to raise awareness for the Bengalis. The concept of Bangladesh and the lack of its understanding and awareness in the western world can be understood from the fact that Harrison didn’t use the word “Bangladesh” instead used “bangla desh”. It was the first song made for the charity and three days later on 1st of August George and Shankar gathered up famous musicians like Ringo Starr, Bob Dylan and Ali Akbar Khan, and did a benefit concert to raise funds. According to the data on Wikipedia I was able to find, “Concert for Bangladesh” live album and films raised an estimated value of \$12 million dollars by 1985. This one of its kind contest started what today we call the “benefit concerts”. Fifteen years later, building on this new form of “activism”, a larger and perhaps the biggest one in the history was organised by Bob Geldof and Midge Ure in 1985 when Ethiopian famine ravaged the country and left 300,000 to a million people dead. Featuring more than 70 artists including David Bowie, Queen, U2 etc. the event was broadcast to an estimated 1.5 billion viewers spreading across 100 countries. This was the peak of the process which began in the 60s and in this event the power of music to influence and bind people for a common cause reached its furthest end. While George's concert was first of its kind, Live Aid established and cemented the idea inspiring future artists to recreate it and spread their message. Now, artists, musicians and celebrities from every part of the globe, industry have been using this medium for many causes which have taken a broader form and includes campaigns for illness and other tragedies. Recently, FireAid was organised by top stars in the US to raise funds for people who were affected by the 2025 California fires. The movement which began in the 60s undergoing several transformations has effectively cemented itself as an important force today.

### **UN Programs, USAID, US led Liberal Order**

We have talked about how music and the change in the morality of the people effectively started new forms of politics and also contributed to some humanitarian programs. Therefore, it is imperative to talk about the institutions which were behind in actual distribution and their effective implementation. Foremost of them all is the United Nations which was built just after the end of world war 2. Built for better cooperation and with an informal aim of overcoming the failures of the League of Nations, United Nations was one of the first responses of the US led Liberal order whose primary objective was institutional building to create an international rule based order. This rule based order laid emphasis on a global cooperation and promotion of peace. Many programs which began under the UN were directed towards these goals, one of them being humanitarian assistance. War has devastated almost the entire Europe and South East Asian countries and has left millions of refugees. Therefore, bodies like UNICEF, UNHCR were created to provide relief for the people. This institutional building and programs were an immediate success and many independent countries became part of it who saw it as an opportunity to ease their challenges in developing better standards of life for the people. In the 50s and 60s, as morality shifted, the idea of universal human kind became more prominent. People from affluent countries, mostly from the western hemisphere, contributed immensely to the cause. Gradually, people and the government realised the power this assistance and intervention has changed people’s life and more importantly their thoughts and views.

#### **Humanitarian intervention as foreign policy tools**

As the cold war began, both the US and the USSR realised that such interventions are needed not just for war affected countries but also the newly independent countries. What UN programs and intervention has shown to them was an opportunity to not only change the lives of millions of people but more importantly their thoughts. They came to a conclusion that one of the effective ways of spreading their ideology all over the globe is through these interventions. Not just through the UN, but separate, their own efforts. Efforts which will showcase their countries’ might subtly and more importantly their humane nature. USSR started its own programs for furthering its foreign policy goals. This article’s focus is primarily on the program started by the US, that is, the most famous one - USAID.In 1961, the US president founded the “United States Agency for International Development” whose mission was to promote global development and humanitarian assistance that supports U.S. foreign policy goals. It was an effective soft power counter to the threats of communism spreading across the world. USAID objectives ranged from disaster relief, conflict reduction to poverty reduction and promoting democracy and governance across the globe. The idea was a unique one. Up until that time, interventions from the western countries or any foreign country for that matter in any sphere which involves government was seen as an attempt of colonialism or imperialism. Therefore, though it seems rewarding it has the potential to back fire as well. Primary interests of the US administration back then lay in South America, Africa and parts of Asia mostly which were newly independent and were either loosely or not associated with both Western and Eastern Bloc. Throughout the 60s, one of the first major countries which became the beneficiary of the USAID was India. The US was already supporting India through the PL- 480 program ( Food for peace) contributing massively in order to meet the demand of the country's population. USAID later expanded the program and managed it. One of the contributions was helping in the introduction of High Yield Variety seeds, a program which later came to be known as the Green revolution. Emerging economies who later became the Asian tigers like South Korea and Taiwan became the major beneficiaries as well. A key aspect of the policy of USAID was that every region and the country it targeted had its specific objectives. For example, In the Indian subcontinent, more emphasis was laid on agriculture & food security, SEA saw more focus on industrial development and infrastructure. Through these programs, by and large the US government was able to create a positive image and more importantly found a way to influence the decisions of the governments. Therefore, it also became a tool to strong arm countries. During wars with Pakistan, the US government actively threatened to cut down the USAID to India. It temporarily suspended economic aid during the 1971 war and saw a variety of periods of reduction and cuts whenever US policies didn't align with India. Similar was the case in the Middle East and Africa. The same way it threatened its allies to reduce military aid. As recently as 2018, the Trump administration cut nearly 300 million in aid to Pakistan. Similarly, the Obama administration cut aid to Egypt. One of the ways it used its program to influence government policies was by funding various NGOs and several non governmental institutions. NGOs in India for example have been accused of using the aid money to manufacture artificial protests which has led to several delays in some key and important projects in India. In some areas, there has been a correlation between USAID funded agencies and anti-India activities. Activities whose aim either was to malign India’s image or directly affect its interests. Therefore, USAID though its positive history has been tainted with these negative aspects.

## **Decline of Liberal Order, Institutions Crumbling in the Present Times and Upcoming Challenges**

Before concluding, I would like to summarise whatever we have discussed up until now. The main aim of this article was to understand the circumstances and the outcomes of the post war era. 50s where it all began, the change of morality which gave rise to the counterculture movement. At the same time, the rise of the liberal order and institution building changed the dynamics of how countries interact with each other. Counterculture, which has music as its significant pillar, also played a subtle role in reaching the people and legitimising these institutions, especially the UN in the minds of western people. UN’s programs were supported and funded by many of the music giants. They campaigned for their awareness. While some of the proponents were against these very institutions and government in general, some of others while being critical of them were able to realise the utility of these institutions through which they were able to contribute in human development throughout the globe. In the 80s and 90s, the rise of the globalisation and gradual decline of the USSR led to expansion of these institutions and their reach. Fundings for the programs increased significantly. USAID was actively funding the programs which help in countering the growing concern of AIDS and some of the deadly diseases which affected some parts of the Africa continent. People too in general became more and more concerned about the well being of the population which lived thousands of miles away from them. Documentaries by various news channels and other such initiatives also helped in raising the awareness. Musicians, artists regularly conducted benefit concerts and raised funds. All in all, the idea of these institutions have been accepted by the majority of the population albeit with their own concerns and criticism. But, with the decline of the liberal order especially since 2008, these institutions and programs have come under series of criticism and have been called as a “wastage” or branded as “ stealing” from the honest taxpayers. With the rise of conservatism in Western nations, the idea that the beneficiaries who benefit from these programs are “ungrateful” and “ against the western values” have made a significant impact on the ideas of globalisation and the world coming together to help those who are in need. Recently, back in January, the Trump administration decided to temporarily freeze the operations of the USAID all over the globe except for limited humanitarian programs and military aid to Israel and Egypt. Later, at the end of March, the Trump administration had tried to entirely close the agency. There has been a great amount of criticism for this decision and according to some pundits, there are many legal shortcomings which are still to be resolved. For instance, back in Feb, federal court ruled that the order violates the congress powers of purse therefore, have ordered partial restoration of the funds which had been restored. The US President has also cut the funding of several UN humanitarian assistance as well. With the US retreating, the decline of this order has only accelerated. The values which originated in the US are increasingly finding themselves alien in its own home. Even the famous benefit concerts have generated a series of criticism by the people. Just a few days back, a benefit concert announced for Gaza garnered several negative reactions.

This new “change” of morality have emphasised more of an inward looking approach. One of the key aspect of the liberal order has been that its morality and its institutions grow together or infact, compliments each other. With the west especially US retreating, this significantly will reduce the numbers whose principles are governed by the liberal morality. Interestingly, this actually opens an opportunity for India. With our own past and policies, we have always been one the key contributors in the UN programs and several aid programs as well. We have supported countries like Afghanistan during its crisis, and have aided many disaster struck countries. Given our economy and own shortcomings, it would be a stretch to say India has the ability to actually fill this gap but, nevertheless, in my opinion, with our own ways and some specific areas, India actually can contribute and fill up some void created by the US. Conservative policies largely are guided by the concept of realism and nation first, therefore, naturally, India's first move should be to organise its own backyard and its own areas of struggle. As USAID contributed quite significantly in many NGO’s funding, there is a valid danger that its place might be taken by our enemies, primarily adversaries like China who also have the economic might and means to actually play the same role which USAID once played. Same situations might arise in other countries as well which in many ways will affect our interests in that region. For example, in Africa, China has been eyeing on their resources for a long time. This new development has given a one more opportunity and in many ways an edge over us. For instance, Countries like Congo which produces approximately 70-80% of the world's cobalt has received an average amount of 1 billion dollars of USAID funding over the years and now with 83% of the programs being cut, they have lost a significant amount of aid. Any intervention from China directly threatens our aspirations in industrialising our country and also impacts already existing sectors like smartphone manufacturing etc. Therefore, it becomes more vital for us to navigate through this restructuring of the world.
